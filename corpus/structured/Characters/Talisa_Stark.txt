<h2>Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Talisa was a noblewoman from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free City","link":"Free City","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free City]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/Free_City" title="Free City" class="mw-redirect">Free City</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a>, the oldest and largest of the Free Cities.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Talisa was 12, her parents went to a wedding lasting several days, leaving her with her younger brother. The second day they were gone, it was the hottest day in Volantis, so she and her brother joined the other children to go for a swim in the River <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoyne","link":"Rhoyne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/Rhoyne" title="Rhoyne">Rhoyne</a>. While she was talking with a friend, she noticed that her brother was missing. Eventually, she found him floating face down in the water. Talisa and her friend pulled him to the shore but he was dead. Suddenly, a man with a tattoo of a fish on his face (marking him as a slave) came and pushed her away. Had she reported him, it would have meant death for the slave to touch a noble. The slave kept on pushing on her brother's chest again and again until he was revived. As the slave cradled her brother and calmed him down, Talisa was inspired by the slave's selfless act. She decided two things right there and then: first, that when she came of age, she would not waste her years dancing with other nobles, and second that she would never live in a place where slavery was legal again. To this end, she sought and obtained medical training (rather than dancing with other nobles) and relocated to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> (slavery being outlawed in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>).<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"E18\">\"[[The Prince of Winterfell]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, there is no character named Talisa Maegyr, though one of the triarchs of Volantis is Malaquo Maegyr. Instead, Robb weds Jeyne Westerling, daughter of Lord Gawen Westerling, an impoverished but proud bannerman to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Casterly Rock","link":"Casterly Rock","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Casterly Rock]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/Casterly_Rock" title="Casterly Rock">Casterly Rock</a>. They meet when Robb captures <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Crag","link":"The Crag","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Crag]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/The_Crag" title="The Crag">The Crag</a>, an ancient and decaying stronghold which serves as the seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Westerling","link":"House Westerling","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Westerling]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/House_Westerling" title="House Westerling">House Westerling</a>. In the process of storming the castle Robb is wounded when he takes an arrow to the arm. Jeyne nurses him back to health and they grew close during that time. Robb is still there when word of the deaths of Bran and Rickon arrives (though <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Theon Greyjoy","link":"Theon Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Theon Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> was actually lying when he claimed they were dead). Jeyne comforts Robb in his distress, and they end up having sex together. As she is highborn and as Robb has taken her maidenhead he is not only honor-bound to marry her, but he has fallen in love with her. He does not suspect that the Westerlings collaborate with the Lannisters (as revealed in "A Feast for Crows"). Their assignment is to make Robb breach his pact with the Freys, and it is accomplished.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">All these events happen when Robb is in the west and are not revealed in the books until he returns with Jeyne to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71478248456cca5b33450c" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a>. Robb doesn't discuss breaking his marriage pact with Catelyn, and the marriage is not done in secret. Furthermore, Jeyne never becomes pregnant with Robb's heir and isn't present at the Twins during the "Red Wedding". Thus, she's alive and well afterwards, under the protection of Brynden Tully, who remains in Riverrun as Robb's Warden of the Southern Marches.
</p>
