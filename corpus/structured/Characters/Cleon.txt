<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Cleon was one of the thousands of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"slaves","link":"slavery","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[slavery|slaves]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-166351814456cca68438680" href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> liberated after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of Astapor","link":"Sack of Astapor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of Astapor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-166351814456cca68438680" href="/wiki/Sack_of_Astapor" title="Sack of Astapor">Sack of Astapor</a>.  He isn't called "Cleon the butcher" because he is very violent, but because he used to work as an actual butcher of meat (though he is also very violent).  
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-166351814456cca68438680" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Cleon was once a slave owned by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Grazdan mo Ullhor","link":"Greizhen mo Ullhor","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Greizhen mo Ullhor|Grazdan mo Ullhor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-166351814456cca68438680" href="/wiki/Greizhen_mo_Ullhor" title="Greizhen mo Ullhor">Grazdan mo Ullhor</a>, serving as a butcher. It was said he could cut up a pig faster than any other man in Astapor. After gaining his freedom, Cleon stirs up the people of Astapor, claiming that the council is attempting to reinstate the deposed <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Good Masters","link":"Good Masters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Good Masters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-166351814456cca68438680" href="/wiki/Good_Masters" title="Good Masters">Good Masters</a>. With the backing of the people, Cleon executes the council members and installs himself as king. At his command, every highborn boy in Astapor is seized to make new <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Unsullied","link":"Unsullied","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Unsullied]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-166351814456cca68438680" href="/wiki/Unsullied" title="Unsullied">Unsullied</a> for the trade.  Daenerys ruefully notes that she didn't end slavery in Astapor:  all that changed is that the old slaves are now the masters and the old masters are now the slaves.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He styles himself as "Cleon the Great", though he is often called "the Butcher King" (because he used to be a butcher).  The TV series added the detail that he styles himself as "His Imperial Majesty" - thus while he proclaimed himself "King Cleon" in the books, it isn't clear if his official title is "Emperor Cleon" in the TV series.
</p>
