<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Karl Tanner hails from Gin Alley in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Flea Bottom","link":"Flea Bottom","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Flea Bottom]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Flea_Bottom" title="Flea Bottom">Flea Bottom</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, where he was apparently revered as a legend, making a name for himself as a highly-capable enforcer, assassin, and cutthroat.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Oathkeeper]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Karl was eventually sent to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> and became a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ranger","link":"ranger","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ranger]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Ranger" title="Ranger">ranger</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. He takes part in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Ranging","link":"Great Ranging","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Ranging]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Great_Ranging" title="Great Ranging">Great Ranging</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">His fighting style favored speed and using small, fast weapons like daggers to quickly stab and deliver killing blows. This style reflected his background in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Flea Bottom","link":"Flea Bottom","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Flea Bottom]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Flea_Bottom" title="Flea Bottom">Flea Bottom</a> where he fought in tight, confined spaces and had to use small, easily-concealed weapons to avoid being caught by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"City Watch","link":"City Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[City Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">City Watch</a>.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Karl survives the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a>' <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"attack","link":"Battle of the Fist of the First Men","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Fist of the First Men|attack]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Fist_of_the_First_Men" title="Battle of the Fist of the First Men">attack</a> on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Fist of the First Men","link":"Fist of the First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fist of the First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Fist_of_the_First_Men" title="Fist of the First Men">Fist of the First Men</a>, and regroups with the remaining survivors as the ranging attempts to pull back to the Wall.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Valar Dohaeris]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When the Watch arrive at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Craster's Keep","link":"Craster's Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Craster's Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Craster's_Keep" title="Craster's Keep">Craster's Keep</a> seeking food and shelter, Karl is openly antagonistic towards <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Craster","link":"Craster","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Craster]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Craster" title="Craster">Craster</a>, accusing him of giving his pigs better food than them. Along with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rast","link":"Rast","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rast]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Rast" title="Rast">Rast</a>, he shows an interest in Craster's daughter-wives.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Walk of Punishment]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the death and funeral of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bannen","link":"Bannen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bannen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Bannen" title="Bannen">Bannen</a>, as tensions between Craster and the brothers of the Watch run higher, Karl insults Craster - calling him "a gods-cursed, daughter-fucking wildling bastard" - after the wildling demands the Watch to leave his hall and threatens to kill anyone who insults him. Craster charges against Karl with an axe but the black brother easily kills him by quickly closing the distance between them, blocking Craster's attack by grabbing the wrist of his weapon hand, and stabbing him in the throat with a dagger. Karl's action triggers a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"mutiny","link":"Mutiny at Craster's Keep","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mutiny at Craster's Keep|mutiny]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep" title="Mutiny at Craster's Keep">mutiny</a> that causes the death of Lord Commander Mormont and fight among the black brothers, in which Karl is tackled by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Grenn","link":"Grenn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Grenn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Grenn" title="Grenn">Grenn</a>. <img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[And Now His Watch is Ended]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Grenn and Edd arrive at Castle Black after escaping from their captivity at Craster's Keep. They reveal that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"external","text":"Karl","link":"http:\/\/gameofthrones.wikia.com\/wiki\/Karl","linktype":"text","wasblank":false,"wikitext":"[http:\/\/gameofthrones.wikia.com\/wiki\/Karl Karl]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07"  class="text" href="http://gameofthrones.wikia.com/wiki/Karl">Karl</a> is the ringleader of the mutineers, and that they are content with staying at the keep, eating Craster's food, and raping his wives and daughters.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"Breaker\">\"[[Breaker of Chains]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"\u200bJon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow|\u200bJon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">​Jon Snow</a> then points out that they know about the small garrison at Castle Black and proposes that they should be killed so they won't disclose it to the wildlings.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"Breaker\" \/>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Craster's Keep","link":"Craster's Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Craster's Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Craster's_Keep" title="Craster's Keep">Craster's Keep</a>, Karl drinks wine from the skull of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jeor Mormont","link":"Jeor Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jeor Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a>, drunkenly bragging about himself and his skill as a hired killer back in King's Landing before ordering Rast to "feed the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"beast","link":"Ghost","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghost|beast]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">beast</a>". One of Craster's former wives enters the Keep and presents Karl with Craster's last child, a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"son","link":"Craster's final son","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Craster's final son|son]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Craster's_final_son" title="Craster's final son" class="mw-redirect">son</a>. The women call for it to be given to the gods, which Karl realizes must refer to the White Walkers. Realizing that this was how Craster protected himself from the White Walkers, Karl orders Rast to take the child out into the forest, leaving it behind. Soon after, Bran, Jojen, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Meera","link":"Meera","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Meera]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Meera" title="Meera" class="mw-redirect">Meera</a> are captured and taken to Karl, who threatens to kill Meera and Jojen until Bran admits his true identity as Jon Snow's half-brother.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Oathkeeper]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Karl, called Clubfoot Karl, is a steward of the Night's Watch. Along with Chett, Lark the Sisterman, Paul, Dirk, Ollo Lophand, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Donnel Hill","link":"Donnel Hill","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Donnel Hill]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Donnel_Hill" title="Donnel Hill">Donnel Hill</a> and others, he had conspired to kill Lord Commander Mormont, Sam and other individuals and desert the Watch, but their plan was interrupted by the sudden snowfall and the subsequent White Walkers' <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"attack","link":"Battle of the Fist of the First Men","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Fist of the First Men|attack]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Fist_of_the_First_Men" title="Battle of the Fist of the First Men">attack</a>. He is among the traitors who mutiny against Lord Commander Mormont. However, Dirk - not Karl - is the one who kills Craster and takes one of his wives as hostage. Like the TV version of Karl, Dirk is a ranger.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Karl taunts Craster by calling him niggard and liar, and also speaks rudely to Mormont. However, he is not the leader of the traitors; he is not the one who provokes Craster by calling him "bastard"; and does not take active part in the pursuing brawl.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i>"A Dance with Dragons"</i>, five of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Betrayers","link":"Betrayers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Betrayers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Betrayers" title="Betrayers" class="mw-redirect">Betrayers</a> are killed by the mysterious person (who has not appeared so far in the show) that accompanies Bran and his escorts. Bran wargs into <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer","link":"Summer","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">Summer</a> and finds their bodies, one of them is presumed to be Ollo Lophand. It is unknown if Karl or Dirk are among the other dead four, or among the remaining Betrayers whose fate is not revealed.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">"Tanner" is the (likely unofficial) surname given to Lollys Stokeworth's newborn son, conceived when his unmarried, noble born mother was gang raped behind a tanner's shop during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riot of King's Landing","link":"Riot of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riot of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Riot_of_King's_Landing" title="Riot of King's Landing">Riot of King's Landing</a>. Any relation to the show's Karl Tanner is probably coincidental, as the common surname of noble bastards from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Crownlands","link":"Crownlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Crownlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Crownlands" title="Crownlands" class="mw-redirect">Crownlands</a> is Waters.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bryan Cogman","link":"Bryan Cogman","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bryan Cogman]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" href="/wiki/Bryan_Cogman" title="Bryan Cogman">Bryan Cogman</a> confirmed in an interview during Season 4 that the TV series writers were actually discussing back in Season 3 that they wanted to eventually follow up on the Night's Watch mutineers at Craster's Keep (as their fate was only alluded to in the books). This is why they cast a bigger, more experienced actor such as Burn Gorman to play Karl Tanner (who personally killed Craster in Season 3) because they wanted to return and expand on the character in Season 4. In the books, Craster and Mormont are killed by fairly minor characters, but Season 4 builds up that Karl was a dangerous hired assassin in Flea Bottom.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/winteriscoming.net\/2014\/05\/14\/game-owns-bryan-cogman-returns\/ ''Game of Owns'' Episode 219:  Bryan Cogman returns]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Cogman also spoke about how the TV series developed Karl in an interview with <i>MakingGameOfThrones.com</i>:
</p>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>"We've paid a lot of lip service to idea that Night's Watch is made up of shady characters, rapists and thieves, but we never saw much of that. The mutiny was the beginning. This is the worst of the worst finally free of the shackles of society...
</dd></dl>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>The original idea for Karl was more of a Colonel Kurtz-'Apocalypse Now' situation. The guys and I worked really hard on second draft and made it more about class, how this man feels he had a raw deal his entire life, and how he has real contempt for upper classes and society. He’s also very dangerous and probably a psychopath. So if he has a kingdom of his own, what would a guy like that do?
</dd></dl>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>For Karl to function, he needs weaker people to intimidate and do his dirty work. Although Rast is no saint, you see a glimmer of morality, and some kind of guilt for what's going on. But he's in so deep he can't get out of it, and he's also scared."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.makinggameofthrones.com\/production-diary\/2014\/4\/28\/interview-with-bryan-cogman MakingGameOfThrones.com]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175137136656cca5e522b07" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</dd></dl>
