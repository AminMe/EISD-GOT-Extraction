<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Ser Marston Waters was a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bastard","link":"bastard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[bastard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Bastard" title="Bastard" class="mw-redirect">bastard</a> from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Crownlands","link":"Crownlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Crownlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Crownlands" title="Crownlands" class="mw-redirect">Crownlands</a>. Eventually he became a knight and during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dance of the Dragons","link":"Dance of the Dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dance of the Dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Dance_of_the_Dragons" title="Dance of the Dragons">Dance of the Dragons</a> he joined the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a> of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II Targaryen","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II Targaryen</a>. He was present when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","link":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenyra Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Rhaenyra_Targaryen" title="Rhaenyra Targaryen">Rhaenyra Targaryen</a> was captured and fed to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragon","link":"dragon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon" class="mw-redirect">dragon</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sunfyre","link":"Sunfyre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sunfyre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Sunfyre" title="Sunfyre">Sunfyre</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Red Keep (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, nothing is mentioned of Ser Marston. He's briefly mentioned in <i>The Princess and the Queen" and his story is expanded in </i>The World of Ice and Fire<i>. Marston Waters was the bastard son of a knight and the sister of a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonstone","link":"Dragonstone (island)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone (island)|Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Dragonstone_(island)" title="Dragonstone (island)">Dragonstone</a> local known as Tom Tanglebeard.</i>
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Prior to the fall of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> to Rhaenyra, Ser Marston accompanied the fugitive Aegon II when he was spirited away from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a> by Lord Larys Strong, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Whisperers","link":"Master of Whisperers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Whisperers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Master_of_Whisperers" title="Master of Whisperers">Master of Whisperers</a>. Ser Marston and Aegon II took refuge in Dragonstone, where the knight, his uncle Tom Tanglebeard, and Marston's cousin, Tom Tangletongue, began to recruit men willing to aid Aegon II seize <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonstone","link":"Dragonstone (castle)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone (castle)|Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Dragonstone_(castle)" title="Dragonstone (castle)">Dragonstone</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ser Marston and the 'Two Toms' seized the castle but Lady Baela Targaryen managed to elude them, locate her dragon Moondancer and rise up on her to challenge Aegon and Sunfyre as they made their descent. After hitting the ground she dragged herself away from her dying dragon, Ser Alfred Broome, who had betrayed Dragonstone out of resentment for not having been named castellan, drew his sword to slay her but Ser Marston Waters wrenched the blade from his hand. For his service, Aegon II named Ser Marston to the Kingsguard and took part in the ambush of Rhaenyra. Like everyone else present, he kept silent when Aegon fed her to his dragon.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Afterward, Ser Marston accompanied Aegon II to King's Landing. He somehow retained his post after Aegon II was poisoned, presumably for saving the life of Lady Baela, granddaughter of Lord Corlys Velaryon, who was one of the seven regents for the child-king <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon III Targaryen","link":"Aegon III Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon III Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Aegon_III_Targaryen" title="Aegon III Targaryen">Aegon III Targaryen</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ser Marston somehow became <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-140967738556cca77fe58ba" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a> - though for a very short time - after Lord Thaddeus Rowan was arrested for alleged treason. Marston was eventually slain by his own sworn brother, Ser Mervyn Flowers, when Waters attempted to arrest him for the murder of Queen Jaehaera Targaryen. Some doubt that Mervyn killed her personally, but that if not, he probably allowed her to be assassinated by other agents - most think that either way, the assassination was masterminded by the regent Unwin Peake, who - despite commanding armies in service to Aegon II during the war - wanted his own daughter to marry Aegon III (though as it turned out, Baela convinced Aegon III to marry Daenaera Velaryon instead).
</p>
