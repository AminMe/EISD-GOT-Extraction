</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Galbart Glover</b> is a recurring background character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first season","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a>. He is played uncredited by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mark Coney","link":"Mark Coney","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mark Coney]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Mark_Coney" title="Mark Coney">Mark Coney</a> and debuts in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Pointy End","link":"The Pointy End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Pointy End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/The_Pointy_End" title="The Pointy End">The Pointy End</a>." Galbart Glover is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Deepwood Motte","link":"Master of Deepwood Motte","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Deepwood Motte]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Master_of_Deepwood_Motte" title="Master of Deepwood Motte">Master of Deepwood Motte</a> and a loyal bannerman to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Galbart Glover is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Deepwood Motte","link":"Master of Deepwood Motte","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Deepwood Motte]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Master_of_Deepwood_Motte" title="Master of Deepwood Motte">Master of Deepwood Motte</a> and the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Glover","link":"House Glover","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Glover]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/House_Glover" title="House Glover">House Glover</a>, a vassal family to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>. He is known as a loyal and reliable bannerman.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gregor Forrester","link":"Gregor Forrester","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Forrester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Gregor_Forrester" title="Gregor Forrester">Gregor Forrester</a> famously arm-wrestled Galbart Glover for the privilege of becoming the man's squire; Glover later joked that he'd gladly lose the match all over again.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Galbart Glover dines with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> and his bannermen as they assemble their armies to march south to confront the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannisters","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister|Lannisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannisters</a> and free Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Umber","link":"Jon Umber","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Umber]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Jon_Umber" title="Jon Umber">Jon Umber</a> objects to Glover being placed in command of the vanguard in the order of battle, to the point of threatening to leave and take his troops with him. Robb Stark's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"direwolf","link":"Direwolves","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Direwolves|direwolf]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Direwolves" title="Direwolves">direwolf</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Grey Wind","link":"Grey Wind","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Grey Wind]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Grey_Wind" title="Grey Wind">Grey Wind</a>, rips off two of the Greatjon's fingers in response. The Greatjon laughs off the incident and accepts his place in the army. Later, Galbart Glover joins Robb's strategy meetings as the army marches onward.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Pointy End]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the victorious <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","link":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Whispering Wood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Whispering_Wood" title="Battle of the Whispering Wood">Battle of the Whispering Wood</a>, Robb holds a meeting with his generals as they decide whether to accept <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon|Renly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly</a> or <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a> as king. Galbart angrily says that Renly is not the true king, agreeing with Robb's assertion that Renly is the younger brother. A few minutes later he joins the Greatjon and other lords in declaring Robb as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King in the North","link":"King in the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King in the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">King in the North</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Fire and Blood]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, Galbart Glover is the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Glover","link":"House Glover","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Glover]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/House_Glover" title="House Glover">House Glover</a> of Deepwood Motte. Their lands are deep in the Wolfswood to the north-west of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> and extending along the coast of the Bay of Ice, south of Bear Island. Galbart is noted as a good lord and a loyal vassal. Being childless, his heir is his younger brother Robett.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Before going to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>, Robb commands Galbart and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maege Mormont","link":"Maege Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maege Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Maege_Mormont" title="Maege Mormont">Maege Mormont</a> to take men by longship and sail through the marshes of the Neck to seek out <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Howland Reed","link":"Howland Reed","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Howland Reed]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Howland_Reed" title="Howland Reed">Howland Reed</a>, thus they do not attend the Red Wedding. He gives them letters for the Northern lords, explaining the letters contain false orders, in case they are captured by the Ironborn. While it is not explicitly written, it is presumed Robb also gave Maege and Galbart the royal decree he issued, by which he legitimized <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114197251056cca6b413cd6" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> and named him his successor.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">It is unknown whether Maege and Galbart reached their destination, and if they actually have Robb's decree in possession.
</p>
