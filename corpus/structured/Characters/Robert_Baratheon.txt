</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>King Robert Baratheon</b> is a major character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first season","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a>. He is played by starring cast member <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mark Addy","link":"Mark Addy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mark Addy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Mark_Addy" title="Mark Addy">Mark Addy</a>, and debuts in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"series premiere","link":"Winter is Coming","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winter is Coming|series premiere]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Winter_is_Coming" title="Winter is Coming">series premiere</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Robert Baratheon was the ruler of the Seven Kingdoms, formally styled as <b>Robert of the House Baratheon, the First of His Name, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King of the Andals and the First Men","link":"King of the Andals and the First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Andals and the First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Andals_and_the_First_Men" title="King of the Andals and the First Men">King of the Andals and the First Men</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of the Seven Kingdoms","link":"Lord of the Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of the Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms" title="Lord of the Seven Kingdoms">Lord of the Seven Kingdoms</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Protector of the Realm","link":"Protector of the Realm","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Protector of the Realm]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Protector_of_the_Realm" title="Protector of the Realm">Protector of the Realm</a></b>. He took the throne through conquest in the war known as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>. Robert was a great warrior in his youth. He killed <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegar Targaryen","link":"Rhaegar Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar Targaryen</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Prince of Dragonstone","link":"Prince of Dragonstone","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prince of Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Prince_of_Dragonstone" title="Prince of Dragonstone">Prince of Dragonstone</a>, at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a>. After the war, Robert married <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cersei Lannister","link":"Cersei Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cersei Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a> to cement an alliance with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a> to hold the kingdoms together, but the marriage was loveless. Robert and Cersei have three children: <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon|Joffrey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myrcella","link":"Myrcella Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myrcella Baratheon|Myrcella]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Myrcella_Baratheon" title="Myrcella Baratheon">Myrcella</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tommen","link":"Tommen Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tommen Baratheon|Tommen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen</a>. Unbeknown to Robert, all three were actually fathered by Cersei's twin brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Robert found the winning of his kingdom far more fulfilling than the running of it, and spent much of the next seventeen years whoring, drinking and hunting. So it is his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"small council","link":"small council","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[small council]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Small_council" title="Small council" class="mw-redirect">small council</a> that is delegated the tedious tasks of ruling. He is a childhood friend of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> and considers him his true brother. He names Ned <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a> when the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"previous Hand","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn|previous Hand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">previous Hand</a> dies in mysterious circumstances. Eddard launches an investigation and discovers that his predecessor was scrutinizing the parentage of Cersei's children. Fearful of exposure, Cersei instructs Robert's squire, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lancel Lannister","link":"Lancel Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lancel Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Lancel_Lannister" title="Lancel Lannister" class="mw-redirect">Lancel Lannister</a>, to give him too much wine while hunting. Robert is fatally wounded by a boar and succumbs to his injuries soon afterwards. Robert names Eddard protector of the realm just before his death. Cersei then ignores the proclamation, has Eddard arrested, and seizes the throne for her son, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon|Joffrey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a>. The resulting dispute leads to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Robert is the first son of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Steffon Baratheon","link":"Steffon Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Steffon Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Steffon_Baratheon" title="Steffon Baratheon">Steffon Baratheon</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cassana Baratheon","link":"Cassana Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cassana Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Cassana_Baratheon" title="Cassana Baratheon">Cassana Baratheon</a>, and has two younger brothers, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon|Renly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon|Stannis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis</a>. Steffon was the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Paramount of the Stormlands","link":"Lord Paramount of the Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Paramount of the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Lord_Paramount_of_the_Stormlands" title="Lord Paramount of the Stormlands">Lord Paramount of the Stormlands</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Stormlands","link":"The Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">The Stormlands</a> are one of the constituent regions of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> and House Baratheon is one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Houses","link":"Great Houses","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Houses]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Great_Houses" title="Great Houses" class="mw-redirect">Great Houses</a> of the realm. His parents died in his youth when their ship was caught in a storm; Robert, standing on the walls of Storm's End with Stannis, witnessed his parents ship that was returning from Essos break up on the rocks. He inherited the lordship, and was fostered by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Arryn","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>, the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Arryn","link":"House Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/House_Arryn" title="House Arryn">House Arryn</a>, another Great House. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>, the second son of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a>, was also fostered by Jon Arryn and Robert and Eddard became as close as brothers.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Winter is Coming (episode)|Winter is Coming]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Meanwhile, Robert's bastard Gendry - having now survived imprisonment under <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gregor Clegane","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a> at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a> as well as the massacre back in King's Landing - is now traveling with the outlaw group called the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brotherhood Without Banners","link":"Brotherhood Without Banners","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brotherhood Without Banners]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Brotherhood_Without_Banners" title="Brotherhood Without Banners">Brotherhood Without Banners</a> who ultimately exchange him to the red priestess <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Melisandre","link":"Melisandre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Melisandre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Melisandre" title="Melisandre">Melisandre</a> in exchange for two bags of gold. Unaware of what she intends to do with him, Gendry is initially cordial towards Melisandre who reveals the truth about his father as they pass the Red Keep. They arrive at Dragonstone where Gendry meets his uncle Stannis, who refers to his nephew as "half Robert, half low-born." Melisandre later seduces Gendry before quickly tying him to a bed and leeching his blood as part of a ritual to help Stannis's quest for the Iron Throne. However, Gendry is later freed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Davos Seaworth","link":"Davos Seaworth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Davos Seaworth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Davos_Seaworth" title="Davos Seaworth">Davos Seaworth</a> before Stannis and Melisandre can completely sacrifice him thus preserving Robert's bloodline.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"E28\">[[Second Sons (episode)|Second Sons]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">When Joffrey is assassinated at his own <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wedding feast","link":"Purple Wedding","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Purple Wedding|wedding feast]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Purple_Wedding" title="Purple Wedding">wedding feast</a>, Tommen becomes the heir apparent despite the "rumors" about his actual parentage. As he stands vigil with Cersei and Tywin over Joffrey's corpse in the Great Sept of Baelor, Tywin proceeds to grill Tommen as to what makes a good king. At one point, Tommen asks about strength and Tywin refutes this by pointing out that King Robert was a paragon of strength but ultimately not a good king. Curiously, Tywin does not refer to Robert as "your father" as he is saying this to Tommen.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"E33\">[[Breaker of Chains]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

He inquires about the "rumors" that her children, including the current king <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tommen","link":"Tommen Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tommen Baratheon|Tommen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen</a>, were not fathered by Robert. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a> </i>novels, Robert is in his mid-thirties when the events of the books begin. He is jovial and fun-loving, fond of battle, women and drink. As a young man Robert was a formidable warrior, noted for his chivalry, sense of honor and immense charisma. He won several loyal Targaryen bannermen to his cause through strength of personality alone. Very tall and incredibly muscular, Robert was considered one of the most attractive men in Westeros, desired by every maiden in the realm. However, after becoming king, Robert let himself go somewhat, becoming fat, soft and indolent, though still capable of great generosity to his friends. Robert has also become more bitter following Lyanna's death and his unhappy marriage to Cersei, despite their multiple children.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Bastards</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Robert is rumored to have fathered over a dozen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bastard","link":"Bastards","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bastards|bastard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Bastards" title="Bastards" class="mw-redirect">bastard</a> children over the years, though exactly how many he has is unknown even to Robert. Cersei once visited a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maegi","link":"Maggy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maggy|maegi]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Maggy" title="Maggy">maegi</a> in her youth who predicted that Cersei would have three children (Joffrey, Myrcella, and Tommen) and that Robert would have sixteen - the prophecy about Cersei's children turned out to be true, but it is unknown if the prophecy about Robert's children turned out to be accurate (the TV show rounded this number up from sixteen to twenty for some reason, possibly because sixteen sounded too specific given that it won't introduce as many of them as the novels did). Robert had a voracious sexual appetite since he was a teenager, however, and he had sex with innumerable whores and servant girls for over twenty years before his death, so tracking down all of his bastards would be an exceedingly difficult task. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Varys","link":"Varys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Varys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> himself can confirm of at least eight bastard children which Robert fathered over the years.
</p>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>Robert's only acknowledged bastard is Edric Storm, whom he acknowledged because his mother was not a commoner but a noblewoman from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Florent","link":"House Florent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Florent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/House_Florent" title="House Florent">House Florent</a>- Delena Florent, Selyse's cousin. Robert acknowledged the boy but Cersei would have been furious if he raised him in his own home, so he sent Edric away to be fostered at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a> his whole life. Edric had virtually no contact with Robert: Edric would receive <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"nameday","link":"nameday","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[nameday]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Nameday" title="Nameday">nameday</a> presents every year (a pony one year, a sable cloak the next, and once even a miniature warhammer similar to the one Robert used) and write thank-you letters to Robert for them, but they were actually sent by Varys without Robert's input at all. When reading those letters, Robert would laugh and ask Varys what he sent that year. Edric bears amazing resemblance to his father; when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Davos Seaworth","link":"Davos Seaworth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Davos Seaworth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Davos_Seaworth" title="Davos Seaworth">Davos Seaworth</a> first met Edric, he immediately concluded the youth is a bastard of Robert. Stannis resents Edric because the youth's presence is a constant reminder to him how Robert violated his wedding bed (similarly to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a>'s feelings toward <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a>), but allows him to spend time with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shireen","link":"Shireen Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shireen Baratheon|Shireen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Shireen_Baratheon" title="Shireen Baratheon">Shireen</a>. Although <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Selyse","link":"Selyse Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Selyse Baratheon|Selyse]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Selyse_Baratheon" title="Selyse Baratheon">Selyse</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Melisandre","link":"Melisandre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Melisandre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Melisandre" title="Melisandre">Melisandre</a> urged Stannis to sacrifice Edric (in the TV series he was switched with Gendry), Stannis was not thrilled about killing his nephew, and was not overly furious when it was revealed that Davos sent him away.
</li></ul>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>Another confirmed bastard is Mya Stone, the eldest of Robert's bastards. She is nineteen years old. Robert fathered her in the Vale while he was fostering there with Eddard Stark, a few years before he led the rebellion against King Aerys Targaryen (thus she is apparently the oldest of his bastards, in her late teens/early twenties). Mya was not <i>officially</i> acknowledged by Robert, but her existence is considered to be an open secret. Robert knew her when she was a toddler but he left her behind when he became king; once during a heated argument over Robert's disappointment regarding Joffrey, Robert threatened to punish Cersei by bringing a bastard daughter of his to be raised in the Red Keep (the name of the girl in question is not mentioned, but it's likely to be Mya), and Cersei responded "Do as you please, but you may find that the city is not a healthy place for a growing girl" - bluntly implying that she would harm the girl; Robert angrily punched Cersei, but knowing that she truly meant this threat, he backed down. Thus while Mya has not been officially, legally acknowledged as Robert's bastard, her status as his daughter is such public knowledge that she uses the special bastard surname "Stone" - without complaint by others that Robert technically never officially gave her the right to use the name "Stone". Mya works as a pack mule guide along the treacherous mountain path leading to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Eyrie","link":"the Eyrie","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Eyrie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/The_Eyrie" title="The Eyrie">the Eyrie</a>, and is encountered by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> when she brings <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> to the Eyrie to hold prisoner at her sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lysa","link":"Lysa Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lysa Arryn|Lysa]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Lysa_Arryn" title="Lysa Arryn">Lysa</a>'s court.
</li></ul>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>In the books, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barra","link":"Barra","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barra]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Barra" title="Barra">Barra</a> was the only of Robert's bastards who was reported to be killed at Cersei's command following his death. She was explicitly killed on Cersei's orders, not on Joffrey's as in the TV series.
</li></ul>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>Petyr Baelish also told Eddard Stark that some years earlier Robert fathered bastard twins with a serving woman at Casterly Rock, and that Cersei had both children killed. Ned was surprised that Robert would have stood by and let such a thing happen, but then recalled that Robert seemed to have grown adept at shutting his eyes. It is unknown if the story is true.
</li></ul>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Robert never cared much about his bastards. According to Stannis, he liked the making of children well enough, but after birth they were a bother to him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Gendry, Edric Storm, Mya Stone, plus the deceased Barra and twins at Casterly Rock only account for six bastards, however, when Varys said he knew of at least eight. When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> and Gendry first encounter the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brotherhood Without Banners","link":"Brotherhood Without Banners","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brotherhood Without Banners]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Brotherhood_Without_Banners" title="Brotherhood Without Banners">Brotherhood Without Banners</a> in the Riverlands, they pass through the town of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stoney Sept","link":"Stoney Sept","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stoney Sept]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Stoney_Sept" title="Stoney Sept">Stoney Sept</a>, where they encounter a dark-haired prostitute named Bella who <i>claims</i> to be a bastard of Robert. Bella states that she is the daughter of a prostitute that Robert had sex with as he was passing through the town during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Bells","link":"Battle of the Bells","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Bells]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116406496256cca59b3dc21" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Bells" title="Battle of the Bells">Battle of the Bells</a>, and that she was named "Bella" for the battle. She does generally resemble Robert and her age matches her story of when Robert would have had sex with her mother, but her claim cannot be definitely confirmed.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The TV series has not introduced any other bastards besides Gendry, even going so far as to combine parts of Edric Storm's storyline - being taken to Dragonstone - with Gendry's storyline in Season 3. It is unclear if the TV series means to imply that Gendry is Robert's only surviving bastard, but either way the focus given to Gendry is much greater in the TV continuity.
</p>
