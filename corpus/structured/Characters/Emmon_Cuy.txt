<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-161802214956cca719e9ed7" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ser Emmon Cuy is a member of the Rainbow Guard as "Emmon the Yellow" and hails from House Cuy of Sunflower Hall, a noble house from the Reach. Unlike in the episode, he, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robar Royce,","link":"Robar Royce","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robar Royce|Robar Royce,]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-161802214956cca719e9ed7" href="/wiki/Robar_Royce" title="Robar Royce">Robar Royce,</a> and two guards enter Renly's tent just after his King's assassination. Cuy blames Brienne for the death and attacks her. One of the guards tries to burn her using a flaming torch, but her cloak is too soaked with blood to burn. Brienne cuts the guard's hand, fights Cuy and the remaining guard, and apparently kills the latter. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-161802214956cca719e9ed7" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> convinces Robar that Brienne is innocent and he fights off the men-at-arms that follow Cuy, while Catelyn uses an iron brazier to knock Emmon Cuy unconscious, then she and Brienne escape. Ser Emmon and Ser Robar are later killed by Lord Commander Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Loras Tyrell","link":"Loras Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Loras Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-161802214956cca719e9ed7" href="/wiki/Loras_Tyrell" title="Loras Tyrell">Loras Tyrell</a> in a fit of rage, because of their failure to protect King Renly and their allowing Brienne to escape. As he lay dying, Ser Emmon swears it was Brienne that killed Renly, and thus the rumors of this follow her.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Loras was convinced of Brienne's innocence, he was filled with deep remorse for killing Cuy and Royce.
</p>
