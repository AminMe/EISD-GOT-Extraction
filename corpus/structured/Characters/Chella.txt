</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Chella</b> is a recurring background character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first season","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a>. She is played by an unidentified extra and debuts in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Pointy End","link":"The Pointy End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Pointy End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/The_Pointy_End" title="The Pointy End">The Pointy End</a>." Chella is the leader of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Black Ears","link":"Black Ears","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Black Ears]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/Black_Ears" title="Black Ears">Black Ears</a> clan and is fighting for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Chella, daughter of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cheyk","link":"Cheyk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cheyk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/Cheyk" title="Cheyk">Cheyk</a>, is the leader of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Black Ears","link":"Black Ears","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Black Ears]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/Black_Ears" title="Black Ears">Black Ears</a>, one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"hill tribes","link":"hill tribes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[hill tribes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/Hill_tribes" title="Hill tribes">hill tribes</a> living in the foothills of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mountains of the Moon","link":"Mountains of the Moon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mountains of the Moon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/Mountains_of_the_Moon" title="Mountains of the Moon">Mountains of the Moon</a> on the western edge of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vale of Arryn","link":"Vale of Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vale of Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/Vale_of_Arryn" title="Vale of Arryn" class="mw-redirect">Vale of Arryn</a>. She is not of remarkable stature, but wears a necklace of ears around her neck, with each ear coming from a slain foe. Her success in battle has seen her rise high amongst the hill tribes.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30149290156cca70e85a3f" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Black Ears are noted for their habit of collecting the ears of fallen foes. Chella has forty-six ears on her necklace, and boasts that her sons have nearly fifty between them. She hasn't necessarily killed all of their owners; it is common practice for her tribe to cut the ears off of defeated prisoners as trophies, but they often let them leave with their lives.
</p>
