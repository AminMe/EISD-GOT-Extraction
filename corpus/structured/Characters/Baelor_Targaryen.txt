<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Baelor was a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King","link":"King of the Andals and the First Men","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Andals and the First Men|King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Andals_and_the_First_Men" title="King of the Andals and the First Men">King</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen dynasty","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen|Targaryen dynasty]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">Targaryen dynasty</a>. He had the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Sept of Baelor","link":"Great Sept of Baelor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Sept of Baelor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Great_Sept_of_Baelor" title="Great Sept of Baelor">Great Sept of Baelor</a>, which became the center of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a>, built during his reign in the city of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. A large statue of him was later erected in front of the Great Sept.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Baelor]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During his reign, he had the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maidenvault","link":"Maidenvault","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maidenvault]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Maidenvault" title="Maidenvault">Maidenvault</a> built inside the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a> and imprisoned <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"his","link":"Daena Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daena Targaryen|his]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Daena_Targaryen" title="Daena Targaryen">his</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"three","link":"Rhaena Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaena Targaryen|three]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Rhaena_Targaryen" title="Rhaena Targaryen">three</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sisters","link":"Elaena Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Elaena Targaryen|sisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Elaena_Targaryen" title="Elaena Targaryen">sisters</a> to prevent carnal temptations.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[The Red Keep (Histories & Lore)]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Though famed for his holiness and piety, he was also a religious zealot. Among Baelor's follies, he named a six year old boy <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Septon","link":"High Septon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Septon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/High_Septon" title="High Septon">High Septon</a> because he was convinced the child could work miracles. Baelor starved himself into an early grave with his frequent religious fasting.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Given that Baelor abhorred desires of the flesh, he died without issue. He was succeeded by his uncle and Hand, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys II Targaryen","link":"Viserys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Viserys_II_Targaryen" title="Viserys II Targaryen">Viserys II Targaryen</a>.
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> is dragged out of the dungeons to make a forced (and false) confession of treason on the steps of the Great Sept of Baelor, he spots his daughter <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark|Arya]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a> (who escaped capture by the Lannisters) hiding in the crowd near a statue of King Baelor. He also sees the loyal <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> recruiter <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yoren","link":"Yoren","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yoren]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Yoren" title="Yoren">Yoren</a> in the crowd, and as the guard lead him past he utters one quick word to him: "Baelor". </p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Eddard makes his false confession, Grand Maester <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pycelle","link":"Pycelle","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pycelle]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Pycelle" title="Pycelle">Pycelle</a> pontificates to the crowd that Baelor taught the realm mercy, which is why Lord Stark's life will be spared and he will be exiled to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, Baelor is the second son of Aegon III, the Dragonbane, and ruled after the death of his brother Daeron I Targaryen, the Young Dragon. He wished to become a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"septon","link":"septon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[septon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Septon" title="Septon">septon</a>, but his father refused, instead insisting that he marry his sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daena","link":"Daena Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daena Targaryen|Daena]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Daena_Targaryen" title="Daena Targaryen">Daena</a> in the traditional Targaryen manner. Baelor obeyed, but extremely reluctantly and refused to consummate the marriage.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Upon his brother King Daeron I's death, Baelor is said to have begun his reign by walking the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Boneway","link":"Boneway","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Boneway]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Boneway" title="Boneway">Boneway</a> barefoot to make peace with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a>. It is also said that he saved his cousin Aemon the Dragonknight from a snake pit, where he was bitten many times by the vipers whose venom could not kill him because of his faith in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Seven","link":"the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/The_Seven" title="The Seven">the Seven</a>; this tale may simply be a metaphorical version of his visit to Dorne, referring to Dorne itself as a "snake pit," and the Dornish, "vipers." Whatever the truth, Baelor returned home having negotiated the marriage of his grand-nephew <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daeron","link":"Daeron II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daeron II Targaryen|Daeron]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Daeron_II_Targaryen" title="Daeron II Targaryen">Daeron</a> to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myriah Martell","link":"Myriah Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myriah Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Myriah_Martell" title="Myriah Martell">Myriah Martell</a> as a means of making peace with Dorne after Daeron I's failed conquest. Years after Baelor died, when Daeron was crowned as King Daeron II, he negotiated the marriage of his much younger sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys","link":"Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)|Daenerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)" title="Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)">Daenerys</a> to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Prince of Dorne","link":"Prince of Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prince of Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Prince_of_Dorne" title="Prince of Dorne">Prince of Dorne</a>, thereby uniting the realms.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Baelor apparently suffered from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen family's tendency towards insanity","link":"Targaryen madness","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Targaryen madness|Targaryen family's tendency towards insanity]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Targaryen_madness" title="Targaryen madness">Targaryen family's tendency towards insanity</a>, though he exhibited it much differently than others. Instead of being violent or paranoid, Baelor was obsessed with religion and pacifism, to the point of absurdity. Baelor earned the contempt of his nobles by forcing Lord Belgrave to wash the feet of a leper. He tried to replace all the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ravens","link":"ravens","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ravens]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Ravens" title="Ravens">ravens</a> that delivered messages with doves, wore a crown made only of flowers, and once banished all <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"prostitutes","link":"prostitution","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[prostitution|prostitutes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">prostitutes</a> from King's Landing. Baelor also confined his sisters Daena, Rhaena, and Elaena in the Maidenvault so they would not tempt him with carnal thoughts.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He also attempted to birth new dragons by praying over the dragon eggs, but his prayers went unanswered. Despite his zealous support of the Faith of the Seven, Baelor decided not to re-arm its old military orders, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith Militant","link":"Faith Militant","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith Militant]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Faith_Militant" title="Faith Militant">Faith Militant</a>, as Baelor was a man of peace who believed the only weapon the faithful should have is prayer.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During his reign he had a stonemason named as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Septon","link":"High Septon (title)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Septon (title)|High Septon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/High_Septon_(title)" title="High Septon (title)" class="mw-redirect">High Septon</a>, a man that could carve stonework so beautifully that Baelor believed him to be <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Smith","link":"The Smith","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Smith]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/The_Smith" title="The Smith">The Smith</a> in human form. While a fantastic stonecarver, the new High Septon could not read, write, or recite any prayers. Rumor spread that Baelor's Hand, his uncle <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys","link":"Viserys II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys II Targaryen|Viserys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-10754455556cca655e482c" href="/wiki/Viserys_II_Targaryen" title="Viserys II Targaryen">Viserys</a>, had this High Septon poisoned to end the embarrassment to the realm. Baelor then raised a boy of eight years to High Septon, whom he claimed worked miracles, although the boy could not save the king's life on his deathbed. History says Baelor starved himself to death by prolonged fasting to cleanse himself of lust, but some believe he was poisoned by his Hand and uncle, Viserys (some who believe this have attempted to claim Viserys acted for the good of the realm, to stop Baelor, who had taken it into his head that the Seven wanted him to convert all of Westeros to their worship, from starting a war with the North and the Iron Islands). Baelor never consummated his marriage and therefore died childless, and was succeeded by his uncle.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The TV series made a slight change by stating that the boy whom Baelor named as High Septon was six years old, instead of eight years old as in the books.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Baelor was extremely popular with the smallfolk, due to his fanatical devotion to the religious virtues of charity. In later generations he became remembered as "Baelor the Blessed" and revered as a saintly figure. Some educated members of the nobility, however (such as Tywin) think of him as a befuddled and overzealous fool who had a tenuous grip on reality.
</p>
