<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Karsi is one of the wildling leaders who retreated to Hardhome after the wildings' defeat at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Castle Black","link":"Battle of Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castle_Black" title="Battle of Castle Black">Battle of Castle Black</a>. She lost her father, her uncle, and two brothers fighting the Night's Watch.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 5","link":"Season 5","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">Season 5</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander","link":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander of the Night's Watch|Lord Commander]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch" title="Lord Commander of the Night's Watch">Lord Commander</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tormund","link":"Tormund","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tormund]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Tormund" title="Tormund">Tormund</a> arrive at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hardhome","link":"Hardhome","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hardhome]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Hardhome" title="Hardhome">Hardhome</a> to offer the Free Folk to retreat to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Black","link":"Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a> and settle <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"south","link":"The Gift","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Gift|south]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/The_Gift" title="The Gift">south</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wall","link":"The Wall","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Wall|Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">Wall</a>, a meeting of the clan chieftains is held, where Karsi represents her clan. The Lord Commander's appeals to their shared purpose against the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a> and offer them a cache of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragonglass","link":"dragonglass","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragonglass]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Dragonglass" title="Dragonglass">dragonglass</a> as a weapon against the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a>, which <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Magnar","link":"Magnar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Magnar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Magnar" title="Magnar">Magnar</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Loboda","link":"Loboda","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Loboda]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Loboda" title="Loboda">Loboda</a> dismisses as just as unlikely as the mythical <i>"<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ice spiders","link":"Ice spiders","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ice spiders|ice spiders]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Ice_spiders" title="Ice spiders">ice spiders</a> as big as hounds"</i>. Karsi replies that with everything they have seen none of this is unbelievable, and she decides to side with the Lord Commander's plan as long as Tormund vouches for him, but Loboda remains stubborn, claiming that all the Free Folk who get on the Crows' boats will be executed, as they have always been enemies and his ancestors would spit on him if he broke bread with a crow —though Karsi retorts that so would her ancestors, but it doesn't matter as they are dead. Nevertheless, Loboda abandons the hall and he is followed by many other chieftains, to the dismay of Jon, Tormund, and Karsi, who exclaims <i>"I fucking hates Thenns."</i>
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Lord Commander <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tormund","link":"Tormund","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tormund]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Tormund" title="Tormund">Tormund</a> arrive to retrieve the wildlings to escape the imminent invasion of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a>, Karsi initially is hostile towards Jon, but after listening to him and Tormund, she agrees to their plan and begins boarding her people into longboats.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The HBO Viewer's Guide identifies Karsi as a chieftain of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ice-river clans","link":"Ice-river clans","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ice-river clans]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Ice-river_clans" title="Ice-river clans">Ice-river clans</a>, but this may be an error, for two reasons. First, the Ice-river clans in the novels are savage cannibals, considered half-feral even by the other wildlings - though the TV series also made changes like this before, such as how they made the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Thenns","link":"Thenns","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Thenns]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Thenns" title="Thenns">Thenns</a> fierce cannibals, when in the novels they are actually the most culturally sophisticated of the wildlings, even forging their own bronze weapons (the TV version of the Thenns are basically a condensation of the Thenns with the Ice-River clans).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Second, the ice-rivers that the clans come from are actually deep in the interior of the lands beyond the Wall, far away from the coasts. Their lands are so far north that little grows, which is why they actively hunt other wildlings for their meat. Karsi and her followers, however, are visibly decorated with seashells, and costume designer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Michele Clapton","link":"Michele Clapton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Michele Clapton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Michele_Clapton" title="Michele Clapton">Michele Clapton</a> said that this is to indicate wildlings that live near the coasts. There is no clear explanation for the discrepancy - so far it was only mentioned exactly once in the Viewer's Guide entry for Karsi, but not in the episode itself.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Speaking in an interview with <i>The New York Times</i>, actress <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Birgitte Hjort S\u00f8rensen","link":"Birgitte Hjort S\u00f8rensen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Birgitte Hjort S\u00f8rensen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Birgitte_Hjort_Sørensen" title="Birgitte Hjort Sørensen">Birgitte Hjort Sørensen</a> responded to a comment about how "maternal" Karsi was to her children, by saying how delighted she was that her character wasn't written in a patronizing way or downplayed as a leader because she was female:
</p>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>"You say 'maternal' and certainly she was in that moment. But I was quite pleasantly surprised that this was a role where gender really didn’t matter. I didn’t feel I was 'the woman chieftain.' I felt like we were all on the same team together. That was a fun experience for me because it’s rare."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com\/2015\/06\/01\/game-of-thrones-white-walkers-battle-interview-birgitte-hjort-sorensen\/?_r=0 ]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</dd></dl>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As it turns out, however, director <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Miguel Sapochnik","link":"Miguel Sapochnik","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Miguel Sapochnik]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Miguel_Sapochnik" title="Miguel Sapochnik">Miguel Sapochnik</a> explained in a separate interview with <i>MTV.com</i> that the reason for this is that Karsi was originally written to be a man, and only recast with a female actress late in production:
</p>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>"She was a guy originally, and then somewhere in the process we thought it might be cool if she were a mother, and show her sending off her own kids to make that moment with the corpse children really resonate emotionally. As the sequence was refined, she emerged as this clear representative of all the wildlings, which was organic, and it made us care. Then we started casting and saw Birgitte’s work and she got the part."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.mtv.com\/news\/2173859\/game-of-thrones-hardhome-interview\/ ]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</dd></dl>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, one of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tormund","link":"Tormund","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tormund]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Tormund" title="Tormund">Tormund</a>'s sons died and was later raised as a wight, and he actually killed the wight himself. Regardless of being a man, Tormund was still distraught about it as a father:
</p>

<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels there are several female chieftains and raiders among the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Folk","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">Free Folk</a>. One of the most prominent is Morna White Mask, a warrior and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"woods witch","link":"woods witch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[woods witch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Woods_witch" title="Woods witch">woods witch</a> dubbed "White Mask" for the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"weirwood","link":"weirwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[weirwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Weirwood" title="Weirwood">weirwood</a> mask she wears. When Tormund convinces prominent wildling leaders to join forces with the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> and be accepted at the other side of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-80231030456cca5f3c34c9" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>, Morna offers Jon Snow to be his man or woman, whatever he desires. She lifts her mask just briefly enough to kneel and kiss his hand as a sign of fealty.
