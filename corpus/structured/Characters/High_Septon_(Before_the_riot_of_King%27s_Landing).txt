</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The <b>High Septon</b> is a background character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first season","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a> and a minor one in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"second","link":"Season 2","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2|second]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second</a>. He is portrayed by an unidentified extra in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baelor","link":"Baelor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Baelor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/Baelor" title="Baelor">Baelor</a>" and by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"David Verrey","link":"David Verrey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[David Verrey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/David_Verrey" title="David Verrey">David Verrey</a> and in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Old Gods and the New","link":"The Old Gods and the New","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Old Gods and the New]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/The_Old_Gods_and_the_New" title="The Old Gods and the New">The Old Gods and the New</a>." "High Septon" is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"title","link":"High Septon (title)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Septon (title)|title]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/High_Septon_(title)" title="High Septon (title)" class="mw-redirect">title</a> given to the head of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a>, the dominant religion of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. It is a position of supreme authority within the church, but the holder has little power outside of the religion. This High Septon is killed in the riots of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Simon Fisher-Becker","link":"Simon Fisher-Becker","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Simon Fisher-Becker]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/Simon_Fisher-Becker" title="Simon Fisher-Becker">Simon Fisher-Becker</a> was originally set to play him, but was replaced by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"David Verrey","link":"David Verrey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[David Verrey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/David_Verrey" title="David Verrey">David Verrey</a>.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the High Septon is an obese man who dresses opulently. In the TV Series, he is walking back to the Red Keep, but in the books he is always carried in a litter or carriage. Just as in the TV series, he is torn to pieces during the riot of King's Landing. As Tyrion quips, "starving men take a dim view of priests too fat to walk". The royal jester, Moon Boy, describes him as "a pious man who worships the Seven so fervently that he eats a meal for each of them whenever he sits to table".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When a new High Septon assumes office, he renounces his given name and ever after becomes known simply as "the High Septon", so the name of this particular High Septon is never revealed. For the sake of differentiating one from another, he is usually referred to by a descriptive term: this High Septon is usually referred to simply as "The Fat One".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, the High Septon is also present at the execution of Ned Stark and was offended by it. The High Septon had been assured that Eddard Stark was going to be shown mercy, that is why the trial was held on the steps of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Sept of Baelor","link":"Great Sept of Baelor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Sept of Baelor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24350192756cca6889cda2" href="/wiki/Great_Sept_of_Baelor" title="Great Sept of Baelor">Great Sept of Baelor</a>. He gives the speech that was given by Grand Maester Pycelle in the TV series.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While the fat High Septon is rather corrupt, even he is outraged that Joffrey has Ned Stark executed on the very steps of the Great Sept of Baelor, violating holy ground. Cersei lists this as one of the problems that Eddard Stark's execution has caused her.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The crown of this High Septon, which contains valuable crystals, is lost in the riot that kills him. Tyrion sends men out to look for it and they eventually discover that the pieces were broken up and sold. The Lannisters subsequently replace it with a much larger crown.
</p>
