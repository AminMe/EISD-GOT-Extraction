</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Danelle Lothston</b> was a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lady of Harrenhal","link":"Lord of Harrenhal","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Harrenhal|Lady of Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-36630069356cca792f3af0" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Harrenhal" title="Lord of Harrenhal">Lady of Harrenhal</a> and the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lothston","link":"House Lothston","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lothston]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-36630069356cca792f3af0" href="/wiki/House_Lothston" title="House Lothston">House Lothston</a>. She's also known as <b>Mad Lady Lothston</b> and is the stuff of tales and horror stories.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to tales, Mad Lady Lothston cooked children, which she captured by sending a giant bat - the sigil of House Lothston - out of the castle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-36630069356cca792f3af0" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>, bathed in blood, and served feasts of human flesh.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Harrenhal (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-36630069356cca792f3af0" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-36630069356cca792f3af0" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Mad Danelle's backstory is the same. She ruled Harrenhal during the reign of Aerys I and was a loyal vassal of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-36630069356cca792f3af0" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>. She had long red hair and wore tight-fitting black armor.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Lothstons held Harrenhal during the time of the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","link":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tales of Dunk and Egg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-36630069356cca792f3af0" href="/wiki/Tales_of_Dunk_and_Egg" title="Tales of Dunk and Egg">Tales of Dunk and Egg</a></i> prequel novellas, with Mad Lady Lothston herself appearing in the third story in the series, <i>The Mystery Knight</i>​. Thus she might appear in any potential adaptation of the prequels.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The legends about Mad Lady Lothston bathing in the blood of slain children are loosely similar to the real-life seventeenth century Hungarian noblewoman <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"external","text":"Elizabeth B\u00e1thory","link":"http:\/\/en.wikipedia.org\/wiki\/Elizabeth_B%C3%A1thory","linktype":"text","wasblank":false,"wikitext":"[http:\/\/en.wikipedia.org\/wiki\/Elizabeth_B%C3%A1thory Elizabeth B\u00e1thory]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-36630069356cca792f3af0"  class="text" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elizabeth_Báthory">Elizabeth Báthory</a>.
</p>
