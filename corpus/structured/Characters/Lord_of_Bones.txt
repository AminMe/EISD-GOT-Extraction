<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The Lord of Bones is an infamous <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildling","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk|wildling]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63623440856cca6107e458" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildling</a> raider. He is known for wearing a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"giant","link":"Giants","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Giants|giant]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63623440856cca6107e458" href="/wiki/Giants" title="Giants">giant</a>'s skull instead of a helmet and armor made of bones, which he wears to intimidate his enemies. His real name is unknown.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63623440856cca6107e458" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Lord of Bones is one of a number of formerly fiercely independent wildling warrior leaders who has been recruited by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63623440856cca6107e458" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a>, the King-Beyond-the-Wall, to join his forces. He is a fierce enemy of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63623440856cca6107e458" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. Though he refers to himself as the Lord of Bones, the Night's Watch dubbed him "Rattleshirt" as a mockery of the rattle sound produced by his armor. His true name is unknown.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">It is not <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tormund","link":"Tormund","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tormund]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63623440856cca6107e458" href="/wiki/Tormund" title="Tormund">Tormund</a> but the Lord of Bones who is captured by the Watch during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Castle Black","link":"Battle of Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63623440856cca6107e458" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castle_Black" title="Battle of Castle Black">Battle of Castle Black</a>. Tormund never goes to Hardhome and thus never kills him there, as he did in the TV show. Instead, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Melisandre","link":"Melisandre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Melisandre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63623440856cca6107e458" href="/wiki/Melisandre" title="Melisandre">Melisandre</a> has him burned alive, but switches his appearance with that of Mance Rayder to make it look as if Mance is the one being burned, and Mance masquerades as the Lord of Bones for a while before revealing himself.
</p>
