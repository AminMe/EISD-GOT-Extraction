<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Two of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Craster","link":"Craster","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Craster]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Craster" title="Craster">Craster</a>'s wives deliver Gilly's baby in a birthing hut. At the same time the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> survivors of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Fist of the First Men","link":"Battle of the Fist of the First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Fist of the First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Fist_of_the_First_Men" title="Battle of the Fist of the First Men">Battle of the Fist of the First Men</a> take shelter at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Craster's Keep","link":"Craster's Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Craster's Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Craster's_Keep" title="Craster's Keep">Craster's Keep</a>. After Gilly and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Samwell Tarly","link":"Samwell Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Samwell Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a> notice that her new baby is a boy, they realize that Craster will want to sacrifice him to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Walk of Punishment]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Sam visits Gilly, who is deeply distraught about the impending fate of her newborn son. She is frantic, and yells at Sam not to draw attention to his gender, as Craster will want to offer it up as a sacrifice when he finds out. He asks her if she has decided on a name, but she responds that there is no point in naming her baby if he is only going to be offered up as a human sacrifice. She gives Sam back his mother's thimble and says she doesn't care about such stupid things, and is focused only on protecting the life of her baby.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"mutiny","link":"Mutiny at Craster's Keep","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mutiny at Craster's Keep|mutiny]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep" title="Mutiny at Craster's Keep">mutiny</a> breaks out that results in the deaths of Craster and Lord Commander <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jeor Mormont","link":"Jeor Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jeor Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a>, Sam rushes to Gilly's hut and tells her that they must leave. Sam runs out of Craster's Keep with Gilly and her baby, as loyal Night's Watch members fall to the mutineers, who also kill or rape Craster's other wives. Gilly leads the way into the night because she knows the woods around her home.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[And Now His Watch is Ended]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After several days of travelling, Sam, Gilly, and her baby arrived at an abandoned cabin in a forest. After lighting a fire, Sam suggests that Gilly name her son but she does not know any boy names. Sam gives her some examples, and also explains the difference between first and last names. They are interrupted by the squawks of several crows outside. When Sam and Gilly, who is carrying the baby, went out to investigate, they saw hundreds of crows squawking angrily at them. Shortly later, Gilly witnesses a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walker","link":"White Walker","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walker]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/White_Walker" title="White Walker">White Walker</a> approaching and realized that the creature has come for the baby due to Craster's habit of sacrificing male babies to these creatures. The three of them then flee into the night.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Second Sons]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Gilly's child actually does not possess a name.  Due to the harsh conditions <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Beyond the Wall","link":"Beyond the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Beyond the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Beyond_the_Wall" title="Beyond the Wall">Beyond the Wall</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildlings","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk|wildlings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildlings</a> have a high infant mortality rate and believe naming a baby after birth is an ill-omen. Therefore, they do not give their babies names at all until they have survived to fully two years of age. Gilly follows that custom, and for that reason she has not given any name to her baby yet. Wildlings do sometimes give their babies nicknames in the meantime, but Gilly hasn't called her baby "Sam" in the books.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Some people in Castle Black call the baby "Monster" affectionately, while others call him "Abomination" because he is born of incest.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i>"A Feast for Crows"</i>, Jon sends Gilly and Mance Rayder's baby son alongside Sam and Maester Aemon to Oldtown via Braavos, fearing that Melisandre may sacrifice either Aemon or the baby for their "king's blood". Gilly is hearbroken that she has to leave her own baby behind, but Jon forces her to (he feels sorry for her, but reluctantly sticks to his decision). He promises Gilly that her boy will be safe and be raised at Castle Black under Jon's protection. Before departing, Gilly begs Jon to take good care of the baby, and not to name him before he is two years old. Most of the journey Gilly is overcome with grief at being separated from her child. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Currently, the child is still in Castle Black.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Maester <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aemon","link":"Aemon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aemon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-38763429256cca6611d5f1" href="/wiki/Aemon" title="Aemon">Aemon</a>'s death, Gilly actually says that she intends to name Mance Rayder's baby "Aemon Steelsong" or "Aemon Battleborn" in his honor, when he reaches two years of age. She does not choose a name for her own baby.
</p>
