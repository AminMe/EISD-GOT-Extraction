<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Mago attends Drogo's wedding to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westerosi</a> exile <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a>. He watches as two warriors squabble over a dancer at the wedding.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Winter is Coming (episode)|Winter is Coming]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Mago objects to Daenerys intervening and denying the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> to take their share of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazareen","link":"Lhazareen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazareen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Lhazareen" title="Lhazareen">Lhazareen</a> women as spoils of war. He refuses to recognize her authority as <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khaleesi","link":"khaleesi","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khaleesi]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Khaleesi" title="Khaleesi">khaleesi</a></i>, calling her a foreign whore, and also defies Drogo. The Khal fights him, disdainfully disarming himself and deliberately accepting an <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"arakh","link":"arakh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[arakh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Arakh" title="Arakh">arakh</a></i> wound to show contempt for Mago's combat skills before slitting his throat with his own weapon and then pulling his tongue out through the neck wound.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Pointy End]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Shortly after Mago's death, the wound he gave Drogo begin to festers, eventually leading to Drogo's incapacitation and subsequent death.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Mago is part of Khal Drogo's <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khalasar","link":"khalasar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khalasar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a></i>, serving under <i>Ko</i> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jhaqo","link":"Jhaqo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jhaqo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Jhaqo" title="Jhaqo">Jhaqo</a>. He complains that Daenerys deprived him of his rape victim, but then the book only depicts the conversational confrontation among Mago, Drogo and Daenerys about this affair, and this section concludes with Drogo orally denying Mago; no following force conflict is described. On the other hand, Drogo was injured not by Mago, but during a battle against the <i>khalasar</i> of <i>Khal</i> Ogo.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following Drogo's death, Mago becomes a bloodrider for Jhaqo, one of the several <i>Kos</i> that proclaim themselves <i>Khals</i>, followed by a portion of Drogo's <i>Khalasar</i>. He seizes Eroeh, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazareen","link":"Lhazareen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazareen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-61833187056cca66043452" href="/wiki/Lhazareen" title="Lhazareen">Lhazareen</a> girl that Daenerys saved earlier from him (Eroeh is not included in the TV series), then he and the other bloodriders gang-rape her, and finally cut her throat. When Daenerys hears about this, she swears that she will pay Mago back for what he did to Eroeh. So far she has not had a chance to fulfill her oath.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the DVD commentary for the episode "The Pointy End", George R.R. Martin comments on the "butterfly effect" created when a character is killed off in the TV series, given that Mago continues to appear in the books.
</p>
