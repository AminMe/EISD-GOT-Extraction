</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Lyman Beesbury</b> is an unseen character in <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Game of Thrones","link":"Game of Thrones (TV series)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Game of Thrones (TV series)|Game of Thrones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones_(TV_series)" title="Game of Thrones (TV series)">Game of Thrones</a></i>. He is long dead before the start of the series, and thus is not expected to appear. Lyman Beesbury was the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Beesbury","link":"House Beesbury","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Beesbury]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/House_Beesbury" title="House Beesbury">House Beesbury</a> and a member of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Small Council","link":"Small Council","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Small Council]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/Small_Council" title="Small Council">Small Council</a> prior to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dance of the Dragons","link":"Dance of the Dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dance of the Dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/Dance_of_the_Dragons" title="Dance of the Dragons">Dance of the Dragons</a>.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Lyman Beesbury is never mentioned. He appears, however, in <i>The Princess and the Queen</i> short story. Lord Lyman was Lord of Honeyholt and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Coin","link":"Master of Coin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Coin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/Master_of_Coin" title="Master of Coin">Master of Coin</a> during the reign of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaehaerys the Conciliator","link":"Jaehaerys I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaehaerys I Targaryen|Jaehaerys the Conciliator]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen" title="Jaehaerys I Targaryen">Jaehaerys the Conciliator</a> and also under Viserys I, as replacement of Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daemon Targaryen","link":"Daemon Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daemon Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/Daemon_Targaryen" title="Daemon Targaryen">Daemon Targaryen</a>. He was eighty when Viserys I passed away. When the small council met to plot crowning Prince Aegon, Viserys's eldest son by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"his second wife","link":"Alicent Hightower","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Alicent Hightower|his second wife]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/Alicent_Hightower" title="Alicent Hightower">his second wife</a>, over Princess Rhaenyra, Viserys's designated heir, Lord Lyman soundly protested even though he was outnumbered. Ser Criston Cole silenced the discussion by slitting Lord Lyman's throat with his dagger. He was replaced as Master of Coin by Ser Tyland Lannister, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Ships","link":"Master of Ships","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Ships]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-87048563956cca81b9dd67" href="/wiki/Master_of_Ships" title="Master of Ships">Master of Ships</a>.
</p>
