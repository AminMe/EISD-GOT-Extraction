During the retreat Mormont saves Samwell Tarly from a wight, only to learn that he had failed to dispatch <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ravens","link":"ravens","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ravens]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Ravens" title="Ravens">ravens</a> requesting aid as ordered, leaving the Night's Watch to fend for itself. Craster insists that the black brothers should be grateful for his generosity, and that he is a "godly man" for helping them. Mormont tensely questions that he is a godly man, but Craster insists that he is - to the "real gods", the White Walkers, who consume entire armies on their way to the Wall but will spare Craster for his loyalty.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Walk of Punishment]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the main hall, Mormont is checking a map in his journal, as Craster continues to crassly berate the men of the Night's Watch. Mormont says that they have to stay long enough for their wounded to recover enough to be fit to travel, but Craster waves this off, saying they've recovered as much as they ever will. Craster openly suggests that they should just kill the men who are so severely wounded that they won't be able to travel, and if Mormont is reluctant to do the deed himself, he can just leave and Craster will finish them off. Mormont declines. Another young Night's Watch man, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Karl","link":"Karl","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Karl]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Karl" title="Karl" class="mw-redirect">Karl</a>, comes in to complain that Craster is feeding them nothing but bread cut with sawdust, and he wants to know where Craster keeps his hidden larder. Meanwhile, Craster is sitting there getting quite drunk on the wine they gifted to him when they first came. Rast joins in the accusations, and Craster admits that he has winter stockpiles, but he needs those to feed his women and refuses to share them. Rast calls Craster a bastard - at which Craster becomes enraged and threatens Rast with an axe. Mormont restraints Rast, and Craster shouts that he's throwing them all out to lay down outside in the cold on their empty bellies. Craster says he will chop the hands off the next man who calls him "bastard". A tense moment of silence passes, and Mormont grabs Rast to lead him out the doorway...

Lord Commander Mormont bellows that the gods will curse him for this, as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"a guest killing a host who has formally accepted him into his home breaks all the laws of gods and men","link":"Guest right","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Guest right|a guest killing a host who has formally accepted him into his home breaks all the laws of gods and men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Guest_right" title="Guest right">a guest killing a host who has formally accepted him into his home breaks all the laws of gods and men</a>. </p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The desperate Night's Watch recruits like Rast, mostly conscripted criminals exiled to the Wall, turn on officers who are loyal to Mormont, as well as some of the other common recruits like Grenn who stay loyal. Mormont grabs Rast by the throat and lifts him off his feet, one-handed, then spins him around and hurls him against the opposite wall. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Jeor Mormont is old but still hale and capable of wielding a sword in battle. He is a strong, resolute leader, a formidable battle commander and is also fearless in the face of adversity. He is deeply concerned by the declining power of the Watch and the rising threat of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildling","link":"wildling","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildling]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Wildling" title="Wildling" class="mw-redirect">wildling</a> King-Beyond-the-Wall, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a>. Jeor Mormont marched to war leading the forces of House Mormont under Eddard Stark during Robert's Rebellion, and voluntarily abdicated his lordship six years later to join the Night's Watch. Due to the added two years to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"timeline","link":"timeline","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[timeline]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Timeline" title="Timeline">timeline</a> of the TV series, in which Robert's Rebellion happened 17 years before the beginning of the narrative instead of 15 years before, this means that Jeor joined the Night's Watch 9 years ago in the books, but 11 years ago in the TV series. Either way, he rapidly rose through the ranks to be elected Lord Commander, and has held the position for almost ten years.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The man who immediately preceded Jeor Mormont as head of the Night's Watch was "Lord Commander Qorgyle", though the man's first name has not been revealed. He was apparently a member of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Qorgyle","link":"House Qorgyle","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Qorgyle]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/House_Qorgyle" title="House Qorgyle">House Qorgyle</a>, and thus a "Sandy <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dornishman","link":"Dornishmen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dornishmen|Dornishman]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Dornishmen" title="Dornishmen">Dornishman</a>" from the central deserts of Dorne.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the book, Mormont collapses after being stabbed by Ollo Lophand, but does not die immediately. Sam stays with Mormont, trying to comfort him in his last moments. Mormont asks Sam to go to the Wall and tell the Watch the events of the Great Ranging, - the Battle at the Fist of the First Men, the wildlings moving in force against the Wall, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonglass","link":"Dragonglass","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonglass]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Dragonglass" title="Dragonglass">Dragonglass</a> being capable of slaying an Other - and finally, he asks Sam to tell his son Jorah to take the black, and that he forgives him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-89953988056cca5ba5d3ee" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Jeor Mormont" is pronounced "JEE-or MORE-mont".
</p>
