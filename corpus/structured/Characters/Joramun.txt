<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">According to legend, Joramun gathered the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Folk","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">Free Folk</a> to battle the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's King","link":"Night's King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Night's_King" title="Night's King">Night's King</a>, who had made the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> into his personal army. He joined forces with the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stark","link":"Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Stark" title="Stark" class="mw-redirect">Stark</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King of Winter","link":"King in the North","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King in the North|King of Winter]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">King of Winter</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brandon the Breaker","link":"Brandon Stark (the Breaker)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brandon Stark (the Breaker)|Brandon the Breaker]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Brandon_Stark_(the_Breaker)" title="Brandon Stark (the Breaker)">Brandon the Breaker</a> and together they <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"brought down","link":"Fall of the Night's King","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fall of the Night's King|brought down]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Fall_of_the_Night's_King" title="Fall of the Night's King">brought down</a> the Night's King.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Night's Watch (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Joramun is a legendary <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-Beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/King-Beyond-the-Wall" title="King-Beyond-the-Wall">King-Beyond-the-Wall</a> who not only fought the Night's King, but also blew the Horn of Winter to wake <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"giants","link":"giants","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[giants]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Giants" title="Giants">giants</a> from the earth. According to legend, the Horn is capable of bringing the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wall","link":"Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Wall" title="Wall" class="mw-redirect">Wall</a> down.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Given that the Night's King was only the thirteenth Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, he and Joramun lived relatively soon after the Wall was completed - which <i>may</i> imply that Joramun was the very first King-Beyond-the-Wall, though this has not been confirmed.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> comes to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"parley","link":"parley","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[parley]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Parley" title="Parley">parley</a> with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a> (actually to kill him), Mance shows him a huge horn, claiming it is the legendary Horn of Winter. He tells Jon to deliver an ultimatum to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Black","link":"Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153923312456cca6585feab" href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a>: unless the wildlings are allowed to pass the Wall within three days, he will blow the horn and destroy the Wall. The horn is destroyed while Mance is executed. However, later Tormund tells Jon that the destroyed horn was a fake; Mance has never found the true horn.
</p>
