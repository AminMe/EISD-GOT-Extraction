<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-8871476556cca6518bd8e" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Maekar Targaryen is the fourteenth Targaryen to sit the Iron Throne. He was the fourth son of King Daeron II and Myriah Martell. As the son of Daeron II, he was also the grandson of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon IV Targaryen","link":"Aegon IV Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon IV Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-8871476556cca6518bd8e" href="/wiki/Aegon_IV_Targaryen" title="Aegon IV Targaryen">Aegon IV Targaryen</a>. Maekar was given the Targaryen seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summerhall","link":"Summerhall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summerhall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-8871476556cca6518bd8e" href="/wiki/Summerhall" title="Summerhall">Summerhall</a>, and was a capable army commander who served in the Blackfyre Rebellion. After a number of unexpected deaths in the royal family, he came to the throne in 221AL. He ruled until 233AL, when he fell in battle against a rebel lord.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He was the father of Daeron, Aerion, Aemon, Aegon, Rhae and Daella.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Maekar appears as a major character in the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","link":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tales of Dunk and Egg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-8871476556cca6518bd8e" href="/wiki/Tales_of_Dunk_and_Egg" title="Tales of Dunk and Egg">Tales of Dunk and Egg</a></i> prequel novellas, and would appear in any potential live-action adaptation.
</p>
