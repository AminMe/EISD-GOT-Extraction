<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Daeron was the youngest son of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon V Targaryen","link":"Aegon V Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon V Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Aegon_V_Targaryen" title="Aegon V Targaryen">Aegon V Targaryen</a>. He was betrothed to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Olenna Redwyne","link":"Olenna Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Olenna Tyrell|Olenna Redwyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Olenna_Tyrell" title="Olenna Tyrell">Olenna Redwyne</a>, but she took a great dislike to his "twitchy little ferret's face and ludicrous silver hair", and their engagement ended when Olenna seduced her sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viola","link":"Viola Redwyne","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viola Redwyne|Viola]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Viola_Redwyne" title="Viola Redwyne">Viola</a>'s intended husband <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Luthor Tyrell","link":"Luthor Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Luthor Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Luthor_Tyrell" title="Luthor Tyrell">Luthor Tyrell</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Oathkeeper]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">After <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Margaery Tyrell","link":"Margaery Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Margaery Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Margaery_Tyrell" title="Margaery Tyrell">Margaery Tyrell</a> unexpectedly becomes a widow when King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey Baratheon","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey Baratheon</a> is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"murdered at their wedding feast","link":"Purple Wedding","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Purple Wedding|murdered at their wedding feast]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Purple_Wedding" title="Purple Wedding">murdered at their wedding feast</a> and worries she will not be married to his younger brother and successor <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tommen","link":"Tommen Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tommen Baratheon|Tommen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen</a>, her grandmother Olenna recounts the story of how she escaped her engagement to Daeron as a lesson on how to manipulate Tommen into wanting their marriage.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Olenna Tyrell","link":"Olenna Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Olenna Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Olenna_Tyrell" title="Olenna Tyrell">Olenna Tyrell</a> tells <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa Stark","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> over supper at their first meeting about how she ended her betrothal to a Targaryen prince, but he is never specifically named.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> companion book, this Prince is revealed as Daeron Targaryen, the youngest son of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon V Targaryen","link":"Aegon V Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon V Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Aegon_V_Targaryen" title="Aegon V Targaryen">Aegon V Targaryen</a>. <i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> changes that Olenna was not the one to breakoff the betrothal, but Daeron himself because he preferred the companionship of Ser Jeremy Norridge, a young knight that he befriended while <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"squiring","link":"Squire","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Squire|squiring]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Squire" title="Squire">squiring</a> together at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Highgarden","link":"Highgarden","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Highgarden]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Highgarden" title="Highgarden">Highgarden</a>. The two later die together in battle quashing a small rebellion.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Games of Thrones","link":"Game of Thrones (TV series)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Game of Thrones (TV series)|Games of Thrones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones_(TV_series)" title="Game of Thrones (TV series)">Games of Thrones</a></i> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"finale episode","link":"Fire and Blood","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fire and Blood|finale episode]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Fire_and_Blood" title="Fire and Blood">finale episode</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maester Aemon","link":"Aemon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aemon|Maester Aemon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Aemon" title="Aemon">Maester Aemon</a> says King Aegon V Targaryen was his younger brother and Aegon V's successor was his son King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-127144467856cca6892bada" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a> better known as the Mad King. In <i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i> novels the Mad King's father was King Jaehaerys II Targaryen, King Aegon V's second son and Prince Daeron's older brother. Removing this generation from <i>Games of Thrones</i> canon makes conjecture based off the <i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i> novels and <i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> companion book more difficult.
</p>
