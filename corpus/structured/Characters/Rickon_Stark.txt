<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Background</h3>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">After the Red Wedding, the Starks' former bannerman <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roose Bolton","link":"Roose Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roose Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Roose_Bolton" title="Roose Bolton">Roose Bolton</a>, who personally killed Robb, is appointed as the new Warden of the North. His bastard son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ramsay Snow","link":"Ramsay Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ramsay Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Ramsay_Snow" title="Ramsay Snow" class="mw-redirect">Ramsay Snow</a> has physically and psychologically tortured <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Theon Greyjoy","link":"Theon Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Theon Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a>, the Starks' former ward, turning him into a broken pet called "Reek", who reveals to Roose that he did not in fact kill Bran and Rickon, and that they may be with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>. Roose sends his best hunter <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Locke","link":"Locke","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Locke]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Locke" title="Locke">Locke</a> to find Bran and Rickon, promising him a 1000 acres and a holdfast.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Lion and the Rose]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Locke infiltrates the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> and eventually catches up with Bran at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Craster's Keep","link":"Craster's Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Craster's Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Craster's_Keep" title="Craster's Keep">Craster's Keep</a>, but Bran wargs into <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hodor","link":"Hodor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hodor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Hodor" title="Hodor">Hodor</a> and kills Locke before he can interrogate him as to Rickon's whereabouts.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[First of His Name]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After the Bolton army drives the remaining ironborn from the North and takes Winterfell, Roose Bolton asks Ramsay if he has heard anything from Locke. Ramsay informs him that he has not. Roose dismisses Locke's absence as unimportant, speculating that Bran and Rickon may already be dead anyway.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Watchers on the Wall]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 5","link":"Season 5","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">Season 5</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Rickon is still largely believed to have died along with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"external","text":"Bran","link":"http:\/\/gameofthrones.wikia.com\/wiki\/Bran_Stark","linktype":"text","wasblank":false,"wikitext":"[http:\/\/gameofthrones.wikia.com\/wiki\/Bran_Stark Bran]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5"  class="text" href="http://gameofthrones.wikia.com/wiki/Bran_Stark">Bran</a> at the hands of Theon. When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa Stark","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> returns to Winterfell to be wed to Ramsay at the behest of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a>, Ramsay initially forces Theon to keep up the charade and apologize to Sansa.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Kill the Boy]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Eventually, however, an irate Sansa forces Theon to finally admit that he did not kill her brothers. Stunned by this admission, her anger towards Theon cools but when she asks if he knows where they might have gone to, he breaks down and leaves the room, fearful of what Ramsay will do if and when he finds out what he just told her. However, Sansa appears to be left with a sense of renewed hope by the revelation that she still has surviving family left.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Hardhome (episode)|Hardhome]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138052518956cca5a2e63e5" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels Rickon is three years old when the narrative begins. He is very young, but fierce and strong for his age. He has the auburn hair and blue eyes of the Tullys. He and Robb are the only ones out of Ned Stark's six children who are not POV characters. Much as in Seasons 1 and 2, Rickon usually appears in chapters narrated by Bran's POV. Another similarity is that as Rickon gets older he gradually gets a larger speaking role, as seen in both book 2 and Season 2 compared to his small role in book 1 and Season 1.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Rickon is deeply affected by the massive losses occurring in his young life, compounded by the fact that he is only three to six old (between the first and third novels) and thus has difficulty comprehending why these horrible things are even happening. Within a span of only two years, the Lannisters and their allies killed Rickon's father, burned down his home castle of Winterfell, and killed his mother and oldest brother. The loss of Maester Luwin was also a heavy blow to him. As a result Rickon becomes very angry and aggressive, which is reflected in the attitude of his direwolf Shaggydog. Rickon is so upset when his mother leaves home to travel with Robb's army to the south that he refuses to have his hair cut. As a result his hair becomes long and very unruly, signifying the mountain of anger and grief the young boy sits on due to the loss of his slain family members and followers.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He is separated from Bran much earlier, just after leaving Winterfell, and Osha plans to take him south, <i>"perhaps to White Harbor,"</i> not to Last Hearth.
</p>
