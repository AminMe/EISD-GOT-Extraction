data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fourth","link":"Season 4","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4|fourth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" Bran Stark is a noble boy living with his family in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>. He relies on the servant <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hodor","link":"Hodor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hodor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Hodor" title="Hodor">Hodor</a> to aid his mobility. He has had prophetic dreams.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Bran adopted the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"direwolf","link":"direwolf","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[direwolf]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Direwolf" title="Direwolf" class="mw-redirect">direwolf</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer","link":"Summer","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">Summer</a> and they have forged a strong connection. He is able to see through the direwolf's eyes while he sleeps, as Bran is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Warg","link":"Warg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Warg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Warg" title="Warg">Warg</a>: a person who possesses the magical ability to enter the minds of animals and people. Bran is also blessed with the magical power of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sight","link":"greensight","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[greensight|Sight]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Greensight" title="Greensight">Sight</a>, which allows him to glimpse the past, elsewhere in the present and the future as well, often in dreams.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Bran is the fourth child and second son of Lady <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark|Catelyn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn</a> and Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>. Eddard is the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Paramount of the North","link":"Lord Paramount of the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Paramount of the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Lord_Paramount_of_the_North" title="Lord Paramount of the North">Lord Paramount of the North</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The North","link":"The North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">The North</a> is one of the constituent regions of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> and House Stark is one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Houses","link":"Great Houses","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Houses]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Great_Houses" title="Great Houses" class="mw-redirect">Great Houses</a> of the realm. House Stark rules the region from their seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> and Eddard also holds the title <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Winterfell","link":"Lord of Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Winterfell" title="Lord of Winterfell">Lord of Winterfell</a>. He is also the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Warden of the North","link":"Warden","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Warden|Warden of the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Warden" title="Warden">Warden of the North</a> to King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/guide\/houses\/stark\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 guide to houses, House Stark entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Bran was born and raised at Winterfell. He has an older brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark|Robb]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb</a>, and a younger brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rickon","link":"Rickon Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rickon Stark|Rickon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Rickon_Stark" title="Rickon Stark">Rickon</a>. He has two older sisters, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark|Sansa]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark|Arya]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a>. He also has a bastard half-brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a>. He is 10 years old at the start of the series. He was named for Eddard's elder brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brandon","link":"Brandon Stark (Son of Rickard)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brandon Stark (Son of Rickard)|Brandon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Brandon_Stark_(Son_of_Rickard)" title="Brandon Stark (Son of Rickard)">Brandon</a>, who was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"slain","link":"Execution of Rickard and Brandon Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Execution of Rickard and Brandon Stark|slain]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Execution_of_Rickard_and_Brandon_Stark" title="Execution of Rickard and Brandon Stark">slain</a> by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mad King","link":"Mad King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mad King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Mad_King" title="Mad King" class="mw-redirect">Mad King</a>. He is only called "Brandon" by his mother when he has done something wrong. He dreams of being a knight of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a> and one day becoming a bannerman for his brother, Robb. His favorite hobby is climbing the walls of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> and using its old rooftops and passageways to get around.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/guide\/houses\/stark\/bran-stark\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 guide to houses, House Stark - Bran Stark entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Abilities</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Bran Stark is one of a few characters known to possess <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"magical","link":"Magic","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Magic|magical]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magical</a> abilities.
</p>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Greensight","link":"Greensight","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Greensight]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Greensight" title="Greensight">Greensight</a> - also called simply "The Sight", is the ability to receive prophetic visions, starting off in the form of dreams.
</li><li><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Warg","link":"Warg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Warg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Warg" title="Warg">Warg</a> - Bran is a warg, a human who can send his consciousness into the mind of an animal, to see through its eyes and control its body as if it were his own. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jojen Reed","link":"Jojen Reed","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jojen Reed]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Jojen_Reed" title="Jojen Reed">Jojen Reed</a> states that this is a different, though related, ability to the Sight. Though he still needs training, Bran is inherently a very powerful warg, even able to enter the mind of another human (albeit a mentally handicapped one, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hodor","link":"Hodor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hodor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Hodor" title="Hodor">Hodor</a>), something the wargs among the wildlings are unable and unwilling to do. Bran has already become skilled at entering the mind of his direwolf <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer","link":"Summer","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">Summer</a>, in which he is a dangerous fighter, capable of dispatching several wildlings or wights.
<ul><li>Warging with a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Heart tree","link":"Heart tree","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Heart tree]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Heart_tree" title="Heart tree">Heart tree</a>, a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Weirwood","link":"Weirwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Weirwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Weirwood" title="Weirwood">Weirwood</a> with a face carved in it by the Children of the Forest, allows Bran (when combined with his Greensight) to have vivid visions of past, present, and future events, including those far away from himself.
</li></ul>
</li></ul>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels Bran is seven years old when the action begins. He has a love of climbing, and has been known to climb the walls of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> and go exploring its ancient nooks and crannies. He dreams of one day being a great knight, but he is also fascinated by magic. Before his disability, he shows some skills with a sword when Robert Baratheon visits Winterfell by befriending and sparring with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tommen Baratheon","link":"Tommen Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tommen Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen Baratheon</a>. Eddard also initially intends to take Bran, Sansa and Arya with him to King's Landing and to have Bran trained to become a Kingsguard, his lifelong dream. Catelyn, however, wishes for Bran to remain in Winterfell with her and prays to the Seven Gods for something to keep Bran from leaving. Bran is later shoved out of a tower window by Jaime Lannister after he catches Jaime and Cersei together, and rendered crippled from the waist down, ironically leading Ned to leave him behind after all, leading Catelyn to blame herself.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">An interesting difference between involving Bran's appearance is that in the books, he is described as having red-auburn hair like his mother, while on the series he has brown hair. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s hair was also similarly darkened for the television series, as it was described as being the similarly reddish in the books. It is likely this was done to increase the contrast between <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark|Sansa]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a> and the rest of her family, who are portrayed as being very much more traditional Northmen in character as well as appearance. Sansa was heavily influenced by her mother thus giving Sansa her mother's auburn hair helps color-code this visually, and similarly, giving Bran his father's dark hair helped color-code how deeply his father influenced him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the television series, Bran's storyline is mostly identical to the books, with the omission of the character Coldhands who briefly accompanies Bran's party to the Three-eyed raven. There is not, however, any interaction between Bran and the Night's Watch mutineers or Vargo Hoat (Locke's literary counterpart), these subplots having been added in order to stretch out Bran's storyline and avoid it ending mid-season 4. As of <i>A Dance with Dragons</i>, the Three-eyed raven trains Bran in his greensight abilities, allowing him to inhabit <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Heart tree","link":"Heart tree","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Heart tree]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Heart_tree" title="Heart tree">Heart trees</a> and see visions of the past and the present, such as a young Eddard Stark sharpening his sword. In one vision, Bran sees Theon Greyjoy/Reek praying in the Godswood for forgiveness, and calls out to him, unknowingly restoring Theon's sanity and compelling him to accept <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a>'s plea for help in rescuing <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jeyne Poole","link":"Jeyne Poole","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jeyne Poole]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Jeyne_Poole" title="Jeyne Poole">Jeyne Poole</a> from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ramsay Bolton","link":"Ramsay Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ramsay Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/Ramsay_Bolton" title="Ramsay Bolton">Ramsay Bolton</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In real life, "Bran" is the word for "raven/crow" in several <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Celtic languages","link":"wikipedia:Celtic languages","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:Celtic languages|Celtic languages]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtic_languages" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Celtic languages">Celtic languages</a> (Welsh, Cornish, Breton, etc.).<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/en.wiktionary.org\/wiki\/bran \"Bran\" on Wiktionary]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Given that Westeros is loosely analogous to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Britain","link":"wikipedia:British Isles","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:British Isles|Britain]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Isles" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:British Isles">Britain</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70371595656cca58cc0f4f" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a> and their <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Gods","link":"Old Gods of the data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Anglo-Saxons","link":"wikipedia:Anglo-Saxons","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:Anglo-Saxons|Anglo-Saxons]]"}" It is unknown if George R.R. Martin intentionally picked the name "Bran" knowing it is the Celtic word for "Crow", but it does neatly associate with Bran and his visions of the three-eyed crow.
