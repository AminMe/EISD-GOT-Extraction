<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Aerys Targaryen was the first of his name to sit the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> and the thirteenth King of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen dynasty","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen|Targaryen dynasty]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">Targaryen dynasty</a>. He was the son of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daeron the Good","link":"Daeron II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daeron II Targaryen|Daeron the Good]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Daeron_II_Targaryen" title="Daeron II Targaryen">Daeron the Good</a> and Queen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myriah","link":"Myriah Martell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myriah Martell|Myriah]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Myriah_Martell" title="Myriah Martell">Myriah</a> and succeeded his father after he perished in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Spring Sickness","link":"Great Spring Sickness","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Spring Sickness]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Great_Spring_Sickness" title="Great Spring Sickness">Great Spring Sickness</a>. Per the Targaryen tradition, he took his own sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aelinor","link":"Aelinor Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aelinor Targaryen|Aelinor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Aelinor_Targaryen" title="Aelinor Targaryen">Aelinor</a> as his wife. His other brothers were <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baelor","link":"Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)|Baelor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)" title="Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)">Baelor</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegel","link":"Rhaegel Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegel Targaryen|Rhaegel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Rhaegel_Targaryen" title="Rhaegel Targaryen">Rhaegel</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maekar","link":"Maekar Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maekar Targaryen|Maekar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Maekar_Targaryen" title="Maekar Targaryen">Maekar</a>, who would succeed him on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Aerys I Targaryen is described as bookish. He was spindly and stooped, with long, straight hair. He had a long, thin face, a long, thin mustache and a long pointed beard. He inherited the Iron Throne after the Great Spring Sickness took not only his father Daeron, but also his nephews Valarr and Matarys, the sons of his elder brother, Prince Baelor "Breakspear", who had perished earlier defending <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duncan the Tall","link":"Duncan the Tall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duncan the Tall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Duncan_the_Tall" title="Duncan the Tall">Duncan the Tall</a> in a Trial of Seven - a particular form of trial by combat.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aerys appointed his uncle Brynden Rivers as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a> and left the governance of the realm to him, while he occupied himself with the study of books and lore. He also shunned the bed of his sister-wife Aelinor. During this time, no one great disaster befell the realm, but it was beset by numerous minor problems which were allowed to flourish all at once due to Aerys I's inept reign. Following the great plague which killed Aerys I's father, the realm suffered a great drought, leading to famine even in breadbasket regions such as the Reach. This instability led to a great proliferation of broken men and highway robbers, and the roads were not considered safe to travel. Meanwhile, the pretenders of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Blackfyre","link":"House Blackfyre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Blackfyre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/House_Blackfyre" title="House Blackfyre">House Blackfyre</a> who had fled across the Narrow Sea remained an ongoing threat. There were as many as five Blackfyre Rebellions in all, the first and greatest of which occurred during the reign of Aerys I's father. Even so, the surviving Blackfyres were continually plotting new intrigues and rebellions to take the throne back, leading to the short-lived Second Blackfyre Rebellion which occurred three years into Aerys I's reign. In addition, the Lord of the Iron Islands, Dagon Greyjoy, took advantage of the chaos of the spring sickness and the Iron Throne's preoccupation with the Blackfyre rebels under Bittersteel, and raided <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Fair Isle","link":"Fair Isle","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fair Isle]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Fair_Isle" title="Fair Isle">Fair Isle</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Arbor","link":"the Arbor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Arbor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/The_Arbor" title="The Arbor">the Arbor</a>, and even <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the North","link":"the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">the North</a>. Other localized rebellions of opportunity popped up throughout the kingdoms trying to take advantage of the general instability, including a revolt by the island of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Skagos","link":"Skagos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Skagos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Skagos" title="Skagos">Skagos</a> against Winterfell, and an attempt by the wildlings to invade south of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> by a new <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-Beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/King-Beyond-the-Wall" title="King-Beyond-the-Wall">King-Beyond-the-Wall</a>. Aerys I is overall remembered as a weak, absentee-king whose time on the throne was marked by plague, famine, Blackfyre intrigues from the Free Cities, ironborn raiding, wildling invasions, and other localized rebellions.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Aerys's death, his brother Maekar inherited the throne, as Aerys had no heir, and their brother Rhaegel was insane.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aerys I is king during much of the timeframe of the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","link":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tales of Dunk and Egg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Tales_of_Dunk_and_Egg" title="Tales of Dunk and Egg">Tales of Dunk and Egg</a></i> prequel novellas, which begin 90 years before the War of the Five Kings and follow the adventures of Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duncan the Tall","link":"Duncan the Tall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duncan the Tall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Duncan_the_Tall" title="Duncan the Tall">Duncan the Tall</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon \"Egg\" Targaryen","link":"Aegon V Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon V Targaryen|Aegon \"Egg\" Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160917019756cca68573323" href="/wiki/Aegon_V_Targaryen" title="Aegon V Targaryen">Aegon "Egg" Targaryen</a>. Aerys's father Daeron II died between the first and second prequel novellas, after which Aerys ascended the throne, and the second novella shows the deteriorating conditions during Aerys I's inept reign (with widespread plague, drought, and bandits). Aerys I might appear in any potential live-action adaptation of the prequel novellas.
</p>
