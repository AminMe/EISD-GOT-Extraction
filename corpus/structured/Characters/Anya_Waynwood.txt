<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 4","link":"Season 4","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">Season 4</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Lady Anya, along with Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yohn Royce","link":"Yohn Royce","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yohn Royce]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Yohn_Royce" title="Yohn Royce">Yohn Royce</a> and Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vance Corbray","link":"Vance Corbray","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vance Corbray]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Vance_Corbray" title="Vance Corbray">Vance Corbray</a>, sits on a tribunal convened to investigate the alleged suicide of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lysa Arryn","link":"Lysa Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lysa Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Lysa_Arryn" title="Lysa Arryn">Lysa Arryn</a>. She is dismissive of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a>'s explanations, noting in particular that Lady Arryn was devoted to her son to such an extent that she would never end her life. The tribunal then summons Littlefinger's niece, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Alayne","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark|Alayne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Alayne</a>, the only other witness. Lady Anya refuses to allow Littlefinger to speak with Alayne first and, upon her entry, offers to dismiss Baelish so she can give her testimony without fear. Lady Waynwood is surprised to learn of Alayne's true identity, but agrees that even without her aunt, she is safe in the Vale. When Sansa breaks down after her testimony, Anya embraces and comforts her. Satisfied with Alayne's testimony, Waynwood and Royce discuss the Vale's next move with Baelish. He suggests that it is long past time that Robin left the Eyrie and conducted an official tour of the Vale.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Mountain and the Viper]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2>In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Lady Anya Waynwood, the Lady of Ironoaks, is a widow and the mother of Morton, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Donnel","link":"Donnel Waynwood","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Donnel Waynwood|Donnel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Donnel_Waynwood" title="Donnel Waynwood">Donnel</a>, and Wallace Waynwood. She's described as having greying hair, loose skin beneath her chin, and crows-feet around her eyes, but also a distinct air of nobility. She's present in the High Hall of the Eyrie, surrounded by her sons, when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> demands a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"trial by combat","link":"trial by combat","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[trial by combat]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Trial_by_combat" title="Trial by combat">trial by combat</a> to prove his innocence of the charges against him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lady Anya Waynwood, Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yohn Royce","link":"Yohn Royce","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yohn Royce]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Yohn_Royce" title="Yohn Royce">Yohn Royce</a> and more nobles from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vale","link":"Vale","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vale]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Vale" title="Vale" class="mw-redirect">Vale</a> come to the Eyrie not to inquire about Lysa's death, but to remove Littlefinger from his office as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Protector","link":"Lord Protector","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Protector]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Lord_Protector" title="Lord Protector">Lord Protector</a> and take Robin to foster at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Runestone","link":"Runestone","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Runestone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189822109356cca6d311b8b" href="/wiki/Runestone" title="Runestone">Runestone</a>. However, Littlefinger is unimpressed by their threats and manipulates them to let Robin stay under his care.
</p>
