<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-59135348656cca78cf0edc" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, nothing is known about Lord Stokeworth other than that he is the husband of Lady Tanda and the father of Falyse and Lollys.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tanda is the head of House Stokeworth in the novels, and her husband has been dead for some time. When House Stokeworth was first mentioned in the TV series in Season 4's "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mockingbird","link":"Mockingbird","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mockingbird]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-59135348656cca78cf0edc" href="/wiki/Mockingbird" title="Mockingbird">Mockingbird</a>", a point of some confusion was that Tyrion said that Lollys's older sister would inherit Castle Stokeworth after their <i>father</i> died. When Lollys first appeared on-screen in Season 5, however, she made it clear that her older sister will inherit the castle after their <i>mother</i> dies. It is possible that the TV writers thought that having a female head of a major House would be confusing and require some sort of lengthy explanation, so they simply pushed back the explanation about Lollys's mother being the actual head of the House to Season 5. It can easily be explained that Tyrion was simply mistaken or misspoke.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the reign of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II","link":"Aerys II","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-59135348656cca78cf0edc" href="/wiki/Aerys_II" title="Aerys II" class="mw-redirect">Aerys II</a>, Manly Stokeworth was Commander of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"City Watch","link":"City Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[City Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-59135348656cca78cf0edc" href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">City Watch</a>. He was succeeded in the position by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Janos Slynt","link":"Janos Slynt","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Janos Slynt]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-59135348656cca78cf0edc" href="/wiki/Janos_Slynt" title="Janos Slynt">Janos Slynt</a>. Littlefinger remarked that the widowed Lady Tanda has no love for the Lannisters: it is quite possible that Manly was in fact her husband, and that he died during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of King's Landing","link":"Sack of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-59135348656cca78cf0edc" href="/wiki/Sack_of_King's_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">Sack of King's Landing</a> committed by Tywin Lannister's army.
</p>
