<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In her bathtub discussion with Viserys in Season 1's "Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things", Doreah speaks of a man from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Asshai","link":"Asshai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Asshai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Asshai" title="Asshai">Asshai</a> with a dagger of real <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonglass","link":"Dragonglass","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonglass]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Dragonglass" title="Dragonglass">Dragonglass</a>, a man who can change his face, and a colorful pirate. The first is a reference to obsidian - which is called by Dragonglass by the smallfolk of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. The second is a reference to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faceless Men","link":"Faceless Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faceless Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Faceless_Men" title="Faceless Men">Faceless Men</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Braavos","link":"Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Braavos" title="Braavos">Braavos</a>. The third could possibly be a reference to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Salladhor Saan","link":"Salladhor Saan","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Salladhor Saan]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Salladhor_Saan" title="Salladhor Saan">Salladhor Saan</a>, a noted pirate who is also from Lys. All of these later appear in Season 2.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Doreah kills Irri.jpg|thumb|Deleted scene of Doreah killing Irri.]]","title":"Doreah_kills_Irri.jpg","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Doreah kills Irri","caption":"Deleted scene of Doreah killing Irri.","captionParsed":"Deleted scene of Doreah killing Irri."}}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" alt="" src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/80/Doreah_kills_Irri.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20121002181537" width="180" height="119" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Doreah kills Irri.jpg" data-image-key="Doreah_kills_Irri.jpg"  type="image" />According to an interview Amrita Acharia gave after her departure, a death scene for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Irri","link":"Irri","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Irri]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Irri" title="Irri">Irri</a> was actually filmed but was not included in the final edit of the episode: "I’m waiting for Daenerys to come back so I’m running up the stairs. “Khaleesi, are you back yet?” And the dragons are gone. Out of nowhere, there’s a noose around my neck. I think it’s hard to be strangled onscreen because obviously to an extent to make it look real, you really have to be a bit strangled. Death scenes are fun."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/winteriscoming.net\/2012\/05\/interview-with-amrita-acharia\/ WinterIsComing.net interview with Amrita Acharia]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Doreah is from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free City","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities|Free City]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free City</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lys","link":"Lys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Lys" title="Lys">Lys</a>. She is beautiful and has an unusual look compared to Daenerys's other <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> servants, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Irri","link":"Irri","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Irri]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Irri" title="Irri">Irri</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jhiqui","link":"Jhiqui","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jhiqui]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Jhiqui" title="Jhiqui">Jhiqui</a>. Doreah's role is to instruct Daenerys in the "womanly arts of love".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, Doreah has the distinctive Lysene features of blonde hair and blue eyes. The TV series didn't have actress Roxanne McKee change her appearance for the role.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In contrast to the series, Doreah dies of a wasting disease in the second book while traversing the Red Waste. Daenerys personally buries her, and weeps as she does so. In the TV series, it is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rakharo","link":"Rakharo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rakharo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Rakharo" title="Rakharo">Rakharo</a> who dies, followed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Irri","link":"Irri","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Irri]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133685196056cca5c886b7b" href="/wiki/Irri" title="Irri">Irri</a> dying in Qarth soon afterwards.
</p>
