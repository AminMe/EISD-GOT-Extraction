<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">This woman was a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"slave","link":"slave","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[slave]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Slave" title="Slave" class="mw-redirect">slave</a> of apparently <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"eastern origin","link":"Yi Ti","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yi Ti|eastern origin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Yi_Ti" title="Yi Ti">eastern origin</a>. According to the Volantene tattoo on her face, she was trained as a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"prostitute","link":"prostitute","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[prostitute]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Prostitute" title="Prostitute" class="mw-redirect">prostitute</a>. It seems that she was eventually bought by the Red Temple in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a>, or was freed by some other means and joined the Red Temple thereafter. She was eventually ordained as a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Priest","link":"Red Priest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Priest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Red_Priest" title="Red Priest">Red Priest</a>.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> Behind the scenes </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The Red Priestess's necklace, belt and chest piece clearly displays a motif of elongated hexagons, a shape associated with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Asshai","link":"Asshai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Asshai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Asshai" title="Asshai">Asshai</a> and featuring prominently in the wardrobes of the Red Priestess's fellow missionary, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Melisandre","link":"Melisandre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Melisandre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Melisandre" title="Melisandre">Melisandre</a>, and the Asshai'i expatriate <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Quaithe","link":"Quaithe","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Quaithe]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Quaithe" title="Quaithe">Quaithe</a> who was encountered in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qarth","link":"Qarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Qarth" title="Qarth">Qarth</a>. See "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Costumes: Essos - Asshai","link":"Costumes: Essos#Asshai","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Costumes: Essos#Asshai|Costumes: Essos - Asshai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Costumes:_Essos#Asshai" title="Costumes: Essos">Costumes: Essos - Asshai</a>".<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref> \"A Man Without Honor\" HBO featurette<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Rila Fukushima is the first East Asian actor to appear as a character with speaking lines in the TV series. There are people in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Known World","link":"The Known World","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Known World]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/The_Known_World" title="The Known World">The Known World</a> of the novels who are ethnically East Asian in appearance, but they live in the far east of Essos, far east of Qarth and the Dothraki Sea, in the great empire of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yi Ti","link":"Yi Ti","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yi Ti]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Yi_Ti" title="Yi Ti">Yi Ti</a> (the narrative's analogue to ancient China).  As <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a> has explained, Yi Ti is so far away from Westeros that encountering Yi Tish people in King's Landing is as unlikely as encountering people from China in Medieval England - medieval long-distance travel was quite limited.  Further east in Essos, it becomes slightly more probable to encounter travelers from Yi Ti, though it is still quite rare (i.e., a missionary from the east preaching in Volantis would be rare, but not implausible).  In the novels, the only time Yi Tish people ever appear is when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> is even further east at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> city <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vaes Dothrak","link":"Vaes Dothrak","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vaes Dothrak]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Vaes_Dothrak" title="Vaes Dothrak">Vaes Dothrak</a>, and mentions in passing that several of the merchants at the city's market are from Yi Ti.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the books, a male Red Priest in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Selhorys","link":"Selhorys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Selhorys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Selhorys" title="Selhorys">Selhorys</a>, a town under Volantene rule, plays the same role. Benerro, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Priest","link":"High Priest of R'hllor","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Priest of R'hllor|High Priest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/High_Priest_of_R'hllor" title="High Priest of R'hllor">High Priest</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"R'hllor","link":"R'hllor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[R'hllor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/R'hllor" title="R'hllor">R'hllor</a> at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Temple","link":"Red Temple","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Temple]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-146347918556cca63371e40" href="/wiki/Red_Temple" title="Red Temple">Red Temple</a> in Volantis, preaches a similar message in Volantis itself, very much like the Red Priestess.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, all slaves bought by the Red Temple of Volantis are branded with tattoos in the shape of flames, be them Red Priests, temple guards (known as the Fiery Hand) or temple prostitutes. The Red Priestess implies that she was a conventional bed-slave rather than a temple prostitute, however.
</p>
