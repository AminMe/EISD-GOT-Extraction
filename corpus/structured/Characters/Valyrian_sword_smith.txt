<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The smith is played by behind-the-scenes artisan and weaponsmith Tommy Dunne, who forges the weapons used in the series.
</p>
<h2>In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177443021856cca78adcccb" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ice is re-forged by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tobho Mott","link":"Tobho Mott","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tobho Mott]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177443021856cca78adcccb" href="/wiki/Tobho_Mott" title="Tobho Mott">Tobho Mott</a> rather than a smith from Volantis. The knowledge of reworking Valyrian steel is still a closely guarded secret, but by the smiths of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qohor","link":"Qohor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qohor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177443021856cca78adcccb" href="/wiki/Qohor" title="Qohor">Qohor</a>, who number much more than three.
