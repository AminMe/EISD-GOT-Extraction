</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Prince Viserys Targaryen</b> is a major character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first season","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a>. He is played by starring cast member <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harry Lloyd","link":"Harry Lloyd","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harry Lloyd]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Harry_Lloyd" title="Harry Lloyd">Harry Lloyd</a>, and debuts in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"series premiere","link":"Winter is Coming","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winter is Coming|series premiere]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Winter_is_Coming" title="Winter is Coming">series premiere</a>. Viserys is the exiled head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a> and considers himself the rightful ruler of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. He arranges the marriage of his younger sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Khal","link":"Khal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Khal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Khal" title="Khal">Khal</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogo","link":"Drogo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drogo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Drogo" title="Drogo">Drogo</a> to provide the army he needs to retake the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>. He eventually grows impatient with Drogo and threatens to kill Daenerys if Drogo does not deliver his crown as agreed. Drogo responds by crowning Viserys in molten gold, killing him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He formally styled himself as <b>Viserys of House Targaryen, the Third of His Name, King of the Andals and the First Men, Lord of the Seven Kingdoms and Protector of the Realm</b>.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Personality</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Viserys was, in so many ways, the precise opposite of his sister Daenerys Targaryen. He was condescending, violent, arrogant, immoral and extremely cruel. He suffered from maniacal delusions of grandeur, and was intensely self-righteous. Much like Joffrey Baratheon, Viserys believed that people should obey him simply in regards to status and, in his view, birthright. Viserys was a violent and aggressive man, and described his temper as 'a dragon', but also referring to himself as such, showing that he believes he is the greatest of the Targaryens. Viserys's temper hopelessly outweighs his common sense, making him rash and exceptionally reckless.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In regards to his sister Daenerys, Viserys is extremely cruel to her - Viserys does not regard her as a human being with emotions and rights, but rather as a property for him to abuse or hand out to others whenever he pleases. He constantly beats and abuses her verbally, and levels her self-esteem regardless of her human nature. Viserys, nevertheless, self-righteously believes that she is loyal to him and nobody else, shown by the fact that, before his death, he begs for her to help him, despite him giving her absolutely no moral provocation to do so. In fact, only moments ago, he had threatened to cut her open with a sword.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121719064756cca5a9172b8" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, Viserys is an ambitious, arrogant and power-hungry young man with a streak of cruelty and a famous temper, though he is occasionally affectionate towards his sister. The books give more detail in explaining just how much of a paper tiger Viserys is, and is largely consumed by delusions of grandeur. In the first chapter the Targaryens appear in, Viserys toys with a sword he borrowed from Illyrio while promising to kill Robert Baratheon in single combat. Daenerys notes to herself that Viserys has <i>no idea</i> how to use a sword, never owned his own sword, never had any kind of combat training, and is just a boy playing with a borrowed sword.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Nonetheless in the books Daenerys still shows some sympathy for Viserys after his death, though she does not regret that Drogo ultimately had to execute him. Dany notes that while Viserys was weak, petty, and cruel, he had spent most of his life as a frightened refugee after his entire family was killed and his home taken away, and thus he was largely a product of his hard childhood on the run. At the least, he had managed to keep Daenerys alive during their life in exile.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, Viserys was about seven years old at the end of Robert's Rebellion, but in "A Golden Crown" Viserys states that he was five years old at the time, a rare instance of someone being aged <i>down</i> in the TV continuity.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official pronunciation guide developed for the cast and crew, "Viserys Targaryen" is pronounced "Vi-SAIR-iss Tar-GAIR-ee-in".
</p>
