<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">At some point he purchased Beskha and when she was seven he forced her to fight in an arena, after growing bored of watching her kill animals he sent in other children instead and forced them to kill each other for nothing more than his own amusement. Ten years ago, Beskha bought her freedom, however, she still wishes for vengeance.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sons of Winter","link":"Sons of Winter","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sons of Winter]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29802534956cca73f671c7" href="/wiki/Sons_of_Winter" title="Sons of Winter">Sons of Winter</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Beskha will leave <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Croft's","link":"Croft","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Croft|Croft's]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29802534956cca73f671c7" href="/wiki/Croft" title="Croft">Croft's</a> planned route through Meereen. When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Asher Forrester","link":"Asher Forrester","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Asher Forrester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29802534956cca73f671c7" href="/wiki/Asher_Forrester" title="Asher Forrester">Asher Forrester</a> and Croft follow, they find her holding Dezor at sword-point. When Croft objects to the plan, Asher can choose whether to allow Beskha to kill him which will improve her opinion of Asher but risk the wrath of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29802534956cca73f671c7" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> who had ordered them not to kill any of the masters, he can follow Daenerys' orders and stop Beskha which greatly annoys her or Asher can just kill him himself much to the dismay of both Beskha and Croft.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">While there is no equivalent character in the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29802534956cca73f671c7" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, there is a mention that in some fighting pits on <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Astapor","link":"Astapor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Astapor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29802534956cca73f671c7" href="/wiki/Astapor" title="Astapor">Astapor</a> slave children are employed as entertainment. For example in Douquor's pit there is mention of a "folly" involving a bear and three small boys - One boy is to be rolled in honey, one in blood and one in rotting fish, and spectators can bet on which child will the bear devour first. There is also a mention of a nine year old girl fighting at Jothiel's Pit.
