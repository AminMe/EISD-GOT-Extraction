<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121735976556cca5ff2cea9" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a> </i>novels, Joanna Lannister was a daughter of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tytos Lannister","link":"Tytos Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tytos Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121735976556cca5ff2cea9" href="/wiki/Tytos_Lannister" title="Tytos Lannister">Tytos Lannister</a>'s younger brother, Jason Lannister, making her a first cousin of Tywin Lannister.  Her surname was already "Lannister" before she married Tywin.  First cousin marriage is not uncommon among the nobility in Westeros, and apparently isn't considered <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"incest","link":"Incest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Incest|incest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121735976556cca5ff2cea9" href="/wiki/Incest" title="Incest">incest</a> in their culture.  She was the sister of Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stafford Lannister","link":"Stafford Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stafford Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121735976556cca5ff2cea9" href="/wiki/Stafford_Lannister" title="Stafford Lannister">Stafford Lannister</a>, who was later killed during the War of the Five Kings by Robb Stark's army.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Though Tywin was always a stern and ruthless man, Joanna was said to bring out what little warmth he had, and he was even known to smile in her presence. Tywin and Joanna were very deeply in love, and thus Tywin took her death very hard. He became even more stern and was never known to smile again. Her death also fueled his unreasoning hatred for his younger son Tyrion, whom he blamed for killing her, combined with the shame he brought on their House for being born a dwarf.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tywin later wanted to force all of his children into marriage-alliances to benefit House Lannister (as Cersei was later married off to King Robert), and he was offended that Tyrion risked such a future marriage alliance for himself by marrying a commoner for love when he was young (<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tysha","link":"Tysha","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tysha]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121735976556cca5ff2cea9" href="/wiki/Tysha" title="Tysha">Tysha</a>). The hypocrisy in this is that Tywin <i>himself</i> married for love when he wed his own first cousin Joanna, instead of entering into a marriage-alliance with another powerful noble family. As a minor Lannister cousin, Joanna brought no new alliances, lands, or wealth with her when she married Tywin.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121735976556cca5ff2cea9" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Mad King","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen|the Mad King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121735976556cca5ff2cea9" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">the Mad King</a> lusted after Joanna. At the wedding of Tywin and Joanna, Aerys drunkenly japed about how it was a pity the First Night tradition was banned and took too many liberties during Joanna's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bedding","link":"bedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[bedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121735976556cca5ff2cea9" href="/wiki/Bedding" title="Bedding">bedding</a>. Tywin was not amused, and that caused further friction between him and Aerys.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the books, Cersei and Jaime were eight or nine years old when their mother died, while in the TV series Cersei said she was four years old.
</p>
