<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun </b>is a recurring character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fifth","link":"Season 5","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5|fifth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">fifth</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sixth","link":"Season 6","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 6|sixth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Season_6" title="Season 6">sixth</a> seasons. He is a wildling <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Giant","link":"Giant","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Giant]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant" class="mw-redirect">Giant</a> who resides at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hardhome","link":"Hardhome","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hardhome]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Hardhome" title="Hardhome">Hardhome</a>.  He is more commonly referred to by the other wildlings as "<b>Wun Wun</b>".
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Wun Wun is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Giant","link":"Giant","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Giant]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Giant" title="Giant" class="mw-redirect">Giant</a>, and possibly the only non-human to reside in Hardhome. 
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 5","link":"Season 5","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">Season 5</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Wun Wun is present when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tormund Giantsbane","link":"Tormund Giantsbane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tormund Giantsbane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Tormund_Giantsbane" title="Tormund Giantsbane" class="mw-redirect">Tormund Giantsbane</a> battle against the White Walkers. Wun Wun appears willing to support Tormund. Later, when the Night's Watch is gathering the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragonglass","link":"dragonglass","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragonglass]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Dragonglass" title="Dragonglass">dragonglass</a> that Jon shared with the wildlings, as it can kill the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a>, Wun Wun is holding a dragonglass arrowhead, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddison Tollett","link":"Eddison Tollett","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddison Tollett]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Eddison_Tollett" title="Eddison Tollett">Eddison Tollett</a> approaches to tell the giant to give it up, though he is too afraid to do so because of his intimidating size. Wun Wun gets annoyed by his inaction and asks <i>"The fuck you looking at?"</i> in the giants' rudimentary <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"form","link":"Old Tongue#Mag Nuk","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Tongue#Mag Nuk|form]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Old_Tongue#Mag_Nuk" title="Old Tongue">form</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Tongue","link":"Old Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a>, so Edd goes away quietly, without collecting the arrowhead.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"S05E08\">\"[[Hardhome (episode)|Hardhome]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->When the Wights attack the village, Wun Wun single-handedly defeats about a dozen of them by ripping them apart, stomping on them, and defending himself with a large burning log. He then retreats into the sea with Jon and the remaining defenders. Being too large for the rowboats, he simply wades out into the bay for a long distance (not drowning due to his great height) to reach the larger ships waiting off shore. He is one of the few wildlings to escape the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Massacre at Hardhome","link":"Massacre at Hardhome","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Massacre at Hardhome]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Massacre_at_Hardhome" title="Massacre at Hardhome">Massacre at Hardhome</a>, and the fleet travels back to the Wall.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"S05E08\" \/>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Alongside the other wildlings, he passes the Wall and leaves Castle Black to settle in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Gift","link":"the Gift","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Gift]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/The_Gift" title="The Gift">the Gift</a>. Many brothers of the Night's Watch are intimidated by him, having never seen a giant before.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Dance of Dragons]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<ul><li>Stuntman <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ian Whyte","link":"Ian Whyte","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ian Whyte]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Ian_Whyte" title="Ian Whyte">Ian Whyte</a> has previously played several roles in <i>Game of Thrones</i>, including <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dongo","link":"Dongo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dongo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Dongo" title="Dongo">Dongo</a> - the first giant to appear in the TV series back in the Season 3 premiere, who later died in the Battle of Castle Black at the end of Season 4.
</li><li>Wun Wun is also the first giant to have any speaking lines in the TV series.
</li></ul>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"<i>A Song of Ice and Fire<\/i>","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire|''A Song of Ice and Fire'']]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire"><i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i></a> novels, Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun is at least fourteen feet tall, even larger than <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mag the Mighty","link":"Mag the Mighty","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mag the Mighty]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Mag_the_Mighty" title="Mag the Mighty" class="mw-redirect">Mag the Mighty</a>, and possesses the strength of twelve men. Whilst typically passive and quiet, he will lash out if provoked. Unlike the savage giants in Old Nan's stories, he is vegetarian. He speaks the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Tongue","link":"Old Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Along with some wildlings, Wun Wun is discovered by Jon Snow when he leads new recruits to the weirwood grove in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Haunted Forest","link":"Haunted Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Haunted Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Haunted_Forest" title="Haunted Forest">Haunted Forest</a>. With the help of some diplomacy by the new wildling recruit Leathers, they avoid a fight and manage to convince the wildlings and the giant to return with them to Castle Black.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Wun Wun does not take part in the battle at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hardhome","link":"Hardhome","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hardhome]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Hardhome" title="Hardhome">Hardhome</a>. It is unknown if he ever was there at all.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Shortly after Jon announces he intends to travel south and fight Ramsay, he hears a commotion at Hardin’s Tower. He rushes there and sees Wun Wun smashing the head of a knight named Ser Patrek of King’s Mountain. Jon commands the gathering people to keep back and put away their weapons, and tries to calm the giant down. It is then <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon is attacked by his own men","link":"Mutiny at Castle Black","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mutiny at Castle Black|Jon is attacked by his own men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/Mutiny_at_Castle_Black" title="Mutiny at Castle Black">Jon is attacked by his own men</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Wun Wun's name is an in-joke by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97639461056cca5fd9e56f" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a>: an avid American Football fan, Martin named him after Phil Simms, quarterback for the New York <i>Giants</i>, whose jersey number was "#11" - "one one", or "wun wun".
</p>
