<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-129470265556cca6780e113" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, there is no prostitute named Genna. The only character by that name is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-129470265556cca6780e113" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a>'s sister. This would seem to continue the TV producers' general practice that when they invent new characters for the TV series, they still try to use names from the books which are established to exist in Westeros (instead of making up entirely new ones, which might not sound like they fit).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the TV series, Tyrion pronounced "Genna" with a soft G, as opposed to a hard G.
</p>
