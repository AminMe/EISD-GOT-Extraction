<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i> novels, the First King led this people across the Arm of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-37205855956cca74e169ff" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> in their migration from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-37205855956cca74e169ff" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> into <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-37205855956cca74e169ff" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. According to folklore his grave is located in the Great Barrow on the outskirts of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barrowton","link":"Barrowton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barrowton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-37205855956cca74e169ff" href="/wiki/Barrowton" title="Barrowton">Barrowton</a>, seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Dustin","link":"House Dustin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Dustin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-37205855956cca74e169ff" href="/wiki/House_Dustin" title="House Dustin">House Dustin</a>.  In <i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> it is revealed that House Dustin, which once ruled the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barrowlands","link":"Barrowlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barrowlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-37205855956cca74e169ff" href="/wiki/Barrowlands" title="Barrowlands">Barrowlands</a> as Barrow Kings, claims descent from the First King himself.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">On the other hand, while there are several myths about a first king of the First Men leading them into Westeros, there are rival myths in different kingdoms, with each attempting to claim that their legendary founder was also the First King, i.e. in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Reach","link":"The Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-37205855956cca74e169ff" href="/wiki/The_Reach" title="The Reach">The Reach</a>, their local version of the myth claims that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Garth Greenhand","link":"Garth Greenhand","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Garth Greenhand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-37205855956cca74e169ff" href="/wiki/Garth_Greenhand" title="Garth Greenhand" class="mw-redirect">Garth Greenhand</a> was not only the founder of the Reach, but leader of all the First Men.  Maesters also debate if the First Men could possibly have spread from Dorne up to the North within a <i>single generation</i>, and argue that it must have been a migration spanning many centuries.  If this is the case, there may have been one king who led all of the First Men across the land bridge into what is now Dorne, but he would have been succeeded by later kings.  The other option is that the First Men weren't even unified under a single ruler when they came to Westeros, but different sub-groups and clans migrated in waves.
</p>
