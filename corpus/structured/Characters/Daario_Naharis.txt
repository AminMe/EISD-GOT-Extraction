</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Daario Naharis</b> is a major character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fifth","link":"Season 5","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5|fifth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">fifth</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sixth","link":"Season 6","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 6|sixth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Season_6" title="Season 6">sixth</a> seasons. He initially appeared as a recurring character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"third","link":"Season 3","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3|third]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">third</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fourth","link":"Season 4","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4|fourth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">fourth</a> seasons. He is played by starring cast member <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Michiel Huisman","link":"Michiel Huisman","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Michiel Huisman]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Michiel_Huisman" title="Michiel Huisman">Michiel Huisman</a>, taking over the role from guest star <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ed Skrein","link":"Ed Skrein","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ed Skrein]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Ed_Skrein" title="Ed Skrein">Ed Skrein</a>, who briefly portrayed the character in Season 3.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Originally a lieutenant in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Second Sons","link":"Second Sons (mercenary company)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Second Sons (mercenary company)|Second Sons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Second_Sons_(mercenary_company)" title="Second Sons (mercenary company)" class="mw-redirect">Second Sons</a>, a sellsword company, he takes over the company after killing his superiors <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mero","link":"Mero","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mero]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Mero" title="Mero">Mero</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Prendahl na Ghezn","link":"Prendahl na Ghezn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prendahl na Ghezn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Prendahl_na_Ghezn" title="Prendahl na Ghezn">Prendahl na Ghezn</a> and then aligns with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a>. Daario serves Daenerys as her advisor and enforcer, and also eventually becomes her second lover. After Daenerys goes missing, he ventures out to find her alongside Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a>.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Daario is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrosh","link":"Tyrosh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrosh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Tyrosh" title="Tyrosh">Tyroshi</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsword","link":"Mercenary","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mercenary|sellsword]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellsword</a>, employed as a lieutenant in the Second Sons. He is apparently held in high enough regard by the company's co-captains, Mero and Prendahl na Ghezn, that they allow him to join them in contract negotiations and discussions of the company's future.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to Daario, his mother was a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"prostitute","link":"Prostitution","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prostitution|prostitute]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">prostitute</a>, and he comes from low beginnings. At the age of 12, Daario was sold by his mother to a slave master from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tolos","link":"Tolos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tolos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Tolos" title="Tolos">Tolos</a> due to her declining income, alcoholism and his aggressive, unruly behavior and tendency to get into fights with other boys. He was trained as a pit fighter and had his first match in the pits when he was 16. Daario rose to fame within the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fighting pits","link":"Fighting pit","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fighting pit|fighting pits]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Fighting_pit" title="Fighting pit">fighting pits</a> earning his master so much money that at the time of his master's death Daario was rewarded with freedom. Daario chose to fight as a sellsword for the Second Sons sometime after that.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\":0\">\"[[The Wars to Come]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Besides being fluent in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Common Tongue","link":"Common Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Common Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Common_Tongue" title="Common Tongue">Common Tongue</a>, Daario is also fluent in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Valyrian","link":"High Valyrian","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Valyrian]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/High_Valyrian" title="High Valyrian">High Valyrian</a> considering he is of Tyroshi origin.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daario has an unusual code for a sellsword; he won't sleep with prostitutes and only kills soldiers who are trying to kill him, not bystanders. He does this because he feels that otherwise there is no sport and it is not exciting. Daario has said that the gods gave men two things to entertain themselves with before they die: the thrill of having sex with a woman who earnestly wants it, and the thrill of killing a man who is trying to kill you first.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daario is a highly skilled and extremely capable warrior. Being trained a pit fighter since the age of 12 and then fighting as a sellsword after that, mostly all of Daario's life has revolved around fighting. During his years as a pit fighter he learned to fight with several different fighting styles; like a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> screamer, a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Norvos","link":"Norvos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Norvos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Norvos" title="Norvos">Norvoshi</a> priest and a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westerosi</a> knight. Daario has over several different occasions proven his skills in combat: when he reveals to Daenerys that he single-handedly managed to kill both of the commanding officers of the Second Sons, when he along Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont|Jorah]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Grey Worm","link":"Grey Worm","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Grey Worm]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Grey_Worm" title="Grey Worm">Grey Worm</a> fight severly outnumbered against several guards in the streets of Yunkai, when he kills the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Champion of Meereen","link":"Oznak zo Pahl","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Oznak zo Pahl|Champion of Meereen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Oznak_zo_Pahl" title="Oznak zo Pahl">Champion of Meereen</a> in single combat with ease or when protecting Daenerys, Tyrion, and Missandei from hundreds of Sons of the Harpy along with Ser Jorah, coming out on top, unharmed, despite being outnumbered severely.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daario's weapons-of-choice are a Dothraki <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"<i>arakh<\/i>","link":"arakh","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[arakh|''arakh'']]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Arakh" title="Arakh"><i>arakh</i></a><i> </i>and a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myrish</a> stiletto<i>, </i>both which have gold-plated custom-made hilts in the shape of beautiful, naked women. His fighting style is based on speed and quick, accurate strikes with few, mostly finishing, blows based on pure strength due to his relatively small size and lean build compared to the larger, stronger opponents he frequently faced in the fighting pits.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Daario Naharis joins the captains when they visit <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a>, who hopes to convince them to break their contract with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yunkai","link":"Yunkai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yunkai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Yunkai" title="Yunkai">Yunkai</a> and fight for her instead. Daario sees through Daenerys' bluff that she has 10,000 <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Unsullied","link":"Unsullied","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Unsullied]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Unsullied" title="Unsullied">Unsullied</a>, reminding her that she has only 8,000. When the Second Sons depart with Daenerys' offer and a barrel of wine, Daario's gaze lingers on the young queen.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Back at their own camp, Daario, Mero and Prendahl discuss the situation, with Mero and Prendahl deciding to assassinate Daenerys. At Mero's behest, a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bedslave","link":"Yunkai'i whore","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yunkai'i whore|bedslave]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Yunkai'i_whore" title="Yunkai'i whore">bedslave</a> hands out random coins – Daario draws the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Braavos","link":"Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Braavos" title="Braavos">Braavosi</a> coin that indicates he is the one to do the deed. His only response is "valar morghulis" - "all men must die". That night, Daario, wearing the armor of an Unsullied slips into Daenerys' tent and takes <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Missandei","link":"Missandei","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Missandei]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Missandei" title="Missandei">Missandei</a> hostage. He then presents the heads of Mero and Prendahl and informs her he has taken command of the Second Sons. Intrigued, Daenerys demands that he swear fealty to her. Daario bends the knee and swears the Second Sons, his sword, his life, and his heart to Daenerys.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"S03E08\">\"[[Second Sons (episode)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daario later assists in planning the siege of Yunkai, and is selected, along with Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Grey Worm","link":"Grey Worm","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Grey Worm]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Grey_Worm" title="Grey Worm">Grey Worm</a> to infiltrate the city and open the gates from the inside. The plan succeeds brilliantly, and Jorah and Grey Worm return to their queen triumphant. Daenerys expresses concern over Daario's whereabouts, but he appears a moment later, presenting Yunkai's bloodstained flag to her.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Rains of Castamere (episode)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daario is present outside the walls of Yunkai when Daenerys is greeted by the former slaves who have been liberated. He witnesses as the crowd lifts Daenerys up in the air as they shout "Mhysa" to her.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[Mhysa]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Daario Naharis is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrosh","link":"Tyrosh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrosh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Tyrosh" title="Tyrosh">Tyroshi</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsword","link":"Mercenary","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mercenary|sellsword]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellsword</a> and is one of the three leaders of the mercenary company known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stormcrows","link":"Stormcrows","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stormcrows]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Stormcrows" title="Stormcrows">Stormcrows</a>, who are active in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a> region of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a>. The Second Sons is a different company.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the TV series, Daario says that he was the son of a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"prostitute","link":"Prostitution","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prostitution|prostitute]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">prostitute</a> in Tyrosh, who later sold him into slavery when he was twelve years old, and he was bought by a many in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tolos","link":"Tolos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tolos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Tolos" title="Tolos">Tolos</a> and trained for combat in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fighting pits","link":"Fighting pit","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fighting pit|fighting pits]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Fighting_pit" title="Fighting pit">fighting pits</a>, where he did very well and was freed upon his master's death in reward. No information at all has been given about Daario's past in the novels, though that also means that his backstory in the TV series doesn't contradict anything, and it is entirely plausible.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, he is described as having dyed blue long curly hair and a matching dyed blue beard with three prongs, though he dyed his mustachios gold. He also has a gold tooth. His loud physical appearance is typical of Tyroshi sellswords, who have a reputation for being flashy and flamboyant. He wears a Dothraki <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"arakh","link":"arakh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[arakh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Arakh" title="Arakh">arakh</a> on his left hip and a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myrish</a> stiletto on his right hip, their golden hilts custom-made in the shape of naked wanton women.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daario states that he "count[s] no day as lived unless I have loved a woman, slain a foeman, and eaten a fine meal".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As in the series, the Daario of the books is quite taken with Daenerys, and kills Prendahl so he can switch sides. He slays not Mero, but Sallor the Bald, the third captain of the Stormcrows. Mero is killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110905245656cca592d0c0d" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a> when he attempts to kill Daenerys while she's hailed by the liberated Yunkish slaves.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the show, Daario takes the role of Daenerys's champion against the champion of Meereen, instead of Strong Belwas. Also, Daario forms a friendly rivalry with Grey Worm, which is absent in the books.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daario and the Stormcrows remain loyal to Daenerys, although her position grows worse with every passing day.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As a part of the peace agreement with Yunkai Wise Masters, Daenerys delivers them Daario and six more of her court as hostages. At the point the fifth novel ends, Daario and two others (Hero the Unsullied and Jhogo the bloodrider) are still held captive by the Yunkai. Ser Barrsitan Selmy has a plan to release them, but it is uncertain if they are still alive. In Daario's absence, the Stormcrows are represented at the ruling council by two seasoned sellswords named Jokin and the Widower. He has not yet met Tyrion Lannister.
</p>
