<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Lord Hoster finally passed away shortly after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Blackwater","link":"Battle of the Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a>. Hoster's brother Brynden, son Edmure, daughter Catelyn, and grandson <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> are present for his funeral at Riverrun. As per Tully funeral tradition, his body is set adrift on the Red Fork of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Trident","link":"the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/The_Trident" title="The Trident">the Trident</a> River in a funeral boat, and then set on fire by a flaming arrow - so that his ashes will return to the river which sustains their lands. Brynden tells Catelyn that he and Hoster managed to make peace before his death. Hoster told Brynden to stop calling himself "The Blackfish" as it is an old joke and it wasn't very funny to begin with. Following Hoster's death, his son Edmure inherits his titles and lands, and officially becomes the new head of House Tully.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"S3E03\"\/>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Hoster is an old, spent man close to the end of his life. In his prime he was a canny politician and effective military commander, and was a vital supporter of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>'s uprising against the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen|Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">Targaryen</a> dynasty. He was married to Lady Minisa Whent, of the great castle of Harrenhal, who passed away some years before the events of the series begin.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i>A Game of Thrones</i>, Catelyn visits her father after <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> breaks the siege in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Camps","link":"Battle of the Camps","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Camps]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Camps" title="Battle of the Camps">Battle of the Camps</a>. At that point, Hoster is still lucid. In the next books, his health deteriorates rapidly. Most of the time he loses contact with reality, does not recognize his children, and mumbles incoherently.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At one of those occasions, Catelyn hears her father mumbles: "Tansy... forgive me... the blood... oh, please... Tansy... dead. You'll have others... Sweet babes, and trueborn. Be a good wife and the gods will bless you... sons... trueborn sons". At first Catelyn thinks that her father might have wronged some village maiden named Tansy, but after thinking it over, recalling Lysa's tearful reaction when Catelyn let her hold baby <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark|Robb]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131858314956cca5ef1f0ae" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb</a>, she realizes the terrible secret that haunts her father on his deathbed: Lysa got pregnant some time before her marriage to Jon Arryn, and their father forced her to undergo an abortion by drinking Moon tea (one of its ingredients is the herb called Tansy). Near his death, Hoster feels deep regret for what he did to his daughter. Catelyn writes to her sister about it, telling her that their father's thoughts are all of the wrong he did Lysa, and that he needs her forgiveness, begging her at least to write him a few words of love so he may die in peace. Catelyn feels the letter is too little and too late for her father is likely to die before Lysa answers, but sends it anyway. Lysa does not respond.
</p>
