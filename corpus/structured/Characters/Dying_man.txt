<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 4","link":"Season 4","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">Season 4</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">When his hut is ransacked by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rorge","link":"Rorge","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rorge]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Rorge" title="Rorge">Rorge</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Biter","link":"Biter","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Biter]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Biter" title="Biter">Biter</a> the man is mortally wounded, but it is taking hours for him to slowly die. He is later found by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sandor Clegane","link":"Sandor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sandor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor Clegane</a>.  Asked he thought of ending his own life, he says he was considering it, but when Arya asks why he's still going on then, he says its probably out of "habit".  Arya then points out nothing is worse than his current suffering. He counters that nothing may indeed be worse. Arya insists that nothing is not better or worse than anything, it is just "nothing". The man says dying is thirsty work and Sandor gives him one last drink of water (both darkly wishing it were wine) before stabbing him in the heart, ending his suffering. Arya and Sandor are then attacked by Rorge and Biter and kill them, avenging the man's death.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Mockingbird]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The dialogue in Arya and Sandor's scene with the dying farmer is a reference to avant-garde Irish playwright Samuel Beckett's <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Waiting for Godot","link":"Wikipedia:Waiting for Godot","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Waiting for Godot|Waiting for Godot]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waiting_for_Godot" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Waiting for Godot">Waiting for Godot</a></i>.  Actor <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barry McGovern","link":"Barry McGovern","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barry McGovern]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Barry_McGovern" title="Barry McGovern">Barry McGovern</a>, who plays the dying farmer, has appeared in several stage productions of the play, as well as a 2001 film adaptation of it.  A similar event occurred in the books, but with different dialogue:  immediately after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>, Arya and Sandor come across a bowman from House Piper who managed to escape from the main massacre but is slowly dying from his mortal wounds, and Sandor gives him the mercy of a quick death.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/observationdeck.io9.com\/waiting-for-got-dot-1578297724\/1578773827\/+charliejane io9.com]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.reddit.com\/r\/asoiaf\/comments\/25yzjh\/spoilers_asos_parallels_between_s4e7_and_waiting\/ Parallels with ''Waiting for Godot'']<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />  The Season 4 Blu-ray commentary confirms that Benioff and Weiss specifically named Barry McGovern for the role in the script, and that they are fans of Samuel Beckett.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.denofgeek.us\/tv\/game-of-thrones\/243771\/67-things-you-didnt-know-about-game-of-thrones-season-4]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, in an earthen hollow made by the roots of a fallen oak Arya and Sandor Clegane find a survivor of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>, a severely injured bowman who says he is in service to Ser Marq Piper. He tells them that he had a drink with a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bolton","link":"Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Bolton" title="Bolton" class="mw-redirect">Bolton</a> soldier, and that soldier attacked him without provocation and injured him severely. Arya believes the injured man. He is feverish, his shoulder is swollen grotesquely, and pus and blood stain his whole left side. He smells like a corpse. The man begs them for a drink of wine, and the Hound answers that he has no wine, but he can give him water and the gift of mercy. The man recognizes the Hound, and asks for both. The Hound sends Arya to bring water, using his helm as a bucket. The bowman drinks the water quickly, wishing it was wine. Then the Hound gives him the gift of mercy. Arya suggests to bury the corpse, but the Hound refuses. They take the dead man's money, dagger and helm and leave the place. No one attacks them.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">This incident takes place before the fight at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Inn at the Crossroads","link":"Inn at the Crossroads","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Inn at the Crossroads]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Inn_at_the_Crossroads" title="Inn at the Crossroads">Inn at the Crossroads</a> in which Arya and the Hound face <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Polliver","link":"Polliver","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Polliver]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Polliver" title="Polliver">Polliver</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Tickler","link":"the Tickler","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Tickler]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/The_Tickler" title="The Tickler">the Tickler</a>. Before Polliver and the Tickler are killed, they manage to injure the Hound severely in his head, neck and thigh. The Hound instructs Arya how to treat his wounds, and she complies: she boils wine and pours it on the injuries, then binds straps around the wounds as bandages.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Arya and the Hound never encounter Rorge and Biter (nor any of their victims), who are raiding the Riverlands along with several of the Brave Companions after abandoning <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a> and their leader, Vargo Hoat, to Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gregor Clegane","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a>. Rorge's gang encounter <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brienne","link":"Brienne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brienne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Brienne" title="Brienne" class="mw-redirect">Brienne</a> near the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Inn at the Crossroads","link":"Inn at the Crossroads","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Inn at the Crossroads]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Inn_at_the_Crossroads" title="Inn at the Crossroads">Inn at the Crossroads</a>. In the ensuing fight, Brienne kills Rorge and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gendry","link":"Gendry","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gendry]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187821762356cca6d476d50" href="/wiki/Gendry" title="Gendry">Gendry</a> kills Biter.
</p>
