<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Dongo is one of the non-human race of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"giants","link":"Giants","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Giants|giants]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="/wiki/Giants" title="Giants">giants</a> who joined the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Folk","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">Free Folk</a> army assembled by Mance Rayder, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-Beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="/wiki/King-Beyond-the-Wall" title="King-Beyond-the-Wall">King-Beyond-the-Wall</a>.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>It wasn't initially clear that the grey-haired giant seen in the Season 3 premiere was meant to be the same individual as the grey-haired giant who later appeared at the end of Season 4, despite being played by the same actor. It was later confirmed that they were the same individual in the book <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Inside HBO's Game of Thrones: Seasons 3 & 4","link":"Inside HBO's Game of Thrones: Seasons 3 & 4","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Inside HBO's Game of Thrones: Seasons 3 & 4]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="/wiki/Inside_HBO's_Game_of_Thrones:_Seasons_3_&_4" title="Inside HBO's Game of Thrones: Seasons 3 &amp; 4">Inside HBO's Game of Thrones: Seasons 3 &amp; 4</a></i>.
</li><li>The character has no direct counterpart in the novels. The giant never received an official name in on-screen dialogue. The <i>Inside HBO's Game of Thrones: Seasons 3 &amp; 4</i> book, however, mentioned that the production team assembling the costumes took to calling him "Dongo the Doomed" by the end of Season 4 ("doomed" because he gets shot in the back with a ballista bolt). There were in fact only two specific giant costumes, for two distinct individuals: <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mag the Mighty","link":"Mag the Mighty","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mag the Mighty]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="/wiki/Mag_the_Mighty" title="Mag the Mighty" class="mw-redirect">Mag the Mighty</a> and Dongo the Doomed.  The script for "The Watchers on the Wall", however, does refer to him as "Dongo the Doomed".<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>Season 4 Blu-ray featurette, \"Behind the Battle for the Wall\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />  
</li><li>"Mag the Mighty" is really a nickname that the human wildlings came up with for the giant whose full name in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Tongue","link":"Old Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a> is "Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg". While "Dongo" isn't a name from the novels, presumably the in-universe explanation would be that "Dongo" is a shortened nickname for a longer name in the Old Tongue - the few giant names revealed so far always have five syllables in them, written as distinct words. For example, something like "Don Goh Tun Doh Weg", etc.
</li><li>The Blu-ray commentary revealed that the way Dongo falls down after being shot in the back is an homage to the famous scene in the 1971 film <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The French Connection","link":"Wikipedia:The French Connection (film)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:The French Connection (film)|The French Connection]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_French_Connection_(film)" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The French Connection (film)">The French Connection</a></i>, when Gene Hackman's character shoots the hitman Nicoli.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.denofgeek.us\/tv\/game-of-thrones\/243771\/67-things-you-didnt-know-about-game-of-thrones-season-4]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</li><li>Although Dongo died, actor Ian Whyte returned in Season 5 to play a new giant, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun","link":"Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="/wiki/Wun_Weg_Wun_Dar_Wun" title="Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun">Wun Weg Wun Dar Wun</a> ("Wun Wun"), who unlike Dongo, <i>is</i> a character from the novels.
</li></ul>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121370801056cca64687f42" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Mance Rayder uses giants for breaching the tunnels at the Wall. However, none of the giants wield bows during the Battle of Castle Black. The most complex weapons that the giants seem capable of wielding are clubs and simple mauls.
</p>
