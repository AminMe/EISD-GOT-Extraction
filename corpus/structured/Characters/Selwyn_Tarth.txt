<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Selwyn Tarth is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Evenfall Hall","link":"Lord of Evenfall Hall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Evenfall Hall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Evenfall_Hall" title="Lord of Evenfall Hall">Lord of Evenfall Hall</a> and the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tarth","link":"House Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/House_Tarth" title="House Tarth">House Tarth</a>, a vassal house of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a>. He is the ruler of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tarth","link":"Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Tarth" title="Tarth">Tarth</a>, an island located in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shipbreaker Bay","link":"Shipbreaker Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shipbreaker Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Shipbreaker_Bay" title="Shipbreaker Bay">Shipbreaker Bay</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stormlands","link":"Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Stormlands" title="Stormlands" class="mw-redirect">Stormlands</a>. He trained his daughter <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brienne","link":"Brienne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brienne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Brienne" title="Brienne" class="mw-redirect">Brienne</a> to be an accomplished warrior. After the outbreak of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, Lord Selwyn declared for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly Baratheon","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a> and sent Brienne to join his army.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 2","link":"Season 2","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">Season 2</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">After Brienne defeats <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Loras Tyrell","link":"Loras Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Loras Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Loras_Tyrell" title="Loras Tyrell">Loras Tyrell</a> in single combat, King Renly is impressed by the warrior-woman's prowess and states that she is everything her father said she was and more.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[What is Dead May Never Die]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> meets Brienne for the first time, he spends a moment trying to recall Tarth, then remembers that its lord is her father, Selwyn of House Tarth.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Prince of Winterfell]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">When the captured Brienne is about to be raped by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Locke","link":"Locke","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Locke]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Locke" title="Locke">Locke</a> and then by his men, Jaime successfully stops them by promising Lord Selwyn Tarth would pay a high ransom for his only surviving child's return, but only if Brienne's honor remains "unbesmirched." Jaime tells Locke that Tarth is famous as "the Sapphire Isle" because it is the location of all sapphire mines in Westeros, and Lord Sewlyn will give him Brienne's weight in sapphires for her release. Locke agrees and calls off his men before they can rape Brienne.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Walk of Punishment]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Brienne subsequently points out to Jaime in private that Tarth is called "the Sapphire Isle" because of the blue of the surrounding waters - there are no sapphire gemstones. She realizes Jaime must have known this, but embellished her father's wealth in order to convince Locke to leave her unharmed.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[And Now His Watch is Ended]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lord Selwyn responds to Locke's ransom demand via <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"raven","link":"Ravens","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ravens|raven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Ravens" title="Ravens">raven</a>, offering a ransom of three hundred <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gold Dragons","link":"Currency","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Currency|Gold Dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">Gold Dragons</a> in exchange for Brienne's release. Unfortunately, Locke became so convinced by Jaime's previous spur of the moment lie to save Brienne that he refuses Selwyn's offer, and will settle for nothing less than literally Brienne's weight in sapphire gemstones. Selwyn's messages insist there are no sapphires on Tarth but Locke simply thinks he is refusing to pay. Locke and his men decide to entertain themselves by putting Brienne into <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>'s bear pit, but she is saved by the Jaime's intervention.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Bear and the Maiden Fair (episode)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-199160564256cca656c2ed9" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Selwyn Tarth is described as a good man of fifty-four years old. Also known as "The Evenstar", he is a widower remembered by Brienne as having a new woman every year. He initially declares House Tarth's support for Renly. After Renly dies, it isn't clearly stated whom he supports. He certainly didn't send troops to aid Stannis, but it's unclear if he bent the knee to Joffrey, outright lent his support to him, or simply adopted an isolationist stance, like Lord Gulian Swann.
