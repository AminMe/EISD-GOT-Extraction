<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Mirri Maz Duur</b> is a recurring character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first season","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a>. She is played by guest star <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mia Soteriou","link":"Mia Soteriou","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mia Soteriou]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Mia_Soteriou" title="Mia Soteriou">Mia Soteriou</a> and debuts in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Pointy End","link":"The Pointy End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Pointy End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/The_Pointy_End" title="The Pointy End">The Pointy End</a>." She was a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazareen","link":"Lhazareen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazareen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Lhazareen" title="Lhazareen">Lhazareen</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"godswife","link":"godswife","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[godswife]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Godswife" title="Godswife">godswife</a> enslaved by the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khalasar","link":"khalasar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khalasar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a></i> led by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogo","link":"Drogo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drogo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Drogo" title="Drogo">Drogo</a>. She treats Drogo for a small wound he took during the sack of her village. He becomes gravely unwell and she convinces his wife <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> to let her use blood magic, promising to save his life. She leaves Drogo in a vegetative state and takes the blood price of Daenerys' unborn child in vengeance for the attack on her people. She is burned to death on Drogo's funeral pyre by Daenerys.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Mirri Maz Duur is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"godswife","link":"godswife","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[godswife]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Godswife" title="Godswife">godswife</a> - a healer and wise woman - living amongst the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazareen","link":"Lhazareen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazareen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Lhazareen" title="Lhazareen">Lhazareen</a>, worshippers of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Shepherd","link":"Great Shepherd","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Shepherd]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Great_Shepherd" title="Great Shepherd">Great Shepherd</a>. She's also a <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maegi","link":"maegi","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[maegi]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Maegi" title="Maegi">maegi</a></i>, a practitioner of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"blood magic","link":"blood magic","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[blood magic]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Blood_magic" title="Blood magic">blood magic</a>.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Mirri Maz Duur 1x09.png|thumb|Mirri Maz Duur prepares to sacrifice Drogo's horse]]","title":"Mirri_Maz_Duur_1x09.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Mirri Maz Duur 1x09","caption":"Mirri Maz Duur prepares to sacrifice Drogo's horse","captionParsed":"Mirri Maz Duur prepares to sacrifice Drogo's horse"}}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" alt="" src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/f/f3/Mirri_Maz_Duur_1x09.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20110622081405" width="180" height="133" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Mirri Maz Duur 1x09.png" data-image-key="Mirri_Maz_Duur_1x09.png"  type="image" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Duur's village is raided by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khalasar","link":"khalasar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khalasar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a> under Khal <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogo","link":"Drogo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drogo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Drogo" title="Drogo">Drogo</a>. The captive women are taken into slavery and raped. The Khaleesi <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> orders that the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazareen","link":"Lhazareen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazareen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Lhazareen" title="Lhazareen">Lhazareen</a> women - including Mirri Maz Duur - be handed over to her custody to protect them. One of Drogo's warriors, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mago","link":"Mago","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mago]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Mago" title="Mago">Mago</a>, objects. When Drogo agrees to Daenerys' request, Mago challenges Drogo to single combat. Drogo kills Mago, but takes a minor <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"arakh","link":"arakh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[arakh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Arakh" title="Arakh">arakh</a> wound to his chest. Mirri Maz Duur volunteers to heal the wound, saying that she is a godswife, or priestess of the Lhazareen god, the Great Shepherd. Drogo's bloodrider <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qotho","link":"Qotho","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qotho]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Qotho" title="Qotho">Qotho</a> objects, calling Duur a <i>maegi</i> - a witch - but Drogo accepts her offering.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Pointy End]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Mirri 1x10.png|thumb|Mirri Maz Duur awaits her death]]","title":"Mirri_1x10.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Mirri 1x10","caption":"Mirri Maz Duur awaits her death","captionParsed":"Mirri Maz Duur awaits her death"}}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" alt="" src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/c/c0/Mirri_1x10.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20110622080814" width="180" height="231" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Mirri 1x10.png" data-image-key="Mirri_1x10.png"  type="image" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->In spite of - or maybe because of - Mirri Maz Duur's attentions, Drogo's wound becomes infected, making him seriously ill, and he falls from his horse, a sign of weakness among the Dothraki that threatens his position of <i>khal</i>. Duur claims that she can save him, but requires a blood sacrifice. She suggests killing <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogo's horse","link":"The red","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The red|Drogo's horse]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/The_red" title="The red">Drogo's horse</a>. Qotho becomes enraged at her use of magic to save Drogo and tries to stop the ceremony, but is killed by Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a>. Daenerys is thrown to the ground and goes into labor, so Mormont takes her into the tent where Duur is carrying out the ceremony – in spite of Duur's strict warning not to interrupt – to ask for her aid.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Baelor]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Daenerys awakens she learns that her son was stillborn and malformed. She finds <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogo","link":"Drogo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drogo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Drogo" title="Drogo">Drogo</a> alive but in a vegetative state. Daenerys asks desperately when will Drogo be as he was, and Duur answers: "<i>When the sun rises in the west, sets in the east when the seas go dry. When the mountains blow in the wind like leaves</i>". Duur explains that Daenerys got what she traded for, and that before she was "saved" by Daenerys, she had already been raped several times and her temple had been burned. Now thanks to her spell, neither Drogo nor <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"his son","link":"Rhaego","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaego|his son]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Rhaego" title="Rhaego">his son</a> will cause more suffering. Daenerys builds a funeral pyre for Drogo and orders Jorah to bind Duur to the pyre. The <i>maegi</i> claims she won't scream. She begins chanting as the flames slowly engulf her, though her singing quickly changes to shrieks of pain as she is burnt alive. True to Daenerys's word, Duur dies screaming.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Fire and Blood]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Mirri Maz Duur's role in the series is largely unchanged from the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, with the books giving more details about her history. She is the godswife of a Lhazareen village and is rumored to have unearthly powers. Her mother had been a godswife before her and taught her all the spells and songs. As a young girl Mirri had traveled to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Asshai","link":"Asshai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Asshai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Asshai" title="Asshai">Asshai</a> to learn the arts of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"shadowbinder","link":"shadowbinder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[shadowbinder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Shadowbinder" title="Shadowbinder">shadowbinders</a>. She also studied the lore of the moonsingers of the Jogos Nhai, the herb lore of the Dothraki, and medicines and anatomy with the then-travelling <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Archmaester","link":"Maester","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maester|Archmaester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Maester" title="Maester" class="mw-redirect">Archmaester</a> Marwyn.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Duur's treachery makes Daenerys reluctant to trust <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Quaithe","link":"Quaithe","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Quaithe]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/Quaithe" title="Quaithe">Quaithe</a>, who amongst other things, seems to imply that Daenerys should visit Asshai next. When Daenerys was at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House of the Undying","link":"House of the Undying","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House of the Undying]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171983531556cca5e04c7ad" href="/wiki/House_of_the_Undying" title="House of the Undying">House of the Undying</a>, one of the prophecies she heard was "Three treasons will you know, once for blood and once for gold and once for love". Daenerys believes that Mirri Maz Duur committed the blood treason.
</p>
