<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-165196157756cca5e834b63" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Rickard's role is unchanged. His wife Lyarra, with whom we had his four aforementioned children, was his first cousin once removed (her name was already "Lyarra Stark" before they were married). Her father was named Rodrik Stark, and he was the younger brother of his grandfather William Stark.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i>A Clash of Kings</i>, Jaime Lannister explains to Catelyn Stark just how he died: Rickard demanded trial by combat to clear his son's name, assuming he would face a Kingsguard such as Jaime, but the Mad King had him locked in a cage with fire spewing beneath it, saying "<i>Fire</i> is the champion of House Targaryen" when Brandon tried to stop his father's death, he ended up strangling himself due to the device the Targaryen men had strapped him to. Jaime did not say how many knights were present, only that Gerold Hightower was there, and (having noticed Jaime's visible disgust at the proceedings) told him afterwards that they were sworn to defend the king, not to judge him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i>A Storm of Swords</i>, Jaime offers to swear an oath not to harm <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brienne of Tarth","link":"Brienne of Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brienne of Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-165196157756cca5e834b63" href="/wiki/Brienne_of_Tarth" title="Brienne of Tarth">Brienne of Tarth</a> in exchange for being unchained so that the two of them can share watches after they are attacked. When Brienne reminds Jaime about how he broke his oath to the Mad King, Jaime alludes to Rickard's death saying she never cooked anyone in their armor as far as he knows.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Rickard's father was Lord Edwyle Stark. Edwyle's sister (Rickard's aunt) married a junior member of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Royce","link":"House Royce","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Royce]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-165196157756cca5e834b63" href="/wiki/House_Royce" title="House Royce">House Royce</a> from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Vale","link":"The Vale of Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Vale of Arryn|the Vale]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-165196157756cca5e834b63" href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale</a>, and through her the Royces are blood relatives of the Starks. The Royce whom Rickard's aunt married was a member of the cadet branch of the main line of that family known as House Royce of the Gates of the Moon (or perhaps, he was specifically the founder of that cadet branch). Thus Nestor Royce, the head of House Royce of the Gates of the Moon at the time of the War of the Five Kings, is Eddard Stark's second cousin. After hearing of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of Winterfell","link":"Sack of Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-165196157756cca5e834b63" href="/wiki/Sack_of_Winterfell" title="Sack of Winterfell">Sack of Winterfell</a> and the alleged deaths of his brothers, Eddard's son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-165196157756cca5e834b63" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> felt the need to name a new heir (otherwise the Lannisters would press a claim to Winterfell through Sansa's forced marriage to Tyrion). Eddard's widow Catelyn Tully suggested naming one of the Royces due to this distant blood relationship - either way the point was rendered moot after the deaths of them both and the destruction of the Northern army at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-165196157756cca5e834b63" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>.
</p>
