<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Harren the Black was the last King of the Iron Islands and the Riverlands, grandson of Harwyn Hardhand, a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King of the Iron Islands","link":"King of the Iron Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Iron Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Iron_Islands" title="King of the Iron Islands">King of the Iron Islands</a> who took the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Trident","link":"Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/Trident" title="Trident" class="mw-redirect">Trident</a> from Arrec, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm King","link":"Storm King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/Storm_King" title="Storm King">Storm King</a> (grandfather of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Argilac the Arrogant","link":"Argilac Durrandon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Argilac Durrandon|Argilac the Arrogant]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/Argilac_Durrandon" title="Argilac Durrandon">Argilac the Arrogant</a>). Harren's father extended their domains further east by conquering even more territory from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingdom of the Stormlands","link":"Kingdom of the Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingdom of the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Stormlands" title="Kingdom of the Stormlands">Kingdom of the Stormlands</a>, advancing east from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gods Eye","link":"Gods Eye","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gods Eye]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/Gods_Eye" title="Gods Eye">Gods Eye</a> to the Narrow Sea and south to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackwater Rush","link":"Blackwater Rush","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackwater Rush]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/Blackwater_Rush" title="Blackwater Rush">Blackwater Rush</a> (more or less the area of the later <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Crownlands","link":"The Crownlands","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Crownlands|Crownlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/The_Crownlands" title="The Crownlands">Crownlands</a>).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Harren the Black was a vain, bloody tyrant hated by his vassals. His cruelty became legendary across Westeros. Harren had a very long reign: he had been king for forty years by the time of the War of Conquest. Argilac Durrandon was a strong king and managed to stem the tide of ironborn expansion, starting when he became king 20-30 years before the War of Conquest. Even so, Harren focused on strengthening his hold on the large territories that House Hoare already held, pouring massive resources into finishing Harrenhal (breaking the backs of thousands of enthralled Rivermen).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Harren's growing strength and expansionist mindset indirectly led to the War of Conquest, or at least its immediate cause. After so many years Argilac was starting to grow old, and with only one daughter as an heir, he feared that Harren would sense weakness and mount another major invasion against the Stormlands. Argilac therefore proposed a marriage-alliance to Aegon Targaryen, whose dragons could stop Harren. Aegon declined the offer, however, pointing that he already had <i>two</i> wives - and that the vast lands that Argilac promised as dowry (from Gods Eye to the Blackwater) were in fact still held by Harren (and Aegon would only gain them if they succeeded). Aegon did give a counter-offer, that Argilac's daughter could marry his best friend (and rumored bastard brother) <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Orys Baratheon","link":"Orys Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Orys Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-12779847656cca68fa7809" href="/wiki/Orys_Baratheon" title="Orys Baratheon">Orys Baratheon</a>. Argilac was incensed at giving his daughter's hand in marriage to a bastard, so he cut off the hands of Aegon's envoy and mailed them back to Dragonstone in a box, saying they were the only hands he would ever receive from him. Aegon responded by calling his bannermen, and announcing that he intended to conquer not merely the Stormlands or the ironborn, but all of the Seven Kingdoms.
</p>
