<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a> </i>novels, Pyp is an actor who wandered the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> with a troupe of players. He can imitate other people's voices and boasts that he can identify where in Westeros someone has come from just by their accent. He is named to the rangers. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a> has stated that he is a big fan of J.R.R. Tolkien's <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>, in which main character Frodo Baggins's best friend is Samwise Gamgee, called Sam for short. Similarly, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a>'s best friend <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Samwell Tarly","link":"Samwell Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Samwell Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a> (also called "Sam" for short) is also an overweight, not martially skilled Everyman character who enjoys reading stories in books. Moreover, Pypar (called Pyp) for short, has a similar name to Frodo's other companion Pippin Took (sometimes shortened to "Pip"). Thus both Jon Snow and Frodo Baggins have two companions named "Sam and Pyp".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While the ranging force travels beyond the Wall, Pyp remains at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Black","link":"Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a>. He takes part in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Castle Black","link":"Battle of Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castle_Black" title="Battle of Castle Black">Battle of Castle Black</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Despite Pyp and Grenn dying in the TV series, so far the two are still alive and well in the books.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The show scene where Sam holds Pyp's head in his last moments is maybe based on the book scene in which Sam comforts the fatally-injured <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jeor Mormont","link":"Jeor Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jeor Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a> during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mutiny at Craster's Keep","link":"Mutiny at Craster's Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mutiny at Craster's Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111707701156cca60b12298" href="/wiki/Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep" title="Mutiny at Craster's Keep">Mutiny at Craster's Keep</a>.
