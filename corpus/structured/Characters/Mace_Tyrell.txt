<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Mace Tyrell is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Highgarden","link":"Lord of Highgarden","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Highgarden]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Highgarden" title="Lord of Highgarden">Lord of Highgarden</a> and head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tyrell","link":"House Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">House Tyrell</a>, one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Houses","link":"Great Houses","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Houses]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Great_Houses" title="Great Houses" class="mw-redirect">Great Houses</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. He is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Paramount of the Reach","link":"Lord Paramount of the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Paramount of the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Lord_Paramount_of_the_Reach" title="Lord Paramount of the Reach">Lord Paramount of the Reach</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Warden of the South","link":"Warden","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Warden|Warden of the South]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Warden" title="Warden">Warden of the South</a>. The Tyrells are one of the richest and most powerful families in Westeros, second only to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannisters","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister|Lannisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannisters</a> - for while the mountains of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Westerlands","link":"the Westerlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Westerlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/The_Westerlands" title="The Westerlands">the Westerlands</a> possess rich gold mines, the fields of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Reach","link":"The Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/The_Reach" title="The Reach">The Reach</a> are the most fertile in Westeros, capable of supporting armies almost twice the size of any other kingdom.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Mace is not considered a great strategist or general himself - his own mother Olenna openly describes him as an oaf. On the other hand, he has shown good judgment in delegating such tasks to trusted, skilled subordinates, such as the famous general <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Randyll Tarly","link":"Randyll Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Randyll Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Randyll_Tarly" title="Randyll Tarly">Randyll Tarly</a>, who often commands Mace's forces in battle.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Mace might not be the most intelligent man, but he is an amiable fellow well-liked by most of his bannermen, as well a loving husband and father. Mace has one son, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Loras","link":"Loras Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Loras Tyrell|Loras]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Loras_Tyrell" title="Loras Tyrell">Loras</a>, and a daughter, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Margaery","link":"Margaery Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Margaery Tyrell|Margaery]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Margaery_Tyrell" title="Margaery Tyrell">Margaery</a>, arguably the most eligible woman in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. Thus entering into the royal bloodline would increase House Tyrell's prestige and secure their rule over their fractious vassals in the Reach.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[Complete Guide to Westeros]]]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>, Mace and House Tyrell stayed loyal to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a>. The Tyrells owed their rule over Highgarden and the Reach to the Targaryen kings, who after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest|Targaryen Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">Targaryen Conquest</a> three centuries before had awarded rule of the Reach to the Tyrells ahead of other families from the region which actually had better claims (such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Florent","link":"House Florent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Florent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/House_Florent" title="House Florent">House Florent</a>). The Tyrell armies were able to inflict the only defeat that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> suffered during the entire war at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Ashford","link":"Battle of Ashford","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Ashford]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ashford" title="Battle of Ashford">Battle of Ashford</a>. Afterwards, Mace's massive army moved east to lay siege to the mighty castle-seat of the Baratheons, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a>. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Siege of Storm's End","link":"Siege of Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Siege of Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Storm's_End" title="Siege of Storm's End">Siege of Storm's End</a> lasted the better part of a year, while Mace and his commanders feasted in sight of the starving garrison led by Robert's younger brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon|Stannis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis</a>. Ultimately Stannis's determined defense held, and the siege only succeeded in pinning down Mace's army and preventing it from assisting the Targaryens in the climactic battles occurring further north. After the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of King's Landing","link":"Sack of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Sack_of_King's_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">Sack of King's Landing</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> rode south with the main rebel army to relieve the siege. With the capital city in rebel hands, and both Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegar Targaryen","link":"Rhaegar Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar Targaryen</a> and his father <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mad King Aerys","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen|Mad King Aerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Mad King Aerys</a> dead, Mace saw that the war was clearly lost and gave no battle to Lord Eddard. Instead he peacefully dipped his banners and submitted to Robert's rule, and in return was treated leniently, pardoned, and allowed to continue ruling the Reach as his family had before the war. On the way to the Iron Bank, he talks with Tycho about the harvest in the Reach and King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maegor III","link":"Maegor III","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maegor III]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Maegor_III?action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Maegor III (page does not exist)">Maegor III</a> who once outlawed moneylending. Unbeknownst to all of them, they are followed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a>, who recognised Ser Meryn. Arriving at the Iron Bank, Mace admires the facade of the enormous building, stating that the men of the Iron Bank are the world's best gamblers much to Tycho Nestoris' disapproval. After an unspecified time of negotiation, Tycho tries to bid farewell to Mace (whom he clearly dislikes due to his bumbling behaviour), but Mace does not realise this and continues plaguing Tycho on the way to the docks, loudly singing.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At night, while following Trant, Arya overhears Meryn telling two Lannister soldiers how poorly he thinks of Mace and the Tyrells, going as far as calling them traitors.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Dance of Dragons]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Lord Mace Tyrell is described as hungry for power and something of a braggart, claiming the victory of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Randyll Tarly","link":"Randyll Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Randyll Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Randyll_Tarly" title="Randyll Tarly">Randyll Tarly</a> over Robert Baratheon in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Ashford","link":"Battle of Ashford","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Ashford]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ashford" title="Battle of Ashford">Battle of Ashford</a> as his own, even though the battle was over before he even arrived. Mace is not particularly intelligent - his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"own mother","link":"Olenna Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Olenna Tyrell|own mother]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Olenna_Tyrell" title="Olenna Tyrell">own mother</a> describes him as an oaf - and may be more of a figurehead for the more intelligent and scheming members of his family, a trait the series exaggerates. He is handsome and a once powerful looking man, but has since turned to fat. Nonetheless, he is a kind ruler and loving father, resulting in him being well-liked and respected by his children and his subordinates in the Reach.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the early stages of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, Mace Tyrell is named <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a> by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly Baratheon","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a>. When Tywin leads the Tyrell forces on King's Landing during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Blackwater","link":"Battle of Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Blackwater" title="Battle of Blackwater" class="mw-redirect">Battle of Blackwater</a>, Mace commands the left field and is rewarded with the title of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Ships","link":"Master of Ships","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Ships]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Master_of_Ships" title="Master of Ships">Master of Ships</a>, a position left vacant since <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a> fled to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonstone","link":"Dragonstone","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Dragonstone" title="Dragonstone" class="mw-redirect">Dragonstone</a>. He is present on the Small Council throughout <i>A Storm of Swords</i> (Seasons 3 and 4 of the show), with Lords Paxter Redwyne and Mathis Rowan serving as advisors.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Tywin's death, Lord Tyrell suggests to Cersei that he would be honored to be Tommen's Hand, yet Cersei refuses and names Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harys Swyft","link":"Harys Swyft","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harys Swyft]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Harys_Swyft" title="Harys Swyft">Harys Swyft</a> instead, a patsy and potential hostage as Kevan Lannister's father by law, instead of leaving the position vacant. Mace also offers his uncle Garth "the Gross" Tyrell as Master of Coin, a choice he had discussed with Tywin, but Cersei lies that she had already offered the position to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gyles Rosby","link":"Gyles Rosby","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gyles Rosby]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Gyles_Rosby" title="Gyles Rosby">Gyles Rosby</a>. Mace's position as Master of Ships is given to Aurane Waters, a bastard of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Velaryon","link":"House Velaryon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Velaryon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/House_Velaryon" title="House Velaryon">House Velaryon</a> who had been taken hostage after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Blackwater","link":"Battle of the Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196343640356cca5d5ee8f3" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a> and bent the knee to Joffrey. Mace doesn't return to the small council until later in the story, and he isn't sent to dealt with the Iron Bank; this task is given to Harys Swyft, who is made Master of Coin after Gyles Rosby's untimely death. However, Cersei does consign Mace to a mission away from King's Landing in order to get rid of him for a while, so that she can scheme against the Tyrells more freely, but it is to siege and retake Storm's End. However, after Margaery is imprisoned on false charges, Mace returns to King's Landing to free her. Following Cersei's disgrace, Mace is appointed Hand of the King by Kevan Lannister in an attempt to appease House Tyrell and mend the damage Cersei has done.
