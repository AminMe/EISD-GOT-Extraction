<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->Salladhor Saan is a pirate-lord from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lys","link":"Lys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Lys" title="Lys">Lys</a>, though a native of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Isles","link":"Summer Islands","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Islands|Summer Isles]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Summer_Islands" title="Summer Islands">Summer Isles</a>. He commands a fleet of thirty <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ships","link":"Ships","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ships|ships]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Ships" title="Ships">ships</a> that frequently prey on merchant ships traveling between <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> to the east. Sometimes in pursuit of extra wealth, he shifts from pure piracy to being a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsail","link":"Mercenary","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mercenary|sellsail]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellsail</a>: hiring out the services of his ships and crews to the highest bidder, to act as mercenaries in various conflicts.

<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 2","link":"Season 2","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">Season 2</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Davos Seaworth and his son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Matthos","link":"Matthos Seaworth","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Matthos Seaworth|Matthos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Matthos_Seaworth" title="Matthos Seaworth">Matthos</a> meet Saan on a beach nearby <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonstone","link":"Dragonstone","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Dragonstone" title="Dragonstone" class="mw-redirect">Dragonstone</a>. Davos promises the pirate he will be paid a fortune in gold when they take <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. Salladhor is dubious of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>'s chances as he has the smallest army of all the claimants to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>, to which Davos answers that Stannis is a proven battle commander unlike the inexperienced <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon|Renly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly</a> or <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon|Joffrey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a>, adding that Stannis has just begun the fight and expects more of his bannerman to join his cause because he is the most honorable man in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. Salladhor notes the irony of a smuggler vouching for the honor of a king. Matthos warns Salladhor to mind his words, calling him a pirate. Davos silences his son and Salladhor points out he is not insulted, asserting being not only a pirate but an excellent one and that he does not sail for promises. Davos challenges this, explaining that Salladhor sails on a promise of ships to plunder every time he leaves port. Salladhor says that promise always comes true and Davos says that his promise is the same. Davos points out that Salladhor is not a young man and that pirate's lives are short. Salladhor jokes that only the clever pirates grow old. Davos suggests that Salladhor could continue to steal from cargo ships and live an easy life while it lasts but that he is offering a harder alternative with greater rewards. He promises riches and fame.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Salladhor says that he has a good name for songs and then tells Davos that he wants Queen Cersei. He says that he will lend his thirty ships provided he can get Cersei when they take King's Landing. Matthos is annoyed by Salladhor's selfishness and says they are not fighting for Salladhor to rape the Queen. Salladhor makes the distinction that he does not intend to rape her and jokes that Matthos has not experienced his persuasiveness. Matthos insists that Stannis has a divine right to rule and that the Lord of Light is the only god. However, Salladhor is dismissive of religion. Matthos turns away and Davos says that he cannot promise the Queen, only the gold and glory. Salladhor asks if Davos believes that Stannis can win and Davos replies that Stannis is the “one true king.” Salladhor contrasts Davos's loyalty to Stannis against their history together; Stannis chopped off Davos's fingers for smuggling. Salladhor says that he will sail with Davos because Davos is the most honest smuggler he has ever met and asks Davos to make him rich. They shake hands and Salladhor leaves.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Later that night Davos informs Stannis that Salladhor has agreed to join his forces. Stannis asks Davos if they can trust Salladhor, and he responds that he has known Salladhor for thirty years but has never "trusted" him. However, Davos is certain that Salladhor has been tantalized by his promise of vast wealth.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Night Lands]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Salladhor Saan is a flamboyant and colorful figure, a respected ship captain and leader of men. While he sells his skills to the highest bidder, he has some genuine loyalty to his friends and allies. His personal flagship is the <i>Valyrian</i>, a great war galley that dips three hundred oars and is a famous - and sometimes feared - sight on the Narrow Sea.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, the inhabitants of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lys","link":"Lys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Lys" title="Lys">Lys</a> distinctively have bronze skin. Salladhor himself is not said to be an exception, and possesses long white hair. Some of the other <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> have populations that have distinctive dark features, such as the olive-skinned inhabitants of Myr, Lys' great rival. The TV series changed this, as Salladhor is played by a dark-skinned actor of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tanzanian","link":"wikipedia:Tanzanian","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:Tanzanian|Tanzanian]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzanian" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Tanzanian">Tanzanian</a> descent. However, it actually isn't that unusual to encounter dark-skinned people throughout the continent of Essos. The cities of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a> routinely conduct slaving raids on the coast of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sothoryos","link":"Sothoryos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sothoryos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Sothoryos" title="Sothoryos">Sothoryos</a>, the continent south of Essos. Sothoryos is loosely analogous to Africa, as Essos is loosely analogous to Eurasia, and the inhabitants of Sothoryos are distinctively dark-skinned.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Writer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bryan Cogman","link":"Bryan Cogman","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bryan Cogman]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Bryan_Cogman" title="Bryan Cogman">Bryan Cogman</a> later confirmed that, within the TV series continuity, Salladhor Saan is actually from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Islands","link":"Summer Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Summer_Islands" title="Summer Islands">Summer Islands</a> (whose inhabitants are notably dark-skinned), and apparently moved to Lys (similar to how <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Xaro Xhoan Daxos","link":"Xaro Xhoan Daxos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Xaro Xhoan Daxos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos" title="Xaro Xhoan Daxos">Xaro Xhoan Daxos</a> was changed to also originally be from the Summer Islands, but moved to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qarth","link":"Qarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Qarth" title="Qarth">Qarth</a> years ago, and considers himself to be "from" Qarth). In Cogman's words, they chose Msamati based on the strength of the actor's performance. At any rate, this means that TV-series Salladhor is originally from the Summer Islands, but he has been based out of Lys for many years (which actually isn't very far from the Summer Islands, located to the southwest) - and thus Salladhor considers himself to be "from Lys".<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/thinkprogress.org\/alyssa\/2012\/06\/08\/496478\/game-of-thrones-story-editor-bryan-cogman-on-the-second-season-adapting-books-he-loves-and-the-shows-secret-main-character\/ ThinkProgress.org, Bryan Cogman interview]], June 2012.<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, Salladhor Saan is actually related to Samarro Saan, a pirate who became one of the infamous "Band of Nine" during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","link":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Ninepenny Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings" title="War of the Ninepenny Kings">War of the Ninepenny Kings</a>, fought about forty years before the War of the Five Kings. An assorted band of mercenaries, pirates, and adventurers, the Band of Nine conquered the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Disputed Lands","link":"Disputed Lands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Disputed Lands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Disputed_Lands" title="Disputed Lands">Disputed Lands</a>, then <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrosh","link":"Tyrosh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrosh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Tyrosh" title="Tyrosh">Tyrosh</a>, and were conquering the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a> to use as a base for a future conquest of Westeros itself when the Targaryens pre-emptively attacked. Samarro was also a Lysene and known as the "Last Valyrian". It isn't clear how exactly Samarro was related to Salladhor. Moreover, if Salladhor's backstory was significantly changed within the TV series, it isn't clear if they could still share the same relationship in the TV continuity - though it's entirely possible that one of Samarro's descendants simply moved to the Summer Islands (or perhaps fled, after the Band of Nine were deposed) and intermarried there. Moreover, <i>The World Of Ice and Fire</i> reveals that the Saans have a long and illustrious history in piracy, stretching back at least three centuries. Samarro was himself a descendant of Salassor Saan, a famous pirate-lord who conquered most of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a> island chain in the Narrow Sea late in the reign of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen|Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon the Conqueror</a>, requiring military intervention by Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maegor","link":"Maegor Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maegor Targaryen|Maegor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Maegor_Targaryen" title="Maegor Targaryen">Maegor</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Blackwater","link":"Battle of the Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Imry Florent","link":"Imry Florent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Imry Florent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Imry_Florent" title="Imry Florent">Imry Florent</a> places Salladhor and his crew at the rear of the fleet. Davos thinks it is a mistake, for Salladhor and his men are experienced sailors and warriors, and were wasted at the rear. Salladhor and his fleet stay outside of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackwater Bay","link":"Blackwater Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackwater Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Blackwater_Bay" title="Blackwater Bay">Blackwater Bay</a> to keep watch for any hidden fleets that may sneak up from behind. This snub turned out to be a blessing in disguise, because as a result Salladhor's sellsail ships were so far behind that they were the only part of Stannis's fleet that was not caught in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildfire","link":"wildfire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildfire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a> trap and destroyed. After Stannis's army is smashed by the combined <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister|Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannister</a>-<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrell","link":"House Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tyrell|Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">Tyrell</a> force, Saan orders his ships to pick up the remnants along the shore, and transports them back to Dragonstone.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, Salladhor decides to abandon Stannis, sick of waiting for payment from him. Ironically, he does that shortly before Stannis is finally able to pay him, having granted a loan from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Bank of Braavos","link":"Iron Bank of Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Bank of Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Iron_Bank_of_Braavos" title="Iron Bank of Braavos">Iron Bank of Braavos</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valar Dohaeris","link":"Valar Dohaeris","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valar Dohaeris]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-121345579856cca6255398d" href="/wiki/Valar_Dohaeris" title="Valar Dohaeris">Valar Dohaeris</a>", Salladhor says that he has been married four times, and that he had a son who died. This information has not been given in the books.
</p>
