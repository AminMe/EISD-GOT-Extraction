<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-44384373356cca67cc2baf" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Dorna is the daughter of Ser Harys Swyft, the Knight of Cornfield and a loyal <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bannerman of House Lannister","link":"Lannister bannerman","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lannister bannerman|bannerman of House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-44384373356cca67cc2baf" href="/wiki/Lannister_bannerman" title="Lannister bannerman">bannerman of House Lannister</a>. Her father married her to Kevan Lannister and they have three sons: Lancel and the twins Martyn and Willem. They also have a daughter, Janei. Lancel is their eldest child and Janei the youngest.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to Kevan, Dorna is a gentle soul, never comfortable except at home with friends and kin around her. She prays seven times a day and loves needlework and flowers.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Dorna is described "chinless lady, flat-chested and chicken-legged" with her lack of chin shared by her father, yet she is still very loved by her husband.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">It is unclear from Robert's comment about Dorna being a "dumb whore with a fat arse" whether her physical description was changed in the series (as done with many characters) or he called her that way just to insult Lancel.
</p>
