<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Ser Meryn Trant is a member of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Trant","link":"House Trant","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Trant]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/House_Trant" title="House Trant">House Trant</a>, a vassal house to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a>. He becomes a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"knight","link":"knight","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[knight]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Knight" title="Knight" class="mw-redirect">knight</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> after <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>. He is a successful tourney knight, though <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sandor Clegane","link":"Sandor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sandor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor Clegane</a> is scornful of Trant's skills, remarking that "any boy whore with a sword could beat three Meryn Trants."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[First of His Name]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He takes great pride in his status as a knight of the Kingsguard, even though he routinely follows orders which break a knight's sworn duty to defend the weak and the innocent without hesitation or question.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After King Robert's death, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannisters","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister|Lannisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannisters</a> seek to consolidate Joffrey's heirship to the throne by making sure Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> cooperates, and so they send Ser Meryn Trant with a group of Lannister guardsmen to apprehend <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Syrio Forel","link":"Syrio Forel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Syrio Forel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Syrio_Forel" title="Syrio Forel">Syrio Forel</a> defends her, armed only with a wooden practice sword. He disables the Lannister guardsmen, to Meryn's fury and disbelief. Syrio tells Arya to run as he faces down the heavily-armored knight. Later, while the newly coronated King Joffrey holds court, Ser Meryn stands amongst the other Kingsguard in the throne room, and Syrio's fate is not mentioned. When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cersei","link":"Cersei Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cersei Lannister|Cersei]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei</a> dismisses Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a> as head of the Kingsguard, the old knight is insulted and draws his sword in fury, inducing Meryn Trant and the rest of the Kingsguard to do the same and to block Ser Barristan's path to the Iron Throne and Joffrey. In response, Barristan sneers that even now he could kill Ser Meryn and the other four Kingsguard as easily as <i>"carving a cake"</i>. There is a tense pause in the throne room, but in the end Selmy throws his sword at Joffrey's feet and storms out in disgust.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Pointy End]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Sansa_and_Meryn_1x10.png|thumb|Ser Meryn holds Sansa at King Joffrey's orders.]]","title":"Sansa_and_Meryn_1x10.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Sansa and Meryn 1x10","caption":"Ser Meryn holds Sansa at King Joffrey's orders.","captionParsed":"Ser Meryn holds Sansa at King Joffrey's orders."}}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" alt="" src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/a/ae/Sansa_and_Meryn_1x10.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20110624065146" width="180" height="166" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Sansa and Meryn 1x10.png" data-image-key="Sansa_and_Meryn_1x10.png"  type="image" />

<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> Personality </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Meryn Trant is known for being a casually cruel man who follows any orders from the king no matter how ridiculous or unnecessary, such as beating Sansa in plain view of the court to the disgust of Tyrion, Bronn, and the Hound.  He also blindly follows orders from Cersei.  It is also heavily implied that he is a cowardly and inept fighter due to having no problem combating Syrio Forel whilst in full armor or abandoning Sansa to the angry mob during the Riot of King's Landing, using his loyalty to King Joffrey as a convenient excuse.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ser Meryn Trant is described as having red hair, red beard, and deep circles under his eyes. He is a skilled warrior and is seen several times in the books as having success in tournaments, particularly at jousting. He went far in the Hand's Tourney, before being unhorsed by Ser Loras. He is considered to have a sly and cruel streak. Even though the Trants are sworn to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a>, Ser Meryn is regarded by Varys as "Cersei's creature".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Meryn, along with the other four Kingsguard members present, openly laughs when his Lord Commander <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a> is unlawfully dismissed from service.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ser Meryn in the TV series is something of a condensation of Meryn and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Boros Blount","link":"Boros Blount","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Boros Blount]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Boros_Blount" title="Boros Blount">Boros Blount</a> in the books; while Boros is in the TV series most of his lines and actions have been given to Meryn, such as beating Sansa in the Throne Room and subsequently being threatened by Tyrion. In the books, Meryn and Boros often appear together, acting as Joffrey's leading enforcers, carrying out his orders to have Sansa beaten in public, or to attack commoners.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At the riot started by Joffrey, Ser Meryn was riding next to Sansa but quickly abandoned her, saying he needed to focus on protecting the King. Tyrion thinks that the sight of two other Kingsguard members being downed by the rioters made Meryn panic, and that he simply used protecting Joffrey as an excuse.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Jaime interviews the present Kingsguard members, he confronts Ser Meryn about beating Sansa, asking him mockingly where in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Book of Brothers","link":"Book of Brothers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Book of Brothers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Book_of_Brothers" title="Book of Brothers" class="mw-redirect">Book of Brothers</a> it is written that the Kingsguard swear to beat women and children. Ser Meryn claims he was just obeying <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the king","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon|the king]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">the king</a>'s orders. Jaime, who has witnessed firsthand what disasters can happen as a result of blind obedience to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"a king","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen|a king]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">a king</a>, tells Ser Meryn bluntly "Henceforth you will temper that obedience. Use that ugly thing you keep inside your helm. If Tommen wants you to saddle his horse, obey him. If he tells you to kill his horse, come to me".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ser Meryn is the second to testify in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion's trial","link":"Court trial of Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Court trial of Tyrion Lannister|Tyrion's trial]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51967491856cca5adeda64" href="/wiki/Court_trial_of_Tyrion_Lannister" title="Court trial of Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion's trial</a>. His testimony is partially false, and he "forgets" to mention crucial facts, in order to make Tyrion look guilty.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ser Meryn is not sent to Braavos as a guard for the new Master of Coin (who is Harys Swift, not Mace Tyrell), thus is not killed by Arya. Instead, another man in Arya's kill list, Rafford (also known as Raff the Sweetling), is sent with Ser Harys, and meets his fate. As of <i>A Dance with Dragons</i>, Ser Meryn is still alive and active, though his death in the TV series suggests that he will die in a later novel.
</p>
