<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Raymun Redbeard and army climb the Wall.png|thumb|300px|Raymun Redbeard's wildling army climbed over the Wall to bypass the dwindling Night's Watch garrisons.]]","title":"Raymun_Redbeard_and_army_climb_the_Wall.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Raymun Redbeard and army climb the Wall","caption":"Raymun Redbeard's wildling army climbed over the Wall to bypass the dwindling Night's Watch garrisons.","width":300,"captionParsed":"Raymun Redbeard's wildling army climbed over the Wall to bypass the dwindling Night's Watch garrisons."}}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" alt="" src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/7/74/Raymun_Redbeard_and_army_climb_the_Wall.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20150920230306" width="300" height="167" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Raymun Redbeard and army climb the Wall.png" data-image-key="Raymun_Redbeard_and_army_climb_the_Wall.png"  type="image" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->Raymun Redbeard assumed the title of King-Beyond-the-Wall and led an invasion of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the North","link":"the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">the North</a>. He figured that the Wall's massive size was both its greatest advantage and its greatest flaw, making it impossible to patrol the entire fortification day and night. So when the patrols passed, he sent his climbers to scale the Wall and leave ropes and ladders for his army to follow after.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Night's Watch did not even know of his passing until his force was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"already cornered and destroyed","link":"Battle at Long Lake","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle at Long Lake|already cornered and destroyed]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/Battle_at_Long_Lake" title="Battle at Long Lake">already cornered and destroyed</a> by the combined efforts of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Umber","link":"House Umber","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Umber]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/House_Umber" title="House Umber">House Umber</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Wall (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Raymun Redbeard was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-Beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/King-Beyond-the-Wall" title="King-Beyond-the-Wall">King-Beyond-the-Wall</a> in the time of Lord William Stark, the grandfather of Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rickard Stark","link":"Rickard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rickard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/Rickard_Stark" title="Rickard Stark">Rickard Stark</a>. Having seen the decline of the Night's Watch under the leadership of Lord Commander Jack Musgood, Raymun and his raiders crossed over <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> and invaded the North. Eventually, the invading force engaged in battle against the Northerners led by William Stark and Lord Harmond of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Umber","link":"House Umber","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Umber]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/House_Umber" title="House Umber">House Umber</a>, "the Drunken Giant". Lord William was slain and his younger brother, Artos Stark, "the Implacable", slew Raymun. Raymun's younger brother fled back <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"beyond the Wall","link":"beyond the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[beyond the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/Beyond_the_Wall" title="Beyond the Wall">beyond the Wall</a> and became known as "the Red Raven", for having fled from battle, "Red" for the color of his hair and "Raven" because it rhymed with "craven".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Night's Watch arrived too late to fight so Artos, grieving over the headless corpse of his brother, assigned them the shameful task of burying the dead. Lord Commander Musgood was forever after known as "Sleepy Jack".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The wildling raider Gerrick Kingsblood boasts direct descent from Raymun Redbeard but is actually descended from the Red Raven, a fact most Free Folk are aware of and use to disparage him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","link":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tales of Dunk and Egg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-56297653856cca7524c408" href="/wiki/Tales_of_Dunk_and_Egg" title="Tales of Dunk and Egg">Tales of Dunk and Egg</a></i> prequel novellas begin in 209 AL, and while the three currently published ones only extend to the year 211 AL, future installments are intended to stretch forward to over the next fifty years of history, using time jumps. Raymund Redbeard led his attack over the Wall in 226 AL, meaning that he is the current King-Beyond-The-Wall during the <i>Tales of Dunk and Egg</i> era, and that he might appear in any potential live-action adaptation of the prequels.
</p>
