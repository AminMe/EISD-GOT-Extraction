<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Tormund is a trusted leader in his own right of a large warband of wildlings. He has joined his forces to those of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-Beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/King-Beyond-the-Wall" title="King-Beyond-the-Wall">King-Beyond-the-Wall</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a>, and now functions as one of Mance's most trusted lieutenants.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He boasts of having bedded a she-bear, which he calls Shella. Ygritte insists that everyone knows he just made this story up.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tormund has two daughters, though he hasn't mentioned if he has any sons.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Hardhome (episode)|Hardhome]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Tormund Giantsbane, also known as Tormund Thunderfist, is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildling","link":"wildling","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildling]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Wildling" title="Wildling" class="mw-redirect">wildling</a> chieftain following <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-Beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/King-Beyond-the-Wall" title="King-Beyond-the-Wall">King-Beyond-the-Wall</a>. He serves alongside the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Bones","link":"Lord of Bones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Bones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Bones" title="Lord of Bones">Lord of Bones</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Styr","link":"Styr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Styr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Styr" title="Styr">Styr</a> the Magnar of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Thenn","link":"Thenn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Thenn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Thenn" title="Thenn" class="mw-redirect">Thenn</a> and several other chieftains as an advisor and lieutenant to "the Mance". He is a charismatic, hearty, good-humored man and, by wildling standards, has a rough sense of honor. He styles himself Tall-talker, Horn-blower, Breaker of Ice, Husband to Bears, Mead-King of Ruddy Hall, Speaker to Gods and Father of Hosts. He has four sons (Toregg, Torwynd, Dryn, and Dormund) and a daughter (Munda). In contrast, the TV series only mentioned in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hardhome","link":"Hardhome (episode)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hardhome (episode)|Hardhome]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Hardhome_(episode)" title="Hardhome (episode)">Hardhome</a>" that he has two daughters.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tormund is older in the books than in the TV series: he is described as having a white beard so he is not a young man, but he is also described as large, broad, and still very strong. He wears thick gold bands graven with runes on his massive arms, and a heavy shirt of black ringmail that could only have come from a dead ranger. Tormund likes to brag about his deeds, but he is not arrogant.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While Jon travels with the wildlings, he and Tormund become friends despite Jon's effort to keep his distance from the wildlings. Jon is rather amused to hear Tormund's stories, one of them is about having sex with a woman who was a she-bear (in the show, Tormund tells Ygritte a story about the same subject, but much shorter). Tormund approves of the growing romance between Jon and Ygritte, and encourages Jon to have sex with her. When the Lord of Bones acts hostilely and suspiciously toward Jon, Tormund supports Jon.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books it is not Tormund but Styr that leads the wildling party to scale the Wall. Tormund stays behind with the main wildling force, and later he and other lieutenants are sent by Mance to attack several areas of the Wall, in order to draw attention away from the main host and to draw away the defenders. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bowen Marsh","link":"Bowen Marsh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bowen Marsh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Bowen_Marsh" title="Bowen Marsh">Bowen Marsh</a> falls for the trick and takes most of the garrison from Castle Black.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tormund does not take part in the repeated attacks on Castle Black. It is not him but <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Bones","link":"Lord of Bones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Bones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Bones" title="Lord of Bones">Lord of Bones</a> that is captured by the Watch.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Jon goes to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"parley","link":"parley","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[parley]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Parley" title="Parley">parley</a> with Mance (actually, to kill him), he first meets Tormund. Although they are enemies, Tormund remains friendly toward Jon. He compliments the defenders for putting up such valiant fight against the wildlings despite being outnumbered, and is filled with awe to hear that one-armed blacksmith Donal Noye killed <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg","link":"Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Mag_Mar_Tun_Doh_Weg" title="Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg">Mag Mar Tun Doh Weg</a>. They go to Mance's tent, chatting leisurely, sharing Tormund's mead waterskin. Tormund is deeply saddened to hear about <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ygritte","link":"Ygritte","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ygritte]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Ygritte" title="Ygritte">Ygritte</a>'s death, and is indifferent to hear <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Styr","link":"Styr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Styr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Styr" title="Styr">Styr</a> died too. He proudly tells Jon that Longspear Ryk kidnapped his daughter Munda, and they like each other. When they reach the camp, Harma Dogshead and Varamyr Sixskins want to kill Jon, but Tormund protects him, insisting that he has the right to speak with Mance.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Stannis attacks the wildlings, Tormund leads a triple line of spearmen, but they are attacked on the flank by Stannis's knights and break. Tormund and many of his troops escape. He manages to regather the dispersed wildlings, preparing for new assault.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Some time afterwards, Jon sends Val (Mance's sister in law) to parley with Tormund. Tormund agrees to make peace with the Watch, and surrenders willingly with three or four thousand wildlings. The negotiation between him and Jon is initially unpleasant: he roars and calls Jon a craven, a liar, and a turncloak, curses him for a black-hearted buggering kneeler, a robber, and a carrion crow. Twice he flings his drinking horn at Jon’s head, though only after he has emptied it (Tormund is not the sort of man to waste a good mead). Jon lets it all wash over him, never raises his own voice nor answers a threat with a threat, but neither does he give more ground than he had come prepared to give. Finally they reach an agreement.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">One of the terms that Jon demands from Tormund that he and his followers give away their valuables, to pay for the food and shelter the Watch will give them. Without hesitation, Tormund give Jon as a first payment two golden heavy armbands, engraved with the ancient runes of the First Men. Jon feels sorry that such precious items will be melted down by the Braavosi, and suggests that Tormund keeps them. Tormund answers "No. I’ll not have it said that Tormund Thunderfist made the free folk give up their treasures whilst he kept his own”.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Jon receives the Bastard Letter from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ramsay","link":"Ramsay Bolton","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ramsay Bolton|Ramsay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-91268341856cca5d10b720" href="/wiki/Ramsay_Bolton" title="Ramsay Bolton">Ramsay</a>, Tormund guesses by his expression the letter contains bad news. He admits he is illiterate: "Tormund Thunderfist had better things to do than learn to make papers talk at him".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Stannis asks Jon what kind of man is Tormund. Jon answers: "Tormund Giantsbane seemed to me the sort of man who would make a good friend and a bad enemy".
</p>
