<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70122988256cca5fc35c4c" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Night's King fell in love with a woman "with skin as white as the moon and eyes like blue stars"; he loved her though "her skin was cold as ice", and when he gave his seed to her he gave her his soul as well. He brought her to the Nightfort and bound the brothers of the Night's Watch to his will through sorcery. He declared himself "Night's King" and ruled over the Wall and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Gift","link":"The Gift","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Gift]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70122988256cca5fc35c4c" href="/wiki/The_Gift" title="The Gift">The Gift</a> as his own. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King in the North","link":"King in the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King in the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70122988256cca5fc35c4c" href="/wiki/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">King in the North</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brandon the Breaker","link":"Brandon Stark (the Breaker)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brandon Stark (the Breaker)|Brandon the Breaker]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70122988256cca5fc35c4c" href="/wiki/Brandon_Stark_(the_Breaker)" title="Brandon Stark (the Breaker)">Brandon the Breaker</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joramun","link":"Joramun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joramun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-70122988256cca5fc35c4c" href="/wiki/Joramun" title="Joramun">Joramun</a>, the King-Beyond-the-Wall, joined forces to defeat him and his queen.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After their deaths, it was discovered that they had been making human sacrifices to the Others - the White Walkers.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After their defeat, the rule was enforced that the castles of the Night's Watch along the Wall should never be fortified against approach from the south, so that they cannot oppose the lands south of the Wall which they are meant to defend. The downfall of the Night's King and Queen also resulted in the strict enforcement of the rule that the Night's Watch is meant to be politically neutral, as guardians who do not "rule" the Wall but who serve the realms of men.
</p>
