</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Qhorin</b> is a recurring character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"second season","link":"Season 2","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2|second season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a>. He is played by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Simon Armstrong","link":"Simon Armstrong","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Simon Armstrong]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Simon_Armstrong" title="Simon Armstrong">Simon Armstrong</a>. Qhorin Halfhand is a member of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>, a senior and experienced ranger who is regarded as an elite scout and soldier. He is known as <b>Qhorin Halfhand</b> or simply <b>The Halfhand</b>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Qhorin is considered to be one of the best and most experienced rangers within the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. He is called half-hand because he lost most of his fingers on his right hand in a fight with a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildling","link":"wildling","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildling]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Wildling" title="Wildling" class="mw-redirect">wildling</a>. His injury has forced him to train with his left hand, resulting in his becoming as proficient with his left as he ever was with his right. He was once a good friend to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a> before Mance turned traitor against the Night's Watch.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Qhorin is based at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Shadow Tower","link":"The Shadow Tower","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Shadow Tower]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/The_Shadow_Tower" title="The Shadow Tower">The Shadow Tower</a> along <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> but spends a significant portion of his time ranging <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"beyond the Wall","link":"beyond the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[beyond the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Beyond_the_Wall" title="Beyond the Wall">beyond the Wall</a>. He was stranded beyond the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Skirling Pass","link":"Skirling Pass","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Skirling Pass]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Skirling_Pass" title="Skirling Pass">Skirling Pass</a> during the last winter before the series begins. He survived alone in the wild for an entire winter, returning to the Wall once the snow thawed.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Ghost of Harrenhal]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels Qhorin Halfhand is regarded as an extremely capable scout, based at the Shadow Tower, the western-most manned castle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>. He is held in high esteem by Lord Commander <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jeor Mormont","link":"Jeor Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jeor Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He has a clean shaven face and is tall. It is never explicitly stated he wears a hat. He is very calm and collected. Qhorin specifically lost three fingers on his right hand, all except his index finger and thumb (thus he is still able to more or less functionally use his hand). In the TV series, of course, the actor is wearing heavy gloves due to the cold, and this obscures that he actually has these fingers, but they do refer to him in dialogue as "Halfhand". His personality differs quite a bit from the books in the TV series in that while he is still a rather calm person, he is shown to be more gruff and shows a disdain for southerners. Unlike his counterpart in the books, he sports a moutache and a goatee.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Qhorin's story is significantly different than in the TV series: first, his reason for sending the scout parties was to find out what was the secret power that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a> was looking for in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Frostfangs","link":"Frostfangs","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Frostfangs]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Frostfangs" title="Frostfangs">Frostfangs</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Second, Qhorin never ordered Jon to kill <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ygritte","link":"Ygritte","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ygritte]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211058247456cca5f267010" href="/wiki/Ygritte" title="Ygritte">Ygritte</a>: he told him "you must do what needs be done". After Jon let Ygritte go and returned to the others, Qhorin explained Jon it was not bad choice to spare her life. If he wanted to kill her, he'd do it himself, or order Ebben to do that. He left Ygritte's fate for Jon to decide in order to know him better.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Third, when they were chased by the wildlings and had no hope of survival, Qhorin specifically ordered Jon to yield: "If we are taken, you will go over to them, as the wildling girl you captured once urged you. They may demand that you cut your cloak to ribbons, that you swear them an oath on your father's grave, that you curse your brothers and your Lord Commander. You must not balk, whatever is asked of you. Do as they bid you... but in your heart, remember who and what you are. Ride with them, eat with them, fight with them, for as long as it takes". Qhorin also ordered Jon to find out what was the secret power that the wildlings sought.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Once they were surrounded, Qhorin deliberately picked a fight with Jon, and let Jon kill him. The ploy worked well, and the wildlings were convinced that Jon truly turned his cloak.
</p>
