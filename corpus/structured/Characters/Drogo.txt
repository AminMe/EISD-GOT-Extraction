</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Drogo</b> is a major character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first ","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first ]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first </a> season. He is played by starring cast member <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jason Momoa","link":"Jason Momoa","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jason Momoa]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Jason_Momoa" title="Jason Momoa">Jason Momoa</a>, and debuts in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"series premiere","link":"Winter is Coming","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winter is Coming|series premiere]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Winter_is_Coming" title="Winter is Coming">series premiere</a>. Drogo is a <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khal","link":"khal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Khal" title="Khal">khal</a>,</i> or chieftain, of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> people and is often referred to with his full title, <b>Khal Drogo</b>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Drogo is a powerful warlord and feared warrior. He marries <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> and subsequently grows to love her. He promises to retake the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> for her after she survives an assassination attempt. He raids a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazareen","link":"Lhazareen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazareen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Lhazareen" title="Lhazareen">Lhazareen</a> village to take slaves to fund the planned invasion. After the fight was over he was challenged to a fight by one of his <i>ko</i> who believed him unworthy to continue leading the Khalasar, as it appeared the Khal was obeying the orders of a woman. Drogo kills the warrior but receives a scratch to his chest and is treated by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"godswife","link":"godswife","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[godswife]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Godswife" title="Godswife">godswife</a> and <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maegi","link":"maegi","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[maegi]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Maegi" title="Maegi">maegi</a></i> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mirri Maz Duur","link":"Mirri Maz Duur","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mirri Maz Duur]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Mirri_Maz_Duur" title="Mirri Maz Duur">Mirri Maz Duur</a>. His wound festers and he becomes gravely unwell. Daenerys asks Mirri Maz Duur to save him using <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"blood magic","link":"blood magic","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[blood magic]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Blood_magic" title="Blood magic">blood magic</a>, but Mirri Maz Duur uses a spell that leaves him in a vegetative state in revenge for the destruction of her village. Daenerys ends his suffering by smothering him, then burning the body. Daenerys walks into the flames with her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragon","link":"dragon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon" class="mw-redirect">dragon</a> eggs, but survives the inferno and hatches three dragons. She names the largest one <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogon","link":"Drogon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drogon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Drogon" title="Drogon">Drogon</a> to commemorate her late husband.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Drogo is a <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khal","link":"khal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Khal" title="Khal">khal</a></i> or warlord of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a>, the famed nomadic horse-lords of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"eastern continent","link":"Essos","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos|eastern continent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">eastern continent</a> beyond the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a>. The Dothraki are fierce warriors, skilled in battle, unrelenting in combat and known for savagery towards non-Dothraki. Amongst their own people they have a code of honor, albeit still a harsh and unforgiving one. Drogo was very young to have his own <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khalasar","link":"khalasar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khalasar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a></i>, particularly such a large one. <!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->In <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vaes Dothrak","link":"Vaes Dothrak","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vaes Dothrak]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Vaes_Dothrak" title="Vaes Dothrak">Vaes Dothrak</a>, Drogo is pleased to learn that his son will be the '<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stallion Who Mounts the World","link":"Stallion Who Mounts the World","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stallion Who Mounts the World]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Stallion_Who_Mounts_the_World" title="Stallion Who Mounts the World">Stallion Who Mounts the World</a>', a prophesied <i>khal of khals</i> who will command the largest Khalasar ever seen and lead the Dothraki to dominion over all the lands of the world. He also approves his wife naming him <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaego","link":"Rhaego","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaego]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Rhaego" title="Rhaego">Rhaego</a> after her slain brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegar","link":"Rhaegar Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar Targaryen|Rhaegar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar</a>, a great warrior defeated in battle by "the usurper" Robert Baratheon. However, a drunken and aggressive Viserys petulantly demands that Drogo took Daenerys but never provided the army he promised. He then threatens that unless he is given his army, he will take back his sister, but leave Drogo the child that he will cut out and leave for him. Furious, but careful to control his emotions for the sake of Daenerys and their child, Drogo agrees to give Viserys "a golden crown that men shall tremble to behold." Not understanding the language, Viserys believes he is finally getting what he wants, and lowers his guard, allowing two of Drogo's bloodriders to disarm and restrain him. Drogo melts a gold belt in a pot, then '"crowns" Viserys with the molten gold, killing him.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[A Golden Crown]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daenerys asks Drogo to continue with the invasion, but with his obligations to Viserys gone, Drogo is reluctant to attempt to cross the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> because of his people's fear of the "poison water" (the sea, which the Dothraki fear, because they distrust any liquid that a horse will not drink) and "wooden horses" (ships). Ser Jorah later saves Daenerys from an <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"assassin","link":"Wineseller","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wineseller|assassin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Wineseller" title="Wineseller">assassin</a> sent by King Robert. Learning of the attempt, Drogo rewards Jorah with any horse he desires from Drogo's own herd. Drogo swears a blood oath that he will punish Westeros for their attempt on the life of his wife and child, lead the Dothraki beyond the poison water to invade the Seven Kingdoms and seat his son on the "Iron Chair" of Westeros.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[You Win or You Die]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Drogo leads his men to raid and plunder a village of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazareen","link":"Lhazareen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazareen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Lhazareen" title="Lhazareen">Lhazareen</a>, peaceful sheep-herders. He plans to take slaves to trade for a fleet to invade Westeros. Daenerys protests when the Dothraki begin raping the women of the village, and has her retinue stop the practice. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mago","link":"Mago","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mago]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Mago" title="Mago">Mago</a> becomes infuriated at Daenerys' presumption to give him orders and complains to Drogo.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Khal Drogo commands forty thousand Dothraki warriors and is a skilled and respected war leader. His bloodriders are Cohollo, Qotho, and Haggo. <i>Ko</i> in his khalasar include Jhaqo, Pono, and Mago. He owns a palace in Vaes Dothrak and a nine-towered mansion in Pentos, given to him by the magisters of the city to buy him off to avoid the Dothraki looting the city.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Of note is his treatment of Daenerys on their wedding night, which is significantly more sensitive than in the TV series, including explicitly asking her permission with the word "No?", to which Daenerys replies "Yes."
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the book, Mago does not defy Drogo nor picks a fight with him. Drogo is injured not by Mago, but during a battle against another <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khalasar","link":"khalasar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khalasar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a>, by a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bloodrider","link":"bloodrider","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[bloodrider]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Bloodrider" title="Bloodrider">bloodrider</a> of a rival <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khal","link":"khal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/Khal" title="Khal">khal</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Dothraki custom dictates that defeated warriors should shave their heads, but Drogo dies with all of his immensely long hair, having never once been defeated in combat. In the books, the Dothraki adorn their long hair braids with various small bells, adding new ones for each victory. Jason Momoa said the bells weren't included in the TV series because they weren't very scary in real life: the constant jingling noises were somewhat comical, and it was felt they detracted from the performance.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.thisisfakediy.co.uk\/articles\/television\/qa-game-of-thrones\/ Jason Mamoa Q&A]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the Dothraki tradition he has a long moustache, but no beard.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170515229356cca59d0bb5e" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a> has stated that he is a huge fan of J.R.R. Tolkien's <i>The Lord of the Rings</i>, in which the father of the main character Frodo Baggins is named Drogo Baggins.
</p>
