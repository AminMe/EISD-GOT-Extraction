</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Barra</b> is a minor character in <i>Game of Thrones</i>. The young actor or actress playing her has not been identified. She only appears in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fifth episode","link":"The Wolf and the Lion","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Wolf and the Lion|fifth episode]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/The_Wolf_and_the_Lion" title="The Wolf and the Lion">fifth episode</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first season","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first episode","link":"The North Remembers","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The North Remembers|first episode]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/The_North_Remembers" title="The North Remembers">first episode</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"second season","link":"Season 2","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2|second season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a>. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Barra is an infant, the bastard child of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mhaegen","link":"Mhaegen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mhaegen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Mhaegen" title="Mhaegen">Mhaegen</a>. Mhaeghen is a prostitute working for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. Barra lives with Mhaegen in one of his brothels. Her name may be a reference to her father's noble House, Baratheon.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Barra is identified as an infant with thin, dark hair. Her mother is 15 years old and her name is never mentioned. When Eddard Stark sees baby Barra, he thinks of how much she resembles her half-sister, the eldest of Robert's bastards Mya Stone (whom Robert fathered at a young age when they were fostered by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Arryn","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a> at the Eyrie, and has not appeared yet in the TV series).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the bloody brawl with Jaime, Cersei accuses Eddard of being drunk after a night of debauchery in the brothel. Eddard tells Robert he had only gone there to see the baby, Barra. Robert is quite embarrassed to hear that. However, by telling Robert why he was at the brothel, Ned also made Cersei aware of the baby's existence (though Cersei could also have been informed about Barra from her spies, so it is uncertain if Ned indirectly caused the baby's death). 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When a dying Robert makes Eddard swear that he will protect and be a second father to his children, it is Barra, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gendry","link":"Gendry","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gendry]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Gendry" title="Gendry">Gendry</a>, and Mya Stone that Eddard thinks of when he takes the oath.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Afterwards, Barra is ordered to be killed - though in the books, no mention is made that a string of other bastard children are also killed (it is said that Cersei has had two of Robert's bastards secretly murdered in the past). It is one of the gold cloaks, Allar Deem, instead of Janos Slynt himself, who kills the baby, and her mother as well who tries to protect the child. When Slynt is made Lord of Harrenhal, he recommends Allar Deem to replace him as commander of the Gold Cloaks. However, Tyrion orders that Deem be sent to the Wall with Slynt. Tyrion also sends the captain of the ship that is taking them there a message that if a wave washes Deem overboard it would not be amiss.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Tyrion discusses Barra's death with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Varys","link":"Varys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Varys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a>, Varys says, looking grief-stricken "There was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"another bastard","link":"Gendry","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gendry|another bastard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Gendry" title="Gendry">another bastard</a>, a boy, older. I took steps to see him removed from harm's way... but I confess, I never dreamed the babe would be at risk. A baseborn girl, less than a year old, with a whore for a mother. What threat could she pose?". Tyrion answers bitterly "She was Robert's. That was enough for Cersei, it would seem". While Tyrion genuinely grieves for Barra, it is unclear if Varys is really sorry for her. His excuse for not acting to save Barra is questionable, given that everyone knows the Lannisters and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"their henchmen","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane|their henchmen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">their henchmen</a> are not above killing <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"small children","link":"Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)|small children]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)" title="Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)">small children</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"infants","link":"Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)|infants]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-73070289756cca606ce165" href="/wiki/Aegon_Targaryen_(Son_of_Rhaegar)" title="Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)">infants</a>.
