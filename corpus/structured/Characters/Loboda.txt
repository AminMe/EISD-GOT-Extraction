<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Loboda is an elder of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Thenn","link":"Thenn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Thenn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Thenn" title="Thenn" class="mw-redirect">Thenn</a>, a fierce tribe of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Folk","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">Free Folk</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Loboda is a fierce and highly skilled warrior, and just like <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Styr","link":"Styr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Styr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Styr" title="Styr">Styr</a> he also fights wielding a huge axe. Loboda seems to show little sign of fear even when he is confronted by a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walker","link":"White Walker (Hardhome)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walker (Hardhome)|White Walker]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/White_Walker_(Hardhome)" title="White Walker (Hardhome)">White Walker</a>, and without hesitation engages the Walker in single combat.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 5","link":"Season 5","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">Season 5</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander","link":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander of the Night's Watch|Lord Commander]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch" title="Lord Commander of the Night's Watch">Lord Commander</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tormund","link":"Tormund","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tormund]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Tormund" title="Tormund">Tormund</a> arrive at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hardhome","link":"Hardhome","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hardhome]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Hardhome" title="Hardhome">Hardhome</a> to offer the Free Folk to retreat to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Black","link":"Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a> and settle <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"south","link":"The Gift","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Gift|south]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/The_Gift" title="The Gift">south</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wall","link":"The Wall","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Wall|Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">Wall</a>, a meeting of the clan chieftains is held, where Loboda represents the Thenns. Loboda doesn't believe the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> cares about what happens to them, despite the Lord Commander's appeals and his offer of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragonglass","link":"dragonglass","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragonglass]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Dragonglass" title="Dragonglass">dragonglass</a> as a weapon against the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a>, which Loboda dismisses as just as unlikely as the mythical <i>"ice spiders as big as hounds"</i>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Karsi","link":"Karsi","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Karsi]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Karsi" title="Karsi">Karsi</a> replies that with everything they have seen none of this is unbelievable. Jon argues <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a> would have supported his suggestion, so Loboda asks what happened to him. When Snow admits he killed him, Loboda prepares to kill him, only to be stopped by Tormund, who reveals it was a mercy kill. Karsi decides to side with the Lord Commander's plan as long as Tormund vouches for him, but Loboda remains stubborn, claiming that all the Free Folk who get on the Crows' boats will be executed, as they have always been enemies and his ancestors would spit on him if he broke bread with a crow. Loboda abandons the hall, and he is followed by many other chieftains, to the dismay of Jon, Tormund, and Karsi, who exclaims she <i>"fucking hates Thenns."</i>

<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, there is no mention of a character named Loboda or of the presence of any Thenns at Hardhome. However, the show character plays a role similar to an unnamed, seasoned Thenn warrior mentioned in the prologue of <i>A Dance with Dragons</i>, who in the aftermath of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mance Rayder","link":"Mance Rayder","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mance Rayder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a>'s defeat convinces a large group of Thenns to go back to their lands in the far north and are never heard of again. A second group of Thenns follow Styr's son Sigorn to the Wall and submit to the rule of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>. As the son of Styr, Sigorn is the new Magnar of Thenn.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Loboda's threat to pluck Jon's eyes out is a nod to another Wildling chieftain from the novels who likes to do this to his enemies and is only known among the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> by the nickname of "the Weeper" or "the Weeping Man", because he suffers from epiphora. The Weeper leads 300 warriors against the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shadow Tower","link":"Shadow Tower","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shadow Tower]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Shadow_Tower" title="Shadow Tower" class="mw-redirect">Shadow Tower</a> in conjunction with Mance's attack on <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Black","link":"Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a>, and while he is also defeated, he lives and manages to regroup his forces, remaining a constant annoyance to the Night's Watch.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Nothing from the TV episode or supplementary materials actually said that Loboda succeeded Styr as the new <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Magnar","link":"Magnar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Magnar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-171861263956cca632b0637" href="/wiki/Magnar" title="Magnar">Magnar</a> of the Thenns: he is apparently just a major chieftain/lieutenant of Styr.
</p>
