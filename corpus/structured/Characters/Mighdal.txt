<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-148805863356cca8385e590" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, there is no character named Mighdal. His name was chosen by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"D.B. Weiss","link":"D.B. Weiss","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[D.B. Weiss]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-148805863356cca8385e590" href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> in honor of his beloved childhood friend David Migdal.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The characters of Mighdal and Fennesz were invented to present a problem alluded to in the books, the plight of slaves whose lives actually worsened with freedom.
</p>
