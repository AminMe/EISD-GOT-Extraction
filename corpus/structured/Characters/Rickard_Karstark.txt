Though <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Edmure Tully","link":"Edmure Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Edmure Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Edmure_Tully" title="Edmure Tully">Edmure Tully</a>, Talisa, and Catelyn attempt to advise Robb to hold Karstark hostage and send him to the Night's Watch after the war - on the basis that the Karstarks form a significant portion of Robb's army, and they will abandon Robb if he executes their lord. Despite the warnings of all of his councilors, Robb refuses to listen.

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Rickard Karstark is the ruling lord of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Karstark","link":"House Karstark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Karstark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/House_Karstark" title="House Karstark">House Karstark</a> of Karhold. Their lands are along a heavily-forested headland far to the north-east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>, overlooking the Shivering Sea. He is named for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark's","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark|Eddard Stark's]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark's</a> father, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rickard","link":"Rickard Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rickard Stark|Rickard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Rickard_Stark" title="Rickard Stark">Rickard</a>, and fought in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a> alongside his liege lord. He has four children: Harrion, Torrhen, Eddard and Alys. He's described as gaunt and long-bearded, and Jon Snow implies he is a fierce warrior.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Two of Lord Rickard's sons, Torrhen and Eddard, are struck down by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","link":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Whispering Wood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Whispering_Wood" title="Battle of the Whispering Wood">Battle of the Whispering Wood</a>, while he kills <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stafford Lannister","link":"Stafford Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stafford Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Stafford_Lannister" title="Stafford Lannister">Stafford Lannister</a> at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Oxcross","link":"Battle of Oxcross","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Oxcross]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Oxcross" title="Battle of Oxcross">Battle of Oxcross</a>. Lord Rickard's mention of losing a son to Jaime prior to Torrhen's death seems to reference Eddard's death at the Whispering Wood. Though clearly angered that Jaime is kept alive, Lord Rickard doesn't openly demand the execution of Jaime. Catelyn's decision to send Jaime back to King's Landing isn't caused by Karstark's actions but of fear that Joffrey may execute Sansa on a whim, as she believes Theon has executed Bran and Rickon. Lord Rickard becomes furious when Robb forgives Catelyn for her actions, and murders the hostages <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Willem Lannister","link":"Willem Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Willem Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Willem_Lannister" title="Willem Lannister">Willem Lannister</a> and Tion Frey - not <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Martyn Lannister","link":"Martyn Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Martyn Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Martyn_Lannister" title="Martyn Lannister">Martyn Lannister</a>. Robb subsequently orders Rickard executed, and the Karstarks abandon him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Rickard is succeeded by his son Harrion, who is currently held prisoner by the Lannisters and his whereabouts are unknown. Rickard's execution has severe consequences in the books. While the TV series make it clear that <i>all</i> of the Karstark forces abandoned Robb, it is implied in the books that the few Karstarks who remained with Robb following Rickard's execution played a role in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-30300912656cca5f89f2ea" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>.
</p>
