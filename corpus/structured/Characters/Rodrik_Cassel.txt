<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ser Rodrik Cassel is an honorable knight and stalwart warrior, advancing in years but still hale. He is proud of his facial hair, which he cultivates as "whiskers". He is very unhappy when on the trip to King's Landing he must cut them off. He is fiercely loyal to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a>. His older brother Martyn, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jory","link":"Jory Cassel","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jory Cassel|Jory]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Jory_Cassel" title="Jory Cassel">Jory</a>'s father, died at the Tower of Joy during Robert's Rebellion. Rodrik has married thrice but only has one surviving child, his daughter Beth. He also dislikes musicians.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the show, the death of Rodrik Cassel is a combination of the deaths of two other characters in the book: <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Farlen","link":"Farlen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Farlen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Farlen" title="Farlen">Farlen</a>, the kennelmaster at Winterfell, and Benfred Tallhart, heir to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tallhart","link":"House Tallhart","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tallhart]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/House_Tallhart" title="House Tallhart">House Tallhart</a>. Farlen is beheaded by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Theon","link":"Theon Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Theon Greyjoy|Theon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon</a>, as punishment for killing three of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ironborn","link":"Ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">Ironborn</a> (who were actually killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ramsay Snow","link":"Ramsay Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ramsay Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Ramsay_Snow" title="Ramsay Snow" class="mw-redirect">Ramsay Snow</a> at Theon's command) who participated in the abduction and killing the two boys Theon passes off as Bran and Rickon - Theon knows Farlen is innocent but needs someone had to be punished. Benfred Tallhart, a former friend of Theon and Robb, leads a Northern scout force ambushed at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stony Shore","link":"Stony Shore","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stony Shore]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Stony_Shore" title="Stony Shore">Stony Shore</a> when they come to confront the ironborn raiders. He refuses to answer questions about the defenses at Torrhen's Square. He insults and spits on Theon, and Theon's uncle <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aeron Damphair","link":"Aeron Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aeron Greyjoy|Aeron Damphair]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Aeron_Greyjoy" title="Aeron Greyjoy">Aeron Damphair</a> tells Theon that he must must be executed for his disrespect. He is drowned on the shores as a sacrifice to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drowned God","link":"Drowned God","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drowned God]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Drowned_God" title="Drowned God">Drowned God</a>. In the novel, Ser Rodrik dies prior to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of Winterfell","link":"Sack of Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Sack_of_Winterfell" title="Sack of Winterfell">Sack of Winterfell</a> - he is killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ramsay Snow","link":"Ramsay Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ramsay Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Ramsay_Snow" title="Ramsay Snow" class="mw-redirect">Ramsay Snow</a>, who pretended to have come to aid the Northern host - while besieging Winterfell.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of Winterfell","link":"Sack of Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Sack_of_Winterfell" title="Sack of Winterfell">Sack of Winterfell</a>, Beth Cassel is brought to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dreadfort","link":"Dreadfort","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dreadfort]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Dreadfort" title="Dreadfort" class="mw-redirect">Dreadfort</a>. Her fate is unknown.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-144814691956cca5fb86479" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Cassel" is pronounced "Cuss-EL", not "Kas-el" (that is, "Cassel" doesn't rhyme with "castle").
</p>
