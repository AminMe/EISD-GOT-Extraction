<h2>Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Daemon Blackfyre was the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"legitimized","link":"Legitimization","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Legitimization|legitimized]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Legitimization" title="Legitimization">legitimized</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bastard","link":"bastard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[bastard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Bastard" title="Bastard" class="mw-redirect">bastard</a> son of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon IV Targaryen","link":"Aegon IV Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon IV Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Aegon_IV_Targaryen" title="Aegon IV Targaryen">Aegon IV Targaryen</a> and his cousin, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daena Targaryen","link":"Daena Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daena Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Daena_Targaryen" title="Daena Targaryen">Daena Targaryen</a>. He was one of the infamous <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Bastards","link":"Great Bastards","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Bastards]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Great_Bastards" title="Great Bastards">Great Bastards</a>. After his legitimization, he founded his own new noble House as a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"cadet branch","link":"cadet branch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[cadet branch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Cadet_branch" title="Cadet branch">cadet branch</a> of House Targaryen: <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Blackfyre","link":"House Blackfyre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Blackfyre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/House_Blackfyre" title="House Blackfyre">House Blackfyre</a>, named after the ancestral <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian steel","link":"Valyrian steel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian steel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Valyrian_steel" title="Valyrian steel">Valyrian steel</a> sword of House Targaryen which his father gave him. He led <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"a rebellion","link":"Blackfyre Rebellion","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackfyre Rebellion|a rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Blackfyre_Rebellion" title="Blackfyre Rebellion">a rebellion</a> against his half-brother King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daeron II Targaryen","link":"Daeron II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daeron II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Daeron_II_Targaryen" title="Daeron II Targaryen">Daeron II Targaryen</a>, the legitimate Targaryen heir, but was defeated and killed at the climactic <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Redgrass Field","link":"Battle of the Redgrass Field","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Redgrass Field]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Redgrass_Field" title="Battle of the Redgrass Field">Battle of the Redgrass Field</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">With <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Serena Blackfyre","link":"Serena Blackfyre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Serena Blackfyre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Serena_Blackfyre" title="Serena Blackfyre">Serena Blackfyre</a> he fathered the twins Aegon and Aemon Blackfyre, and Daemon II Blackfyre.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Daemon is mentioned several times. More of his backstory is revealed in the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","link":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tales of Dunk and Egg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Tales_of_Dunk_and_Egg" title="Tales of Dunk and Egg">Tales of Dunk and Egg</a></i>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daemon was born Daemon Waters, only taking the name Blackfyre after he had been legitimized by his father. He was named after his great-grandfather Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daemon Targaryen","link":"Daemon Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daemon Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Daemon_Targaryen" title="Daemon Targaryen">Daemon Targaryen</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After the Dance of the Dragons, the civil war between <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaenyra","link":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenyra Targaryen|Rhaenyra]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Rhaenyra_Targaryen" title="Rhaenyra Targaryen">Rhaenyra</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II Targaryen","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II Targaryen</a>, House Targaryen's royal succession laws were revised to put female heirs behind all possible male ones - specifically that if a all of a lord's sons die, the throne will pass to his younger brother before it passes to his own daughters. Under the normal succession laws of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andals","link":"Andals","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andals]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Andals" title="Andals">Andals</a>, a daughter inherits before an uncle. Both Rhaenyra and Aegon II died in the Dance, but were succeeded by Rhaenyra's son Aegon III. His two sons Daeron and Baelor became king after him but died without issue. Aegon III did have three daughters, but at this point the new succession laws came into play, so Baelor was succeeded by Aegon III's younger brother Viserys II, who in turn was succeeded by his own son Aegon IV.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Had the new succession laws not gone into effect, Aegon III's daughters would have inherited ahead of Aegon IV, instead of being skipped over for their uncle Viserys II. As it happened, the oldest of these three daughters, Daena, had an affair with Aegon IV, her own first cousin, which produced their bastard son Daemon Blackfyre.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The result is that Daemon and the rest of House Blackfyre are actually descended from older brother Aegon III, while all subsequent members of the main line of House Targaryen (i.e. Daenerys) are descended from younger brother Viserys II.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daemon was a bastard, and though legitimized, he was simply younger than Daeron II. Therefore, part of his claim to the throne rested on the flimsy accusation that Daeron II was himself a bastard, fathered by Aegon IV's younger brother Aemon the Dragon-Knight. However, others pointed out that Daemon's mother Daena <i>would</i> have been ahead of Daeron II's grandfather Viserys II, had the succession laws not been arbitrarily changed.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ultimately Daemon Blackfyre proclaimed himself king, plunging the Seven Kingdoms into the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackfyre Rebellion","link":"Blackfyre Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackfyre Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Blackfyre_Rebellion" title="Blackfyre Rebellion">Blackfyre Rebellion</a>. At the climactic <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Redgrass Field","link":"Battle of Redgrass Field","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Redgrass Field]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-94043211056cca66895fa7" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Redgrass_Field" title="Battle of Redgrass Field" class="mw-redirect">Battle of Redgrass Field</a>, Brynden Rivers and his company of longbowman, the Raven's Teeth, took the Weeping Ridge, and rained arrows down onto Daemon Blackfyre from 300 yards away, killing Daemon and his twin sons. Brynden was cursed as a "kinslayer" for this, despite there being no way of knowing whose arrow had struck the fatal blow itself (though either way, it was a company of archers he commanded). Years later, however, Brynden personally beheaded Daemon's younger son Aenys Blackfyre (his own half-nephew) while under a flag of truce, making himself both certainly a kinslayer and an oathbreaker - for which he was exiled to the Night's Watch.
</p>
