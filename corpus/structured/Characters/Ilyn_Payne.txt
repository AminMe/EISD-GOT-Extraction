<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">On January 9, 2013 actor Wilko Johnson announced that he had been diagnosed with terminal cancer of the pancreas.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.guardian.co.uk\/music\/2013\/jan\/09\/wilko-johnson-terminal-cancer The Guardian]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Though he initially refused treatment, he underwent a successful tumour-removal operation in 2014.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.bbc.co.uk\/news\/entertainment-arts-29727632 Wilko Johnson says he is 'cancer free' - BBC News]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Johnson's declining health has apparently led to him being gradually phased out of the TV series. He only appeared in one episode of Season 2 ("Blackwater"), and did not appear in Season 3 at all. Then again, Ser Ilyn did not prominently appear in the first half of the third novel either (which was adapted as Season 3). (On the other hand, Ser Ilyn's most important role in Season Four was instead given to Bronn.) Given that Ser Ilyn cannot speak or convey his thoughts through writing, he does not have a particularly major presence in the storyline, but he is usually seen hovering around in the background at Joffrey's royal court.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tywin's dialogue in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Children","link":"The Children","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Children]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/The_Children" title="The Children">The Children</a>" confirms that the character is still alive and active.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ilyn Payne served as Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a>'s captain of household guards when Tywin served <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a> as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>. After being heard to joke that it was Tywin who truly ruled the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>, King Aerys ordered Payne's tongue be removed with hot pincers. Even though he was high in Tywin Lannister's service, Tywin Lannister couldn't stop the Mad King, and this was another step in the increasing tensions between the two which eventually led to Tywin's resignation. King Robert Baratheon named Ser Ilyn as King's Justice as a present to Tywin Lannister to compensate Payne for the loss of his tongue while in service to the Lannisters.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the book Ser Ilyn arrives at Robert's camp with Renly and Ser Barristan to provide escort back to King's Landing. When Gregor Clegane is terrorizing the Riverlands, Ser Ilyn's name is put forward to be sent to carry out the Hand's orders to arrest him, as King's Justice that's his job, but because of his ties to the Lannisters Lord Eddard sends Beric Dondarrion instead.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Tyrion Lannister is leading the hill tribes in the army of Tywin Lannister in the Riverlands against the forces of Robb Stark, he is assigned a squire named <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Podrick Payne","link":"Podrick Payne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Podrick Payne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/Podrick_Payne" title="Podrick Payne">Podrick Payne</a>. Podrick is a distant relation of Ser Ilyn.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At the execution of Eddard Stark, Arya realizes to her dismay that the sword he uses to carry it out is the Valyrian great sword of House Stark, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ice","link":"Ice","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ice]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/Ice" title="Ice">Ice</a>. The sword was given to Ser Ilyn afterwards.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Jaime returns to King's Landing, he practices first with his friend <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Addam Marbrand","link":"Addam Marbrand","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Addam Marbrand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-116260932256cca5c683864" href="/wiki/Addam_Marbrand" title="Addam Marbrand">Addam Marbrand</a>, but fearing that Addam may get drunk and reveal that Jaime is a poor fighter now, Jaime moves to practice with Ilyn, not Bronn. Ilyn is much tougher with Jaime than Addam, but Jaime prefers it this way, knowing that his secret is safe with Ilyn. After every practice, they sit and drink together. Jaime finds Ilyn the perfect drinking companion, for he never interrupts Jaime, never disagrees, never complains or asking for favors or tells long pointless stories. Jaime feels comfortable to chat with Ilyn that he reveals to him his darkest secrets.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ser Ilyn is a highly skilled executioner having never botched an execution and seldom requiring more than one blow. Ser Ilyn Payne is described as thin, bald, with clean-shaven but hollow and pockmarked cheeks, with colorless deep-set eyes. He wears iron-grey chainmail over boiled leather.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ilyn is illiterate, thus after his tongue was removed he can no longer communicate complex messages to other people.
</p>
