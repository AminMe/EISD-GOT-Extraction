<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 4","link":"Season 4","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">Season 4</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Mossador attempts to convince his fellow slaves to join <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> and defeat the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Masters","link":"Great Masters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Masters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" href="/wiki/Great_Masters" title="Great Masters">Great Masters</a>, though he meets resistance from skeptical older slaves who have lived through failed slave rebellions. After <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Grey Worm","link":"Grey Worm","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Grey Worm]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" href="/wiki/Grey_Worm" title="Grey Worm">Grey Worm</a> motivates them even further than Mossador and provides them with weapons, they launch a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"revolt","link":"Siege of Meereen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Siege of Meereen|revolt]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Meereen" title="Siege of Meereen">revolt</a> against the Masters.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Oathkeeper]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Mossador is the name of one of the three brothers of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Missandei","link":"Missandei","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Missandei]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" href="/wiki/Missandei" title="Missandei">Missandei</a> who were enslaved alongside her and made into <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Unsullied","link":"Unsullied","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Unsullied]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27851285756cca64a23176" href="/wiki/Unsullied" title="Unsullied">Unsullied</a> and one of two who survived the training. After being liberated by Daenerys, Mossador is one of the Unsullied who reclaims his birth name. While patrolling the streets of Meereen at night, Mossador and another of the Unsullied were ambushed by the Sons of the Harpy. They were crushed to death by falling stones beneath the river wall.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">"Mossador" in the TV series is <i>not</i> Missandei's brother: the TV writers just re-used a name from the novels for a separate character. While he has no direct counterpart in the novels, TV-Mossador is a condensation of several former slave characters in Meereen who don't want peace with the former slave masters.
</p>
