<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Visenya Targaryen</b> is an unseen character mentioned in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"second season","link":"Season 2","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2|second season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a>. She was the sister and wife of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen|Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon the Conqueror</a> and helped him in his invasion and conquest of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> three centuries before the events of the series.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Visenya is said to have been the more temperamental of Aegon's sisters. She was a noted warrior but also had diplomatic skills. After the razing of Harrenhal, she flew on her dragon to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Crackclaw Point","link":"Crackclaw Point","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Crackclaw Point]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/Crackclaw_Point" title="Crackclaw Point">Crackclaw Point</a> and won the support of the native inhabitants by promising that they would hold fealty only to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> and no other lords.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The <i>World of Ice and Fire</i> confirms that Visenya was the more martial of the two sisters. She supported the Targaryen fleet when it attempted to take <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gulltown","link":"Gulltown","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gulltown]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/Gulltown" title="Gulltown">Gulltown</a> early during the War of Conquest, but was unable to prevent its destruction at the hands of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arryn","link":"House Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Arryn|Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/House_Arryn" title="House Arryn">Arryn</a> fleet. She did destroy much of the Arryn fleet with her dragon in vengeance, but could not follow up on the victory. Later, she joined her brother-husband on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Field of Fire","link":"Field of Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Field of Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/Field_of_Fire" title="Field of Fire">Field of Fire</a>, helping win a great victory there (despite sustaining a wound from an arrow). Afterwards she managed to secure the loyalty of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Arryn","link":"House Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/House_Arryn" title="House Arryn">House Arryn</a> by simply flying her dragon to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Eyrie","link":"the Eyrie","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Eyrie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/The_Eyrie" title="The Eyrie">the Eyrie</a> and allowing the King of the Vale (a young boy) a ride on her dragon's back, thus avoiding a bloody conflict.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Though beautiful, skilled in battle and fierce, Visenya was somewhat stand-offish in matters of the heart. Aegon allegedly spent only one night with her for every ten he spent with Rhaenys. For that matter, Visenya was also a skilled ruler: Aegon did weigh in on major decisions both before and after the Conquest, but he usually left his sisters to handle the day-to-day tasks of governance (aided after the conquest by the new "Hand of the King", their bastard half-brother Orys Baratheon).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Rhaenys died in Dorne before the end of Aegon's reign, and a few years after Aegon's death their bastard half-brother Orys died during a rebellion in the Vale, meaning that Visenya was the last surviving member of the Conquest-generation of Targaryens. She continued to exert political influence during the reign of Aegon's successor, Rhaenys's son Aenys I. Through unexplained circumstances, despite the fact that Aenys I had a son, he was succeeded by his half-brother Maegor. Visenya died later in Maegor's reign.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Maegor was the only child that Visenya ever had. When Maegor came of age, he married one of the daughters of Visenya's sister Rhaenys (the daughter was thus Maegor's half-sister, and full sister of King Aenys). Maegor died without surviving issue, and was succeeded by Aenys's son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaehaerys I","link":"Jaehaerys I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaehaerys I Targaryen|Jaehaerys I]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170921144756cca64e92480" href="/wiki/Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen" title="Jaehaerys I Targaryen">Jaehaerys I</a> - which means that later generations of Targaryens (such as Daenerys) are not directly descended from Visenya.
</p>
