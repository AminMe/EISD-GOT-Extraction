<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Edd, also called <b>Dolorous Edd</b>,<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>HBO Viewer's Guide, \"Night's Watch\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> is a member of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Edd is described as being thin and, despite being young, gray-haired. Edd is apparently a member of a lesser branch of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tollett","link":"House Tollett","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tollett]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/House_Tollett" title="House Tollett">House Tollett</a>, a noble house from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Vale","link":"the Vale","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Vale]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/The_Vale" title="The Vale" class="mw-redirect">the Vale</a> sworn to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Royce","link":"House Royce","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Royce]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/House_Royce" title="House Royce">House Royce</a>, as he frequently makes comments indicating that he grew up in poverty, and his family were essentially living on the same level as peasants. He was recruited to the Watch by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yoren","link":"Yoren","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yoren]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Yoren" title="Yoren">Yoren</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Edd takes part in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Ranging","link":"Great Ranging","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Ranging]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Great_Ranging" title="Great Ranging">Great Ranging</a>. He and about a dozen more remain loyal to Lord Commander <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jeor Mormont","link":"Jeor Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jeor Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a> during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mutiny at Craster's Keep","link":"Mutiny at Craster's Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mutiny at Craster's Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Mutiny_at_Craster's_Keep" title="Mutiny at Craster's Keep">Mutiny at Craster's Keep</a>. Being outnumbered 2:1 by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Betrayers","link":"Betrayers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Betrayers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Betrayers" title="Betrayers" class="mw-redirect">Betrayers</a>, they have to retreat, reluctantly leaving <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sam","link":"Sam","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sam]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Sam" title="Sam">Sam</a> behind. They make it to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Black","link":"Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a> long before <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon","link":"Jon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Jon" title="Jon">Jon</a> and Sam.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While the wildlings head for Castle Black, Mance sends several of his lieutenants to attack several areas of the Wall, in order to draw attention away from the main host and to draw away the defenders. Bowen Marsh falls for the trick and takes most of the garrison from Castle Black, among them Edd, to chase after the Weeper. Thus, unlike in the TV show, Edd is not among the defenders of Castle Black.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the siege, in order to make the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Thenns","link":"Thenns","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Thenns]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Thenns" title="Thenns">Thenns</a> think that there are many more defenders than there actually are, the blacksmith Donal Noye commands to put scarecrows on the battlements. To keep the morale high, the defenders start naming the straw sentinels after their absent brothers (among them Edd), wagering as to which of them will collect the most arrows before they are done. When Edd returns to Castle Black after the battle is over, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pypar","link":"Pypar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pypar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Pypar" title="Pypar">Pypar</a> tells him about the contest. Edd is disappointed to hear that the scarecrow named after him took the second place, after Watt of Long Lake.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Some time after the battle is over, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sam","link":"Sam","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sam]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Sam" title="Sam">Sam</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gilly","link":"Gilly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gilly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Gilly" title="Gilly">Gilly</a> finally arrive. On the way to the castle they meet Edd, along with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Denys Mallister","link":"Denys Mallister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Denys Mallister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Denys_Mallister" title="Denys Mallister">Denys Mallister</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bowen Marsh","link":"Bowen Marsh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bowen Marsh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111456486156cca5f5614ea" href="/wiki/Bowen_Marsh" title="Bowen Marsh">Bowen Marsh</a>, Dywen, and Bedwyck (aka Giant). Sam hears from them about the battle.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In "A Dance with Dragons" Jon gives the command of Long Barrow to the ranger Iron Emmett, and assigns Edd as the chief steward and Emmett's second-in-command.
</p>
