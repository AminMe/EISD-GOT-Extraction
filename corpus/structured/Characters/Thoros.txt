<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Thoros_Pyke.png|thumb|left|Thoros fighting in the [[Siege of Pyke]]]]","title":"Thoros_Pyke.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Thoros Pyke","align":"left","caption":"Thoros fighting in the [[Siege of Pyke","captionParsed":"Thoros fighting in the <a _rte_dataidx=\"0005\" href=\"\/wiki\/Siege_of_Pyke\" title=\"Siege of Pyke\">Siege of Pyke<\/a>"}}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" alt="" src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/d/d2/Thoros_Pyke.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20131021145641" width="180" height="200" class="image thumb alignLeft withCaption" data-image-name="Thoros Pyke.png" data-image-key="Thoros_Pyke.png"  type="image" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Thoros was born on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free City","link":"Free City","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free City]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Free_City" title="Free City" class="mw-redirect">Free City</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myr</a>. Being an eighth son, Thoros' father had no means to support him and gave him up to the local <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Temple","link":"Red Temple","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Temple|Temple]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Red_Temple" title="Red Temple">Temple</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Light","link":"Lord of Light","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Light]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Light" title="Lord of Light" class="mw-redirect">Lord of Light</a>. There, he was raised to become a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Priest","link":"Red Priest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Priest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Red_Priest" title="Red Priest">Red Priest</a>, though he grew up a gluttonous and lustful man, constantly raiding the kitchens and taking girls to his chambers.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He was tasked by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Priest","link":"High Priest of R'hllor","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Priest of R'hllor|High Priest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/High_Priest_of_R'hllor" title="High Priest of R'hllor">High Priest</a> to turn future King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> away from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Seven","link":"the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/The_Seven" title="The Seven">the Seven</a> and convert him to the Lord of Light. He was already present in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a> by the time the bodies of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaenys","link":"Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)|Rhaenys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)" title="Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)">Rhaenys</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon Targaryen","link":"Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)|Aegon Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Aegon_Targaryen_(Son_of_Rhaegar)" title="Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)">Aegon Targaryen</a> were presented to Robert. Thoros became part of the court and a drinking and whoring companion of King Robert. He also became famous for using a sword coated with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildfire","link":"wildfire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildfire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a> and set ablaze before combat as a way of intimidating his enemies.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He fought for King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Greyjoy Rebellion","link":"Greyjoy Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Greyjoy Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Greyjoy_Rebellion" title="Greyjoy Rebellion">Greyjoy Rebellion</a>. He was the first man, followed closely by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ser Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont|Ser Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Ser Jorah Mormont</a>, through the breach during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Siege of Pyke","link":"Siege of Pyke","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Siege of Pyke]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Pyke" title="Siege of Pyke">Siege of Pyke</a>, stabbing and terrifying the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> with his flaming sword.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"E4\">\"[[Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Thoros is described as tall, fat, shaved bald, and wearing loose fitting red robes so faded that they appear pink. After some time spent in the Brotherhood, he becomes a loose-skinned, grey-haired man in faded robes and piecemeal armor. Thoros originally was sent to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> by his order to convert King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a> to the faith of R'hllor, because of the King's obsession with fire. However, his efforts to preach his religion were in vain, and combined with the atrocities of Aerys' reign he came to question his own faith and grew despondent. Following <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>, Thoros became a regular hanger-on in the court of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>, kept on in part due to his martial skills. King Robert was also fond of keeping around various interesting foreign visitors as drinking companions, so Thoros also stayed at court due to the good will he enjoyed with Robert.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During his time in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> Thoros became a noted tourney champion, besting many skilled opponents with his flaming sword, such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sandor Clegane","link":"Sandor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sandor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor Clegane</a>, who is afraid of fire. Despite his many victories, and popularity with the King, Thoros earned the animosity of master blacksmith <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tobho Mott","link":"Tobho Mott","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tobho Mott]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Tobho_Mott" title="Tobho Mott">Tobho Mott</a>, who regarded Thoros' habit of dousing his blades in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildfire","link":"wildfire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildfire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a> - which subsequently wrecked the steel - as desecration of his work.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Thoros competes in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tourney","link":"tournament","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[tournament|Tourney]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Tournament" title="Tournament">Tourney</a> of the Hand, unseating <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Beric Dondarrion","link":"Beric Dondarrion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Beric Dondarrion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Beric_Dondarrion" title="Beric Dondarrion">Beric Dondarrion</a> in the joust. The next day he wins the melee competition. Eddard Stark orders Thoros to accompany Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Beric Dondarrion","link":"Beric Dondarrion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Beric Dondarrion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Beric_Dondarrion" title="Beric Dondarrion">Beric Dondarrion</a> to arrest Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gregor Clegane","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a> for his raids in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverlands","link":"Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-16980193056cca5d2cde95" href="/wiki/Riverlands" title="Riverlands" class="mw-redirect">Riverlands</a>. Though Beric briefly appeared in the same scene in the TV series, Thoros was absent.
</p>
