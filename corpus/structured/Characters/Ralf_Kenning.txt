</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Ralf Kenning </b>is a minor character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fourth season","link":"Season 4","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4|fourth season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">fourth season</a>. He is an <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> raider, and a member of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Kenning","link":"House Kenning","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Kenning]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/House_Kenning" title="House Kenning">House Kenning</a>. He is portrayed by guest star <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Grahame Fox","link":"Grahame Fox","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Grahame Fox]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Grahame_Fox" title="Grahame Fox">Grahame Fox</a> and only appears in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Mountain and the Viper","link":"The Mountain and the Viper","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Mountain and the Viper]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/The_Mountain_and_the_Viper" title="The Mountain and the Viper">The Mountain and the Viper</a>".
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Kenning is the commander of the ironborn garrison at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Moat Cailin","link":"Moat Cailin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Moat Cailin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Moat_Cailin" title="Moat Cailin">Moat Cailin</a>.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 4","link":"Season 4","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">Season 4</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Theon Greyjoy","link":"Theon Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Theon Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> enters Moat Cailin to discuss terms of surrender, Kenning asks him why is marching with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Bolton","link":"House Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/House_Bolton" title="House Bolton">House Bolton</a>. Theon informs him that the Boltons took him hostage after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of Winterfell","link":"Sack of Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Sack_of_Winterfell" title="Sack of Winterfell">Sack of Winterfell</a> and sent him to treat with Kenning in good faith as his fellow ironborn. Theon gives a message from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roose Bolton","link":"Roose Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roose Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Roose_Bolton" title="Roose Bolton">Roose Bolton</a> imploring the ironborn to surrender due to the overwhelming odds against them, promising them justice and safe passage to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stony Shore","link":"Stony Shore","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stony Shore]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Stony_Shore" title="Stony Shore">Stony Shore</a>. Kenning is hesitant and Theon recalls seeing <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"his own father","link":"Balon Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Balon Greyjoy|his own father]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Balon_Greyjoy" title="Balon Greyjoy">his own father</a> bend the knee to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> after <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"his rebellion","link":"Greyjoy Rebellion","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Greyjoy Rebellion|his rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Greyjoy_Rebellion" title="Greyjoy Rebellion">his rebellion</a> failed, insisting that there was no shame in it nor any honor lost. The ailing Kenning collapses and is helped up by his men. He spits at Theon and mocks him as a whipped dog, stating that the ironborn will not surrender. Kenning is then killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Adrack Humble","link":"Adrack Humble","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Adrack Humble]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Adrack_Humble" title="Adrack Humble">Adrack Humble</a>, who would rather surrender and live than die. Ramsay is then legitimized as a true member of House Bolton for taking Moat Cailin.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Mountain and the Viper]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ralf Kenning is an ironborn captain who takes part in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Moat Cailin","link":"Battle of Moat Cailin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Moat Cailin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Moat_Cailin" title="Battle of Moat Cailin">Battle of Moat Cailin</a>. Before <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Victarion Greyjoy","link":"Victarion Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Victarion Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Victarion_Greyjoy" title="Victarion Greyjoy">Victarion Greyjoy</a> leaves, he gives the command of the stronghold to Kenning. Some time later, Kenning is shot by one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Crannogmen","link":"Crannogmen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Crannogmen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Crannogmen" title="Crannogmen">Crannogmen</a> with a poisonous arrow. The other ironborn try to treat the wound by pouring boiling wine on it, but to no avail. Kenning has been lying for many days in agony, his condition worsening slowly but surely.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Theon is sent to have the ironborn at Moat Cailin surrender, he finds Kenning barely alive. He lies shivering beneath a mountain of furs. The room is filled with foul smell, a miasma of mold and piss and nightsoil, of smoke and sickness. Kenning is naked and feverish, his pale puffy flesh covered with weeping sores and scabs. His head is misshapen, one cheek grotesquely swollen, his neck so engorged with blood that it threatens to swallow his face. The arm on that same side is big as a log and crawling with white worms. No one had bathed him or shaved him for many days, from the look of him. One eye weeps pus, and his beard is crusty with dried vomit. A brief echo of Reek's memories of being Theon resurfaces, of his dignity and honor, causing him to do what the other ironborn would not and personally slit Kenning's throat, to put him out of his misery.  The skin splits open in a gout of black blood and yellow pus. Kenning jerks violently, then lies still. An awful stench fills the room, making Theon and the others stumble outside.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ralf Kenning in the TV version is not poisoned and immobilized, though he does seem to be suffering from some sort of swamp disease, hinting at how badly the ironborn garrison's conditions are deteriorating.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The ironborn killed by one of his own men is Dagon Codd. His House is held in contempt by the other ironborn houses, since the Codds descended from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"thralls","link":"Thrall","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Thrall|thralls]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Thrall" title="Thrall">thralls</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"salt wives","link":"Salt wife","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Salt wife|salt wives]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Salt_wife" title="Salt wife">salt wives</a>, their men are said to be thieves and cowards, and their women are wantons who practice <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"incest","link":"Incest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Incest|incest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Incest" title="Incest">incest</a> with their own fathers and brothers. A few days before Theon comes to Moat Cailin, Dagon discovered that out of hunger two of the ironborn had been eating the flesh of their dead comrades, so he executed them. While the other ironborn consider Theon's offer, Dagon objects firmly, reminding the others that he is the one called "The Turncloak" and they should not believe him. He warns Theon to go back to his master or he will kill him. Then Dagon is murdered with an axe by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Adrack Humble","link":"Adrack Humble","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Adrack Humble]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23847967956cca6f982143" href="/wiki/Adrack_Humble" title="Adrack Humble">Adrack Humble</a>. Adrack takes command and demands to accept the offer. The other ironborn comply and leave. As things turn out, Dagon was right about mistrusting Theon, and died quickly and painlessly. Adrack and other ironborn, who were fooled by the offer, died horrible and agonizing deaths, flayed alive by the Boltons.
</p>
