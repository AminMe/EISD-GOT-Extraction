<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lyonel participated in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Conquest","link":"Conquest of Dorne","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Conquest of Dorne|Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Conquest_of_Dorne" title="Conquest of Dorne">Conquest</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> and was declared Steward of Dorne by King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daeron I Targaryen","link":"Daeron I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daeron I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Daeron_I_Targaryen" title="Daeron I Targaryen">Daeron I Targaryen</a>, the Young Dragon. He was murdered, however, when he pulled down a sash by his bed to summon his consort and a hundred red scorpions fell on him. His death ignited a revolt that led to the death of Daeron and the end of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> occupation.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[House Martell (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Death of Kings]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Lyonel Tyrell was an infant during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dance of the Dragons","link":"Dance of the Dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dance of the Dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Dance_of_the_Dragons" title="Dance of the Dragons">Dance of the Dragons</a>, with his mother ruling <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Reach","link":"the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/The_Reach" title="The Reach">the Reach</a> as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Regent","link":"Regent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Regent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">Regent</a>. She chose to remain neutral during the civil war, thus the Reach was divided between the supporters of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen|Aegon II]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II</a> and those of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaenyra","link":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenyra Targaryen|Rhaenyra]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Rhaenyra_Targaryen" title="Rhaenyra Targaryen">Rhaenyra</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Decades later, Lyonel commanded the army that invaded Dorne via <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Prince's Pass","link":"Prince's Pass","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prince's Pass]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Prince's_Pass" title="Prince's Pass">Prince's Pass</a>. After the Young Dragon's initial victory, Daeron appointed Lyonel as governor of Dorne. The Dornishmen however proved cunning and impossible to rule, particularly under a Reachman - the Reach and Dorne having been bitter enemies for centuries. Thus, Lord Lyonel moved with his train from one keep to the next, chasing rebels out and keeping the knees of the Dornishmen bent. It was his custom to turn the lords of the keeps he stayed in out of their chambers and sleep in their place. One night at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sandstone","link":"Sandstone","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sandstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">Sandstone</a>, seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Qorgyle","link":"House Qorgyle","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Qorgyle]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-43567108256cca7c91d99a" href="/wiki/House_Qorgyle" title="House Qorgyle">House Qorgyle</a>, finding himself in a bed with a heavy velvet canopy, he pulled a sash near the pillows to summon a wench. When he did so, the canopy opened and a hundred red scorpions fell upon him. His death sparked new revolts, and in a fortnight all the work of the Young Dragon was undone, eventually bringing the about the death of King Daeron himself.
</p>
