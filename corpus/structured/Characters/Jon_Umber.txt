The Greatjon is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Last Hearth","link":"Lord of Last Hearth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Last Hearth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Last_Hearth" title="Lord of Last Hearth">Lord of Last Hearth</a> and a formidable and proud bannerman of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a>. He is not present in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"second season","link":"Season 2","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2|second season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a>, as he is leading northern forces fighting in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a>, nor in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"third","link":"Season 3","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3|third]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">third</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fourth","link":"Season 4","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4|fourth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">fourth</a> seasons. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Jon Umber is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Last Hearth","link":"Lord of Last Hearth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Last Hearth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Last_Hearth" title="Lord of Last Hearth">Lord of Last Hearth</a> and the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Umber","link":"House Umber","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Umber]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/House_Umber" title="House Umber">House Umber</a>, a loyal vassal family to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>. He is a large, proud, and formidable warrior.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> sends ravens to the bannermen of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> instructing them to assemble for a march south to King's Landing to free <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>. The Greatjon leads a contingent of Umber troops to join the Stark army assembling at Winterfell. He is furious when Robb appoints <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Galbart Glover","link":"Galbart Glover","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Galbart Glover]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Galbart_Glover" title="Galbart Glover">Galbart Glover</a> to lead the vanguard of the army during a feast at Winterfell. The Greatjon demands the honor for himself and refuses to follow a Glover into battle, threatening to take his troops home. Robb challenges him by saying that he will arrest him as a traitor on his return from the South. The Greatjon draws his knife and Robb's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"direwolf","link":"direwolf","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[direwolf]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Direwolf" title="Direwolf" class="mw-redirect">direwolf</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Grey Wind","link":"Grey Wind","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Grey Wind]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Grey_Wind" title="Grey Wind">Grey Wind</a> barrels down the table and knocks him off his feet. Grey Wind bites off two of the Greatjon's fingers. Robb mercifully offers that The Greatjon drew his knife in order to cut his meat for him and the Greatjon laughs off the incident calling Robb's meat tough.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Pointy End]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> joins the army at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Moat Cailin","link":"Moat Cailin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Moat Cailin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Moat_Cailin" title="Moat Cailin">Moat Cailin</a>. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Jon Umber, dubbed "the Greatjon," is a huge bear of a man, standing slightly under seven feet tall, who makes for a fierce, loyal friend but a savage and dangerous enemy. He is the Lord of Last Hearth, the northern-most castle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the North","link":"the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">the North</a>, barred only the lands held by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. After his initial defiance of Robb in which he loses two of his fingers to Robb's direwolf, he becomes respectful to Robb for standing up to him, and he becomes one of the King in the North's greatest champions and supporters. As in the TV series, it is the Greatjon who first declares that Robb should be crowned king of an independent North, rather than submit to southern kings again. The Greatjon has a son, also named Jon and nicknamed "Smalljon,", and two uncles, Mors and Hother (nicknamed "Crowfood" and "Whoresbane," respectively). "Smalljon" is nicknamed to denote his younger age, not a reference to his size; he is, in fact, almost as large as his father and is noted to still be growing and may even end up larger than his father.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i>A Clash of Kings</i>, the Greatjon remains in Robb Stark's army when the King in the North invades the Westerlands, while each Riverlord is given leave to reclaim their own lands from Lannister occupation.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In <i>A Storm of Swords</i>, the Greatjon attends the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>. Merrett Frey was given the task of getting the Greatjon too drunk to fight by engaging him in a drinking contest. Despite consuming a vast quantity of wine and outdrinking his competitors, when the massacre begins, the Greatjon still grabs a sword and fights valiantly. It takes eight men to overcome him, and he kills one and injures two. Even when he can no longer fight with his hands, he fights with his teeth, and bites off half the ear of one of the assailants. The Greatjon is among the few guests who are taken alive by the Freys, whereas his son, the Smalljon, is killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dreadfort","link":"Dreadfort","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dreadfort]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Dreadfort" title="Dreadfort" class="mw-redirect">Dreadfort</a> men while defending Robb. He remains a hostage at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Twins","link":"the Twins","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Twins]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/The_Twins" title="The Twins">the Twins</a> to ensure House Umber doesn't rise against the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Bolton","link":"House Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/House_Bolton" title="House Bolton">House Bolton</a> or the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-205599656956cca5f0afa45" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>.
</p>
