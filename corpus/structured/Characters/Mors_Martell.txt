<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Mors Martell was one of the many nobles who vied for supremacy over the rest of the lords of Dorne. According to tradition, he fell in love with Princess <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Nymeria","link":"Nymeria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Nymeria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-64582266556cca71622b08" href="/wiki/Nymeria" title="Nymeria">Nymeria</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-64582266556cca71622b08" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> warrior-queen, the first time he saw her. The two married and joined their strengths, thus allowing House Martell to defeat all rival houses and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"unify Dorne","link":"Nymeria's War","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Nymeria's War|unify Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-64582266556cca71622b08" href="/wiki/Nymeria's_War" title="Nymeria's War">unify Dorne</a> under their rule. In the Rhoynish fashion, House Martell adopted the title of "Prince" as well as equal rights of primogeniture, regardless of gender.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[House Martell (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-64582266556cca71622b08" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-64582266556cca71622b08" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Mors Martell's history is the same. In <i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> book it is revealed that Mors bore the title of Lord of the Sandship - the seat of House Martell that would become Sunspear - and died in the Third Battle of the Boneway, slain by King Yorick V Yronwood, though Nymeria completed the conquest of Dorne, dubbed by historians as "Nymeria's War", and passed the crown to her daughter by Mors.
</p>
