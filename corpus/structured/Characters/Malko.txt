<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Malko</b> is a recurring character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fifth season","link":"Season 5","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5|fifth season]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">fifth season</a>. He is played by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje","link":"Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Adewale_Akinnuoye-Agbaje" title="Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje">Adewale Akinnuoye-Agbaje</a> and debuts in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken","link":"Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Unbowed,_Unbent,_Unbroken" title="Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken">Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken</a>". Malko is the captain of a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"slaving","link":"slavery","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[slavery|slaving]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaving</a> ship.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Malko is the captain of a slaving ship operating in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Sea","link":"Summer Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Summer_Sea" title="Summer Sea">Summer Sea</a>. He speaks the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Common Tongue","link":"Common Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Common Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Common_Tongue" title="Common Tongue">Common Tongue</a> of Westeros and appears to be knowledgeable of Westerosi history, such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Siege of Pyke","link":"Siege of Pyke","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Siege of Pyke]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Pyke" title="Siege of Pyke">Siege of Pyke</a>.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 5","link":"Season 5","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">Season 5</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Malko's ship anchors on the shoreline while on a voyage to Volantis so that the slaver's can replenish their water supply. The slavers, led by Malko, discover <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> and capture them. Malko plans on using Jorah as a galley slave and orders Tyrion to be killed and his penis cut off, as dwarfs' penises are believed to be magic. Tyrion warns Malko that no merchant will believe him unless he sees the source of the penis. Then he attempts to convince Malko to take them to Meereen to the fighting pits where Jorah can prove his worth. After hearing Jorah killed <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qotho","link":"Qotho","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qotho]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Qotho" title="Qotho">Qotho</a>, a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bloodrider","link":"bloodrider","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[bloodrider]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Bloodrider" title="Bloodrider">bloodrider</a>, Malko seems to change his mind.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Once he has arrived in Slaver's Bay, Malko proceeds to auction off Jorah while changing details to make his story sound more impressive (and to get a higher price), including Jorah killing <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Khal Drogo","link":"Khal Drogo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Khal Drogo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Khal_Drogo" title="Khal Drogo" class="mw-redirect">Khal Drogo</a> (instead of Qotho) and taking part in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"attack on \"Spike\"","link":"Siege of Pyke","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Siege of Pyke|attack on \"Spike\"]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Pyke" title="Siege of Pyke">attack on "Spike"</a> while wielding a flaming sword (instead of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Thoros of Myr","link":"Thoros of Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Thoros of Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Thoros_of_Myr" title="Thoros of Myr" class="mw-redirect">Thoros of Myr</a>). He manages to sell Jorah to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yezzan zo Qaggaz","link":"Yezzan zo Qaggaz","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yezzan zo Qaggaz]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Yezzan_zo_Qaggaz" title="Yezzan zo Qaggaz">Yezzan zo Qaggaz</a>, a Meereenese <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Master","link":"Great Masters","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Masters|Great Master]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Great_Masters" title="Great Masters">Great Master</a> for 20 of an unspecified coin. As Yezzan leads Jorah away, Tyrion desperately attempts to also be sold to Yezzan. Malko scoffs at this, saying that at least Tyrion is funny. Tyrion proceeds to gain control of his chain and beat the slaver who was holding him. Yezzan, intrigued with Tyrion, ends up buying him for an unspecified amount because he's funny.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Gift (episode)|The Gift]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, there is no character by the name of Malko: he is a condensation into one role of half a dozen separate slaver characters from the books. At Volantis, Jorah and Tyrion board the <i>Selaesori Qhoran</i>, a merchant ship bound to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qarth","link":"Qarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Qarth" title="Qarth">Qarth</a>, since they are told by the widow of the waterfront that the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Priest","link":"Red Priest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Priest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Red_Priest" title="Red Priest">Red Priest</a> Benerro has seen in his flames the ship will reach Slaver's Bay instead. The ship is captured by unidentified <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yunkish","link":"Yunkai","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yunkai|Yunkish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Yunkai" title="Yunkai">Yunkish</a> slavers. Unlike in the show, Jorah fights the slavers and kills three of them before he is overcome. As a result, he is chained to an oar, beaten very severely, starved, and branded with a demon’s mask on his cheek. Tyrion and the rest of the passengers do not resist, and the slavers do not harm them. None of the slavers seeks to castrate Tyrion or kill him. Jorah and Tyrion are offered on a slave auction block set up before the gates of Meereen by the Yunkish <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wise Masters","link":"Wise Masters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wise Masters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Wise_Masters" title="Wise Masters">Wise Masters</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The slavers have no idea who Jorah and Tyrion are. Jorah is introduced by the auctioneer simply as "big and strong" man, without any exaggerated descriptions of his deeds; Tyrion and the other companion are introduced as "A pair of dwarfs, well trained for your amusement". When the price offered for them increases to 5,000, Tyrion yells that it is an insult and he is worth much more, and implies that he is a Lannister. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, instead of making peace with Daenerys, the Yunkai'i decide to lay siege to Meereen with their slave soldiers and along with three mercenary companies - the Long Lances, the Company of the Cat, and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Windblown","link":"Windblown","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Windblown]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Windblown" title="Windblown">Windblown</a> - as well as forces of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mantarys","link":"Mantarys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mantarys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Mantarys" title="Mantarys">Mantarys</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tolos","link":"Tolos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tolos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Tolos" title="Tolos">Tolos</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Elyria","link":"Elyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Elyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Elyria" title="Elyria">Elyria</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qarth","link":"Qarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Qarth" title="Qarth">Qarth</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"New Ghis","link":"New Ghis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[New Ghis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/New_Ghis" title="New Ghis">New Ghis</a>, and remain outside the walls after Daenerys announces her marriage to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hizdahr zo Loraq","link":"Hizdahr zo Loraq","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hizdahr zo Loraq]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Hizdahr_zo_Loraq" title="Hizdahr zo Loraq">Hizdahr zo Loraq</a> to make peace with the besiegers and stop the attacks of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sons of the Harpy","link":"Sons of the Harpy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sons of the Harpy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Sons_of_the_Harpy" title="Sons of the Harpy">Sons of the Harpy</a>. While slavery remains forbidden in Meereen, it is openly practiced outside its walls, while Daenerys also gave leave for adults to sell themselves into slavery if they so desired.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">One of the slavers who bids on Tyrion and then Jorah is Zahrina, a crone who purchases cheap slaves for the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fighting pit","link":"fighting pit","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[fighting pit]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Fighting_pit" title="Fighting pit">fighting pits</a>, "meat" for the more competent and formidable fighters. After Tyrion and his other companion are bought by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yezzan zo Qaggaz","link":"Yezzan zo Qaggaz","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yezzan zo Qaggaz]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Yezzan_zo_Qaggaz" title="Yezzan zo Qaggaz">Yezzan zo Qaggaz</a>, he persuades Yezzan's overseer Nurse to buy Jorah too.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As for Malko in the TV show, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Basilisk Isles","link":"Basilisk Isles","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Basilisk Isles]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Basilisk_Isles" title="Basilisk Isles">Basilisk Isles</a> south of Valyria and Slaver's Bay are a major location of pirate dens, even worse than the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a>, preying on ships trying to round the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Peninsula","link":"Valyrian Peninsula","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Peninsula]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Peninsula" title="Valyrian Peninsula">Valyrian Peninsula</a> and selling their crews into slavery. The Basilisk Isles are just off the north coast of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sothoryos","link":"Sothoryos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sothoryos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Sothoryos" title="Sothoryos">Sothoryos</a> (the narrative's fantasy analogue to Africa), and many of its inhabitants are notably dark-skinned. Malko probably isn't from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Islands","link":"Summer Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186362495656cca674570a0" href="/wiki/Summer_Islands" title="Summer Islands">Summer Islands</a>, which are further west than the Basilisk Isles and whose inhabitants are also black, because the Summer Islanders abhor slavery.
</p>
