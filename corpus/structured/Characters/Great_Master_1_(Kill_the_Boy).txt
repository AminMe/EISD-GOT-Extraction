<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> Behind the scenes </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The character was originally listed as "Master Eaton" in the actor's CV, however "Eaton" doesn't fit the established naming conventions of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ghiscari","link":"Ghiscari","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghiscari]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-151420745156cca6f8534fc" href="/wiki/Ghiscari" title="Ghiscari">Ghiscari</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-151420745156cca6f8534fc" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a>. Some CV's have already shown typing mistakes in character names, in this case a case of misreading "Eaten master" or "Maester eaten", in reference to the character's fate.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"<i>A Song of Ice and Fire<\/i>","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire|''A Song of Ice and Fire'']]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-151420745156cca6f8534fc" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire"><i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i></a><i> </i>novels there is no equivalent character. Daenerys doesn't have the head of the ancient slaver families arrested as a reaction to the murders carried out by the Sons of the Harpy. Instead, she has each family of dubious loyalty deliver a child hostage, hoping this will diminish the violence. When this measure fails, Dany's counselor and noted loyalist Skahaz mo Kandaq urges her to kill a hostage for every murder of the Sons but she still refuses to harm them.
</p>
