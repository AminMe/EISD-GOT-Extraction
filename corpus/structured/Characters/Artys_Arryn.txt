<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175343341556cca6f8cf083" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ser Artys is the hero of a thousand tales. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Arryn","link":"Robin Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robin Arryn|Robert Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175343341556cca6f8cf083" href="/wiki/Robin_Arryn" title="Robin Arryn">Robert Arryn</a>, the child lord of the Eyrie, is fond of Ser Artys' stories. In the <i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> book it is revealed that the historical Ser Artys Arryn has been conflated by singers with a previous legendary First Men figure known as the Winged Knight.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The historical Ser Artys was known as the Falcon Knight and was born on the Vale, at the shadow of the Giant's Lance, long after the first Andal invaders had landed and carved out petty kingdoms for themselves. After King Robar II Royce unified all the First Men houses and clans of the Vale under his ruler to strike against the divided Andal houses and petty kings, the Andals united under Ser Artys, who was of an age with King Robar II. He led the Andal army in the Battle of Seven Stars, taking part in its final stage. Artys led a force of over five hundred knights through a goat track on the Giant's Lance to take the already weakened First Men in the rear, leaving a decoy, who was slain by Robar, among the main fighting force. It is possible he slew Robar himself, though other figures also boasted the same. In any event, after the Andal victory, the First Men houses bent the knee to Ser Artys, who was declared King of Mountain and Vale.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Meanwhile, the Winged Knight was a legendary figure of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Age of Heroes","link":"Age of Heroes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Age of Heroes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175343341556cca6f8cf083" href="/wiki/Age_of_Heroes" title="Age of Heroes">Age of Heroes</a> - made a knight by singers and storytellers as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"knighthood","link":"knighthood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[knighthood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175343341556cca6f8cf083" href="/wiki/Knighthood" title="Knighthood">knighthood</a> was an Andal tradition - said to have flown upon a huge falcon and had armies of eagles at this command. He won the the Vale by slaying the Griffin King atop the Giant's Lance. He is said to have been friendly with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"giants","link":"giants","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[giants]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175343341556cca6f8cf083" href="/wiki/Giants" title="Giants">giants</a> and merlings. His wife, one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"children of the forest","link":"Children of the Forest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Children of the Forest|children of the forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175343341556cca6f8cf083" href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Forest" title="Children of the Forest">children of the forest</a>, died giving birth to his son. Maester Yandel speculates singers conflated the two figures to curry favor with the Arryn dynasty, giving rise to legends that Ser Artys was the Winged Knight who drove the First Men from the Vale and took <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Eyrie","link":"the Eyrie","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Eyrie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-175343341556cca6f8cf083" href="/wiki/The_Eyrie" title="The Eyrie">the Eyrie</a>, instead of its construction being carried out by descendants of Ser Artys ever since his grandson, Roland I Arryn, ordered it.
</p>
