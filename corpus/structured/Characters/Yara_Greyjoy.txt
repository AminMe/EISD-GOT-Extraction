<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Yara is the only daughter of Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Balon","link":"Balon Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Balon Greyjoy|Balon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Balon_Greyjoy" title="Balon Greyjoy">Balon</a> and his wife, Lady <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Alannys","link":"Alannys Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Alannys Greyjoy|Alannys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Alannys_Greyjoy" title="Alannys Greyjoy">Alannys</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Harlaw","link":"House Harlaw","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Harlaw]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/House_Harlaw" title="House Harlaw">House Harlaw</a>. Balon is the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Greyjoy","link":"House Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/House_Greyjoy" title="House Greyjoy">House Greyjoy</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of the Iron Islands","link":"Lord of the Iron Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of the Iron Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Iron_Islands" title="Lord of the Iron Islands">Lord of the Iron Islands</a>. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Islands","link":"Iron Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Iron_Islands" title="Iron Islands">Iron Islands</a> are one of the constituent regions of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> and House Greyjoy is one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Houses","link":"Great Houses","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Houses]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Great_Houses" title="Great Houses" class="mw-redirect">Great Houses</a> of the realm. House Greyjoy rules the region from their seat at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pyke","link":"Pyke (castle)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pyke (castle)|Pyke]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Pyke_(castle)" title="Pyke (castle)">Pyke</a> and Balon also holds the title <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Reaper of Pyke","link":"Lord Reaper of Pyke","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Reaper of Pyke]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Lord_Reaper_of_Pyke" title="Lord Reaper of Pyke">Lord Reaper of Pyke</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Yara was born and raised at Pyke. She was Balon and Alannys's third child after <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rodrik","link":"Rodrik Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rodrik Greyjoy|Rodrik]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Rodrik_Greyjoy" title="Rodrik Greyjoy">Rodrik</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maron","link":"Maron Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maron Greyjoy|Maron]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Maron_Greyjoy" title="Maron Greyjoy">Maron</a>. She also has a younger brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Theon","link":"Theon Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Theon Greyjoy|Theon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon</a>. Eight years before the start of the series, Balon rose in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"rebellion","link":"Greyjoy Rebellion","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Greyjoy Rebellion|rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Greyjoy_Rebellion" title="Greyjoy Rebellion">rebellion</a> against the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> and was defeated in a bloody war. Balon's older two sons were killed and Theon was taken by Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> as a hostage for Balon's good behavior and obedience.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"Balon Greyjoy viewers guide\">[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/guide\/houses\/greyjoy\/balon-greyjoy\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 guide to houses, House Greyjoy - Balon Greyjoy entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> With Yara as Balon's only remaining child in the Iron Islands, he raised her as a surrogate son, encouraging her to become a reaver in her own right, which is unusual for women in the misogynistic culture of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a>. Balon refuses to yield the lands and castles taken by the ironborn and dismisses Theon for having disobeyed him and for no longer "being a man" and unable to continue the Greyjoy line with children of his own. Yara, however decides to take the fastest ship of the Iron Fleet along with the 50 best killers of the Isles, sail up <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Narrow Sea","link":"the Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/The_Narrow_Sea" title="The Narrow Sea" class="mw-redirect">the Narrow Sea</a> and into the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Weeping Water","link":"Weeping Water","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Weeping Water]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Weeping_Water" title="Weeping Water">Weeping Water</a>, with the intention of marching against the Dreadfort to rescue her brother and bring him home.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Mhysa]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Yara in the dreadfort.jpg|thumb|247x247px|Yara enters the Dreadfort.]]","title":"Yara_in_the_dreadfort.jpg","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Yara in the dreadfort","caption":"Yara enters the Dreadfort.","width":247,"height":247,"captionParsed":"Yara enters the Dreadfort."}}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" alt="" src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/4/40/Yara_in_the_dreadfort.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/247?cb=20150504051339" width="247" height="139" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Yara in the dreadfort.jpg" data-image-key="Yara_in_the_dreadfort.jpg"  type="image" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->Yara carries out her intentions of sailing to rescue Theon. She utilizes longboats and canoes to get upriver and approach the Dreadfort, and she and the ironborn soldiers use grappling hooks to scale the walls and find out where Theon is being held. However, when they enter the kennels where he is sleeping, Theon claims he is Reek and refuses to go. Ramsay enters with some guards, and in the ensuing fight most of both sides are killed. Yara offers Ramsay his life if he surrenders Theon, but Ramsay instead releases his hounds and the Ironborn are forced to retreat without Theon. Once back to the boats, Yara claims her brother is dead.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Laws of Gods and Men]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels Yara is called <b>Asha Greyjoy</b>, but her name was changed in the TV series; possibly to prevent confusion with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Osha","link":"Osha","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Osha]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Osha" title="Osha">Osha</a> the wildling. Ironically, as the daughter of a Great House and one of Balon Greyjoy's only two surviving children, Asha Greyjoy is actually a much more prominent character than Osha the wildling; Asha Greyjoy is even a POV character in several chapters. Thus it is curious why they changed Asha's name and not the other way around. It is probably because Osha was already introduced in Season 1 and the similarity with Asha Greyjoy's name wasn't realized until production on Season 2 began.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the German dub of the TV series, however, the character is still called "Asha" as in the books.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Another small change is that in the books, "Asha Greyjoy" has short black hair, a lean build, and a sharp beak of a nose. In the TV series, "Yara Greyjoy" has blonde hair. Other than these minor naming and cosmetic differences, Gemma Whelan's portrayal is actually quite faithful to Asha Greyjoy's behavior and actions in the books.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Balon is greatly proud of his wild headstrong daughter, and believes she can succeed him, although it is doubtful the other ironborn would accept her as their queen. He wishes Theon dead so he would not stand in Asha's way, though this resentment has been cut from the television series, where Balon appears to be genuinely saddened after "losing" Theon to the Starks.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Asha's uncles <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Victarion","link":"Victarion Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Victarion Greyjoy|Victarion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Victarion_Greyjoy" title="Victarion Greyjoy">Victarion</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aeron","link":"Aeron Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aeron Greyjoy|Aeron]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106233785156cca5c0850ef" href="/wiki/Aeron_Greyjoy" title="Aeron Greyjoy">Aeron</a>, and Rodrik "the Reader" Harlaw respect her and acknowledge her power, but do not believe she can rule the ironborn due to her gender. Other ironborn often mock Asha ribaldy. However, Asha is confident and strong-willed enough to dismiss such attitude scornfully, and she always talks back sharply and make fun of those who taunt her.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Asha's crew adore her devotedly. Half of them love her as their daughter, the other half want to have sex with her, and all of them would die for her.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Asha becomes a POV narrator starting in the fourth novel, <i>A Feast for Crows</i>.
</p>
