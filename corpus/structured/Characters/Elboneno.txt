<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149609641456cca7b6ac3ac" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels there is no mention of a character named Elboneno. The name is "one noble" spelled backwards. The name doesn't fit the established naming conventions of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ghiscari","link":"Ghiscari","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghiscari]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149609641456cca7b6ac3ac" href="/wiki/Ghiscari" title="Ghiscari">Ghiscari</a> inhabitants of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149609641456cca7b6ac3ac" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books Daenerys never has the heads of the ancient slaving families arrested after the attacks of the Sons of the Harpy. Instead she has these families deliver a child as hostage.
</p>
