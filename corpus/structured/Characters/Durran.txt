<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Durran</b>, also known as <b>Durran Godsgrief</b>, is an unseen character in <i>Game of Thrones</i>. He is deceased when the series begins and has only appeared in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Complete Guide to Westeros","link":"Complete Guide to Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Complete Guide to Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Complete_Guide_to_Westeros" title="Complete Guide to Westeros" class="mw-redirect">Complete Guide to Westeros</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Histories & Lore","link":"Histories & Lore","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Histories & Lore]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Histories_&_Lore" title="Histories &amp; Lore">Histories &amp; Lore</a> featurettes.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Durran was a legendary <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a> figure of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Age of Heroes","link":"Age of Heroes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Age of Heroes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Age_of_Heroes" title="Age of Heroes">Age of Heroes</a> and the first of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm Kings","link":"Storm Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Storm_Kings" title="Storm Kings" class="mw-redirect">Storm Kings</a>, who ruled the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stormlands","link":"Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Stormlands" title="Stormlands" class="mw-redirect">Stormlands</a> until the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Targaryen" title="Targaryen" class="mw-redirect">Targaryen</a> invasion of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. According to legend, Durran won the love of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Elenei","link":"Elenei","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Elenei]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Elenei" title="Elenei">Elenei</a>, daughter of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sea god","link":"God of the Sea","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[God of the Sea|sea god]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/God_of_the_Sea" title="God of the Sea">sea god</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wind goddess","link":"Goddess of the Wind","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Goddess of the Wind|wind goddess]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Goddess_of_the_Wind" title="Goddess of the Wind">wind goddess</a>, and took her as his wife.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In rage, the gods sent a storm to destroy his keep over <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shipbreaker Bay","link":"Shipbreaker Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shipbreaker Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Shipbreaker_Bay" title="Shipbreaker Bay">Shipbreaker Bay</a>, killing his family and guests. Declaring war against them, Durran built new castles, each larger and more formidable than the previous one, though all were the destroyed by the divine storms. Finally, the seventh castle, which Durran built with the aid of either the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Children of the Forest","link":"Children of the Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Children of the Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Forest" title="Children of the Forest">Children of the Forest</a> or a very young <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bran the Builder","link":"Brandon Stark (the Builder)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brandon Stark (the Builder)|Bran the Builder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Brandon_Stark_(the_Builder)" title="Brandon Stark (the Builder)">Bran the Builder</a>, withstood the rage of the gods and became known as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a>, while Durran earned the nickname of "Durran's Godsgrief" and ruled as the first <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm King","link":"Storm King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Storm_King" title="Storm King">Storm King</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Age of Heroes type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Durran's dynasty, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Durrandon","link":"House Durrandon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Durrandon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/House_Durrandon" title="House Durrandon">House Durrandon</a>, perished with the death of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Argilac Durrandon","link":"Argilac Durrandon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Argilac Durrandon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/Argilac_Durrandon" title="Argilac Durrandon">Argilac Durrandon</a>. However, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a> descends from it through its female bloodline.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[House Baratheon (Complete Guide to Westeros)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28107292256cca6bc81376" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
