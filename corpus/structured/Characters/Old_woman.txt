<h2>Biography</h2>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">She is among the lowborn people gathered to rebuild <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>.

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">She greets <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa Stark","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> after she is taken to Winterfell for her impending marriage to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ramsay Bolton","link":"Ramsay Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ramsay Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" href="/wiki/Ramsay_Bolton" title="Ramsay Bolton">Ramsay Bolton</a>. She warmly tells her that "the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"North","link":"North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" href="/wiki/North" title="North" class="mw-redirect">North</a> remembers".<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[High Sparrow (episode)|High Sparrow]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Sansa urges <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Theon Greyjoy","link":"Theon Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Theon Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> to help her escape Winterfell by lighting a candle as the old woman had told her to. Ramsay mentions that her heart gave out before Ramsay had the chance to flay her face. <img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Gift (episode)|The Gift]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the novels there is no equivalent scene or character. However, the phrase "the North remembers" is common among certain Northern nobles who were left reeling by the betrayals House Stark and the North suffered, even those who felt slighted by the Starks (<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lady Dustin","link":"Barbrey Dustin","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barbrey Dustin|Lady Dustin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" href="/wiki/Barbrey_Dustin" title="Barbrey Dustin">Lady Dustin</a>) or have allied with the Boltons resent <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Frey","link":"House Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" href="/wiki/House_Frey" title="House Frey">House Frey</a> for the relatives and men lost during at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-101190697056cca6dd82285" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>. In the novels, the level of involvement of the Boltons in the Red Wedding is not common knowledge, though it is suspected by several prominent characters.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">None of the servants in Winterfell offered to help Jeyne Poole (who was replaced in the show by Sansa), either because they did not care or too afraid from the Boltons.  Few of them even <i>were</i> direct members of the Stark household - Ramsay killed most of them when be burned Winterfell originally, except for a few women he took back as prisoners to the Dreadfort.
</p>
