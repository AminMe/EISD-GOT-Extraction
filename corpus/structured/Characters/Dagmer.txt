<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Dagmer is an <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> raider and reaver, noted for his skill in battle and his loyalty to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Greyjoy","link":"House Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/House_Greyjoy" title="House Greyjoy">House Greyjoy</a>.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 2","link":"Season 2","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">Season 2</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Dagmer emerges from the tavern and offers to take Theon to their ship, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sea Bitch","link":"Sea Bitch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sea Bitch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Sea_Bitch" title="Sea Bitch">Sea Bitch</a>. Theon asks who he is and Dagmer explains that he is to act as Theon’s first mate. Theon questions why Dagmer is separated from the rest of the crew and wonders if they have sent Dagmer to kill him when they are on their way to the ship. Dagmer says that the men will not respect Theon until he has proven himself. Theon wonders how he can prove himself when his father has ordered him to attack fishing villages on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stony Shore","link":"Stony Shore","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stony Shore]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Stony_Shore" title="Stony Shore">Stony Shore</a>. Theon asks Dagmer how he can prove himself a true iron islander with such a task. Dagmer tells him that his crew are all iron islanders and asks Theon how they respond to orders. Theon sees logic in Dagmer’s words and suggests attacking <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Torrhen\u2019s Square","link":"Torrhen\u2019s Square","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Torrhen\u2019s Square]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Torrhen’s_Square" title="Torrhen’s Square" class="mw-redirect">Torrhen’s Square</a> that lies near to the Stony Shore. Dagmer says that the seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tallhart","link":"House Tallhart","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tallhart]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/House_Tallhart" title="House Tallhart">House Tallhart</a> here is a worthy prize as Theon contemplates his idea. Dagmer wonders if Theon doubts their ability to take it. Theon says that they could but would only be able to hold it for a few days. He explains that Winterfell would hear of their victory and then <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> would send men of their own to take it back. Theon considers this opportunity and then instructs Dagmer to take him to his ship.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Ghost of Harrenhal]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Theon besieges Torrhen's Square and then strikes at Winterfell once it has been left poorly defended. He seizes the castle and forces <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bran Stark","link":"Bran Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bran Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a> to yield to him by threatening his people. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lorren","link":"Lorren","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lorren]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Lorren" title="Lorren">Lorren</a> captures Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rodrik Cassel","link":"Rodrik Cassel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rodrik Cassel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Rodrik_Cassel" title="Rodrik Cassel">Rodrik Cassel</a> as he returns from Torrhen's Square. The master-at-arms is defiant and spits in Theon's face. Dagmer insists that Theon must have him executed to maintain the respect of the crew. Rodrik baits Theon into swinging the sword himself and Theon fumbles the task, requiring four blows to sever his head.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Old Gods and the New]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Theon_and_Dagmer.jpg|thumb|Theon and Dagmer at Winterfell in &quot;[[The Prince of Winterfell]].&quot;]]","title":"Theon_and_Dagmer.jpg","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Theon and Dagmer","caption":"Theon and Dagmer at Winterfell in \"[[The Prince of Winterfell]].\"","captionParsed":"Theon and Dagmer at Winterfell in \"<a _rte_dataidx=\"0019\" href=\"\/wiki\/The_Prince_of_Winterfell\" title=\"The Prince of Winterfell\">The Prince of Winterfell<\/a>.\""}}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" alt="" src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/c/ca/Theon_and_Dagmer.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20120523200305" width="180" height="101" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Theon and Dagmer.jpg" data-image-key="Theon_and_Dagmer.jpg"  type="image" />

</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As Theon is rallying the last remaining men to fight, Dagmer hits him over the head, knocking him out cold. When Maester <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Luwin","link":"Luwin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Luwin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Luwin" title="Luwin">Luwin</a> tries to intervene, Dagmer stabs him.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Valar Morghulis]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Dagmer Cleftjaw is the captain of the <i>Foamdrinker</i> and is remembered fondly by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Theon Greyjoy","link":"Theon Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Theon Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> from his childhood. Dagmer is a solid and dependable commander, but has been known to take gambles if he believes the reward is great enough. A longaxe blow shattered his jaw as a child, leaving him with four lips rather than two and an enormous scar, the source of his nickname. This is changed to a scar along the left of his jaw instead of directly down the middle to save money on CGI or make-up. He is the master-at-arms at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pyke","link":"Pyke (castle)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pyke (castle)|Pyke]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Pyke_(castle)" title="Pyke (castle)">Pyke</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Theon sends Dagmer to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Torrhen\u2019s Square","link":"Torrhen\u2019s Square","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Torrhen\u2019s Square]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Torrhen’s_Square" title="Torrhen’s Square" class="mw-redirect">Torrhen’s Square</a> as a diversion, to draw out the garrison from Winterfell. The ploy succeeds: Ser Rodrik heads for Torrhen’s Square with most of the garrison, forcing Dagmer to retreat to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stony Shore","link":"Stony Shore","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stony Shore]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-159001055456cca5d6d80ae" href="/wiki/Stony_Shore" title="Stony Shore">Stony Shore</a>, but in the meantime Theon takes Winterfell. After Ser Rodrik and Leobald Tallhart depart to retake Winterfell, Dagmer returns to Torrhen’s Square and easily takes the sparsely defended keep. He takes captive Berena Hornwood and her children Brandon and Beren, as well as her niece Eddara Tallhart. He does not return to Winterfell, and does not encounter Ramsay.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Dagmer does not make appearance in the novels following <i>A Clash of Kings</i>. He is only mentioned very briefly in <i>A Dance with Dragons</i>, in a note stating that he still holds Torrhen’s Square with his force of ironborn. It is unknown what is his exact status or how he treats his captives.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Dagmer's actions at Winterfell in the TV series, such as telling Theon to kill to display dominance, murdering the orphan boys to pass off as Bran and Rickon, and later betraying Theon, in the books were all commited by Ramsay Snow, who at the time was pretending to only be a Bolton servant named "Reek".
</p>
