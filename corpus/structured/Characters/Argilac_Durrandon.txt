</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Argilac Durrandon</b>, also known as <b>Argilac the Arrogant</b>, is an unseen character in <i>Game of Thrones</i>. He is deceased when the series begins and has only appeared in "The Complete Guide to Westeros: House Baratheon" featurette. Argilac was the last of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm Kings","link":"Storm Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211066956cca6da0b9b5" href="/wiki/Storm_Kings" title="Storm Kings" class="mw-redirect">Storm Kings</a>, the rulers of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stormlands","link":"Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211066956cca6da0b9b5" href="/wiki/Stormlands" title="Stormlands" class="mw-redirect">Stormlands</a> since <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Durran Godsgrief","link":"Durran Godsgrief","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Durran Godsgrief]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211066956cca6da0b9b5" href="/wiki/Durran_Godsgrief" title="Durran Godsgrief" class="mw-redirect">Durran Godsgrief</a>, a legendary figure of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Age of Heroes","link":"Age of Heroes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Age of Heroes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211066956cca6da0b9b5" href="/wiki/Age_of_Heroes" title="Age of Heroes">Age of Heroes</a> and founder of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211066956cca6da0b9b5" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a>.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211066956cca6da0b9b5" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Argilac was the leader of House Durrandon, the rulers of the Stormlands for centuries. After his death, Orys Baratheon took the sigil and words of House Durrandon as those of House Baratheon.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Argilac got his nickname for arrogantly refusing Aegon's offer of marrying Orys (a bastard) to his daughter, cutting off the hands of Aegon's envoy and sending them back to him. He was also arrogant in engaging the Targaryens in open battle rather than withstanding a siege in the infamously impregnable castle of Storm's End. However, Argilac was motivated in this by having heard of the death of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harren Hoare","link":"Harren Hoare","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harren Hoare]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211066956cca6da0b9b5" href="/wiki/Harren_Hoare" title="Harren Hoare">Harren Hoare</a> in similar circumstances at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-211066956cca6da0b9b5" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>, who prepared for a siege in his immense castle only for the Targaryen dragons to fly right over the walls. If Argilac resisted a siege by Orys it would only be a matter of time until the Targaryens directed their dragons to the castle, so wishing to avoid this fate Argilac met Orys in pitched battle.
</p>
