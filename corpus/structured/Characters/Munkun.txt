<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Munkun is mentioned as the author of <i>The Dance of the Dragons, A True Telling</i>. It is noted that his work contains some inaccuracies, such as indicating that the dragon that killed Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Byron Swann","link":"Byron Swann","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Byron Swann]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Byron_Swann" title="Byron Swann">Byron Swann</a> was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vhagar","link":"Vhagar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vhagar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Vhagar" title="Vhagar">Vhagar</a> when in fact it was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Syrax","link":"Syrax","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Syrax]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Syrax" title="Syrax">Syrax</a>. Vhagar was ridden by Prince Aemond Targaryen, a brother and supporter of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II Targaryen","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II Targaryen</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Swann","link":"House Swann","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Swann]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/House_Swann" title="House Swann">House Swann</a> was among the supporters of Aegon II. Meanwhile, Syrax was ridden by Aegon II's rival, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","link":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenyra Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Rhaenyra_Targaryen" title="Rhaenyra Targaryen">Rhaenyra Targaryen</a> herself.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Munkun's work was said to be biased in favor of Rhaenyra, in contrast to the history written by Septon Eustace, which was biased in favor of Aegon II.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> reveals that Munkun was a member of the council of seven <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Regent","link":"Regent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Regent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">Regents</a> that ruled the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> after the death of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II Targaryen","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II Targaryen</a>, as his successor, his nephew <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon III Targaryen","link":"Aegon III Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon III Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Aegon_III_Targaryen" title="Aegon III Targaryen">Aegon III Targaryen</a>, was still a child. Munkun was the only member of the council that held office from 131 AC to 136 AC, when the council was dissolved. Munkun would also hold the office of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71581482556cca802a7493" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>.
</p>
