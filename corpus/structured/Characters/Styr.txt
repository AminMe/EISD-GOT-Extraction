<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Styr is the Magnar of the Thenn, a fierce tribe of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Folk","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">Free Folk</a> known for their practice of ritual self-scarification and engaging in cannibalism of their fallen enemies. Styr is a very highly-skilled warrior and is capable of wielding a great axe with ease due to his size and strength.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At some point, Mance Rayder convinced Styr to join his army and acknowledge him as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-Beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-Beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/King-Beyond-the-Wall" title="King-Beyond-the-Wall">King-Beyond-the-Wall</a>. Styr became one of Mance's lieutenants, along chieftains such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tormund Giantsbane","link":"Tormund Giantsbane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tormund Giantsbane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Tormund_Giantsbane" title="Tormund Giantsbane" class="mw-redirect">Tormund Giantsbane</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Bones","link":"Lord of Bones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Bones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Bones" title="Lord of Bones">Lord of Bones</a>.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Styr is one of Mance Rayder's lieutenants and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Magnar","link":"Magnar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Magnar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Magnar" title="Magnar">Magnar</a> - "Lord" in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Tongue","link":"Old Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a> - of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Thenn","link":"Thenn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Thenn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Thenn" title="Thenn" class="mw-redirect">Thenn</a> people, a clan of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Folk","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">Free Folk</a> established in a valley north of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Frostfangs","link":"Frostfangs","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Frostfangs]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Frostfangs" title="Frostfangs">Frostfangs</a>. Unlike the other wildling clans, the Thenn actually have lords (more like hereditary chieftains), and live under established laws that they enforce. As a result they are the most disciplined of the wildling clans, making them organized and dangerous in combat; they also possess the most advanced armor and weaponry among the Free Folk. The Magnar's followers worship the man like a god, and follow every order, however ridiculous.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the series, much of his plotline from <i>A Storm of Swords</i> is taken by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tormund Giantsbane","link":"Tormund Giantsbane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tormund Giantsbane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Tormund_Giantsbane" title="Tormund Giantsbane" class="mw-redirect">Tormund Giantsbane</a> - including being mistaken by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> for Mance, and leading the climb over <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>. He is described as a tall lean man with no ears, clean shaven, bald with a straight nose and grey eyes. He doesn't speak the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Common Tongue","link":"Common Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Common Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Common_Tongue" title="Common Tongue">Common Tongue</a>, speaking only the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Tongue","link":"Old Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Styr is also not a cannibal, and doesn't possess scars on his head.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Styr is not killed by Jon in personal combat. During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Castle Black","link":"Battle of Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-78371587856cca613733b0" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castle_Black" title="Battle of Castle Black">Battle of Castle Black</a> he leads the majority of the Thenns to mount the stair and push back the crumbling defense, killing some of the brothers and villagers. Because Jon warned the Watch of their approach, the garrison knew they were coming and sprung a trap: the stair, soaked in oil between the last Watch line of defense and the base of the structure, is set ablaze, destroying the stair and killing Styr and most of his men.
