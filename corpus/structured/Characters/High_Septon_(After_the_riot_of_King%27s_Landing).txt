<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-209172852856cca635ce408" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the preceding High Septon was killed in the riot of King's Landing. Tyrion influences the Most Devout to choose this High Septon to replace him. Unlike his corrupt and fat predecessor, the new High Septon is considered to be a good man personally, but weak-willed, quietly obeying the Lannisters' political dictates. Tyrion scornfully describes the new High Septon as "a trained seal who barks prettily on command".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When a new High Septon assumes office, he renounces his given name and ever after becomes known simply as "the High Septon", so the name of this particular High Septon, like those of his predecessors, is never revealed.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Wary over him having been selected by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion","link":"Tyrion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-209172852856cca635ce408" href="/wiki/Tyrion" title="Tyrion" class="mw-redirect">Tyrion</a>, Cersei has him killed by her agent Ser Osney Kettleblack, who suffocates him with a pillow. Cersei is also motivated by Lancel Lannister revealing to have confessed to him having bedded her and giving <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-209172852856cca635ce408" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> the more potent wine that led to his death.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Cersei has this meek High Septon assassinated, instead of thrown into a cell. After the High Septon's death, it is Septon Ollidor of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Most Devout","link":"Most Devout","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Most Devout]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-209172852856cca635ce408" href="/wiki/Most_Devout" title="Most Devout">Most Devout</a>, considered one the most likely candidates to become his <i>successor</i>, who is followed into a brothel by the Sparrows and dragged naked into the streets, not this High Septon. They were condensed together in the TV version. In the novels, there was no particular mention that Septon Ollidor was having sex with prostitutes dressed up as the Seven (though he could have).
</p>
