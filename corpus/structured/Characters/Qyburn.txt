<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1"> Background </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Qyburn expulsion.png|thumb|left|Qyburn is expelled from [[The Citadel]]]]","title":"Qyburn_expulsion.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Qyburn expulsion","align":"left","caption":"Qyburn is expelled from [[The Citadel","captionParsed":"Qyburn is expelled from <a _rte_dataidx=\"0007\" href=\"\/wiki\/The_Citadel\" title=\"The Citadel\">The Citadel<\/a>"}}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" alt="" src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/f/f0/Qyburn_expulsion.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20150303020419" width="180" height="101" class="image thumb alignLeft withCaption" data-image-name="Qyburn expulsion.png" data-image-key="Qyburn_expulsion.png"  type="image" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->Qyburn is a former <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maester","link":"Maesters","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maesters|maester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">maester</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Citadel","link":"the Citadel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Citadel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/The_Citadel" title="The Citadel">the Citadel</a>. He was stripped of his chain by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Archmaester","link":"Archmaester","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Archmaester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/Archmaester" title="Archmaester">Archmaesters</a> for engaging in forbidden human experimentation: it was discovered that he had been dissecting living men in his pursuit of medical knowledge. Qyburn held to the belief that the death of a few men was justifiable if it would lead to medical breakthroughs that would save the lives of many more.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While his unethical experiments have led to him being reviled, they also made him arguably one of the most talented healers in all of Westeros.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Qyburn is a former <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maester","link":"maester","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[maester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/Maester" title="Maester" class="mw-redirect">maester</a> who acts as a healer in the Brave Companions <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsword","link":"sellsword","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[sellsword]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/Sellsword" title="Sellsword" class="mw-redirect">sellsword</a> company. Qyburn never met Robb Stark, nor was he imprisoned while staying at Harrenhal, and he never met Gregor Clegane until after the latter's fight with Oberyn Martell. Qyburn is rumored to practice black sorcery and necromancy. Despite his horrific experiments, Qyburn has a very kindly, fatherly appearance - Cersei thinks he looks like some little girl's image of her favorite grandfather.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Qyburn fell in with the Brave Companions at some point, who had been hired by the Lannisters and were based around Harrenhal. When Roose Bolton leads the eastern half of the Northern army to advance on Harrenhal they betrayed the Lannisters to side with Bolton, and remain at Harrenhal. This entire subplot was extensively condensed, so that Robb's <i>entire</i> army goes to the Westerlands, then to Harrenhal, instead of splitting into two groups (one commanded by Robb Stark, the other by Roose Bolton). The Brave Companions were also condensed from being a sellsword company to just a particularly vicious group of Bolton soldiers. Thus Qyburn simply appears in the TV series without further explanation as a refugee/prisoner that just happened to turn up at Harrenhal. In the novels, the Brave Companions are fond of cutting off the hands and feet of their prisoners, so Qyburn has become quite skilled at treating such injuries.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Qyburn treats Jaime's festering stump as in the TV series. The chapter is told from Jaime's POV, which explains that the main reason he refuses to take <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Milk of the poppy","link":"Milk of the poppy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Milk of the poppy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/Milk_of_the_poppy" title="Milk of the poppy">Milk of the poppy</a> is because he fears that if he allows Qyburn to render him unconscious for the surgery, he will break his word and amputate the rest of his right arm instead of just the gangrene parts. Qyburn's treatment works quite well and he manages to save Jaime's right arm from the gangrene, and travels to King's Landing with him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When treating Gregor Clegane, Qyburn theorizes that Oberyn had adulterated the manticore venom on his spear with sorcery; thickening it in order to slow its progress through the veins towards the heart, dragging out Gregor's agony.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Qyburn is made Master of Whisperers, Cersei gives him control over the dungeons, allowing him use the prisoners in his experiments. She starts disposing of enemies or unwanted persons (i.e. maids she thinks are spying on her for Margaery Tyrell) by sending them off to Qyburn - other than a few screams emanating from his dungeon, they are never heard from again. At one point, when Cersei muses that she cannot have <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> killed, since he is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>'s bastard who was elected the new <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","link":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander of the Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-41923085456cca5d04481d" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch" title="Lord Commander of the Night's Watch">Lord Commander of the Night's Watch</a>, Qyburn advises that she send assassins posing as recruits.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Cersei plots Margaery's downfall by framing her for a serial adulterer (which Cersei ironically is herself) she uses Qyburn to torture suspects into submission, driving them half mad. They tell to story Cersei wants them to tell. After Margaery is arrested, she gives these suspects to the High sparrow to further build up the false case against Margaery. However, Cersei forgets an important fact: she is not the only one who can extract confessions by torturing. One of her henchmen, Osney Kettleblack, is tortured at the High Sparrow's command, and the truth comes out, including about the murder of the previous High Septon. After Cersei herself is imprisoned and the High Sparrow learns of her plot against Margaery, the suspects continue to admit their guilt, but the High Sparrow is not impressed, as he can see they are clearly half mad. However, the High Sparrow still means to try Margaery, though the case against her is weak, primarily because of the unreliability of the suspects.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Qyburn's character might have been inspired by the Nazi physician Josef Mengele or by the fictional Dr. Frankenstein, or both.
</p>
