Kevan is married to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorna Lannister","link":"Dorna Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorna Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Dorna_Lannister" title="Dorna Lannister">Dorna Lannister</a> and they have a son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lancel","link":"Lancel Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lancel Lannister|Lancel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Lancel_Lannister" title="Lancel Lannister" class="mw-redirect">Lancel</a>, who is a squire to King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>. <h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">While talking with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"his mother","link":"Cersei Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cersei Lannister|his mother]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">his mother</a> on how to handle <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the North","link":"the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">the North</a> should it come to conflict with the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Starks","link":"House Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark|Starks]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">Starks</a>, Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey","link":"Joffrey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Joffrey" title="Joffrey" class="mw-redirect">Joffrey</a> muses on crushing the Northerners with a new "Royal Army" and giving <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> to his great-uncle Kevan to rule over. Though, Cersei dismisses the plan as impractical. Kevan is also indirectly (and inaccurately) accused of being a "half-wit with a stutter" by King Robert when he taunts Lancel about his alliterative name.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"Lord Snow\">\"[[Lord Snow]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Kevan is going over troop movements with his brother Tywin in their army's command tent when Tyrion enters, having escaped from his capture by Catelyn Stark, and with a makeshift army of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"hill tribes","link":"hill tribes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[hill tribes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Hill_tribes" title="Hill tribes">hill tribesmen</a> in tow. Kevan is pleasantly surprised to see Tyrion safe and free, though Tyrion's own father seems mildly disappointed at both prospects. Kevan explains that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister|Jaime]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime</a> crushed the Tully army at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Golden Tooth","link":"Battle of the Golden Tooth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Golden Tooth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Golden_Tooth" title="Battle of the Golden Tooth">Battle of the Golden Tooth</a> and is laying siege to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a>. Tyrion wonders how King Robert would allow this, but Tywin explains that Robert is dead and Eddard Stark imprisoned. A messenger then arrives saying that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s army has passed south of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Neck","link":"the Neck","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Neck]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/The_Neck" title="The Neck">the Neck</a>. Tywin orders Kevan to have the drummers signal the army to assemble to march, and to send word to Jaime that their army will be moving against Robb.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Pointy End]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Kevan is later part of Tywin's pre-battle strategy meeting, in which Tywin declares that Tyrion and his hill tribe allies will be in the vanguard. Tyrion balks at this, not only because it will put him in the greatest danger, but because the hill tribes are inexperienced at pitched battle. Kevan tries to console his nephew that the hill tribes do seem to be rather ferocious, and should be up for it. Kevan takes part in the following <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Green Fork","link":"Battle of the Green Fork","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Green Fork]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Green_Fork" title="Battle of the Green Fork">Battle of the Green Fork</a>, and Tywin's army is victorious. Even Tyrion and his hill tribes did fairly well. It turns out, however, that this was simply a diversionary force so Robb could use the bulk of his army to ambush the western half of the Lannister army under Jaime at Riverrun.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Baelor]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the Lannister defeat at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","link":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Whispering Wood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Whispering_Wood" title="Battle of the Whispering Wood">Battle of the Whispering Wood</a> and the capture of Jaime, Kevan joins Tywin's council as they discuss their next move. Kevan points out that with the Starks attacking from the north and both of Robert Baratheon's brothers attacking in southern Westeros, they are facing a war on two fronts. With half of their armies destroyed, Kevan asks if they should consider negotiating with Robb Stark for peace. Tyrion bluntly points out that all chance for a negotiated peace died with Robb's father Eddard, whom Joffrey petulantly had executed. Tywin angrily orders everyone out, except for Tyrion. He agrees that they could have negotiated if Eddard was still alive, but now they cannot hold their position, so he and Kevan will lead their remaining army-group south to regroup at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Fire and Blood]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

Tywin asserts that Robb has gotten too close to Casterly Rock. Kevan relays news that Robb has sent a splinter force to recapture <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> and theorizes that the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Greyjoys","link":"House Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Greyjoy|Greyjoys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/House_Greyjoy" title="House Greyjoy">Greyjoys</a>' <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"seizure","link":"Fall of Winterfell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fall of Winterfell|seizure]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Fall_of_Winterfell" title="Fall of Winterfell">seizure</a> of the castle is a boon to them because Robb will not march against Casterly Rock until he is at full strength. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ser Kevan is a solid, reliable, and serious man. His appearance in the books is different: Kevan being blonde, green-eyed, bearded and somewhat fat. He is content with living in his elder brother's shadow, serving as a follower and not trying to usurp Tywin's authority. This does not, however, mean that Kevan is an unintelligent man, simply that he recognizes Tywin's superior capabilities, and he occupies a position of power and responsibility in House Lannister as Tywin's most trusted lieutenant.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Kevan is loyal to his brother, loves his wife and children, and is proud of his family and heritage. He is married to the Lady Dorna of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Swyft","link":"House Swyft","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Swyft]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/House_Swyft" title="House Swyft">House Swyft</a>. As well as Lancel he has two younger sons, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Willem","link":"Willem Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Willem Lannister|Willem]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Willem_Lannister" title="Willem Lannister">Willem</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Martyn","link":"Martyn Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Martyn Lannister|Martyn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Martyn_Lannister" title="Martyn Lannister">Martyn</a>, and an infant daughter, Janei. Tyrion always got along much better with his uncle Kevan than his tyrannical father Tywin, as well as having a good relationship with his younger two uncles (Kevan's younger brothers) Tygett and Gerion (who are both deceased (Tygett) or disappeared on a travel without a trace (Gerion) by the time the books begin). Kevan always displays the basic courtesy and family respect to Tyrion that Tywin does not.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Kevan's son Willem and his cousin Tion Frey were taken captive by the Starks in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","link":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Whispering Wood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Whispering_Wood" title="Battle of the Whispering Wood">Battle of the Whispering Wood</a>, imprisoned at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a>, and killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rickard Karstark","link":"Rickard Karstark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rickard Karstark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Rickard_Karstark" title="Rickard Karstark">Rickard Karstark</a>. Kevan's son Martyn was captured in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Oxcross","link":"Battle of Oxcross","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Oxcross]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Oxcross" title="Battle of Oxcross">Battle of Oxcross</a> and later Robb exchanged him for Robett Glover, who was taken captive by the Lannisters in the Battle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duskendale","link":"Duskendale","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duskendale]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Duskendale" title="Duskendale">Duskendale</a>. The TV series, however, had Lord Karstark kill both Martyn and Willem. Tion Frey was actually the younger son of Genna Lannister, Tywin and Kevan's sister, but the TV series hadn't introduced her by Season 3. Moreover, Genna's oldest son Cleos Frey was changed in the TV adaptation to be "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Alton Lannister","link":"Alton Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Alton Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Alton_Lannister" title="Alton Lannister">Alton Lannister</a>", a more distant cousin. Apparently, the TV producers didn't want viewers to be confused - at the time - why someone named "Frey" would be fighting for the Lannisters (given that this was before the Freys betrayed the Starks at the Red Wedding). Thus, similar to how Cleos Frey was changed around, the TV series killed both of Kevan's younger sons instead of only one.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Kevan doesn't appear in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"4","link":"Season 4","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4|4]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">4</a> whereas in the third novel he is present at several important events: he participates in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Blackwater","link":"Battle of the Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a> and is later made <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Laws","link":"Master of Laws","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Laws]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Master_of_Laws" title="Master of Laws">Master of Laws</a>. He becomes consumed by worry and grief due to the death of his son Willem, the sickness of Lancel, and Martyn's captivity. He is present at Tyrion and Sansa's wedding. During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"small council","link":"small council","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[small council]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Small_council" title="Small council" class="mw-redirect">small council</a> meeting in which the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a> is reported and it is Kevan, not <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Varys","link":"Varys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Varys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a>, who reminds Joffrey that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark|Sansa]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a> is his aunt-by-marriage when the King shares his plan to have <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s head served to her. After Tyrion is arrested for being accused of murdering Joffrey, it is Kevan, not Jaime, who sees Tyrion in the cell for several times; he informs Tyrion about the judges, and offers to help Tyrion to find any witness that the latter requests; Tyrion asks his uncle to find <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Podrick Payne","link":"Podrick Payne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Podrick Payne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Podrick_Payne" title="Podrick Payne">Podrick Payne</a>, whom Kevan brings to him; Sansa, whom no one in King's Landing can find; and Varys, who is already summoned by Cersei as her witness. It is also Kevan who relays Tywin's offer to Tyrion to allow him to join the Night's Watch in exchange for a plea for mercy. Later Kevan is present at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the trial by combat of Tyrion","link":"Second Trial by combat of Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Second Trial by combat of Tyrion Lannister|the trial by combat of Tyrion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Second_Trial_by_combat_of_Tyrion_Lannister" title="Second Trial by combat of Tyrion Lannister" class="mw-redirect">the trial by combat of Tyrion</a>, in which <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gregor Clegane","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a> brutally kills <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Oberyn Martell","link":"Oberyn Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Oberyn Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Oberyn_Martell" title="Oberyn Martell">Oberyn Martell</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following Tywin's death, Kevan is not offered the position of "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of War","link":"Master of War","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of War]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Master_of_War" title="Master of War">Master of War</a>" - which doesn't exist in the novels as the "supreme commander" of the realm's armies is whoever holds the title of "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Protector of the Realm","link":"Protector of the Realm","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Protector of the Realm]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Protector_of_the_Realm" title="Protector of the Realm">Protector of the Realm</a>" - but that of Hand of the King, which he rejects as Cersei refuses to name him <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Regent","link":"Regent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Regent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Regent" title="Regent">Regent</a> - the title she holds. The "Master of War" title however, could be a reference to how Cersei starts basically <i>making up</i> titles on the Small Council.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to Tyrion, "the gods shaped him to be a follower, not a leader, well the gods and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"my lord father","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister|my lord father]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">my lord father</a>", and thinks that Kevan never had a thought that Tywin had not had first. It turns out, however, that many underestimated him: in truth he always did as his older brother instructed because Tywin was a political and military genius, so Kevan happened to <i>agree</i> with his decisions most of the time. This loyalty and obedience only extended to Tywin, so when he realizes that Cersei is running House Lannister into the ground, he quickly turns against her.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">To spite him, Cersei names her cousin Daven Lannister, son of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stafford Lannister","link":"Stafford Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stafford Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Stafford_Lannister" title="Stafford Lannister">Stafford Lannister</a>, as the new <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Warden of the West","link":"Warden of the West","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Warden of the West]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Warden_of_the_West" title="Warden of the West" class="mw-redirect">Warden of the West</a> while the title of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castellan","link":"Castellan","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castellan]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" href="/wiki/Castellan" title="Castellan">Castellan</a> of Casterly Rock is given to Ser Damion Lannister, another cousin from Stafford's branch of the family. Following Cersei's arrest, Kevan returns to King's Landing, but serves as Lord Regent and Protector of the Realm instead of Hand of the King. The title of Hand of the King is granted to Mace Tyrell (who did not go to Braavos in the books) in order to appease House Tyrell. Kevan takes steps to repair the damages Cersei caused with her plots, and to prevent her from ruining Tommen as she had Joffrey.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Unlike in the show, Kevan visits the imprisoned Cersei and confronts her angrily for corrupting Lancel. He tells her that she must perform the walk of atonement, and informs her of the latest bad news, among them about Myrcella (injured severely by Ser Gerold "Darkstar" Dayne, survived but was horribly disfigured).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the walk of atonement, Kevan does not gloat at Cersei but feels sorry for her. Recalling a similar event that he witnessed many years ago, he muses sadly that his elder brother never dreamed that his own daughter would have to go through the same humiliating punishment he imposed on his father's mistress.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">"Kevan" is pronounced like the common real-life name "Kevin", as opposed to "Kee-van", etc.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"Lord Snow\" \/>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52928235156cca5c9654c4" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
