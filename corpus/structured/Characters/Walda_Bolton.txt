<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Fat Walda is the second of three daughters of Merrett Frey and Mariya Darry. Merret is himself the ninth son of Lord Walder. She is known for her gluttony, hence her nickname, which her father considers shameful, though less shameful than her older sister Amerei, known as "Gatehouse Ami" for her blatant promiscuity.¬†
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lord Walder taunted Merrett that Walda is "a sow in silk", her weight being the only reason Roose married her. He remarks that the Freys would have had the same alliance at half the price if "the little porkling" put down her spoon from time to time.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">She is described as a round pink butterball of a girl, with watery eyes, a huge bosom and limp yellow hair. Her voice is thin and squeaky. She is fifteen years old when she marries Roose. Walda has a giggling, fun-loving, even charming personality, and is known for cracking bawdy jokes. It is loosely implied, however, that this is simply an outward persona that she affects to fool others into thinking she is harmless, when she is actually very intelligent - some of her bawdy jokes can actually be quite witty.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Roose stays in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>, Walda writes him letters almost every day, all the letters are the same: "I pray for you morn, noon, and night, my sweet lord, and count the days until you share my bed again. Return to me soon, and I will give you many trueborn sons to take the place of your dear <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Domeric","link":"Domeric Bolton","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Domeric Bolton|Domeric]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/Domeric_Bolton" title="Domeric Bolton">Domeric</a> and rule <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Dreadfort","link":"the Dreadfort","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Dreadfort]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/The_Dreadfort" title="The Dreadfort">the Dreadfort</a> after you." <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> is present when Roose receives one such letter, but he may have never responded to any of them. Roose tells Arya to burn Walda's love letters, though this is not particularly significant, as Roose burns all of his personal correspondence to prevent his enemies or rivals from reading it.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Fat Walda attends the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a> and sits next to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a>. It is Fat Walda who tells Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wendel Manderly","link":"Wendel Manderly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wendel Manderly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/Wendel_Manderly" title="Wendel Manderly">Wendel Manderly</a>, while Catelyn overhears, about her grandfather's dowry offer to Lord Bolton. She is one of the many Frey girls that King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> politely dances with during the festivities.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Walda is fully aware that Roose chose to marry her instead of another Frey girl - such as Fair Walda, granddaughter of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stevron Frey","link":"Stevron Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stevron Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40613875256cca5e1d3752" href="/wiki/Stevron_Frey" title="Stevron Frey">Stevron Frey</a> - simply so he could get her weight in silver, but nonetheless she is very grateful to him, as marrying the head of a powerful House drastically elevated her social standing. Roose initially thought Walda would be an annoying distraction, but later says that despite himself he has become oddly fond of her. His first two wives were silent and still when he had sex with them, but Walda makes a lot of noise and openly enjoys it, which Roose finds somewhat endearing.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">On one occasion Ramsay, while talking to his father, refers to Walda as "your fat pig wife" - making it clear he does not care much for his stepmother.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Roose never announces that Walda is pregnant. The only reference to her pregnancy is in the last of Theon's chapters in the fifth book, when Theon sees Roose Bolton entering the Great Hall at Winterfell accompanied by his plump and pregnant wife.  Roose also remarked to Reek earlier that he feared that if he had new children with Walda, Ramsay would try to kill them to remove any rival heirs to House Bolton.
