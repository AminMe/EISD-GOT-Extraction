<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-145603527956cca7e8eb835" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Arya tries to trade the pigeon to a man selling pies, first asking for lemon, but then any kind. When he refuses, she considers grabbing it and running as the man has a limp, but as there are <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"gold cloaks","link":"City Watch","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[City Watch|gold cloaks]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-145603527956cca7e8eb835" href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">gold cloaks</a> patrolling nearby she thinks better of it. Earlier she had been successful in trading pigeons she caught to a pot-shop (apparently as an ingredient in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bowls of brown","link":"Bowl of brown","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bowl of brown|bowls of brown]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-145603527956cca7e8eb835" href="/wiki/Bowl_of_brown" title="Bowl of brown">bowls of brown</a>), which in return would give her back half of the pigeon's meat cooked - before this, she had to eat the pigeon meat raw.
