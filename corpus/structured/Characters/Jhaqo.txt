<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40651120156cca62d270be" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Jhaqo is a <i>ko</i> in Khal <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogo","link":"Drogo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drogo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40651120156cca62d270be" href="/wiki/Drogo" title="Drogo">Drogo</a>'s khalassar. After Drogo's fall from his horse and subsequent incapacitacion, Jhaqo is one of the several kos that declares himself Khal and takes a portion of the khalasar as his own. Among those who follow him is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mago","link":"Mago","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mago]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40651120156cca62d270be" href="/wiki/Mago" title="Mago">Mago</a> (who is killed for challenging Drogo in the TV series, but in the book he is still alive for no such challenge occurs), who becomes Jhaqo's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bloodrider","link":"bloodrider","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[bloodrider]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40651120156cca62d270be" href="/wiki/Bloodrider" title="Bloodrider">bloodrider</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At the end of the fifth novel, Jhaqo and about fifty warriors of his khalasar encounter Daenerys while she is in the Dothraki sea after Drogon has flown her there. His reaction is not mentioned.
</p>
