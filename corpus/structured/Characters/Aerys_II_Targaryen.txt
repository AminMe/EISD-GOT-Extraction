<!-- RTE::{"spaces":1,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Aerys II Targaryen</b>, popularly called <b>"the Mad King"</b>, is an unseen character in <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Game of Thrones","link":"Game of Thrones (TV series)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Game of Thrones (TV series)|Game of Thrones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones_(TV_series)" title="Game of Thrones (TV series)">Game of Thrones</a></i>. Deceased prior to the events of the series, Aerys Targaryen was the last member of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a> to rule from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>. Although his rule began benevolently, he succumbed to madness and was eventually deposed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> in a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"civil war","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion|civil war]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">civil war</a>. Infamously, Aerys was murdered by a member of his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>, Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a>, earning the latter the sobriquet "Kingslayer".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aerys's two surviving children, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys","link":"Viserys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys Targaryen|Viserys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen|Daenerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a>, were taken to the Free Cities of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a>, intent on one day returning to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> to reclaim what they regard as their birthright. He was formally styled as <b>Aerys of House Targaryen, the Second of His Name, King of the Andals and the First Men, Lord of the Seven Kingdoms and Protector of the Realm</b>.
</p>

Aerys had him arrested for treason and then offered to ransom him to his father. </p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The noble houses of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark|Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">Stark</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tully","link":"House Tully","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tully|Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/House_Tully" title="House Tully">Tully</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arryn","link":"House Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Arryn|Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/House_Arryn" title="House Arryn">Arryn</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon|Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">Baratheon</a> raised the flags of rebellion and destroyed the royalist armies in several major engagements, culminating in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a> where Prince Rhaegar fell. </p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aerys had already instructed Wisdom <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rossart","link":"Rossart","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rossart]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Rossart" title="Rossart">Rossart</a>, now his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>, to hide caches of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildfire","link":"wildfire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildfire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a> all over King's Landing. Now, he decreed that the capital <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"be set ablaze","link":"Wildfire plot","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wildfire plot|be set ablaze]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Wildfire_plot" title="Wildfire plot">be set ablaze</a>. Aerys seemingly believed the massive inferno would transmute him into a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragon","link":"Dragons","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragons|dragon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragon</a> and he would emerge to obliterate his enemies. To prevent the King incinerating the city and its half a million inhabitants, Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a>, a member of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>, stabbed Aerys in the back and then cut his throat.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Kissed by Fire]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Aerys, the Mad King.png|thumb|266px|Aerys in his final years]]","title":"Aerys,_the_Mad_King.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Aerys, the Mad King","caption":"Aerys in his final years","width":266,"captionParsed":"Aerys in his final years"}}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" alt="" src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/4/4a/Aerys,_the_Mad_King.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/266?cb=20130623030909" width="266" height="150" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Aerys, the Mad King.png" data-image-key="Aerys,_the_Mad_King.png"  type="image" /> In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Aerys II's reign began with great promise. Taking the throne at only the age of 20, Aerys installed a number of younger, vigorous and capable men in positions of power, while his own son Rhaegar showed promise of being a great king in his turn. Aerys, impressed by the young Ser Tywin Lannister's ruthlessness and effectiveness in crushing the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Reyne Rebellion","link":"Reyne Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Reyne Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Reyne_Rebellion" title="Reyne Rebellion">Reyne Rebellion</a>, made Tywin, then heir to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Casterly Rock","link":"Casterly Rock","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Casterly Rock]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Casterly_Rock" title="Casterly Rock">Casterly Rock</a>, his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>. Tywin served very effectively as Hand for twenty years, during which the Seven Kingdoms prospered.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">However, Aerys, who occasionally showed signs of a terrible temper, became erratic following the brief uprising known as the Defiance of Duskendale, when he was held prisoner for several months by a rebellious lord before finally being rescued by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a>. Aerys's fury on that occasion was terrible: all of those involved in the incident were killed, several by being burned alive. After that, his rage could no longer be held in check.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aerys's relationship with Tywin Lannister began to deteriorate in these later years, as Aerys grew ever more paranoid about Tywin's ambition. When Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ilyn Payne","link":"Ilyn Payne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ilyn Payne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Ilyn_Payne" title="Ilyn Payne">Ilyn Payne</a>, captain of the guards for Tywin Lannister, made a joke that Tywin as Hand was the real ruler of Westeros, Aerys had his tongue cut out for the insult. Tywin could neither protest nor stop the punishment. The strains on their relationship were exacerbated by Aerys' well known lust for Tywin's wife <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joanna Lannister","link":"Joanna Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joanna Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Joanna_Lannister" title="Joanna Lannister">Joanna Lannister</a>, which the former crudely expressed during her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bedding","link":"bedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[bedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Bedding" title="Bedding">bedding</a>. <img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>''A Dance with Dragons'',\u00a0Chapter 43, Daenerys VII.<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> His marriage with his own sister-wife, Queen Rhaella, which had never been happy (they were forced to marry against their will) became violent and abusive towards the end.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During Aerys's reign period there were various rumors about him and Joanna: that Aerys took Joanna maidenhead on the night of Jaehaerys's coronation, and that Joanna was one of Aerys's many mistresses, which was the reason for her abrupt dismissal by the queen. Pycelle insisted those rumors were baseless. There were, however, occasions of improper behavior of Aerys toward Joanna: when she returned to the court with her six-year old twins, drunken Aerys asked her if nursing her children had ruined her breasts, humiliating her and Tywin. When she died giving birth to Tyrion, the king commented that was done by the gods to teach Tywin some humility at last.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>''\"The World of Ice and Fire\"'', The Targaryen Kings: Aerys II.<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Over the years Aerys became increasingly gripped by madness, experiencing vivid hallucinations and delusional paranoia. Aerys became obsessed with fire, frequently burning his perceived enemies alive. Eventually, he was having women and babies roasted on spits in the throne room because the voices in his head told him they were plotting his ruin. Aerys would often become sexually aroused after burning someone to death, at which he would savagely rape his wife Queen Rhaella (his daughter Daenerys was the product of one such attack, quite possibly the same day he killed Rickard and Brandon Stark). Attempts to restrain Aerys' delusional behavior only fueled his paranoia that all were against him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aerys's growing madness took a drastic toll on his physical appearance. Fearful of allowing blades in his presence (save those of his sworn Kingsguard), Aerys ceased to have his hair or nails cut for years. By the time of the rebellion, his fingernails had grown nearly a foot long, and his long, filthy, tangled hair and beard hung below his shoulders. Increasingly paranoid that someone would attempt to poison him, he ate so rarely that he became extremely gaunt. Combined with the stress from his madness, by the time of the rebellion, when Aerys was only forty years old, he looked as old and haggard as a man twice his age. Due to his insane meanderings, he accidentally cut his hands and thighs so many times on the Iron Throne that they were practically covered in scabs, leading some to refer to him as "King Scab" behind his back.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tywin had long hoped that Aerys would reward his service with a royal wedding, between his daughter <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cersei","link":"Cersei Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cersei Lannister|Cersei]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei</a> and Prince Rhaegar. However, Tywin was infuriated when Aerys snubbed him by saying that Tywin was a mere servant of the crown, and no servant's daughter was fit to marry a prince of royal blood, and agreed to a marriage between Rhaegar and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Elia Martell","link":"Elia Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Elia Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Elia_Martell" title="Elia Martell">Elia Martell</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> instead. The last straw came when Aerys appointed Tywin's eldest son and heir <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> to his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>. Normally this was the highest honor a knight could achieve, and Jaime himself was eager to accept, but it meant that Jaime had to forsake all claims as Tywin's heir (leaving his stunted and hated younger son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister|Tyrion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a> as his heir). Privately, Aerys had truly agreed to promote Jaime to the Kingsguard to keep him around the royal court at all times, essentially as an unwitting hostage in case Tywin ever decided to turn on him. Outraged by the continual insults he endured from Aerys, Tywin resigned as Hand of the King on some thin pretext; the Hands that followed were a succession of Aerys' cronies, who he later turned on for various failures, exiling or executing them depending on his whims.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After he burned several prominent lords alive and sanctioned his son's kidnap of Lyanna Stark, half of the realm rose in revolt against Aerys in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>. Many who fought on the Targaryen side in the war weren't even fighting for the insane Aerys so much as for his promising son, Crown Prince Rhaegar. Indeed, one of the main reasons so many put up with Aerys' insanity for as long as they did was because they hoped they could simply wait out the few remaining years in Aerys' reign until Rhaegar took the throne, instead of having to face the serious moral dilemma of breaking their oaths against the currently ruling king. Robert Baratheon's victory over Rhaegar at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a> was therefore a double-blow to the Targaryen side: not only had the main royal army been defeated and scattered, but with Rhaegar dead, lords who had been on the fence decided to openly side with the rebels rather than fight for Aerys. Chief among these was Tywin Lannister himself, who had stayed out of the war until that point, who marched the 10,000 strong Lannister army to the gates of King's Landing, and once let inside, promptly began to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sack the capital city","link":"Sack of King's Landing","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of King's Landing|sack the capital city]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Sack_of_King's_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">sack the capital city</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aerys's manner of death is slightly different from book to series. In the TV series, it is mentioned several times that Jaime stabbed him in the back, whereas in the book he slit Aerys' throat at the foot of the Iron Throne. It is never mentioned that Aerys ever said the words "Burn them all". However, the Season 3 episode "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kissed by Fire","link":"Kissed by Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kissed by Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Kissed_by_Fire" title="Kissed by Fire">Kissed by Fire</a>" brought Aerys's death closer in line to the books, when Jaime recounts to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brienne of Tarth","link":"Brienne of Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brienne of Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Brienne_of_Tarth" title="Brienne of Tarth">Brienne of Tarth</a> that he stabbed Aerys in the back when he tried to run, and <i>then</i> Jaime proceeded to slit his throat to make sure he was dead.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Another change is that Eddard Stark was not the first to find that Jaime killed Aerys: before Jaime could leave and let some braggart take the credit/blame, Elys Westerling and Lord Roland Crakehall entered and saw him standing over Aerys's body. Crakehall was not surprised, figuring (incorrectly) that Jaime killed the king so the Lannisters could seize the throne. He asked Jaime, "Shall I proclaim a new king as well?" Jaime knew what he meant: either Tywin, or Robert Baratheon, or one of the surviving Targaryens. He considered for a moment to name either the boy Viserys or baby Aegon. But when he glanced down again at Aerys's body, he thought "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"his blood","link":"Targaryen madness","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Targaryen madness|his blood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Targaryen_madness" title="Targaryen madness">his blood</a> is in both of them". "Proclaim who you bloody well like" he told Crakehall. Then he climbed the Iron Throne and seated himself with his sword across his knees, to see who would come to claim the kingdom. As it happened, it had been Eddard Stark.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In a minor change from the books, Aerys II is said to be the son of King Aegon V Targaryen, rather than his grandson. His father, Jaehaerys II, appears to have been eliminated from the roll of kings. The reason for this appears to be to simplify the relationship between Maester <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aemon","link":"Aemon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aemon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Aemon" title="Aemon">Aemon</a> - Aegon V's older brother - and the other Targaryens (by making Aemon <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen|Daenerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-123602108956cca599d4889" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a>' grand-uncle, rather than great-grand-uncle).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Unlike in the show, Daenerys is never told the whole ugly truth about her father: what Ser Barristan Selmy tells her in the novels is a very "sugarcoated" version, sticking to positive facts, belittling greatly the negative facts, and entirely omitting the countless atrocities the Mad King committed (mainly the repeated brutal raping of the queen, the murder of every member of House Darklyn and House Hollard after the Defiance of Duskendale and the execution of Rickard and Brandon Stark). The worst thing Selmy ever tells Daenerys about her father is that he lusted after Tywin's wife and acted indecently at her bedding.
</p>
