<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Marei first appeared in the Season 2 premiere, but wasn't formally introduced and given a name until the third episode of Season 3.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As of Season 5, following the death of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ros","link":"Ros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133616462656cca61d81d97" href="/wiki/Ros" title="Ros">Ros</a> in Season 3, Marei is the longest recurring prostitute character in the series, both in total number of seasons and continuous seasons in a row. By this point she has appeared in six episodes across four seasons starting with Season 2. None of the other minor prostitute characters introduced in Seasons 1 or 2 reappeared afterwards (save for Ros and Shae who are now dead), and the only other prostitutes introduced in Season 3 who returned in Season 4 were <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kayla the contortionist","link":"Kayla","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kayla|Kayla the contortionist]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133616462656cca61d81d97" href="/wiki/Kayla" title="Kayla">Kayla the contortionist</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Olyvar","link":"Olyvar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Olyvar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133616462656cca61d81d97" href="/wiki/Olyvar" title="Olyvar">Olyvar</a>, the male prostitute who worked as a spy for Littlefinger.  Marei and Olyvar returned in Season 5, but Kyra did not (Olyvar was introduced the season after Marei, so he will always lag one season behind).  Ros did appear in more episodes overall (fourteen) and had more screentime, but she only appeared in three seasons (in a row).  Marei surpassed Ros as the longest recurring invented prostitute character in Season 5 - arguably Shae might be counted as recurring longer than Ros, with four continuous seasons, but she wasn't a character invented for the TV series.  Either way, Marei's confirmed return in Season 6 means she will have appeared in five continuous seasons of the TV series, also surpassing Shae's record. Shae is set to reappear in dream sequences in Season 6, meaning she will have "appeared" (in some fashion) in five seasons - but not five seasons in a row, as Marei has.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Josephine Gillan explained in an interview after Season 5 that for the episode "Sons of the Harpy", when the Faith Militant storm Littlefinger's brothel and drag Marei out of the building, it was originally planned that they would punch Marei in the face, so hard (possibly breaking Marei's nose or jaw) that Gillan was going to need to have a large amount of fake blood continuing to pour out of her mouth as she is dragged away. The production team later decided, however, that seeing a woman beaten up like this was too dark and violent for the needs of the scene, and just had her dragged away by her hair.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=JinwSGyeZ78 Josephine Gillan interview, October 2015]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133616462656cca61d81d97" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133616462656cca61d81d97" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, there is a prostitute named Marei at Chataya's establishment. She is a fairly minor character, and apart from the name and the general fact that she is a prostitute in King's Landing, Marei in the TV series isn't particularly based on Marei from the books (in fact, the TV character was only given the name the season after she was introduced). Most of Marei's scenes so far in the TV series have no direct counterpart in the narrative of the novels. Nonetheless she is not, strictly speaking, a character <i>entirely</i> "invented" for the TV series the way Ros or Armeca were.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Marei in the books has green eyes, porcelain skin, and silvery hair, but is also very solemn. She is also literate. While in the TV series Bronn celebrated being knighted by having sex with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mirelle","link":"Mirelle","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mirelle]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-133616462656cca61d81d97" href="/wiki/Mirelle" title="Mirelle">Mirelle</a>, in the books he celebrated by having sex with Marei and another prostitute named Alayaya, simultaneously. This occurred at the beginning of the third novel, and correspondingly, Bronn celebrated his knighthood by going to the brothel at the beginning of Season 3. Marei has not been mentioned in the novels afterwards.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the TV series, Tyrion pronounces her name "Muh-ray" (with a short A), as opposed to "Mare-ay" (that is, with a long A).
</p>
