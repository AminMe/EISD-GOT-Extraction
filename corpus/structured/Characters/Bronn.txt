Bronn is a skilled and dangerous <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsword","link":"sellsword","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[sellsword]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Sellsword" title="Sellsword" class="mw-redirect">sellsword</a> who comes to prominence when he champions for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> in his trial by combat in the Eyrie. His status in Westerosi society rises even further when he is betrothed to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lollys Stokeworth","link":"Lollys Stokeworth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lollys Stokeworth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Lollys_Stokeworth" title="Lollys Stokeworth">Lollys Stokeworth</a>, though the betrothal is annulled and he is sent to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> to rescue <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myrcella Baratheon","link":"Myrcella Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myrcella Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Myrcella_Baratheon" title="Myrcella Baratheon">Myrcella Baratheon</a> from the vengeful family of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Oberyn Martell","link":"Oberyn Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Oberyn Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Oberyn_Martell" title="Oberyn Martell">Oberyn Martell</a>. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Bronn is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsword","link":"Mercenary","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mercenary|sellsword]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellsword</a>; a mercenary soldier whose service as a warrior is for hire. Not much is known about his past or family; Both his parents beat him, and his nose was accidentally broken when his mother hit him with kitchenware, which she had meant for Bronn's younger brother. When Tyrion Lannister introduced him to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a>, Bronn said that his father is no one Lord Tywin would know. During a drinking game with Tyrion and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shae","link":"Shae","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shae]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Shae" title="Shae">Shae</a>, Bronn claims that he killed a woman before he was 12 years old, though it was in self-defense as she was attacking him with an axe. Bronn also confirmed Tyrion's claim that Bronn has been <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"beyond the Wall","link":"beyond the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[beyond the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Beyond_the_Wall" title="Beyond the Wall">beyond the Wall</a>. When asked why he was there, he bluntly answers that it was "work".<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"E9\">\"[[Baelor]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Personality</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Bronn is sarcastic, with a black sense of humor, and a pragmatic, amoral philosophy for life. However, he is not completely heartless, nor is he sadistic. He openly expresses sympathy to Tyrion after making the pragmatic decision not to champion him during his second trial. After Tyrion asks him if he would murder an innocent baby in front of her mother without question, Bronn denies it and claims that he would ask for a price, implying that he would demand a <i>very</i> high price for such a despicable deed. Despite Bronn's avaricious nature, which is sneered at by more honorable <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"knights","link":"knights","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[knights]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Knights" title="Knights" class="mw-redirect">knights</a>, he is a skilled and dangerous fighter.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/guide\/houses\/jbaratheon\/bronn\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 guide to houses, House Baratheon of King's Landing - Bronn entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> His combat style centers around speed and agility, dodging enemy attacks instead of blocking them. For this reason, he does not wear heavy armor or capes or use shields, but instead relies on just his sword and knives. He is also a skilled archer<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"E19\">\"[[Blackwater]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> and is apparently literate. His pragmatic approach to combat is the main reason why he refused to fight the Mountain in Tyrion's second trial by combat despite his superior stamina and speed, explaining "One misstep, and I'm dead".
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<ul><li>Actor Jerome Flynn portrays Bronn with a Northern English accent (most likely Lancashire), but this is not how he actually speaks in real life. Flynn is from Kent, and Bronn's accent is a deliberate choice for his performance.
</li><li>On the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 4","link":"Season 4","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">Season 4</a> Blu-ray, Bronn narrates the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Histories & Lore","link":"Histories & Lore","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Histories & Lore]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Histories_&_Lore" title="Histories &amp; Lore">Histories &amp; Lore</a> videos on <b>"<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sellswords &amp; Hedge Knights","link":"Sellswords & Hedge Knights (Histories & Lore)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sellswords & Hedge Knights (Histories & Lore)|Sellswords &amp; Hedge Knights]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Sellswords_&_Hedge_Knights_(Histories_&_Lore)" title="Sellswords &amp; Hedge Knights (Histories &amp; Lore)" class="mw-redirect">Sellswords <span data-rte-entity="amp">&amp;</span> Hedge Knights</a>"</b>, <b>"<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Kingsguard","link":"The Kingsguard (Histories & Lore)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Kingsguard (Histories & Lore)|The Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/The_Kingsguard_(Histories_&_Lore)" title="The Kingsguard (Histories &amp; Lore)">The Kingsguard</a>"</b> and <b>"<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Justice of the Seven Kingdoms","link":"Justice of the Seven Kingdoms (Histories & Lore)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Justice of the Seven Kingdoms (Histories & Lore)|Justice of the Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Seven_Kingdoms_(Histories_&_Lore)" title="Justice of the Seven Kingdoms (Histories &amp; Lore)" class="mw-redirect">Justice of the Seven Kingdoms</a>"</b>.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Bronn is a sellsword chance-met at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Crossroads Inn","link":"Crossroads Inn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Crossroads Inn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Crossroads_Inn" title="Crossroads Inn" class="mw-redirect">Crossroads Inn</a> by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a>. He is primarily motivated by money and the possibility of personal power, but has a rough loyalty to those who stand next to him in battle. Despite himself, he develops a grudging admiration and liking for Tyrion's craftiness and cunning, not to mention the gold it gets him. He has a black sense of humor, and a pragmatic, thoroughly amoral philosophy. Tyrion describes Bronn as "an insolent black-hearted rogue".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After hearing about <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barra","link":"Barra","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barra]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Barra" title="Barra">Barra</a>'s death, Tyrion asks Bronn "If I told you to kill a babe... an infant girl, say, still at her mother's breast... would you do it? Without question?". Bronn answers "Without question? No. I'd ask how much". Bronn's answer reflects his personality very accurately: there's nothing he wouldn't do for money, but there are still some things which would require a <i>massive</i> amount of money to make him do. Even Bronn, however, is utterly disgusted at Tywin when Tyrion explains what he did to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tysha","link":"Tysha","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tysha]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Tysha" title="Tysha">Tysha</a> when he was 13 years old (in the TV version, Tyrion was 16 when this happened). Bronn bluntly remarks that whether he was 13 or 30, he'd have killed the man who did that to him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Not much is known about Bronn's past before Tyrion encountered him at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Crossroads Inn","link":"Crossroads Inn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Crossroads Inn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Crossroads_Inn" title="Crossroads Inn" class="mw-redirect">Crossroads Inn</a>. So far in the books, Bronn has made no mention that he has ever been north of the Wall, which he briefly mentioned in the TV series. When asked why he was there, he bluntly said that he was "working". Bronn may have been hired as a guard for a smuggling ship, as illicit trade is actually fairly common between the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildlings","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk|wildlings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildlings</a> and smugglers who circumvent the Wall by sailing around it. Wildlings don't possess the technological sophistication to forge their own iron weapons, and thus are eager to trade with smugglers from the Seven Kingdoms or the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> who are willing to provide them. The wildlings don't have much in the way of wealth but can usually offer rare animal furs for trade, which they obtain from densely furred animals from north of the Wall which have adapted to the cold climate. In past centuries, the Night's Watch tried to stop the fur-for-iron trade along the coasts, but in recent centuries as their numbers dwindled, they abandoned any attempts to prevent passage around the Wall by sea. This smuggling is still dangerous, and a good smuggling crew would probably hire a few extra sellswords for protection.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Bronn is not named Commander of the City Watch in the novels, but Jacelyn Bywater is instead. Bronn was made the captain of Tyrion's guard, which is composed of sellswords chosen by Bronn himself.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Bronn is knighted after the Battle of the Blackwater for the vital role he played in destroying Stannis's fleet. Bronn was responsible for pulling a large chain across the entrance of the bay after Stannis's fleet entered, trapping them inside once the wildfire trap was sprung, leading to their destruction. The TV series condensed the battle so the chain subplot was removed, but it did still have Bronn play a vital role in the wildfire trap, by having him personally fire a flaming arrow which ignited the leaking ship filled with thousands of jars of wildfire. Following his knighting, Bronn is permitted to claim his own knightly sigil. He chooses a flaming green chain on a smoking grey field to commemorate the battle in which he earned his knighthood. As the chain was removed from the TV series, Bronn's sigil based on it was also not included. A nod to this occurs in Season 4's "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Two Swords","link":"Two Swords","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Two Swords]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Two_Swords" title="Two Swords">Two Swords</a>" when Podrick is describing the sigils of the Dornish envoys, and Bronn wistfully remarks "I need a sigil."
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Tyrion kills Tywin and flees Westeros, Bronn actually does marry Lollys Stokeworth, and leaves King's Landing for good. Cersei fears that, even though he declined to fight against Gregor Clegane in the trial by combat because it was too dangerous, he is still ultimately loyal to Tyrion, so she starts doing her best to undermine him. When Lollys' gives birth to her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bastard","link":"Bastardy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bastardy|bastard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Bastardy" title="Bastardy">bastard</a> son, conceived when she was raped during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing riot","link":"Riot of King's Landing","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riot of King's Landing|King's Landing riot]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Riot_of_King's_Landing" title="Riot of King's Landing">King's Landing riot</a>, Bronn names him Tyrion, to Cersei's annoyance. Bronn does not actually appear physically again after the third novel, though in the next novel his actions at Castle Stokeworth are mentioned and form a running subplot for Cersei. Believing Bronn played a role in Tyrion's escape or may even be sheltering him, Cersei sends someone to assassinate him, but later learns that Bronn killed him, after extracting a confession from his would-be killer that Cersei put the man up to it. Bronn subsequently goes into hiding, but Cersei herself is ultimately arrested before she can take further action.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, Bronn and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> do not have any scenes together, and there is no indication that they have even met. Ergo, Jaime does not enlist Bronn to train him in left-handed swordsmanship; instead he asks Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Addam Marbrand","link":"Addam Marbrand","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Addam Marbrand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Addam_Marbrand" title="Addam Marbrand">Addam Marbrand</a> and subsequently Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ilyn Payne","link":"Ilyn Payne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ilyn Payne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Ilyn_Payne" title="Ilyn Payne">Ilyn Payne</a>. This was apparently changed because actor <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wilko Johnson","link":"Wilko Johnson","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wilko Johnson]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Wilko_Johnson" title="Wilko Johnson">Wilko Johnson</a> (Ser Ilyn) developed pancreatic cancer and had to leave production on the TV series. Furthermore, Jaime and Bronn's journey to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> to rescue Princess <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myrcella","link":"Myrcella Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myrcella Baratheon|Myrcella]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Myrcella_Baratheon" title="Myrcella Baratheon">Myrcella</a> is an invention of the TV series. Jaime actually travels to the Riverlands to oversee the siege of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24140980056cca5b25456f" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a>, and Bronn does not accompany him, though Bronn is apparently indeed set to accompany Jaime to the Riverlands in the television series.
</p>
