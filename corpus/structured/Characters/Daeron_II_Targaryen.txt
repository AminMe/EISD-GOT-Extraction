<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Daeron II Targaryen</b>, also called <b>Daeron the Good</b>, is a character mentioned in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fourth","link":"Season 4","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4|fourth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">fourth</a> seasons and appearing in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Histories & Lore","link":"Histories & Lore","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Histories & Lore]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Histories_&_Lore" title="Histories &amp; Lore">Histories &amp; Lore</a> features. He is not expected to appear in the series, being long dead by the time it begins.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daeron was the second King of his name to sit on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> and the twelfth king of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen|Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">Targaryen</a> dynasty to rule the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. He was an ancestor of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys","link":"Viserys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys Targaryen|Viserys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a>. He was formally styled <b>Daeron of House Targaryen, the Second of His Name, King of the Andals and the First Men, Lord of the Seven Kingdoms and Protector of the Realm</b>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Daeron was the son of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon IV Targaryen","link":"Aegon IV Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon IV Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Aegon_IV_Targaryen" title="Aegon IV Targaryen">Aegon IV Targaryen</a> and Queen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Naerys Targaryen","link":"Naerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Naerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Naerys_Targaryen" title="Naerys Targaryen">Naerys Targaryen</a>, born on the year 159 since <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon's Landing","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen|Aegon's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon's Landing</a>. He had a sister, Princess <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys","link":"Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)|Daenerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)" title="Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)">Daenerys</a>. He also had a half-brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daemon Blackfyre","link":"Daemon Blackfyre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daemon Blackfyre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Daemon_Blackfyre" title="Daemon Blackfyre">Daemon Blackfyre</a>, born of the affair between his father and Queen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daena Targaryen","link":"Daena Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daena Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Daena_Targaryen" title="Daena Targaryen">Daena Targaryen</a>, the sister-wife of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baelor I Targaryen","link":"Baelor I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Baelor I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Baelor_I_Targaryen" title="Baelor I Targaryen" class="mw-redirect">Baelor I Targaryen</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">He married <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myriah","link":"Myriah Martell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myriah Martell|Myriah]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Myriah_Martell" title="Myriah Martell">Myriah</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Martell","link":"House Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/House_Martell" title="House Martell">House Martell</a> at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sunspear","link":"Sunspear","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sunspear]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Sunspear" title="Sunspear">Sunspear</a>. With her, he fathered several sons: <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baelor","link":"Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)|Baelor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)" title="Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)">Baelor</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys","link":"Aerys I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys I Targaryen|Aerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Aerys_I_Targaryen" title="Aerys I Targaryen">Aerys</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegel","link":"Rhaegel Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegel Targaryen|Rhaegel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Rhaegel_Targaryen" title="Rhaegel Targaryen">Rhaegel</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maekar","link":"Maekar Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maekar Targaryen|Maekar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Maekar_Targaryen" title="Maekar Targaryen">Maekar</a>; and one daughter, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aelinor","link":"Aelinor Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aelinor Targaryen|Aelinor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Aelinor_Targaryen" title="Aelinor Targaryen">Aelinor</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During his reign, his half-brother Daemon rebelled against his rule to claim the Iron Throne for himself. Daeron perished during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Spring Sickness","link":"Great Spring Sickness","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Spring Sickness]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Great_Spring_Sickness" title="Great Spring Sickness">Great Spring Sickness</a>. He was succeeded by his son Aerys.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Daeron II was a dignified man. He had a kind thoughtful face. He was round-shouldered, with thin legs and a small pot belly. His face has a certain quiet strength, though, and his eyes are clear and full of resolve. He wore his father's elaborate dragon Crown. On his coinage, he was depicted as clean-shaven. He was not a warrior by any means, but a cultured, scholarly man who kept company with maesters and other learned men.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After the failed Conquest of Dorne, King Baelor the Blessed arranged the marriage of his Prince Daeron to Princess Myriah Martell of Dorne as part of a dual marriage pact between the two kingdoms, thus bringing Dorne into the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. He also officiated at the wedding ceremony. Princess Myriah brought many of her native manners and customs with her, and many noble Dornishmen and women flocked to his court, all serving to give life in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a> a foreign flavor, and alienating many of Daeron's nobles and courtiers. In 170AL they had their first son, whom they named Baelor in honor of Baelor the Blessed. Daeron founded a new castle at Summerhall, in the Dornish Marches, roughly where Dorne, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Reach","link":"the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/The_Reach" title="The Reach">the Reach</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stormlands","link":"the Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a> come together.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Late in Aegon IV's reign, it was whispered that his father had come to believe that Daeron was not his son, but the product of an illegitimate union between his wife Naerys and their brother, Aemon, and was planning to disinherit Daeron in favor of Daemon Blackfyre. Others counter that Aegon IV <i>himself</i> started this rumor, because he was disgusted that his trueborn son Daeron was an intelligent and scholarly man, and he simply wished he wasn't really his son. As this theory goes, Aegon IV had became so impressed with his bastard son Daemon Blackfyre, who was a consummate warrior and personally charismatic (though he had no experience at politics), that he increasingly wanted to name Daemon as his official heir. Despite all the taunts, japes and disparities he heaped upon his son, Aegon never formally disowned Daeron. Account differ as to why, but the most likely explanation is that Aegon feared a civil war if he did so, as many lords who'd grown sick of Aegon's corruption and depravity would fight to defend Daeron's rights (the Martells chief among them).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daeron ascended to the throne, however, following Aegon IV's sudden illness and death, following his ascension he wore his father's elaborate dragon crown to legitimise his claim, and gave Daemon lands in the Crownlands to build a castle to quench his claim to the Iron Throne. Nevertheless, several military-minded lords gathered around Daemon and supported his claim to the Iron Throne, causing the First Blackfyre Rebellion.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Through his youngest son Maekar, Daeron II was the grandfather of Maester <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aemon","link":"Aemon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aemon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Aemon" title="Aemon">Aemon</a> and his younger brother, King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon V","link":"Aegon V Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon V Targaryen|Aegon V]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Aegon_V_Targaryen" title="Aegon V Targaryen">Aegon V</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daeron II was king at the beginning of the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","link":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tales of Dunk and Egg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Tales_of_Dunk_and_Egg" title="Tales of Dunk and Egg">Tales of Dunk and Egg</a></i> prequel novellas, which begin 90 years before the War of the Five Kings and follow the adventures of Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duncan the Tall","link":"Duncan the Tall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duncan the Tall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Duncan_the_Tall" title="Duncan the Tall">Duncan the Tall</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon \"Egg\" Targaryen","link":"Aegon V Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon V Targaryen|Aegon \"Egg\" Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-176652003756cca675ecae9" href="/wiki/Aegon_V_Targaryen" title="Aegon V Targaryen">Aegon "Egg" Targaryen</a>. The first novellas, <i>The Hedge Knight</i>, takes place in the last few months of Daeron II's reign. Daeron II isn't directly involved in the narrative, but his sons Baelor and Maekar feature prominently in its events. The second novella, <i>The Sworn Sword</i>, takes place a year and a half later, after the Great Spring Sickness struck Westeros and after Daeron II died from it. It is possible that Daeron II might appear in any potential live-action adaptation of the <i>Tales of Dunk and Egg</i>.
</p>
