<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Randyll Tarly is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Horn Hill","link":"Lord of Horn Hill","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Horn Hill]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Horn_Hill" title="Lord of Horn Hill">Lord of Horn Hill</a> and the head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tarly","link":"House Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/House_Tarly" title="House Tarly">House Tarly</a>, a vassal family of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tyrell","link":"House Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">House Tyrell</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Highgarden","link":"Highgarden","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Highgarden]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Highgarden" title="Highgarden">Highgarden</a>. He is a fierce, ruthless warrior and a highly effective general. He is the father of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Samwell Tarly","link":"Samwell Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Samwell Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a> and a younger son, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dickon","link":"Dickon Tarly","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dickon Tarly|Dickon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Dickon_Tarly" title="Dickon Tarly">Dickon</a>, as well as several daughters.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Along with his Tyrell overlords, Randyll fought on the Targaryen side in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>. He commanded the royalist victory at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Ashford","link":"Battle of Ashford","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Ashford]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ashford" title="Battle of Ashford">Battle of Ashford</a> - the only defeat that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> ever suffered in the entire war.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Samwell recounts to Jon Snow that even though he was his father's eldest son and heir, he was disgusted with Samwell's "womanly" interest in books and learning and disdain for martial valor and combat. Soon before Sam turned eighteen, his father took him aside and bluntly told him that he was unworthy to inherit the Tarly lands and titles. Randyll ordered Sam to take the black and join the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. Randyll told his son that if he did not, he would take him out on a hunt in the woods and kill him in such a way that it looked like an accident. Terrified, Sam did as he was bid and left <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Horn Hill","link":"Horn Hill","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Horn Hill]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Horn_Hill" title="Horn Hill">Horn Hill</a> for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Lord Randyll Tarly is considered one of the best battlefield commanders in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>, a general able to read the ground, weather conditions, and how a battle is progressing with impressive accuracy. During <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a> he inflicted Robert's only defeat while commanding the Tyrell vanguard at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Ashford","link":"Battle of Ashford","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Ashford]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ashford" title="Battle of Ashford">Battle of Ashford</a>. During the later stages of the rebellion he helped Lord Mace Tyrell <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"besiege","link":"Siege of Storm's End","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Siege of Storm's End|besiege]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Storm's_End" title="Siege of Storm's End">besiege</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a>, and bent the knee after the death of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a>. He wields the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian steel","link":"Valyrian steel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian steel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Valyrian_steel" title="Valyrian steel">Valyrian steel</a> sword Heartsbane in battle and is said to be utterly merciless to his foes. Although a ruthless and stern man, he is not outright sadistic, often dispensing strict justice that is nonetheless within the bounds of the law.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, Lord Randyll commits himself to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly Baratheon","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a>'s cause, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> encounters him when she visits Renly's camp to treat for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark|Robb]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb</a>. When House Tyrell switches its allegiance to the Lannisters following Renly's death, most of their bannermen join them (except for essentially just <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Florent","link":"House Florent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Florent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/House_Florent" title="House Florent">House Florent</a>): Lord Randyll follows his liege-lords, and House Tarly begins to fight on the Lannister side.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lord Randyll treated <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brienne of Tarth","link":"Brienne of Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brienne of Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Brienne_of_Tarth" title="Brienne of Tarth">Brienne of Tarth</a> with open hostility while she was at Renly's camp. When he found out that several men laid wagers on the first to claim her maidenhead, he put a stop to it, but told Brienne offensively it was her fault because to his opinion women have no place on the battlefield.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, while <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roose Bolton","link":"Roose Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roose Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Roose_Bolton" title="Roose Bolton">Roose Bolton</a> was in charge of the eastern half of the Northern armies based at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>, he sent a large force of three thousand men (about a third of his command) east to attack the Crownlands. Roose had decided to betray the Starks by this point and was laying the groundwork for the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a> and his subsequent takeover of the North, by intentionally killing off Stark loyalists by sending them on suicide missions. Coordinating with the Lannisters in secret, he sent his men into a trap set by a massive combined Lannister-Tyrell army, with Gregor Clegane commanding the Lannister forces and Randyll Tarly commanding the Tyrell forces. The entire Northern force was destroyed at the resulting Battle of Duskendale, all three thousand men, though a few Northern lords were taken prisoner.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Subsequently, Lord Randyll marches his army north from Duskendale to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maidenpool","link":"Maidenpool","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maidenpool]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-147822345856cca5f633cd6" href="/wiki/Maidenpool" title="Maidenpool">Maidenpool</a> (east of Harrenhal, in the northeasternmost corner of the Riverlands) and easily takes the town. Unlike Gregor's Lannister men, who are more concerned with pillaging the already ravaged countryside, Randyll has his forces in Maidenpool set about fixing damage the town suffered in the war and reimposing law and order. He is very strict but not abusive, executing confirmed bandits and deserters who were raiding the smallfolk of the countryside, in order to discourage other outlaws from making further unrest.
</p>
