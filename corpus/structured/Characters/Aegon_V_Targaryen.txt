<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Aegon V Targaryen</b>, also called <b>Aegon the Unlikely</b> and informally known as <b>Egg</b>, is an unseen character in <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Game of Thrones","link":"Game of Thrones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Game of Thrones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. He is long dead by the time the series begins.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aegon V was the fifth king of his name to sit on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> and the fifteenth king of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen|Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">Targaryen</a> dynasty to rule the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. He was the father of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mad King","link":"Mad King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mad King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Mad_King" title="Mad King" class="mw-redirect">Mad King</a> and his sister-wife Queen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaella","link":"Rhaella Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaella Targaryen|Rhaella]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Rhaella_Targaryen" title="Rhaella Targaryen">Rhaella</a>, the grandfather of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"Rhaegar Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar Targaryen|Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Targaryen</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys","link":"Viserys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys Targaryen|Viserys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> and the younger brother of Maester <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aemon Targaryen","link":"Aemon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aemon|Aemon Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Aemon" title="Aemon">Aemon Targaryen</a>. He was dubbed "the Unlikely" because he was the fourth son of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maekar Targaryen","link":"Maekar Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maekar Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Maekar_Targaryen" title="Maekar Targaryen">Maekar Targaryen</a>, a fourth son of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daeron II Targaryen","link":"Daeron II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daeron II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Daeron_II_Targaryen" title="Daeron II Targaryen">Daeron II Targaryen</a> and thus was highly unlikely to have inherited the crown.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Dunk and Egg.png|thumb|right||Aegon riding alongside Ser [[Duncan the Tall]] in his youth]]","title":"Dunk_and_Egg.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Dunk and Egg","align":"right","caption":"Aegon riding alongside Ser [[Duncan the Tall]] in his youth","captionParsed":"Aegon riding alongside Ser <a _rte_dataidx=\"0013\" href=\"\/wiki\/Duncan_the_Tall\" title=\"Duncan the Tall\">Duncan the Tall<\/a> in his youth"}}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" alt="" src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/81/Dunk_and_Egg.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20150303031337" width="180" height="101" class="image thumb alignRight withCaption" data-image-name="Dunk and Egg.png" data-image-key="Dunk_and_Egg.png"  type="image" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->Unlike most of their forebears, Aegon and his brothers were not born out of marriage between brother and sister. Their father and grandfather respectively married <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dyanna","link":"Dyanna Dayne","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dyanna Dayne|Dyanna]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Dyanna_Dayne" title="Dyanna Dayne">Dyanna</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Dayne","link":"House Dayne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Dayne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/House_Dayne" title="House Dayne">House Dayne</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myriah","link":"Myriah Martell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myriah Martell|Myriah]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Myriah_Martell" title="Myriah Martell">Myriah</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Martell","link":"House Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/House_Martell" title="House Martell">House Martell</a>.  Aegon was not originally expected to succeed his father to the throne, but after his eldest brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerion","link":"Aerion Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerion Targaryen|Aerion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Aerion_Targaryen" title="Aerion Targaryen">Aerion</a> died, and his next elder brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aemon","link":"Aemon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aemon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Aemon" title="Aemon">Aemon</a> refused the throne due to his vows as a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maester","link":"Maesters","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maesters|maester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">maester</a>, the crown passed to him. Aemon then joined the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> to remove himself from the royal court, where he might be used as a political tool against his brother.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Starting as a child, Aegon V began an odd friendship with the hedge knight Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duncan the Tall","link":"Duncan the Tall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duncan the Tall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Duncan_the_Tall" title="Duncan the Tall">Duncan the Tall</a>. He became Duncan's squire, and they had many adventures across Westeros. Eventually, after Aegon V came to the throne he named Ser Duncan to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>, and in time he became its Lord Commander.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After ruling for many years Aegon V ultimately died in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tragedy at Summerhall","link":"Tragedy at Summerhall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tragedy at Summerhall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Tragedy_at_Summerhall" title="Tragedy at Summerhall">Tragedy at Summerhall</a>: he attempted to hatch new <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragon","link":"Dragons","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragons|dragon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragon</a> eggs using fire and sorcery, but it caused a fire which spread out of control and destroyed the entire palace. Aegon V died in the conflagration, and was succeeded by his son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Dragons (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Aegon V Targaryen is a major character in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a>'s <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","link":"Tales of Dunk and Egg","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tales of Dunk and Egg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Tales_of_Dunk_and_Egg" title="Tales of Dunk and Egg">Tales of Dunk and Egg</a></i> prequel novellas, which are set around ninety years before the events of <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aegon, using the alias "Egg", spent many years traveling the Seven Kingdoms as a squire in the service of Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duncan the Tall","link":"Duncan the Tall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duncan the Tall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Duncan_the_Tall" title="Duncan the Tall">Duncan the Tall</a>, a hedge knight. At their first meeting Duncan was unaware of Egg's true identity, until he used his royal authority to save Duncan from being killed by his bullying older brother, Prince Aerion. Duncan was able to instead face Aerion on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"tournament","link":"tournament","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[tournament]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Tournament" title="Tournament">tournament</a> field and defeat him in combat. Unfortunately, Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baelor \"Breakspear\" Targaryen","link":"Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)|Baelor \"Breakspear\" Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Baelor_Targaryen_(son_of_Daeron_II)" title="Baelor Targaryen (son of Daeron II)">Baelor "Breakspear" Targaryen</a>, the royal heir, was killed during the tournament. Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maekar","link":"Maekar Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maekar Targaryen|Maekar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Maekar_Targaryen" title="Maekar Targaryen">Maekar</a> - Aegon's father - despaired of Aerion's spoiled indolence and agreed to let Aegon serve as Duncan's squire to build character and gain experience of living in the "real" world.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aegon ascended to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> in 233 AL, following his father Maekar's death in battle with an outlaw lord. Of Aegon's three older brothers, Aerion had poisoned himself to death (by ill-advisedly drinking <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildfire","link":"wildfire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildfire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a>, thinking it might turn him into a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragon","link":"dragon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon" class="mw-redirect">dragon</a>), Daeron had died of a pox and Aemon had disinherited himself by first joining the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maesters","link":"maesters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[maesters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">maesters</a> and later the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. As Aegon V Targaryen, he ruled for twenty-six years. During this time he made Ser Duncan the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander","link":"Lord Commander","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander" title="Lord Commander">Lord Commander</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a> and secured an alliance between the Targaryens and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a> by wedding his daughter Rhaelle to the then-Lord of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a> (she became the grandmother of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon|Robert]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon|Stannis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly Baratheon","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a>, giving the Baratheons their claim to the Iron Throne). He had three sons, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duncan","link":"Duncan Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duncan Targaryen|Duncan]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Duncan_Targaryen" title="Duncan Targaryen">Duncan</a> (often called Duncan the Small), Jaehaerys, and Daeron, as well as his two daughters Shaera and Rhaelle.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Being the fourth son of a fourth son, Aegon V was known as "the Unlikely" because he was unlikely to have inherited the crown. Actually, given that two of his uncles had twin sons, Aegon was initially <i>twelfth</i> in line to the throne when he was born (during the reign of his grandfather Daeron II). Even after his father was crowned he was still fourth in line for many years.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aegon V ruled wisely and well, but he had three major weaknesses. The first was that due to his years travelling across Westeros with Ser Duncan, he gained a great concern for the welfare of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Smallfolk","link":"Smallfolk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Smallfolk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Smallfolk" title="Smallfolk">Smallfolk</a>, yet opinions about this varied. Commoners remember him as a great king who was genuinely concerned about their well-being, who ensured their economic livelihoods by raising taxes on aristocrats and wealthy merchants, and who punished lords who abused the rights of the commoners they ruled over. The nobility of the Seven Kingdoms, meanwhile, grew to resent Aegon V - for exactly the same reasons.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Second, Aegon V indulged his sons: having married for love himself, he permitted his sons to do the same, although this was not politically wise and made him enemies within the Seven Kingdoms. The nobility was already growing to resent him for his liberal policies with the smallfolk, and a marriage-alliance with one of the Great Houses would have helped to stabilize Targaryen rule. Duncan the Small famously abdicated his place in the line of succession to marry a commoner, Jenny of Oldstones (a story which has become a favorite theme of romantic songs, which gave <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa Stark","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> an idealized vision of courtly love). Shaera was supposed to marry <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Luthor Tyrell","link":"Luthor Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Luthor Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Luthor_Tyrell" title="Luthor Tyrell">Luthor Tyrell</a>, Jaehaerys was supposed to marry a Tully girl, and Daeron was supposed to marry the young <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Olenna Redwyne","link":"Olenna Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Olenna Tyrell|Olenna Redwyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Olenna_Tyrell" title="Olenna Tyrell">Olenna Redwyne</a>. Aegon V was determined to put an end to the incestuous marriage practices of the Targaryens but this only seemed to encourage a forbidden romance between Jaehaerys and Shaera, who eloped and married without his permission. Daeron, a splendid and brave knight, was secretly a homosexual, and did not want to marry Olenna either. Thus after his two older brothers broke their betrothals he broke his as well, saying that his father could not rightfully force him marry against his will when he had already relented for his two elder brothers. Olenna Redwyne was then free to marry Lord Luthor, becoming Olenna Tyrell.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Third, Aegon V also had an overwhelming obsession with hatching the surviving Targaryen dragon eggs. He encountered so much resistance from the Great Houses over the years trying to enact reforms that would benefit the smallfolk that he came to believe that the only way to bring them all in line again was to enforce royal power with new dragons. These last two weaknesses culminated in 259 AL at the so-called "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tragedy at Summerhall","link":"Tragedy at Summerhall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tragedy at Summerhall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Tragedy_at_Summerhall" title="Tragedy at Summerhall">Tragedy at Summerhall</a>". This was a fire that got out of control and destroyed the castle, killing Aegon V, Ser Duncan the Tall, and Prince Duncan the Small. On the same night, Aegon's great-grandson <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegar Targaryen","link":"Rhaegar Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar Targaryen</a> was born. Aegon V was succeeded by his son Jaehaerys II.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">For the TV series, the Targaryen lineage was simplified. Aegon V's younger son Jaehaerys, who inherited the throne as Jaehaerys II and ruled for three years before dying of grief, was eliminated from the line of succession and the Mad King, Aerys II, was instead made Aegon V's younger son directly. This makes Aegon V the grandfather of Rhaegar, Viserys, and Daenerys Targaryen, and Maester Aemon their great-uncle.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">So far, it has not been confirmed in the series if Aegon V's daughter Rhaelle did marry into House Baratheon, giving the Baratheons their claim to the throne in the TV series as well as in the books, or if the entire progeny of Aegon V has been replaced with just Aerys and Rhaella. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"HBO","link":"HBO","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[HBO]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> Viewer's Guide confirms the existence of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duncan Targaryen","link":"Duncan Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duncan Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Duncan_Targaryen" title="Duncan Targaryen">Duncan Targaryen</a> in the TV show (making him Aerys and Rhaella's brother instead of their uncle), though in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Complete Guide to Westeros","link":"Complete Guide to Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Complete Guide to Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Complete_Guide_to_Westeros" descent from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Orys Baratheon","link":"Orys Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Orys Baratheon]]"}" I Targaryen","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32013784656cca5e67c444" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" </p>
