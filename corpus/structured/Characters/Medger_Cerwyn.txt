<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roose Bolton","link":"Roose Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roose Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Roose_Bolton" title="Roose Bolton">Roose Bolton</a> sends his recently legitimized bastard son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ramsay","link":"Ramsay Bolton","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ramsay Bolton|Ramsay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Ramsay_Bolton" title="Ramsay Bolton">Ramsay</a> to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Cerwyn","link":"Castle Cerwyn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Cerwyn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Castle_Cerwyn" title="Castle Cerwyn">Castle Cerwyn</a> to collect taxes from his new vassals. Medger, however, refuses to pay; proudly professing his loyalty to House Stark, and denouncing Bolton as a turncloak. For his defiance, Ramsay has Medger flayed alive, along with his wife, and brother, all the while forcing his son to watch the ordeal. When Ramsay relays these events to his father he casually remarks that the new Lord Cerwyn paid his taxes.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[High Sparrow (episode)|High Sparrow]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Lord Medger Cerwyn was among the Northern lords that rode south with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> and participated in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>. He was part of the Northern host that participated in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Green Fork","link":"Battle of the Green Fork","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Green Fork]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Green_Fork" title="Battle of the Green Fork">Battle of the Green Fork</a> and got captured and taken to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> unsuccessfully offers to trade Lord Medger and Ser Donnel Locke for Tion Frey in an exchange of prisoners. Medger suffers from a serious wound taken during the Battle of the Green Fork and never leaves his bed during his stay at Harrenhal, where he is kept while waiting for a prisoner exchange. He eventually dies of his wounds. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> sees <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Silent Sisters","link":"Silent Sisters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Silent Sisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Silent_Sisters" title="Silent Sisters">Silent Sisters</a> take his remains, his body in a cloak of silk decorated with the battle-axe of House Cerwyn.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While Ramsay Bolton did not kill Medger in the novels, he did kill his son Cley Cerwyn, at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of Winterfell","link":"Sack of Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Sack_of_Winterfell" title="Sack of Winterfell">Sack of Winterfell</a>. When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rodrik Cassel","link":"Rodrik Cassel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rodrik Cassel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Rodrik_Cassel" title="Rodrik Cassel">Rodrik Cassel</a> hastily assembled a force to besiege <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Theon Greyjoy","link":"Theon Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Theon Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-187350205456cca64f7ac78" href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> in Winterfell, composed of about 2,000 men from the few household soldiers left in the North, 300 of them were Cerwyn men led by Cley. Ramsay then arrived with 600 Bolton men, whom Rodrik and his lieutenants welcomed as their allies - but as soon as they were in the camp Ramsay's men betrayed the other Northern bannermen in a surprise attack, taking them totally off-guard. When Rodrik walked up to greet Ramsay by extending his hand in friendship, Ramsay quickly drew his sword and cut off Rodrik's outstretched arm at the elbow. Cley Cerwyn was killed in the betrayal by an arrow to the eye.
</p>
