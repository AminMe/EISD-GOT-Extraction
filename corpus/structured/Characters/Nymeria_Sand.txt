<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Nymeria Sand is the second of the eight <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bastard","link":"Bastardy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bastardy|bastard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Bastardy" title="Bastardy">bastard</a> daughters of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Prince","link":"Prince","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prince]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Prince" title="Prince">Prince</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Oberyn Martell","link":"Oberyn Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Oberyn Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Oberyn_Martell" title="Oberyn Martell">Oberyn Martell</a>, known as the "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sand Snakes","link":"Sand Snakes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sand Snakes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Sand_Snakes" title="Sand Snakes">Sand Snakes</a>". Nymeria's mother was an <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eastern noblewoman","link":"Yi Ti","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yi Ti|Eastern noblewoman]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Yi_Ti" title="Yi Ti">Eastern noblewoman</a>, who taught her how to use a whip in combat, but who later died in battle.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.telegraphindia.com\/1150329\/jsp\/t2\/story_11347.jsp#.VR6xovnF9Cj]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Nymeria became very skilled with using her 8 foot long bullwhip in combat.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.hungertv.com\/feature\/game-of-thrones-newbies-are-coming-jessica-henwick\/]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Nymeria inherited her father's cunning, and is the most observant and calculating of the Sand Snakes. Instead of charging head-on into a situation relying on brute strength (like her older sister Obara), she will stand back and observe her enemy's weak points, then move in precisely for an accurate strike.  In this, she is actually more like her uncle, Doran Martell, than like her father. Her choice of a bullwhip as her primary weapon exemplifies this mentality.  She hones her reflexes by playing a sparring game with her younger sister Tyene, in which one slaps the other's hands before they can move them; Nymeria claims she has never lost this game.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.telegraphindia.com\/1150329\/jsp\/t2\/story_11347.jsp#.VR6xovnF9Cj]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Nymeria dresses more femininely than her martial sister Obara, though she is no less deadly. Nymeria has long flowing hair, and alluring Dornish-style dresses.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/nypost.com\/2015\/04\/06\/meet-the-newest-badass-beauties-of-game-of-thrones\/]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">She is named after <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Nymeria","link":"Nymeria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Nymeria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Nymeria" title="Nymeria">Nymeria</a>, the warrior-queen of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> who led her people's migration to Dorne one thousand years ago. The original Nymeria unified Dorne for the first time, and is considered the founder of House Martell in its modern form. "Nymeria" is a popular name in Dorne, much as "Brandon" is in the North, after their founder <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brandon the Builder","link":"Brandon Stark (the Builder)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brandon Stark (the Builder)|Brandon the Builder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Brandon_Stark_(the_Builder)" title="Brandon Stark (the Builder)">Brandon the Builder</a>. Through her father Oberyn, Nymeria Sand is actually a direct descendant of the original Nymeria.
</p>

<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>"I think I had three auditions, originally for a different character. I met David and Dan, and really got along with them. The whole time I wanted Nymeria, and I was fighting for it. But they wanted a black actress, so I couldn't get an audition! Finally, I managed to talk my way in and I got the part!"<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.hungertv.com\/feature\/game-of-thrones-newbies-are-coming-jessica-henwick\/]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</dd></dl>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The fourth Sand Snake, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sarella Sand","link":"Sarella Sand","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sarella Sand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Sarella_Sand" title="Sarella Sand">Sarella Sand</a> - who doesn't appear in Season 5 - is black in the novels, as her mother is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Islander","link":"Summer Islands","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Islands|Summer Islander]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Summer_Islands" title="Summer Islands">Summer Islander</a> ship captain. An initial idea may have been to condense the two roles into one character.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Nymeria, often called Lady Nym, is Oberyn's daughter whom he fathered on a noblewoman from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a>. Like <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a>'s <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"direwolf","link":"Nymeria (direwolf)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Nymeria (direwolf)|direwolf]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Nymeria_(direwolf)" title="Nymeria (direwolf)">direwolf</a>, she is named after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"warrior-queen","link":"Nymeria","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Nymeria|warrior-queen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Nymeria" title="Nymeria">warrior-queen</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> who lived a thousand years ago. Through her father, Nymeria Sand is actually a direct descendant of the original Nymeria.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Nymeria is twenty-five years old, slim and slender as a willow, with straight black hair worn in a long braid which pulls back from a widow's peak. She has dark eyes, high cheekbones, and full lips. She is initially described as having pale white skin, but mentions her olive skin. Nymeria has all the beauty that her elder sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Obara Sand","link":"Obara Sand","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Obara Sand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Obara_Sand" title="Obara Sand">Obara Sand</a> lacks but is no less deadly. She usually can conceal a dozen daggers on her person and is extremely skilled with the blades.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The TV series version of "Nymeria" is a condensation of several different aspects of the eight "Sand Snakes" in the books. Nymeria is half-Volantene but has the classic dark <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> features that most members of House Martell have as "salty Dornishmen". Actress Jessica Henwick, however, while she does have many of the features specifically ascribed to Nymeria, was born to a Singaporean Chinese mother and a British father. Apparently, instead of taking that Nymeria's mother was "from the East" to mean "from the Free City of Volantis" as in the books, the TV series changed this to mean that her mother was from the far east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a>, probably from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yi Ti","link":"Yi Ti","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yi Ti]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Yi_Ti" title="Yi Ti">Yi Ti</a> (which is loosely analogous to Imperial China in the narrative).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Henwick's casting makes her the first major role in the TV series played by an East Asian actress (the first East Asian actor to appear at all was only in the Season 4 finale, a fleeting appearance by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Alice Hewkin","link":"Alice Hewkin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Alice Hewkin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Alice_Hewkin" title="Alice Hewkin">Alice Hewkin</a> as a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Child of the Forest","link":"Child of the Forest 2 (The Children)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Child of the Forest 2 (The Children)|Child of the Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Child_of_the_Forest_2_(The_Children)" title="Child of the Forest 2 (The Children)">Child of the Forest</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"cave of the three-eyed raven","link":"cave of the three-eyed raven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[cave of the three-eyed raven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Cave_of_the_three-eyed_raven" title="Cave of the three-eyed raven">cave of the three-eyed raven</a> - and there was also a one-shot appearance by an East Asian <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Priestess","link":"Red Priestess (High Sparrow)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Priestess (High Sparrow)|Red Priestess]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-155282297256cca5dd7a9c1" href="/wiki/Red_Priestess_(High_Sparrow)" title="Red Priestess (High Sparrow)">Red Priestess</a> in Season 5's "High Sparrow"). This would seem to be a step <i>away</i> from the TV series's previous tendency to use Volantis as their go-to reference for the eastern lands (i.e. changing "Jeyne Westerling" to be "Talisa Maegyr of Volantis").
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, Nymeria mentions that she was "abed with the Fowler twins" when word arrived of her father's death. Jeyne and Jennelyn Fowler are identical twin sisters from House Fowler, stony Dornishmen from the Red Mountains of the west. Nymeria is close friends with them, and they seem to be on good terms with Arianne and Quentyn as well - though Doran considers the twins to be gossips who cannot be trusted to keep a secret. Doran does trust Nymeria, but at times he will withhold sensitive information from her out of fear that the Fowler twins will somehow discover it through her. It is not clear what exactly was meant by Nymeria being "abed" with the Fowler twins: while many noblewomen in Dorne openly have sexual relations with other women, throughout all of Westeros it is common for girls from noble houses to share the same large bed together in their chambers.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the beginning of <i>A Feast for Crows</i>, Nymeria is the second of the Sand Snakes who confronts Prince Doran about his failure to avenge their father. She joins his retinue on the way to Sunspear. She is aware of Obara's plan to declare a war against the Lannisters, but has a different plan: she and Tyene will assassinate Tywin, Cersei, Jaime and Tommen. She says that if Doran had been the one to be killed, Oberyn would have led the Dornish troops north before his corpse was cold. She warns Doran that she and her sisters will not wait seventeen years for their vengeance, and leaves. After Doran arrives at Sunspear, he takes steps to make certain Nymeria and the other Sand Snakes will not ignite a war.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Ser Balon Swann arrives at Dorne to take Myrcella back, Doran releases his nieces. He orders Nymeria to travel to King's Landing with Myrcella and Trystane, where she will take her father's seat at the Small Council. Nymeria swears that she will serve her uncle.  The TV series significantly changed these events, specifically having Doran send Trystane instead of Nymeria.
</p>
