<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Brynden Tully is the younger brother of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hoster Tully","link":"Hoster Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hoster Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Hoster_Tully" title="Hoster Tully">Hoster Tully</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Riverrun","link":"Lord of Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Riverrun" title="Lord of Riverrun">Lord of Riverrun</a> and head of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tully","link":"House Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/House_Tully" title="House Tully">House Tully</a>, and the uncle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Edmure Tully","link":"Edmure Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Edmure Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Edmure_Tully" title="Edmure Tully">Edmure Tully</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lysa Arryn","link":"Lysa Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lysa Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Lysa_Arryn" title="Lysa Arryn">Lysa Arryn</a>. Brynden is a respected knight and formidable battle commander, who first distinguished himself in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","link":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Ninepenny Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings" title="War of the Ninepenny Kings">War of the Ninepenny Kings</a>, nearly forty years before the War of the Five Kings.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Brynden often argued with his older brother Hoster, particularly over the fact that Brynden never married. When Brynden returned home from the War of the Ninepenny Kings, Hoster had already arranged for him to marry <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bethany Redwyne","link":"Bethany Redwyne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bethany Redwyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Bethany_Redwyne" title="Bethany Redwyne">Bethany Redwyne</a>, of the wealthy and powerful <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Redwyne","link":"House Redwyne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Redwyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/House_Redwyne" title="House Redwyne">House Redwyne</a>. Brynden refused this generous match, however, refusing to be told when and whom to marry. Hoster was angry that Brynden would not do his duty to their family and enter into a marriage-alliance to improve their fortunes, and said that he was the black goat of the Tully herd. Brynden joked that given that the Tully sigil is a trout, it would be more fitting to say that he was the "black fish" of the Tully school. The nickname stuck, and Ser Brynden became popularly known as "The Blackfish" ever since. Brynden even took to using a black fish in his own <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"personal heraldry","link":"Heraldry","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Heraldry|personal heraldry]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">personal heraldry</a>: like the standard Tully heraldry it uses a red and blue background, but swaps out the normal silver trout for a black one. Hoster would attempt to contract other arranged marriage-alliances for Brynden over the years, many of them very promising matches, but Brynden turned down them all, which led to constant bickering between the two brothers throughout their lives.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Despite his disagreements with his brother over his refusal to marry, Brynden is very devoted to his family.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Brynden Tully was announced as appearing in <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Game of Thrones","link":"Game of Thrones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Game of Thrones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i> and was expected to debut in the second season based on comments by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a>. He debuted in the first novel, however his introduction to the TV series was pushed back due to time constraints. He was not cast for the first and second seasons, however, a press release confirmed he would be cast in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/winteriscoming.net\/2012\/05\/ew-scoop-on-s3-the-reeds-are-in-and-so-is-blackfish\/  Winter is Coming.net article on season 3 roles]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> He is played by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Clive Russell","link":"Clive Russell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Clive Russell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Clive_Russell" title="Clive Russell">Clive Russell</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/winteriscoming.net\/2012\/06\/clive-russell-cast-as-the-blackfish\/ Clive Russell cast as the Blackfish]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">On the Season 3 Blu-ray, the Blackfish narrates <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Histories & Lore","link":"Histories & Lore","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Histories & Lore]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Histories_&_Lore" title="Histories &amp; Lore">Histories &amp; Lore</a> videos on <b>"<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tully","link":"House Tully (Histories & Lore)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tully (Histories & Lore)|House Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/House_Tully_(Histories_&_Lore)" title="House Tully (Histories &amp; Lore)">House Tully</a>"</b> and <b>"<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Riverlands","link":"The Riverlands (Histories & Lore)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Riverlands (Histories & Lore)|The Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands_(Histories_&_Lore)" title="The Riverlands (Histories &amp; Lore)">The Riverlands</a>"</b>. 
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Blackfish is a canny and respected warrior. He is tall and clean-shaven, and dons a red and blue <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tully","link":"House Tully","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tully|Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/House_Tully" title="House Tully">Tully</a> cape, like his nephew Edmure. Brynden possessed the distinctive auburn hair of the Tully family in his youth, but it has now gone mostly grey.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Brynden is an experienced battlefield commander. He rose to particular distinction during the War of the Ninepenny Kings which was fought over the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a>, in which he fought alongside Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a>. The young <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> highly revered him and was eager to hear of his exploits in the war.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Brynden took up the nickname "the Blackfish", after he refused to marry Bethany Redwyne, which would have advanced the family's fortunes. This led to years of bitter quarrels and estrangement with his brother Hoster. After one such quarrel with his elder brother, when Lord Hoster called him the "black goat of the Tully herd", Ser Brynden pointed out that their sigil was a fish, so he took the name "Blackfish", and changed the color scheme of his personal sigil to a jumping black trout (which is normally silver in the Tully sigil).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">So far, the books have never made exactly clear why Brynden has never married, i.e. if he loved a woman who did not return his attraction, or if his brother refused to allow him to marry anyone else if he wouldn't go through with the arranged marriage to Bethany Redwyne. As the younger brother of Hoster, Lord Paramount of the Riverlands, Brynden was certainly one of the more eligible bachelors in Westeros. This has led to some fan speculation that Brynden may be a homosexual, and that this in-part caused the rift with his older brother when he refused to take part in a marriage-alliance he had brokered, but so far the books have given no particular hint that this is the case. Others speculate that Brynden may have loved Minisa Whent, but after she married his older brother Hoster, he refused to ever love again. Whatever the reason why Brynden never married, it is something of an open secret among his immediate family members: at one point Catelyn comments in passing to Edmure, regarding Brynden's disagreements with her father, that they both know why he never married.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels he is first encountered serving in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Vale of Arryn","link":"the Vale of Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Vale of Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale of Arryn</a>, where he serves his niece, the Lady Lysa, following the death of her husband, as the Knight of the Gate, who commands the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bloody Gate","link":"Bloody Gate","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bloody Gate]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Bloody_Gate" title="Bloody Gate">Bloody Gate</a>. After Catelyn asks for his assistance and Lysa refuses to send aid after the Lannisters invade the Riverlands, Brynden resigns from his post and agrees to return to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverlands","link":"Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Riverlands" title="Riverlands" class="mw-redirect">Riverlands</a> and help in the growing struggle against the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannisters","link":"Lannisters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lannisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Lannisters" title="Lannisters" class="mw-redirect">Lannisters</a>. In the TV series, some of Brynden's activities in the Vale have been taken over by Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vardis Egen","link":"Vardis Egen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vardis Egen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Vardis_Egen" title="Vardis Egen">Vardis Egen</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Along with Edmure, he becomes one of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s most trusted lieutenants, his "eyes and ears". Theon Greyjoy references the Blackfish's talent for battle and strategy.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Blackfish is not present during the events of the Red Wedding because Robb had left him holding Riverrun as "Warden of the Southern Marches", overseeing the defenses of the Stark-loyal territories of the Riverlands, and protecting his Queen, Jeyne Westerling (the book's counterpart of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Talisa Maegyr","link":"Talisa Maegyr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Talisa Maegyr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-189086206756cca5b1131a2" href="/wiki/Talisa_Maegyr" title="Talisa Maegyr" class="mw-redirect">Talisa Maegyr</a>), who also remained at Riverrun.
</p>
