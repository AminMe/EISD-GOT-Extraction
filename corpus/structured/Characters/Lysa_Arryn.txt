</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Lysa Arryn</b>, née <b>Tully</b>, is a recurring character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"first","link":"Season 1","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1|first]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fourth","link":"Season 4","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4|fourth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">fourth</a> seasons. She is played by guest star <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kate Dickie","link":"Kate Dickie","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kate Dickie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Kate_Dickie" title="Kate Dickie">Kate Dickie</a> and debuted in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Wolf and the Lion","link":"The Wolf and the Lion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Wolf and the Lion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/The_Wolf_and_the_Lion" title="The Wolf and the Lion">The Wolf and the Lion</a>." Lysa was the Lady Regent of the Vale, ruling on behalf of her young son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robin Arryn","link":"Robin Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robin Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Robin_Arryn" title="Robin Arryn">Robin Arryn</a>. She doted on her son and pursued a policy of isolationism following the death of her husband <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Arryn","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>. She presided over the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"trial","link":"First Trial by Combat of Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Trial by Combat of Tyrion Lannister|trial]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/First_Trial_by_Combat_of_Tyrion_Lannister" title="First Trial by Combat of Tyrion Lannister">trial</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> for the attempted murder of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bran Stark","link":"Bran Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bran Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a>. He proves his innocence in a trial by combat. Lysa refused to commit her forces to aid her sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In reality, however, Lysa is one of the true orchestrators of the war, having conspired with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a>, whom she is madly in love with, to assassinate Jon and frame the Lannisters in order to provoke a war between <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a> that devastates the Seven Kingdoms and leads to the deaths of thousands, among them her own sister and her nephew <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>. Though Lysa technically betrays her own family by doing this, she is ultimately betrayed herself by Baelish, who pushes her out of the Moon Door and usurps her rule over the Vale once she is of no further use to him.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, Lysa is Catelyn's sister, who is distrustful of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannisters","link":"Lannisters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lannisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Lannisters" title="Lannisters" class="mw-redirect">Lannisters</a>. She dotes on her son excessively. In the books her son's name is "Robert Arryn", but this was changed for the TV series to avoid confusion with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>. Lysa married Lord Jon Arryn to tie three of the Houses involved in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a> - the Starks, Tullys, and Arryns - more closely together. Given the large age difference between her and Jon, their marriage was not a happy one.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">She is noted as overweight in the books, as a result of suffering two stillbirths and four miscarriages. The stress from these many pregnancies which ended in tragedy, combined with her distant relationship with her elderly husband, wore heavily on Lysa's mental state. This is also why she dotes so excessively on Sweetrobin, her only surviving child.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In her youth, Lysa fell in love with Littlefinger, but her feelings were not reciprocated. One night Petyr tried to kiss Catelyn, and she rejected him, laughing. Petyr got drunk because of this, and Lysa climbed into his bed that night to "give him comfort". Petyr took her virginity that night, telling her he loved her, but called her "Cat" before falling asleep. Lysa became pregnant with Petyr's child as a result of this. Her father forced her to abort the child instead, that he could marry her to Jon Arryn. The whole episode had great negative effect on Lysa's spirit.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lysa's marriage to Jon Arryn was very unhappy. She did not love the much older man she was forced to marry; she had five miscarriages, twice in the Eyrie and three at King's Landing; the only child who was born alive turned to be mentally and physically ill.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> sent letters to Lysa, asking her to let his army pass through the Vale and provide ships at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gulltown","link":"Gulltown","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gulltown]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Gulltown" title="Gulltown">Gulltown</a> to take them north. Had Lysa agreed, Robb could have landed at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Harbor","link":"White Harbor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Harbor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/White_Harbor" title="White Harbor">White Harbor</a>, flanked <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Moat Cailin","link":"Moat Cailin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Moat Cailin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Moat_Cailin" title="Moat Cailin">Moat Cailin</a> and driven the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ironmen","link":"Ironborn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ironborn|Ironmen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">Ironmen</a> from the north in half a year. But Lysa did not respond, and kept the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bloody Gate","link":"Bloody Gate","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bloody Gate]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Bloody_Gate" title="Bloody Gate">Bloody Gate</a> closed.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Lysa drags Sansa to the Moon Door, she blurts out the whole truth about her first pregnancy, the abortion, and Jon Arryn's death. The singer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Marillion","link":"Marillion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Marillion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Marillion" title="Marillion">Marillion</a> is present when Lysa struggles with Sansa (and he sings loudly to drown out Sansa's screams). Littlefinger appears and consoles her before telling her he only ever loved Catelyn (in the books, he says "<i>only Cat</i>" instead of "<i>your sister</i>"), shocking Lysa so much that when Littlefinger pushes her out she does not even scream but continues to look at him in disbelief as she falls. Littlefinger blames the murder on Marillion, who confesses after being tortured by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mord","link":"Mord","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mord]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Mord" title="Mord">Mord</a>. Since the singer is despised throughout the Vale for his mocking songs and obnoxious behavior, the Vale bannermen readily believe his guilt.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-71378552756cca5b593ca1" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Lysa Arryn" is pronounced "LIE-ssa AIR-in", as opposed to "Liss-ah" or "Lee-suh".
</p>
