<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Rhaenys is the daughter of Prince Rhaegar Targaryen and Elia Martell and died in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of King's Landing","link":"Sack of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Sack_of_King's_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">Sack of King's Landing</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Varys","link":"Varys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Varys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> talks about her with the imprisoned <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> as an example of how the innocent suffer in times of uprising. She was three or four years old when she died, and took after her mother in appearance with the classic Dornish features of black hair, dark eyes, and olive skin.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Rhaenys had a small black <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"kitten","link":"Cats","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cats|kitten]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Cats" title="Cats">kitten</a> she named "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Balerion","link":"Balerion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Balerion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Balerion" title="Balerion">Balerion</a>", after the mighty dragon of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen|Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon the Conqueror</a>. Varys commented about it sadly, "Rhaenys liked to pretend he was the true Balerion, the Black Dread of old, but I imagine the Lannisters taught her the difference between a kitten and a dragon quick enough, the day they broke down her door." No one knows what happened to the cat after she died: it is implied that it survived the downfall of the Targaryens and lived out the next fifteen years in the Red Keep during the reign of Robert Baratheon. When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> is ordered by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Syrio Forel","link":"Syrio Forel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Syrio Forel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Syrio_Forel" title="Syrio Forel">Syrio Forel</a> to chase cats around the Red Keep to train her in agility (which occurs in the TV series in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things","link":"Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Cripples,_Bastards_and_Broken_Things" title="Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things">Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things</a>") one of the cats she encounters is an incredibly old black cat who is mean to the entire Baratheon household, as well as the Lannisters. Once, when Tywin Lannister was visiting the Red Keep for a feast, the black cat jumped onto the table and snatched a roast quail right out of Tywin's fingers. This may very well be Rhaenys's cat Balerion, angered at the new residents of the castle who are responsible for the death of his original owner.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After King's Landing was seized by the Lannister forces, Rhaenys was found by Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Amory Lorch","link":"Amory Lorch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Amory Lorch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Amory_Lorch" title="Amory Lorch">Amory Lorch</a> hiding under the bed of her father Rhaegar. While <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gregor Clegane","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a> killed her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"baby brother","link":"Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)|baby brother]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Aegon_Targaryen_(Son_of_Rhaegar)" title="Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)">baby brother</a> and mother, Lorch killed Rhaenys by stabbing her about fifty times. When Robert arrived in the capital her body was presented at his feet, and it looked nearly hacked to pieces. Even <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister|Tywin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68199380356cca5fcd0a1d" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin</a> considered that deed overly brutal (not the murder itself, but the manner in which it was done). He asked Lorch why killing a two or three year old girl required so many thrusts, and Lorch answered that she kicked him and would not stop screaming. Tywin commented that if Lorch had half the brain a turnip has, he would have calmed Rhaenys with a few sweet words and used a soft silk pillow to smother her.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The TV series simplified events so that Gregor Clegane killed both of Elia's children, without the involvement of Amory Lorch.
</p>
