</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Edmure Tully</b> is a recurring character in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"third","link":"Season 3","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3|third]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">third</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sixth","link":"Season 6","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 6|sixth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Season_6" title="Season 6">sixth</a> seasons. He is played by guest star <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tobias Menzies","link":"Tobias Menzies","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tobias Menzies]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Tobias_Menzies" title="Tobias Menzies">Tobias Menzies</a> and debuts in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Walk of Punishment","link":"Walk of Punishment","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Walk of Punishment]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Walk_of_Punishment" title="Walk of Punishment">Walk of Punishment</a>". Edmure is the son and heir of the late Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hoster Tully","link":"Hoster Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hoster Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Hoster_Tully" title="Hoster Tully">Hoster Tully</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a>. He is the younger brother of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark|Catelyn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lysa","link":"Lysa Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lysa Arryn|Lysa]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Lysa_Arryn" title="Lysa Arryn">Lysa</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Edmure supports his nephew <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, commanding the Tully forces and becoming the Lord of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a> after his father's death. Edmure marries <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roslin Frey","link":"Roslin Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roslin Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Roslin_Frey" title="Roslin Frey">Roslin Frey</a> to secure an alliance with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Frey","link":"House Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/House_Frey" title="House Frey">House Frey</a> after Robb breaks his oath. During the marriage, Catelyn and Robb are murdered in the event known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a> and Edmure is imprisoned by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Walder Frey","link":"Walder Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Walder Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Walder_Frey" title="Walder Frey">Walder Frey</a>.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, Edmure appears at the very end of the first novel, but in the TV series his debut was pushed back to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a>. Edmure has the auburn Tully hair and beard and blue eyes. He is enthusiastic, outgoing and confident, which all help his skill as a commander, but is also prone to rash actions motivated by desire of personal glory and single-minded thought, Edmure is considered kind, and is shown to care deeply for his subjects.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Edmure-tully-wife.jpg|thumb|Edmure Tully with his new wife Roslin Frey before the horrors of the Red Wedding. ]]","title":"Edmure-tully-wife.jpg","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Edmure-tully-wife","caption":"Edmure Tully with his new wife Roslin Frey before the horrors of the Red Wedding. <!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->Edmure is unwed, because his father continued to chew over various offers for the best match. Since his father's descent into illness and infirmity, Edmure has ruled over Riverrun as <i>de facto</i> lord for some time. Walder Frey was also angry with him as he had repeatedly offered one daughter after another to Lord Hoster Tully for a marriage contract to Edmure.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Riverrun called up their bannermen and rose up when the Lannister forces started attacking their lands in retaliation for Catelyn Stark taking Tyrion prisoner. Edmure was captured when Jaime Lannister defeated his army at Golden Tooth, but he was subsequently freed when Robb Stark destroyed Jaime's army in the Battle of the Whispering Wood and lifted the siege of Riverrun.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After Eddard is executed and Robb's bannermen proclaimed him King in the North, Edmure and the Riverlands do as well, even though they were never part of the original northern kingdom. Robb Stark took most of his Northern army from Riverrun to invade the Lannister's home territory in the Westerlands, leaving Edmure behind to defend Riverrun and hold the line of the Red Fork of the Trident (using mostly the local forces of the Riverlords). Between their headquarters at Riverrun and Harrenhal, Edmure and Tywin Lannister's forces then engaged in numerous skirmishes which harried the Riverlands between the Red Fork and Gods Eye lake.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Edmure Tully is a middling knight and effective, but not outstanding, battle commander. This is not to say he is a complete incompetent, but he is not very well suited to be a wartime leader. However, Catelyn points out that he would have been hailed as a very skilled lord in peacetime, who genuinely cares for the commoners he rules over. When Jaime Lannister first marched towards Riverrun, Edmure allowed fleeing peasants to take refuge inside the castle, even though they would only be a drain on food resources during a siege. Even so, he insisted that they trusted him and he would not abandon them by shutting them out (contrast this with Cersei's orders to bar starving refugees from entering the gates of King's Landing). Despite this, Edmure does manage to win several local victories against Lannister forces.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ultimately Tywin attempted to force a crossing of the Red Fork to return to the Westerlands, but in heavy fighting was repulsed by Edmure's forces. Unfortunately, this ruined Robb's overall military strategy: Robb had invaded the Westerlands hoping to lure Tywin back into his home territory, and Edmure was supposed to <i>let</i> him cross back into the Westerlands, and offer only token resistance so Tywin wouldn't realize this was exactly what Robb wanted. Worse, when Tywin was repulsed he turned southeast, allowing him to follow the course of the Blackwater River and thus arrive at the Battle of the Blackwater just in time to decisively attack Stannis's army from behind. The combined error drastically weakened Robb's position in the war. In both the novels and the TV series, Robb curiously blames Edmure for not <i>anticipating</i> what his real strategy was (to lure Tywin into the west) before he actually told him about it - one of several command blunders by Robb, soon followed by executing <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rickard Karstark","link":"Rickard Karstark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rickard Karstark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153830501156cca5b3ecd3f" href="/wiki/Rickard_Karstark" title="Rickard Karstark">Rickard Karstark</a>, and disastrously choosing to break his marriage-alliance to the Freys.
</p>
