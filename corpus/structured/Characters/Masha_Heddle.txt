<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> and Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rodrik Cassel","link":"Rodrik Cassel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rodrik Cassel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Rodrik_Cassel" title="Rodrik Cassel">Rodrik Cassel</a> are stopping at the Crossroads Inn and are trying to be inconspicuous. The minstrel <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Marillion","link":"Marillion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Marillion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Marillion" title="Marillion">Marillion</a> is trying to make a good impression on them, which fails when he insults the North (not realizing they are Northerners). Masha Heddle tells <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> upon his arrival that they have no spare rooms, but Tyrion holds up a gold coin and asks if anyone has a room for him. The sellsword <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bronn","link":"Bronn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bronn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Bronn" title="Bronn">Bronn</a> says that Tyrion can have his room, and Tyrion throws him the coin. Marillion attracts Tyrion's attention to their table by offering to sing Tyrion songs of his father's war victories. Tyrion remarks that nothing would more likely ruin his appetite.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, Masha Heddle is a middle-aged fat woman, whose teeth have been turned blood-red due to her addiction to sourleaf. She has run the Crossroads Inn for more than twenty years. Catelyn remembers those red teeth being terrifying to her when she was young.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hot Pie","link":"Hot Pie","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hot Pie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Hot_Pie" title="Hot Pie">Hot Pie</a> ends up making bread for a different inn named the Inn of the Kneeling Man.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Masha Heddle was killed at the end of the first novel, when the Lannister armies overrun the Riverlands. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a> has her hanged from a gibbet for allowing Catelyn Stark to kidnap his son. Tyrion is quite pleased at this, although Masha was just an innocent bystander and didn't have any choice whether Catelyn took him prisoner or not. He laughs when he hears his father took the inn for his quarters, thinking "perhaps the gods were just after all". Looking at what remained of Masha's corpse, he says with a sigh of reproach "A room, a meal, and a flagon of wine, that was all I asked".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The inn is subsequently run by Masha's nephew. Masha's death hasn't been explicitly established in the TV series. In the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a> episode "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dark Wings, Dark Words","link":"Dark Wings, Dark Words","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dark Wings, Dark Words]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Dark_Wings,_Dark_Words" title="Dark Wings, Dark Words">Dark Wings, Dark Words</a>", <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brotherhood Without Banners","link":"Brotherhood Without Banners","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brotherhood Without Banners]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Brotherhood_Without_Banners" title="Brotherhood Without Banners">Brotherhood Without Banners</a> have a meal at the Inn at the Crossroads: in the books, they were much further south at a different inn known as the Inn of the Kneeling Man, located south of the Red Fork of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Trident","link":"Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Trident" title="Trident" class="mw-redirect">Trident</a> River. In the subsequent episode, "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Walk of Punishment","link":"Walk of Punishment","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Walk of Punishment]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Walk_of_Punishment" title="Walk of Punishment">Walk of Punishment</a>", <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hot Pie","link":"Hot Pie","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hot Pie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-49200517556cca78b8962f" href="/wiki/Hot_Pie" title="Hot Pie">Hot Pie</a> stays behind at the inn to bake bread, because as a former baker's apprentice in King's Landing, he impressed the innkeeper with his skills. This happens with the innkeeper of the Inn of the Kneeling Man in the books. However, Hot Pie only says that "the innkeeper" was impressed by his baking skills: this doesn't necessarily mean that Masha is still alive in the TV continuity, as Hot Pie doesn't state what the specific name of "the innkeeper" is.
</p>
