<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Tanda Stokeworth is the Lady of Stokeworth. She is very old, and known to not be a very bright or attractive woman.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tanda is the head of House Stokeworth in the novels.  When House Stokeworth was first mentioned in the TV series in Season 4's "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mockingbird","link":"Mockingbird","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mockingbird]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Mockingbird" title="Mockingbird">Mockingbird</a>", a point of some confusion was that Tyrion said that Lollys's older sister would inherit Castle Stokeworth after their <i>father</i> died.  When Lollys first appeared on-screen in Season 5, however, she made it clear that her older sister will inherit the castle after their <i>mother</i> dies.  It is possible that the TV writers thought that having a female head of a major House would be confusing and require some sort of lengthy explanation, so they simply pushed back the explanation about Lollys's mother being the actual head of the House to Season 5.  It can easily be explained that Tyrion was simply mistaken or misspoke.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the reign of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II","link":"Aerys II","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Aerys_II" title="Aerys II" class="mw-redirect">Aerys II</a>, Manly Stokeworth was Commander of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"City Watch","link":"City Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[City Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">City Watch</a>. He was succeeded in the position by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Janos Slynt","link":"Janos Slynt","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Janos Slynt]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Janos_Slynt" title="Janos Slynt">Janos Slynt</a>. Littlefinger remarked that the widowed Lady Tanda has no love for the Lannisters: it is quite possible that Manly was in fact her husband, and that he died during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of King's Landing","link":"Sack of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Sack_of_King's_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">Sack of King's Landing</a> committed by Tywin Lannister's army.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Unable to find a husband for her daughter Lollys, she made several attempts to convince Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a> to marry Lollys by serving him extravagant meals. Littlefinger had no intention of marrying Lollys, finding her to be bad company as well as overweight and unattractive. She later makes similar attempts with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a>, with him repeatedly trying to avoid the invitations.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Once the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a> breaks out, Tanda and Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gyles Rosby","link":"Gyles Rosby","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gyles Rosby]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Gyles_Rosby" title="Gyles Rosby">Gyles Rosby</a> provide half of the food still in arriving <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> as their lands lie close to the city to the north and are still untouched by war.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Along with her daughters, Tanda attends several significant events in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> such as King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey Baratheon","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey Baratheon</a>'s nameday celebration, Princess <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myrcella Baratheon","link":"Myrcella Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myrcella Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Myrcella_Baratheon" title="Myrcella Baratheon">Myrcella Baratheon</a>'s departure for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> (after which the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riot of King's Landing","link":"Riot of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riot of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Riot_of_King's_Landing" title="Riot of King's Landing">Riot of King's Landing</a> takes place, in which Lollys is gang raped and impregnated) and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Purple Wedding","link":"Purple Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Purple Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Purple_Wedding" title="Purple Wedding">Purple Wedding</a>. During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Blackwater","link":"Battle of the Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a>, they take shelter with the other women in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maegor's Holdfast","link":"Maegor's Holdfast","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maegor's Holdfast]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Maegor's_Holdfast" title="Maegor's Holdfast">Maegor's Holdfast</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After the death of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a>, Tanda suggests naming Lollys's child after him, as she claims to have admired him above all other men. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cersei Lannister","link":"Cersei Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cersei Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a> is disgusted by the notion that he father's name would be given to a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bastard","link":"Bastardy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bastardy|bastard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-58553265356cca77e5bb2e" href="/wiki/Bastardy" title="Bastardy">bastard</a> born of rape.  This subplot was omitted from the TV series, in which Lollys was never raped.
</p>
