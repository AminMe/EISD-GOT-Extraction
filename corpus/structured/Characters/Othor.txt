<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Othor is a ranger in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. He and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jafer Flowers","link":"Jafer Flowers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jafer Flowers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Jafer_Flowers" title="Jafer Flowers">Jafer Flowers</a> accompanied <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Benjen Stark","link":"Benjen Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Benjen Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Benjen_Stark" title="Benjen Stark">Benjen Stark</a>'s ranging mission beyond <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>. Both Jafer and Othor are killed while on this mission.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The corpses of Othor and Jafer Flowers are found beyond the Wall by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Samwell Tarly","link":"Samwell Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Samwell Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> and a party led by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Othell Yarwyck","link":"Othell Yarwyck","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Othell Yarwyck]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Othell_Yarwyck" title="Othell Yarwyck">Othell Yarwyck</a>. They return the corpses to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Black","link":"Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a> and suggest they be burned. Lord Commander <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jeor Mormont","link":"Jeor Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jeor Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a> decides to let Maester <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aemon","link":"Aemon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aemon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/Aemon" title="Aemon">Aemon</a> examine them first.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Pointy End]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At night, the corpses rise as wights. Othor attacks Mormont, but Jon Snow intervenes and burns the wight, destroying it. Othor's and Jafer's bodies are re-burned the next day. Samwell notes that according to a book he found in Aemon's library, the touch of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a> can animate the dead.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"The Pointy End\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-108131336656cca69e9f1a2" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Othor is noted as a big, ugly man who enjoyed singing bawdy songs.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, where the bodies of Othor and Jafer were brought to Castle Black, both corpses had blue eyes â€“ different from the eye color they had in their lives. In the series, Othor's eyes were blue only while he was fighting Jon.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Othor came to life as wight, he killed at least one guard before entering Mormont's quarters. Jon found the body sprawled bonelessly, its head had been twisted completely around.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Jon's fight against Othor the wight is slightly different than portrayed in the TV series: first, Othor's blue eyes glowed; second, after Jon cut Othor's arm off, the severed limb continued fighting by grabbing at Jon's calf and he barely managed to pry the fingers off his leg, while in the series it simply fell on the floor; third, Jon destroyed Othor not by throwing a lamp directly at him: instead, he snatched the lamp from Mormont, used it to set the drapes on fire, then grabbed a fistful of the burning drapes and whipped them at the dead man, severely burning his fingers while doing so.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The fight with Othor made Jon realize that Valyrian steel is useless against wights, the same conclusion that Sam reached about dragonglass when fighting against Paul the wight: the only two known materials that are lethal to Others - are ineffective against their minions. Fire is still the only effective weapon against the wights.
</p>
