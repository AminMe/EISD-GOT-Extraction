<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114299711256cca6e526696" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the overall organizational structure of the faith of R'hllor has not been revealed. Individual cities have been said to possess a High Priest, such as High Priest Bennero in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114299711256cca6e526696" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a>. Whether or not there is an overarching international leadership of the entire religion, however, the way the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Septon","link":"High Septon (title)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Septon (title)|High Septon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114299711256cca6e526696" href="/wiki/High_Septon_(title)" title="High Septon (title)" class="mw-redirect">High Septon</a> is the leader of the entire <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114299711256cca6e526696" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a>, is uncertain. It is possible that in the TV version Melisandre was simply referring to the High Priest <i>in Myr</i>, not an overall leader.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Thoros was himself not specified to have been sent to King's Landing by some sort of overall High Priest, but apparently by the local leaders of the Red Priesthood in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114299711256cca6e526696" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myr</a>. In the TV series Thoros is said to have been sent to try to convert King Robert Baratheon, but this is a condensation from the books, in which Thoros was originally sent to King's Landing to try to convert King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-114299711256cca6e526696" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a>, due to his increasing obsession with fire. Thoros's preaching was not successful, but he remained at the royal court after Robert Baratheon seized the throne. He had even less luck preaching the religion to Robert, but he continued to hang around the royal court as one of Robert's drinking companions.
</p>
