<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-18325392856cca62ab93df" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Domeric Bolton was the only son and heir of Lord Roose Bolton and Lady Bethany Ryswell, his second wife. According to Roose, Domeric was quiet but accomplished; he read history, played the harp and rode horses as if born on the saddle. Domeric's mother's younger sister, Lady <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barbrey Dustin","link":"Barbrey Dustin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barbrey Dustin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-18325392856cca62ab93df" href="/wiki/Barbrey_Dustin" title="Barbrey Dustin">Barbrey Dustin</a>, was quite fond of him as he had served as a page for her at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barrowton","link":"Barrowton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barrowton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-18325392856cca62ab93df" href="/wiki/Barrowton" title="Barrowton">Barrowton</a>. He also served as a squire for Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Redfort","link":"Redfort","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Redfort]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-18325392856cca62ab93df" href="/wiki/Redfort" title="Redfort" class="mw-redirect">Redfort</a>Â in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vale","link":"Vale","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vale]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-18325392856cca62ab93df" href="/wiki/Vale" title="Vale" class="mw-redirect">Vale</a>, who said Domeric had the makings of a tournament champion. Domeric enjoyed the company of Redfort's sons in the Vale and, longing for a brother of his own, rode up the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Weeping Water","link":"Weeping Water","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Weeping Water]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-18325392856cca62ab93df" href="/wiki/Weeping_Water" title="Weeping Water">Weeping Water</a> to seek out his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bastard half-brother","link":"Ramsay Snow","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ramsay Snow|bastard half-brother]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-18325392856cca62ab93df" href="/wiki/Ramsay_Snow" title="Ramsay Snow" class="mw-redirect">bastard half-brother</a> despite his father's forbidding it.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the Dreadfort's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maester","link":"maester","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[maester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-18325392856cca62ab93df" href="/wiki/Maester" title="Maester" class="mw-redirect">maester</a>, Uthor, Domeric died of a sickness of the bowels. Roose and Lady Dustin, among others, strongly suspect Ramsay murdered Domeric with poison to take his place as Roose's heir.
</p>
