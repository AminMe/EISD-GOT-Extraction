<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Biography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Lothar is a son of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Walder Frey","link":"Walder Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Walder Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/Walder_Frey" title="Walder Frey">Walder Frey</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of the Crossing","link":"Lord of the Crossing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of the Crossing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Crossing" title="Lord of the Crossing">Lord of the Crossing</a>. He is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"steward","link":"Steward","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Steward|steward]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/Steward" title="Steward">steward</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Twins","link":"The Twins","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Twins|the Twins]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/The_Twins" title="The Twins">the Twins</a>, managing the day-to-day running of the castle. He often plans and organizes the fine details to achieve his father's goals.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Lothar Frey is often referred to as "Lame Lothar", on account of his crippled leg, and serves as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Steward","link":"Steward","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Steward]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/Steward" title="Steward">Steward</a> of the Twins. He is the twelfth son of Lord Walder Frey, the first from his marriage to his fourth wife, Lady Alyssa Blackwood.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lothar is described as plump, with close-set eyes, a pointed beard, and dark hair in ringlets to his shoulders. His leg was twisted at birth. Despite his handicap, he is intelligent, cunning, and well-spoken. As Steward of the Twins he holds the important position of overseeing the day-to-day running of his father's castle. When it comes to politics or war, Walder Frey determines the general course that House Frey will take, but it is often Lame Lothar who then plans and organizes the fine details to achieve his father's goals. Thus he is one of the major ringleaders within House Frey.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While courteous on the surface, Lothar can also be extremely vicious. After his father decided to betray the Starks, Lame Lothar handled most of the specific planning for the ambush (consulting with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roose Bolton","link":"Roose Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roose Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/Roose_Bolton" title="Roose Bolton">Roose Bolton</a>), appointing the specific tasks that would be followed out during the attack. It was his idea to hire the sellswords disguised as musicians to play "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Rains of Castamere","link":"The Rains of Castamere (song)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Rains of Castamere (song)|The Rains of Castamere]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/The_Rains_of_Castamere_(song)" title="The Rains of Castamere (song)">The Rains of Castamere</a>" to signal the start of the Red Wedding massacre. He also has the idea of rigging the tents in the Stark's camp to collapse, trapping the soldiers inside as they're set alight. Even other members of his family believe him to be far more dangerous than he outwardly appears.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lothar doesn't kill Robb's wife in the books because she is not even present at the Red Wedding. Robb's wife in the books is Jeyne Westerling (who was changed to "Talisa Maegyr" in the TV version), and she never became pregnant by Robb in the books. Robb left her for safety at Riverrun before traveling to the Twins with the rest of the Stark army. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Richard Madden","link":"Richard Madden","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Richard Madden]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3544009456cca62263726" href="/wiki/Richard_Madden" title="Richard Madden">Richard Madden</a> (Robb Stark) explained that the TV series simply killed off Talisa at the Red Wedding along with everyone else, because otherwise viewers would hold out hope that she would give birth to a son that would avenge Robb just as viewers used to think that Robb would live to avenge his father Eddard - they wanted to make it clear that they are avoiding such narrative clichés.
</p>
