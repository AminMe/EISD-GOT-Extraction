Ultimately Rhaegar was killed in personal combat with Robert Baratheon at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a>, leaving Elia widowed. He offers Oberyn an opportunity to confront the Mountain privately on the condition that he first preside as the third judge at Tyrion's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"court trial","link":"Court trial of Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Court trial of Tyrion Lannister|court trial]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Court_trial_of_Tyrion_Lannister" title="Court trial of Tyrion Lannister">court trial</a> for Joffrey's murder, which Oberyn agrees to do.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Breaker of Chains]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As the two champions commence battle at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"trial","link":"Second Trial by Combat of Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Second Trial by Combat of Tyrion Lannister|trial]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Second_Trial_by_Combat_of_Tyrion_Lannister" title="Second Trial by Combat of Tyrion Lannister">trial</a>, Oberyn identifies himself to the Mountain as the brother of Elia and reminds his opponent of the crime he committed against House Martell, saying, "You raped my sister, you murdered her, you killed her children." Despite wearing light armor and drinking before their fight, Oberyn initially gains the upper hand and proceeds to slowly cut down the Mountain all the while shouting in an ever louder voice, "Your raped her! You murdered her! You killed her children!" Thinking his opponent to be fully incapacitated, Oberyn again demands the Mountain confess his crime and reveal who gave him the order, pointing accusingly at Tywin. However, in his hubris Oberyn is unexpectedly caught off guard when the Mountain suddenly trips him up. Berserk with fury at nearly being killed by his opponent, the Mountain pins Oberyn to the ground and proceeds to gouge out his eyeballs, making him scream in agony, as he roars for all to hear, "Elia Martell! I killed her children! Then I raped her! Then I smashed her head...in like this!" <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, Elia is also the wife of Rhaegar, and died with her children in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of King's Landing","link":"Sack of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Sack_of_King's_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">Sack of King's Landing</a>. She was the sister of Princes <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doran","link":"Doran Martell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doran Martell|Doran]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Doran_Martell" title="Doran Martell">Doran</a>, the ruling Prince of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Oberyn Martell","link":"Oberyn Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Oberyn Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Oberyn_Martell" title="Oberyn Martell">Oberyn Martell</a>.  Elia was born nine years after Doran, and a year before Oberyn.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gregor Clegane","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a> raped and killed Elia after bashing in her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"son","link":"Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)|son]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Aegon_Targaryen_(Son_of_Rhaegar)" title="Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)">son</a>'s head, while his hands were still tainted with the infant's blood. Elia's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"daughter","link":"Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)|daughter]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Rhaenys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Rhaegar)" title="Rhaenys Targaryen (daughter of Rhaegar)">daughter</a> was killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Amory Lorch","link":"Amory Lorch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Amory Lorch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Amory_Lorch" title="Amory Lorch">Amory Lorch</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a> later admitted to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister|Tyrion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a> that there was no need to kill Elia because "by herself she was nothing" (in contrast to her children - as Rhaegar's blood heirs even Robert knew they had to die for the war to end). <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Mountain","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane|The Mountain]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">The Mountain</a> killed Elia simply because Tywin did not tell him to spare her. Tywin doubted if he had mentioned Elia at all, for he had many pressing concerns to worry about at that time, nor did he yet realize what kind of monster the Mountain was. On the other hand, he never punished Gregor for the rape and murder, because that would be an admission of indirect guilt on his part. This of course backfired: his inaction only served to convince the Martells that he was <i>actively</i> responsible and must have directly ordered Gregor to kill Elia, harming relations with the Martells far more than if he had simply admitted the truth. Tywin was also uncharacteristically defensive when Tyrion voiced his suspicion that he ordered Gregor to not only kill Elia, but rape her first, showing that even he was disgusted by what Gregor did (but still did not punish him for it).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Martells (particularly Oberyn) were so outraged by Elia's brutal and needless murder that they initially intended to continue fighting in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys Targaryen's","link":"Viserys III Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys III Targaryen|Viserys Targaryen's]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Viserys_III_Targaryen" title="Viserys III Targaryen" class="mw-redirect">Viserys Targaryen's</a> name. After the rebellion's end, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Arryn","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>'s first act as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a> of the King was to travel to Dorne and negotiate with Prince Doran; Arryn convinced the Dornish to cease hostilities, but the fact Elia's killers were never punished caused the region to adopt a largely isolationist policy with the rest of the Seven Kingdoms.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As part of the Lannister alliance with the Martells that he negotiates, to prevent them from joining Renly's host, Tyrion pledges to bring those responsible for the death of Elia and her children to justice, including the Mountain. Tywin later reneges on this, intending to place all the blame on the now-deceased Amory Lorch. Oberyn does not seem to believe that new version, and Tyrion assures him that Lorch killed Rhaenys, while the Mountain killed baby Aegon and then proceeded to rape and kill Elia. After Gregor roars his guilt for all to hear during Tyrion's trial by combat, Tywin is forced to have Gregor healed if only so <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ilyn Payne","link":"Ilyn Payne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ilyn Payne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Ilyn_Payne" title="Ilyn Payne">Ilyn Payne</a> can execute him to appease the Martells, for fear inaction might lead to Dorne supporting <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-14830597056cca5bd8541f" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>'s cause.
</p>
