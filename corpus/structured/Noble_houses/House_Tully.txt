<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196915402756cca94863e45" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Tully is an old house which has held the castle at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196915402756cca94863e45" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a> for some centuries, but did not rise to greatness until King Aegon I's invasion. Aegon drove the ironborn out of the Riverlands and appointed Lord Edmyn Tully as the overlord of the area in thanks for his support during the Conquest. The Tullys joined the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Starks","link":"House Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark|Starks]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196915402756cca94863e45" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">Starks</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baratheons","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon|Baratheons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196915402756cca94863e45" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">Baratheons</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arryns","link":"House Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Arryn|Arryns]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196915402756cca94863e45" href="/wiki/House_Arryn" title="House Arryn">Arryns</a> in rising against the Mad King, providing numerous troops for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196915402756cca94863e45" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>'s armies.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Tully banner is different in the books than in the series. In the TV series, it is striped horizontally, with one large red section and one large blue section, separated by white stripes. In the novels it has five <i>vertical</i> stripes of equal width; two muddy-red stripes separate three blue stripes (and there are no white stripes).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In a somewhat unusual in-joke, <i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> reveals that Tullys active during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dance of the Dragons","link":"Dance of the Dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dance of the Dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196915402756cca94863e45" href="/wiki/Dance_of_the_Dragons" title="Dance of the Dragons">Dance of the Dragons</a> are named after <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Muppets","link":"wikipedia:The_Muppets","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:The_Muppets|Muppets]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-196915402756cca94863e45" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Muppets" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Muppets">Muppets</a>, namely Lord Grover Tully, his grandson Ser Elmo Tully, and his son Lord Kermit Tully.
