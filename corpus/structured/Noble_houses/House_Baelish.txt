<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">House Baelish began with Petyr's great-grandfather, a common <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsword","link":"Mercenary","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mercenary|sellsword]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellsword</a> from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Braavos","link":"Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Braavos" title="Braavos">Braavos</a> who traveled to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> to take up service with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Corbray","link":"House Corbray","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Corbray]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/House_Corbray" title="House Corbray">House Corbray</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Vale of Arryn","link":"The Vale of Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Vale of Arryn|the Vale of Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale of Arryn</a>. The sellsword's son, Petyr's grandfather, was a poor wandering <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hedge knight","link":"Hedge knight","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hedge knight]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Hedge_knight" title="Hedge knight">Hedge knight</a>, who managed to earn a minor lordship - the most minor of lordships, just a few stony acres in the smallest peninsula of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Fingers","link":"The Fingers","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Fingers|the Fingers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/The_Fingers" title="The Fingers">the Fingers</a>, with a small keep to reside in.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[House Baelish (Histories & Lore)]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Petyr's father fought in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","link":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Ninepenny Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings" title="War of the Ninepenny Kings">War of the Ninepenny Kings</a>, the preemptive assault on the forces of Maelys Blackfyre, the last of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackfyre Pretenders","link":"House Blackfyre","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Blackfyre|Blackfyre Pretenders]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/House_Blackfyre" title="House Blackfyre">Blackfyre Pretenders</a>, before they could attempt an invasion of Westeros. During the conflict Petyr's father served well under Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hoster Tully","link":"Hoster Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hoster Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Hoster_Tully" title="Hoster Tully">Hoster Tully</a>. Through their good relationship Hoster agreed to foster his son Petyr at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a>, a great honor for a lesser House.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[House Baelish (Histories & Lore)]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Petyr went to Riverrun when he was eight years old, and befriended Hoster's daughters <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn","link":"Catelyn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Catelyn" title="Catelyn" class="mw-redirect">Catelyn</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lysa Tully","link":"Lysa Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lysa Arryn|Lysa Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Lysa_Arryn" title="Lysa Arryn">Lysa Tully</a>. Their brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Edmure","link":"Edmure Tully","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Edmure Tully|Edmure]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Edmure_Tully" title="Edmure Tully">Edmure</a> mockingly nicknamed him "Littlefinger" at the time, in reference to his small height at the time and the location of his minor landholdings. Petyr became smitten with Catelyn but she never returned his affections. Ironically, Lysa was infatuated with Petyr, but he only loved Catelyn. Eventually when she was betrothed to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brandon Stark","link":"Brandon Stark (Son of Rickard)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brandon Stark (Son of Rickard)|Brandon Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Brandon_Stark_(Son_of_Rickard)" title="Brandon Stark (Son of Rickard)">Brandon Stark</a>, heir to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a>, Petyr challenged him to a duel, thinking he could win through pluck and courage - he was promptly overpowered and nearly killed, though Catelyn begged Brandon to spare his life.  Embittered, Petyr was cast out of Riverrun and returned to the Vale. During the course of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>, however, Brandon was killed by the Mad King and Catelyn married his younger brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>, while to secure a political alliance Lysa was wed to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Arryn","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>, ruler of the Vale. Manipulating Lysa's infatuation with himself, Petyr ingratiated himself into Jon's services, first as customs officer at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gulltown","link":"Gulltown","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gulltown]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Gulltown" title="Gulltown">Gulltown</a>, where he increased the port's revenues tenfold.  Seeing Littlefinger's financial acumen, Jon was easily convinced by Lysa to later bring Littlefinger to the royal court at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, and make him <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Coin","link":"Master of Coin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Coin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Master_of_Coin" title="Master of Coin">Master of Coin</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[House Baelish (Histories & Lore)]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In charge of the crown's treasury, Littlefinger rapidly grew into a powerful player of royal politics, and a master of court intrigues. Of an older mindset, few who did not know him suspected Littlefinger as a threat, seeing only that he was a man from a very minor House, with no distinguished lineage, significant landholdings, or armies. Instead, Littlefinger based his newfound power on <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"money","link":"Currency","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Currency|money]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">money</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[House Baelish (Histories & Lore)]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Petyr is the only living member of House Baelish, though he is so poor that he gained all of his current wealth due to his own career as a treasurer. Petyr's great skills with numbers and banking led him to become the tax and revenue collector for Gulltown, the principal city of the Vale. At Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Arryn","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>'s suggestion, King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> later made Petyr his Master of Coin for the whole realm, a role he continues to serve in until he is sent back to the Vale to enter into a profitable marriage-alliance with Lysa Arryn.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The sigil of House Baelish is – or was – the grey stone head with fiery eyes of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Titan of Braavos","link":"Titan of Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Titan of Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Titan_of_Braavos" title="Titan of Braavos">Titan of Braavos</a>, on a light green field. It was chosen by Petyr's grandfather when he was knighted, because his own father was a common sellsword from Braavos. However, the current lord of the house (Littlefinger) prefers to use his personal mockingbird sigil exclusively. Since he is the only living member of the house, the mockingbird sigil has functionally replaced the original. In the books, the Littlefinger's personal heraldry actually consists of a field of multiple silver mockingbirds on a green plain.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Petyr rarely returns home, leaving the keeping of his minor towerhouse to a small staff. The original lands of House Baelish are so meager that their towerhouse doesn't even have a formal name, and it is so small that it doesn't  have a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maester","link":"Maesters","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maesters|maester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-77214536856cca951861a0" href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">maester</a> assigned to it.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The only real benefit Petyr received from being born into House Baelish is that it technically made him a member of the minor nobility, thus allowing his initial entry into court life. Petyr downplays his partial descent from the Free Cities of the east, even abandoning the use of the original sigil of House Baelish, displaying the Titan of Braavos, to his own personal sigil featuring a mockingbird. Thus, it is somewhat ironic that he criticizes Varys for being from the Free Cities.
</p>
