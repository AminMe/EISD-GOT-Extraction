<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27046119756cca96d663f9" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Corbrays are an old but poor house. They hold the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian steel","link":"Valyrian steel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian steel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27046119756cca96d663f9" href="/wiki/Valyrian_steel" title="Valyrian steel">Valyrian steel</a> blade known as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lady Forlorn","link":"Lady Forlorn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lady Forlorn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27046119756cca96d663f9" href="/wiki/Lady_Forlorn" title="Lady Forlorn">Lady Forlorn</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The heraldry of House Corbray in the books is three black ravens in flight, each holding a separate red heart, on a white field. In the TV series continuity (as briefly glimpsed in an animated featurette), it is a single black raven rather than three.
</p>
