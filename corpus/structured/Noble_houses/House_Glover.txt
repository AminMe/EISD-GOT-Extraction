The head of the house is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Master of Deepwood Motte","link":"Master of Deepwood Motte","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Master of Deepwood Motte]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214276849056cca95c79b49" href="/wiki/Master_of_Deepwood_Motte" title="Master of Deepwood Motte">Master of Deepwood Motte</a>.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214276849056cca95c79b49" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Glover is a loyal and reliable vassal of the Starks. Their castle is a motte-and-bailey wooden castle located inland but close to the Bay of Ice. House Glover's sigil is a mailed silver fist on scarlet background.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Other minor vassal houses sworn to the Glovers that have not appeared in the series: House Woods, House Branch, and House Bole.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Technically, the Glovers are stated to be a "Masterly" House, not a full major noble House - which would go towards explaining why Greatjon Umber would be insulted at being commanded by a Glover. However, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214276849056cca95c79b49" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a> has admitted that he didn't really think out a full system of titles for different degrees of rank within the nobility of the Seven Kingdoms. There actually doesn't seem to be any functional difference between "Masterly" Houses, such as the Glovers and other major Houses like the Umbers. There are basically three tiers of noble family under each Great House such as the Starks: major Houses who answer only to the Great House of the region (i.e. the Umbers), minor lordly Houses who serve another intermediary noble House (House Marsh serves House Reed), and knightly Houses - unlike lords, knights do not have the power to dispense justice and executions on their own lands. Some minor lordly Houses also directly serve the Great House of the region, given that a feudal system isn't entirely a delegated hierarchy, but the ruling family gathers strength from its own lands. Thus minor Houses such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cassel","link":"House Cassel","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Cassel|Cassel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214276849056cca95c79b49" href="/wiki/House_Cassel" title="House Cassel">Cassel</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Poole","link":"House Poole","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Poole|Poole]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214276849056cca95c79b49" href="/wiki/House_Poole" title="House Poole">Poole</a> also serve the Starks. At any rate, because the Glovers answer to no intermediary lords but to the Starks themselves, and local lesser Houses in turn serve as vassals to the Glovers, there really doesn't seem to be any functional difference between the titles. Apparently their "Masterly" rank as the "Masters of Deepwood Motte" has to do with being marcher lords of a sort, responsible for governing the wild regions of the Wolfswood.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214276849056cca95c79b49" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a>'s invasion, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Asha Greyjoy","link":"Yara Greyjoy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yara Greyjoy|Asha Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214276849056cca95c79b49" href="/wiki/Yara_Greyjoy" title="Yara Greyjoy">Asha Greyjoy</a> takes Deepwood Motte with her forces, holding Sybelle and her children as hostages.
</p>
