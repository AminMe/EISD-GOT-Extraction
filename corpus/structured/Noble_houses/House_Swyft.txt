<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-26223518056cca96c64ac0" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Swyft is counted as a strong vassal and ally of House Lannister. However, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-26223518056cca96c64ac0" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> considers Ser Harys to be a chinless craven and lickspittle of little worth. Dorna is described as "equally chinless" as her father.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Swyft joined their Lannister lieges in siding with King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II Targaryen","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-26223518056cca96c64ac0" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II Targaryen</a> during the Dance of the Dragons.  At this time they were a full <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"noble house","link":"noble house","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[noble house]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-26223518056cca96c64ac0" href="/wiki/Noble_house" title="Noble house">noble house</a>, but by the time the series begins they are a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"knightly house","link":"Knightly House","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Knightly House|knightly house]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-26223518056cca96c64ac0" href="/wiki/Knightly_House" title="Knightly House" class="mw-redirect">knightly house</a>. How the Swyfts lost their lordship is unknown.​
</p>
