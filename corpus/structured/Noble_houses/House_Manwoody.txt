<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51098430556cca9a2bc0a4" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Manwoody's castle seat guards the western of the two passes that lead through the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Mountains","link":"Red Mountains","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Mountains]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51098430556cca9a2bc0a4" href="/wiki/Red_Mountains" title="Red Mountains">Red Mountains</a> into Dorne, known as the Prince's Pass. While House Fowler's seat at Skyreach is further south near the top of the pass, Kingsgrave is located further north, about halfway through the mountains. As a result, the Manwoodys are marcher lords, always the first line of defense in the frequent border wars which occurred in the Dornish marches between Dorne, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stormlands","link":"the Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51098430556cca9a2bc0a4" href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Reach","link":"the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51098430556cca9a2bc0a4" href="/wiki/The_Reach" title="The Reach">the Reach</a>. The name "Kingsgrave" as well as the Manwoody sigil - a crowned skull - refer to the fact that an old <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King of the Reach","link":"King of the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51098430556cca9a2bc0a4" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Reach" title="King of the Reach">King of the Reach</a> was slain in battle there centuries ago.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As they are located deep within the Red Mountains, the Manwoodys are presumably "Stony Dornishmen" - those with the least <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51098430556cca9a2bc0a4" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> bloodlines. Instead, the Stony Dornishmen are primarily composed of the same <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51098430556cca9a2bc0a4" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a>/<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andal","link":"Andal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-51098430556cca9a2bc0a4" href="/wiki/Andal" title="Andal" class="mw-redirect">Andal</a> ethnic mixture as the rest of southern Westeros. As a result, Stony Dornishmen tend to be tall and light-haired.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The members of House Manwoody at the time of the novels are:
</p>
<ul><li>Lord Dagos Manwoody: Lord of Kingsgrave.
</li><li>Mors Manwoody, his eldest son.
</li><li>Dickon Manwoody, his second son.
</li><li>Ser Myles Manwoody, his brother.
</li></ul>
