<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-167633131456cca9928fc6a" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Waynwoods are powerful vassals of House Arryn. Their seat is the castle of Ironoaks, east of the Eyrie on the coast of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-167633131456cca9928fc6a" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a>. They are known for their insistence on ceremony.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The members of House Waynwood at the time of the novels are:
</p>
<ul><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Lady Anya Waynwood, Lady of Ironoaks. An elderly woman.
<ul><li>Ser Morton Waynwood, her eldest son and heir.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Ser Donnel Waynwood, her second son. Knight of the Gate.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Wallace Waynwood, her youngest son.
</li></ul>
</li></ul>
