<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-182066081756cca954a4db5" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Florents, like the Tyrells, claim descent from the extinct House Gardener, the royal line of the defunct Kings of the Reach. The Florents are known to chafe at the fealty owed to House Tyrell. The Florents believe that they have a superior line of descent, and by rights should possess Highgarden. While many Houses in the Reach can claim some descent from House Gardener, the Florents were a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"cadet branch","link":"cadet branch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[cadet branch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-182066081756cca954a4db5" href="/wiki/Cadet_branch" title="Cadet branch">cadet branch</a> of the Gardeners (descended from a younger son), while the Tyrells can only claim descent from the Gardeners through the female line. Physically, Florents are known for having large and prominent ears.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Heraldry","link":"Heraldry","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Heraldry]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-182066081756cca954a4db5" href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">Heraldry</a> of House Florent in the books is inconsistently described, but apparently consists of a red-gold fox's head, encircled by lapis lazuli flowers, all of which is on an ermine background. The TV version simply omits the lapis lazuli flowers (and predominantly shows a full-bodied fox).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-182066081756cca954a4db5" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, the Florents initially declare for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly Baratheon","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-182066081756cca954a4db5" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a> along with the rest of the noble Houses in the Reach, but following the latter's assassination, they defy the Tyrells and join <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-182066081756cca954a4db5" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>, becoming one of the few Houses of the Reach not to join their Tyrell overlords in switching their allegiance to House Lannister.
</p>
