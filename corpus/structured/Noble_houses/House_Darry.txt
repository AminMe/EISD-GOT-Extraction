<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Darry is a noble house of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andal","link":"Andal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Andal" title="Andal" class="mw-redirect">Andal</a> origin seated at Castle Darry in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a>. Their sigil is a black plowman on a brown field.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Historically, they had been one of the more prominent and powerful houses of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Trident","link":"Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Trident" title="Trident" class="mw-redirect">Trident</a>, until the fall of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a>. Since the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">War of Conquest</a>, they have been noted Targaryen loyalists, supporting <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daeron II Targaryen","link":"Daeron II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daeron II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Daeron_II_Targaryen" title="Daeron II Targaryen">Daeron II Targaryen</a> over <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daemon Blackfyre","link":"Daemon Blackfyre","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daemon Blackfyre]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Daemon_Blackfyre" title="Daemon Blackfyre">Daemon Blackfyre</a> during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackfyre Rebellion","link":"Blackfyre Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackfyre Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Blackfyre_Rebellion" title="Blackfyre Rebellion">Blackfyre Rebellion</a>. A knight known as the Demon of Darry became <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander","link":"Lord Commander","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander" title="Lord Commander">Lord Commander</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Darry fought on the Targaryen side during <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>.  Their Tully overlords were among the leading rebel families, but the Riverlands Houses were heavily divided in the war.  Ser Jonothor Darry was a member of the Mad King's Kingsguard, and perished at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a>.  Following the war, the Darrys lost half of their lands, most of their wealth, and almost all of their power.  At Daenerys's wedding to Khal Drogo, Viserys muses that the Darrys are among the major remaining Targaryen loyalists they can count on if they invade the Seven Kingdoms.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the march south following <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ned Stark","link":"Ned Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ned Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Ned_Stark" title="Ned Stark" class="mw-redirect">Ned Stark</a>'s elevation to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>, the royal party stays at Castle Darry, much to the chagrin of Lord Raymun Darry, who had lost three brothers at the Trident. After <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gregor Clegane","link":"Gregor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gregor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a> begins raiding the Riverlands, Lord Raymun is among the delegation sent by Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hoster Tully","link":"Hoster Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hoster Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Hoster_Tully" title="Hoster Tully">Hoster Tully</a> to demand justice from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> and it is him who identifies the Mountain as the leader of the "brigands". Lord Raymun joins the host led by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Beric Dondarrion","link":"Beric Dondarrion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Beric Dondarrion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Beric_Dondarrion" title="Beric Dondarrion">Beric Dondarrion</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Thoros","link":"Thoros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Thoros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Thoros" title="Thoros">Thoros</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myr</a>, but is killed by the Mountain in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle at the Mummer's Ford","link":"Battle at the Mummer's Ford","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle at the Mummer's Ford]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Battle_at_the_Mummer's_Ford" title="Battle at the Mummer's Ford">Battle at the Mummer's Ford</a>. Castle Darry is later occupied by Lannister forces.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Raymun's son Lyman, a boy of eight years, becomes the new Lord of Darry and is present at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a> following the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Camps","link":"Battle of the Camps","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Camps]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Camps" title="Battle of the Camps">Battle of the Camps</a> and proclaims <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King in the North","link":"King in the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King in the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-188097359856cca989bdd64" href="/wiki/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">King in the North</a>. Castle Darry is later liberated when Robb gives the Riverlords leave to reclaim their seats, but only for a fortnight, as Gregor Clegane descends upon Castle Darry and puts all occupants to the sword, among them Lord Lyman himself, thus extinguishing House Darry. The sole male survivor of House Darry is a bastard cousin of Lord Lyman.  Several female-line descendants also exist, among them several Freys, leading to bickering about who the Lannisters should award the remaining Darry lands.
</p>
