It is by far one of the oldest lines of Westerosi nobility, claiming a line of descent stretching back over eight thousand years. The head of the house was the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Winterfell","link":"Lord of Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Winterfell" title="Lord of Winterfell">Lord of Winterfell</a>. Before the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Targaryen" title="Targaryen" class="mw-redirect">Targaryen</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"conquest","link":"Wars of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wars of Conquest|conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Wars_of_Conquest" title="Wars of Conquest" class="mw-redirect">conquest</a>, the leaders of House Stark ruled over the region as the Kings in the North.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Their rule in the North seemingly ended after the events of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a> when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Frey","link":"House Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/House_Frey" title="House Frey">House Frey</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Bolton","link":"House Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/House_Bolton" title="House Bolton">House Bolton</a> betrayed them, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roose Bolton","link":"Roose Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roose Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Roose_Bolton" title="Roose Bolton">Roose Bolton</a> personally killed King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>. Both the North and Winterfell have since been taken over by the Boltons but their hold has been jeopardized now that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa Stark","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> has escaped their clutches after learning that her brothers <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bran","link":"Bran Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bran Stark|Bran]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rickon Stark","link":"Rickon Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rickon Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Rickon_Stark" title="Rickon Stark">Rickon Stark</a> are still alive.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Stark's sigil is a grey <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"direwolf","link":"Direwolves","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Direwolves|direwolf]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Direwolves" title="Direwolves">direwolf</a> on a white field, sometimes white and pale green. They are one of the few noble Houses whose family words are not a boast or threat. Instead, the House Stark family motto is a warning, one that, no matter the circumstances, will always be relevant: <b>"Winter is Coming.</b>

<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a></h3>

With his host diminished due to the Karstarks' abandonment, Robb opts to have the support of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Frey","link":"House Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/House_Frey" title="House Frey">House Frey</a> but finds it difficult because of his broken vow. However, the Freys agree to support Robb if his uncle Edmure marries <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roslin Frey","link":"Roslin Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roslin Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Roslin_Frey" title="Roslin Frey">Roslin Frey</a>. Robb and Edmure agree to the terms of the Freys and prepare to head to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Twins","link":"Twins","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Twins]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Twins" title="Twins" class="mw-redirect">Twins</a> for the wedding.

</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The civil war known as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a> began when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lyanna Stark","link":"Lyanna Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lyanna Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Lyanna_Stark" title="Lyanna Stark">Lyanna Stark</a> was kidnapped by Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegar Targaryen","link":"Rhaegar Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar Targaryen</a>. Lord Rickard Stark and his son and heir Brandon both demanded her return from King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a> and were executed by the Mad King for their presumption. Eddard Stark, now the head of the family, called the banners of the North to war, supported by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tully","link":"House Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/House_Tully" title="House Tully">House Tully</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Arryn","link":"House Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/House_Arryn" title="House Arryn">House Arryn</a>. It was decided that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> would claim the throne due to his strong claim as the grand-nephew of a previous Targaryen king. During the civil war Stark troops were instrumental in several engagements, including the Battle of the Bells and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a>, and it was Eddard Stark's forces which reached <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> just after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannisters","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister|Lannisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannisters</a> had sacked it, ensuring the city was turned over to Robert's army when it arrived. The Starks also proved instrumental in the later <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Greyjoy Rebellion","link":"Greyjoy Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Greyjoy Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Greyjoy_Rebellion" title="Greyjoy Rebellion">Greyjoy Rebellion</a>, lending troops used by Robert to storm the castle of Pyke and crush the uprising.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, all of Lord Eddard Stark's children take after their mother Catelyn's looks, possessing the auburn hair and blue eyes distinctive of House Tully. The one exception among Catelyn's children is Arya, who possesses the dark hair and stern long face typical of the Starks. While this has resulted in Arya being mockingly called "Horseface", Ned assures her that she bears a striking resemblance to his beautiful deceased sister Lyanna when she was a girl. Jon Snow, as a bastard and not Catelyn's son, does not possess Tully blood. While Ned has never revealed the exact circumstances of Jon's birth, all agree that he bears such a striking resemblance to Ned that he is obviously his blood relative. Indeed, as a little girl Arya once feared that she might be a bastard because she and Jon share the same dark physical features (distinctive of House Stark), though Jon assured her that she was not.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, the Stark banner is a whole <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"direwolf","link":"direwolves","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[direwolves|direwolf]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Direwolves" title="Direwolves">direwolf</a>. In the TV series, the Stark banner most often used is just a direwolf's head, though the alternate version showing the whole direwolf is also seen (i.e. on banners during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tourney of the Hand","link":"Tournament","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tournament|Tourney of the Hand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-75144447256cc7187936b8" href="/wiki/Tournament" title="Tournament">Tourney of the Hand</a>, tapestries at Winterfell, and Ned Stark's wax seal on the letter he sends to Stannis Baratheon). Further, the sigil in the books is just a grey direwolf on a white field. This is used in the TV series, but it also frequently uses a subtle variant which has the grey direwolf on a white field, surmounting a small green escutcheon at the bottom.
