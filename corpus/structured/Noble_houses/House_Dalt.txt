<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22144710256cca988cb148" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Dalt's seat is at Lemonwood, located on the southern bank of the mouth of the mouth of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Greenblood","link":"Greenblood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Greenblood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22144710256cca988cb148" href="/wiki/Greenblood" title="Greenblood">Greenblood</a> River. As a result, they are "Salty Dornishmen": when the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22144710256cca988cb148" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> migrated to Dorne a thousand years ago, they primarily settled around the coasts and river valleys, and in these places their bloodlines had the largest impact. Salty Dornishmen tend to have olive skin, dark hair and eyes, and somewhat short but lithe stature. The coasts of Dorne also enjoy a fair amount of trade with the nearby <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22144710256cca988cb148" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> across the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22144710256cca988cb148" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The heraldry of House Dalt apparently refers to the fact that Dorne, the southernmost of the Seven Kingdoms, is the only region in Westeros where citrus fruits grow. The lemons for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa Stark","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22144710256cca988cb148" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a>'sÂ <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"lemon cakes","link":"Lemon\u00a0Cakes","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lemon\u00a0Cakes|lemon cakes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22144710256cca988cb148" href="/wiki/Lemon_Cakes" title="Lemon Cakes" class="mw-redirect">lemon cakes</a> have to be imported from Dorne.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Dalt is a knightly House, not a lordly House (meaning they do not have the authority to dispense justice and executions). However, they are still considered one of the more powerful Houses from Dorne.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The members of House Dalt at the time of the novels are:
</p>
<ul><li>Ser Deziel Dalt, the Knight of Lemonwood.
</li><li>Ser Andrey Dalt, his brother and heir. Called Drey.
</li></ul>
