The Blackwoods hold an ancient grudge against their neighbors, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Bracken","link":"House Bracken","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Bracken]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-201366764556cca96a268af" href="/wiki/House_Bracken" title="House Bracken">House Bracken</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stone Hedge","link":"Stone Hedge","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stone Hedge]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-201366764556cca96a268af" href="/wiki/Stone_Hedge" title="Stone Hedge">Stone Hedge</a>. House Blackwood, however, is one of the few major noble Houses outside of the North which continues to worship the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Gods of the Forest","link":"Old Gods of the Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Gods of the Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-201366764556cca96a268af" href="/wiki/Old_Gods_of_the_Forest" title="Old Gods of the Forest">Old Gods of the Forest</a>, and never converted to the Faith of the Seven.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season1\/#!\/locations\/raventree-hall\/ HBO viewers guide, season 1 map, Raventree Hall entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-201366764556cca96a268af" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-201366764556cca96a268af" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Blackwood is a loyal vassal of the Tullys. The enmity and competition between <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Bracken","link":"House Bracken","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Bracken]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-201366764556cca96a268af" href="/wiki/House_Bracken" title="House Bracken">House Bracken</a> and House Blackwood, was radically enhanced when Ser Otho Bracken slew Lord Quentyn Blackwood in a tourney accident roughly a century ago.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The sigil of House Blackwood is slightly different in the books, in which the white weirwood tree is set against a black escutcheon (shield), which is itself surrounded by a red background containing black ravens. The TV series version removed the black shield, so the white weirwood tree is directly against the red background.
</p>
