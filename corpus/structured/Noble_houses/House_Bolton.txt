<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:FlayedSkinsDreadfortDungeon.jpg|thumb|House Bolton are said to have worn the skins of their enemies as cloaks after flaying them.]]","title":"FlayedSkinsDreadfortDungeon.jpg","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"FlayedSkinsDreadfortDungeon","caption":"House Bolton are said to have worn the skins of their enemies as cloaks after flaying them.","captionParsed":"House Bolton are said to have worn the skins of their enemies as cloaks after flaying them."}}" data-rte-instance="130814-191252094756cca944a708c" alt="" src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/d/d7/FlayedSkinsDreadfortDungeon.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20140219200952" width="180" height="99" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="FlayedSkinsDreadfortDungeon.jpg" data-image-key="FlayedSkinsDreadfortDungeon.jpg"  type="image" />In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-191252094756cca944a708c" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Bolton is noted as one of the most powerful families of the North. They are noted for flaying their enemies alive and even wearing their skins as cloaks. They have even captured and flayed Starks in the distant past. House Bolton were unruly vassals of the Starks until approximately a thousand years ago, when they finally bent the knee. Three hundred years later they rebelled but were defeated. The armies of House Stark besieged the Dreadfort for two years before finally forcing the Boltons to surrender and submit.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Boltons are considered a sinister and ill-omened house, but Lord Roose Bolton is noted as a capable battle commander. Roose Bolton fought alongside <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-191252094756cca944a708c" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> during <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-191252094756cca944a708c" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>. After the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-191252094756cca944a708c" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a>, Roose suggested executing the defeated <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-191252094756cca944a708c" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a>, but Eddard and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-191252094756cca944a708c" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> refused.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Besides Lord Roose, the members of the family in the books are:
</p>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Bethany Ryswell, Roose Bolton's late wife.
<ul><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">{Domeric Bolton}, their only son and heir.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Ramsay Snow, his bastard son.
</li></ul>
</li></ul>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Domeric Bolton died shortly before the beginning of the series, having sought out his bastard half-brother Ramsay (Domeric having always wanted a brother of his own). With no other trueborn heirs, Roose brought Ramsay to the Dreadfort and began to treat him as his heir.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The sigil of House Bolton in the books is a red flayed man on a field of pink with red blood drops. The TV series design has specified that the flayed man is displayed hanging upside down on an X-shaped cross. The color scheme in the TV series is slightly different: it is a red flayed man, but hanging upside-down on a white X-shaped cross with a black background.
</p>
