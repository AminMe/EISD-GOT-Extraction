<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"double-brackets","lineStart":"1","title":"Quote","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"{{Quote|Know that you never stand alone, you are a Forrester!|[[Elissa Forrester]]|url = http:\/\/gameofthrones.wikia.com\/wiki\/Elissa_Forrester}}"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" class="placeholder placeholder-double-brackets" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="double-brackets" />Due to the location of their stronghold, Ironrath, on the edge of the Wolfswood, the Forresters control the largest <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironwood","link":"ironwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Ironwood" title="Ironwood">ironwood</a> forest in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>, which has allowed them to enrich themselves. They are bitter rivals of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Whitehill","link":"House Whitehill","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Whitehill]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/House_Whitehill" title="House Whitehill">House Whitehill</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The second born son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Asher Forrester","link":"Asher Forrester","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Asher Forrester]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Asher_Forrester" title="Asher Forrester">Asher Forrester</a> was exiled to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> by his father, rather than face war with House Whitehill, after he had fell in love with the daughter of Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ludd Whitehill","link":"Ludd Whitehill","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ludd Whitehill]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Ludd_Whitehill" title="Ludd Whitehill">Ludd Whitehill</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gwyn Whitehill","link":"Gwyn Whitehill","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gwyn Whitehill]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Gwyn_Whitehill" title="Gwyn Whitehill">Gwyn Whitehill</a>. In Essos, Asher lives as a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsword","link":"mercenary","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[mercenary|sellsword]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellsword</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At the outset of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, House Forrester joined their Glover overlords in pledging fealty to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King in the North","link":"King in the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King in the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">King in the North</a>, a decision which placed them in a tenuous position following Stark's death and the defeat of the Northern rebellion at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>http:\/\/www.telltalegames.com\/blog\/discussion\/86265<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">House Forrester was part of the Young Wolf's campaign throughout <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Westerlands","link":"the Westerlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Westerlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/The_Westerlands" title="The Westerlands">the Westerlands</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a> including the forces fighting at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Whispering Wood","link":"Battle of the Whispering Wood","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Whispering Wood|Whispering Wood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Whispering_Wood" title="Battle of the Whispering Wood">Whispering Wood</a>. They are excellent warriors, and were chosen to ride in the vanguard on the planned charge to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Casterly Rock","link":"Casterly Rock","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Casterly Rock]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Casterly_Rock" title="Casterly Rock">Casterly Rock</a>. Since they control the largest ironwood forest in Westeros, their men are equipped with the strong ironwood shields.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>, however, most of their best men were killed. After Ethan's death, many of the remaining men deserted their posts. Ser Royland Degore, the master-at-arms, states he can muster an additional 30 to 40 men, in addition to their current forces and the pit fighters, but most of the remaining Forrester army was killed during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Ironrath","link":"Battle of Ironrath","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Ironrath]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ironrath" title="Battle of Ironrath">Battle of Ironrath</a>, effectively leaving them with no army. It is possible that the Forresters have had an army of 500 men or perhaps even more than that, considering that House Whitehill is also a minor noble house, and have an army of 500 men. It is interesting to note that, there are even smaller houses, as the Forresters and the Whitehills both had minor bannermen ( houses Degore and Warrick, and possibly some other minor houses as well)
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">House Forrester was not purely invented for the 2014 video game, and do exist in the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels. They were mentioned in passing exactly once in the fifth novel, though in this one instance they are indeed described as a minor House in the Wolfswood who serve as vassals to House Glover of Deepwood Motte:
</p>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>"<i>...guides Lady Sybell [Glover] had given them, trackers and hunters sworn to Deepwood with clan names like Forrester and Woods, Branch and Bole.</i>" (<i>A Dance With Dragons</i>, Chapter 42)
</dd></dl>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Nothing else about them is revealed, leaving a blank slate to fill in. Their seat, words, and family members are all inventions of <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Game of Thrones: A Telltale Games Series","link":"Game of Thrones: A Telltale Games Series","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Game of Thrones: A Telltale Games Series]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones:_A_Telltale_Games_Series" title="Game of Thrones: A Telltale Games Series">Game of Thrones: A Telltale Games Series</a></i>, though because so little has been said about them in the novels, this means that the video game isn't prominently contradicting any book information.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">They are called a "clan" in the novel, though this seems to be common for poor smaller Houses northwest of Winterfell. The Northern mountain clans, who live just north of the Wolfswood, are often called "clan" or "House" interchangeably. The clans only control small and poor lands up in the hills, but they are loyal to Winterfell, so the Starks would formally address them as "Houses" out of respect, i.e. the Norreys would be called "House Norrey" as a courtesy. The Forresters in the novels don't seem to be a mountain clan, but maybe "clan" was just being used in this one instance as a broad generalization for "minor vassal House". The in-game map does show that the Forresters and Whitehills are located at the northern edge of the Wolfswood, bordering the hill country where the mountain clans live. Also, the POV narrator of the above quote was actually Asha Greyjoy (renamed as "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yara Greyjoy","link":"Yara Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yara Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137630728456cca94b4b3f8" href="/wiki/Yara_Greyjoy" title="Yara Greyjoy">Yara Greyjoy</a>" in the TV series), who isn't from the North, so in-universe she just might not be very familiar with the local minor noble families and is simply misapplying the term "clan", or using it too broadly - the scouts she's referring to might be from a mix of "mountain clan" Houses plus minor Houses from the Wolfswood. Also, she says these "clans" are sworn to House Glover - but by definition, the Glovers don't rule over <i>any</i> mountain clans, their vassals are all from the Wolfswood to the south. So Asha was probably just misusing the term in this one instance.
</p>
