</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>House Martell</b> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sunspear","link":"Sunspear","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sunspear]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Sunspear" title="Sunspear">Sunspear</a> is one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Houses","link":"Great Houses","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Houses]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Great_Houses" title="Great Houses" class="mw-redirect">Great Houses</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. It rules the peninsula of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> in the far south of the continent from their castle Sunspear. Though loyal to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>, it has pursued a more isolated role in wider political events since <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Martell's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sigil","link":"Heraldry","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Heraldry|sigil]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">sigil</a> is a red sun pierced by a golden spear, on an orange field, a combination of the original Martell sigil - a yellow spear - and the emblem of Princess <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Nymeria","link":"Nymeria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Nymeria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Nymeria" title="Nymeria">Nymeria</a> - a red sun - to symbolize the marriage of the warrior-queen to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mors Martell","link":"Mors Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mors Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Mors_Martell" title="Mors Martell">Mors Martell</a>. The motto of the House is <b>"Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken"</b>​.
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>

<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->House Martell started out as local kings in Dorne, which was long divided between many petty kings of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andals","link":"Andals","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andals]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Andals" title="Andals">Andals</a>. About a thousand years ago, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> refugees <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"fleeing conquest","link":"Rhoynish Wars","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynish Wars|fleeing conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Rhoynish_Wars" title="Rhoynish Wars">fleeing conquest</a> by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> migrated across the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> to Dorne, led by their warrior-queen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Nymeria","link":"Nymeria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Nymeria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Nymeria" title="Nymeria">Nymeria</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mors Martell","link":"Mors Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mors Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Mors_Martell" title="Mors Martell">Mors Martell</a> was smitten with Nymeria and chose to join forces with her through marriage-alliance. With their combined strength, they <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"conquered and unified the rest of Dorne","link":"Nymeria's War","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Nymeria's War|conquered and unified the rest of Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Nymeria's_War" title="Nymeria's War">conquered and unified the rest of Dorne</a>. To symbolize their union, the original golden spear sigil of Mors Martell was combined with the red sun sigil of Nymeria, creating the red sun pierced by a golden spear sigil that House Martell has used ever since. Like their Rhoynar ancestors, House Martell styled its rulers as "princes" instead of "kings".

<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->The Martell words are "Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken" - referring to their history, in which they have never been successfully conquered by outsiders. This makes them unique among all of the Great Houses. During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest|Targaryen Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">Targaryen Conquest</a> (three centuries before the War of the Five Kings), Dorne was the only region of the Seven Kingdoms to successfully resist the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryens","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen|Targaryens]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">Targaryens</a> and their <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"Dragons","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragons|dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a>. Realizing that large armies could not stand against dragons (as seen at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Field of Fire","link":"Field of Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Field of Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Field_of_Fire" title="Field of Fire">Field of Fire</a>), nor large castles (as seen at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Burning of Harrenhal","link":"Burning of Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Burning of Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Burning_of_Harrenhal" title="Burning of Harrenhal">Burning of Harrenhal</a>), the Dornish instead resorted to guerrilla warfare, ambushing Targaryen soldiers and harassing their supply lines as soon as their dragons moved on to the next castle, then disappearing back into the harsh deserts when the dragons returned. The heat of Dorne's deserts also worked to the Martells' advantage, as Dorne's arid climate cannot easily support large invading armies. Losing many men due to attrition, ultimately even <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen|Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon the Conqueror</a> decided to withdraw from Dorne, to focus on reining in the other six newly conquered kingdoms.

<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->House Martell and Dorne have never been <i>successfully</i> conquered. Over a century and a half after the Targaryen Conquest of the rest of Westeros, they were invaded again by King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daeron I Targaryen","link":"Daeron I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daeron I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Daeron_I_Targaryen" title="Daeron I Targaryen">Daeron I Targaryen</a>, the Young Dragon. A military genius, his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Conquest of Dorne","link":"Conquest of Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Conquest of Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Conquest_of_Dorne" title="Conquest of Dorne">Conquest of Dorne</a> actually succeeded - on the battlefield. But the initial conquest was followed by a four year long Dornish insurgency, in which occupying troops were again bled through ambush and guerrilla tactics. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lyonel Tyrell","link":"Lyonel Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lyonel Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Lyonel_Tyrell" title="Lyonel Tyrell">Lyonel Tyrell</a>, the steward that Daeron I put in charge of Dorne, was then assassinated, and all of Dorne rose up in open rebellion. Daeron I himself was killed attempting to put down the insurrection, and after only four short years House Martell once again ruled an independent Dorne.

<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"double-brackets","lineStart":"1","title":"Quote","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"{{Quote|After the tradition of her people, House Martell then ruled Dorne as 'princes', not 'kings' - unless the eldest child was a daughter, for unlike the rest of Westeros, our loyalty isn't commanded by a cock. <!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->Like their <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> ancestors who migrated to Dorne a thousand years ago, Dornish noble Houses follow gender-blind equal primogeniture, in which the eldest child is the heir regardless of whether that child is male or female. This is different from the male-preference inheritance laws in all of the rest of the Seven Kingdoms, the laws of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andals","link":"Andals","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andals]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Andals" title="Andals">Andals</a>, in which a younger son succeeds ahead of an older daughter. In <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a>, for example, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bran","link":"Bran Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bran Stark|Bran]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rickon","link":"Rickon Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rickon Stark|Rickon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Rickon_Stark" title="Rickon Stark">Rickon</a> are officially ahead of their older sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark|Sansa]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a> in the line of succession, and in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister|Tyrion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a> was officially ahead of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cersei","link":"Cersei Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cersei Lannister|Cersei]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei</a> despite being younger than her.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In contrast, a ruler of House Martell is always succeeded by their eldest child, even if it is a daughter. The current ruler of Dorne (when House Martell is fully introduced in Season 5) is Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doran Martell","link":"Doran Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doran Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Doran_Martell" title="Doran Martell">Doran Martell</a>, who has been the head of House Martell for many years. The immediately preceding head of House Martell, however, was Doran's <i>mother</i>, who was also the mother of Elia and Oberyn. Doran's mother actually had a younger brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lewyn Martell","link":"Lewyn Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lewyn Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Lewyn_Martell" title="Lewyn Martell">Lewyn Martell</a>. Under the inheritance laws of the rest of Westeros, Lewyn would have succeeded ahead of Doran's mother, but because Dornish inheritance laws are gender-blind he did not (and therefore he was free to join the elite <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the style of their Rhoynar ancestors who lived in city-states in Essos (near the location of the later <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a>), House Martell styles its rulers as "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Prince of Dorne","link":"Prince of Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prince of Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Prince_of_Dorne" title="Prince of Dorne">Prince of Dorne</a>" (or "Princess") instead of "King" or "Queen". All members of House Martell are called a Prince/Princess, though the current head of the household is the ruling Prince/Princess. There is little functional difference between the "Prince of Dorne" and a "Lord Paramount of the North", though the title "Prince" is considered to be, at least nominally, more prestigious.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Due to uniting with the Iron Throne through marriage-alliance on equal terms instead of through conquest, the Martells were allowed to retain several special privileges, among which were continuing to style their ruler as a "Prince", and continuing to practice gender-blind equal primogeniture.  While many of the Martells are hot-tempered, especially Prince Oberyn, they are a close-knit and loving family, undivided by internal rivalries such as those in House Lannister and House Baratheon.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Martell does not play a major role in events until the third and fourth books in the series, only being referenced in the first two.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Martell was originally a tiny, obscure noble house of Dorne back when the peninsula was home to numerous small, warring states, approximately one thousand years ago. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a>, refugees from a war against <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyria","link":"Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Valyria" title="Valyria" class="mw-redirect">Valyria</a> on the eastern continent, fled to Westeros led by their warrior-queen Nymeria, where they made landfall in Dorne and allied with Lord Mors Martell. After a brief war, Mors named himself the ruler of the unified Kingdom of Dorne, adopting the title "Prince" rather than "King" in Rhoynish fashion. Mors married Nymeria, uniting the bloodlines of the Rhoynar with the native First Men and Andals. As a result, the full name of the family is actually "House Nymeros Martell", with "Nymeros" signifying "of Nymeria". For example, Oberyn's full name is given as "Oberyn Nymeros Martell". However, even the Martells themselves do not usually use the full name, with the inclusion of "Nymeros", except on very formal occasions.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon I Targaryen","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon I Targaryen</a> invaded Westeros, he conquered six of the seven kingdoms extant at that time, but the Dornish refused to give open battle where Aegon could destroy them with his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a>, instead resorting to hit-and-run attacks which severely damaged his army through attrition. Aegon agreed to leave Dorne to its own devices after trying to conquer it again during the First Dornish War of 4 AC to 13 AC. However, the Dornish once again prevailed and the Martells managed to kill Aegon's wife, Rhaenys, before forcing the Conqueror to agree to peace on their terms. Aegon never tried conquering Dorne again. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">A century and a half later, Dorne was briefly occupied by the armies of King Daeron I, but overthrew them in a bloody rebellion. Eventually, roughly a century before the events of the series, Dorne joined the Seven Kingdoms through a peaceful marriage alliance. House Martell's motto, "Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken", directly references the fact that they were never conquered by outsiders. Because they came under the authority of the Iron Throne through marriage instead of the sword, House Martell and the other Dornish houses continue to practice absolute primogeniture, in which the eldest child is the designated heir, regardless of whether they are male or female. Another major difference is that the rulers of Dorne are styled ruling Princes (or ruling Princesses) instead of Lords Paramount. At the time of the beginning of the narrative the current head of House Martell is the aging Prince Doran. His daughter and eldest child is Arianne Martell: despite being female, she has been actively groomed to succeed her father, as are eldest daughters all over Dorne. In his old age and infirmity (though he retains his wits) Doran has semi-retired, delegating most of the day-to-day governance of Dorne to Arianne. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Martells were loyal vassals of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>, with Princess Elia Martell marrying Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegar Targaryen","link":"Rhaegar Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar Targaryen</a> and bearing him two children. During <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>, the Martells were unhappy with Rhaegar's betrayal of Elia by taking <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lyanna Stark","link":"Lyanna Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lyanna Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Lyanna_Stark" title="Lyanna Stark">Lyanna Stark</a> as his paramour and sparking a war over the matter. Still, they supported King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a>'s bid to retain the throne, predominantly due to Aerys effectively holding Elia as a hostage to ensure their loyalty. The Martells lost many soldiers at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a> and Princess Elia and her children were brutally killed during the Sack of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannister","link":"Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Lannister" title="Lannister" class="mw-redirect">Lannister</a> soldiers. Ironically, the Martells had only grudgingly stayed loyal to the Targaryens during the civil war, but the needless and brutal murder of Elia turned Dorne into a hotbed of pro-Targaryen sentiment (to the point that, after the Sack of King's Landing, Oberyn had wanted to continue the fighting on behalf of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys Targaryen","link":"Viserys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys Targaryen</a>). Though House Martell swore fealty to King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> after the war (though this was only due to extensive peace negotiations on the part of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Arryn","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>), they have harbored anger and resentment towards the Lannisters ever since and have adopted a mostly isolationist policy ever since, not mixing with the other Great Houses unless totally necessary. As a result, the Dornish have stronger relations with the Free Cities of Essos than the rest of Westeros.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Martells and Tyrells have been sworn enemies for many generations. Over the centuries, they have fought border wars beyond count, and raided back and forth across mountains and marches even when at peace. The enmity between the two houses waned a bit after Dorne became part of the Seven Kingdoms, but the maiming of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Willas Tyrell","link":"Willas Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Willas Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Willas_Tyrell" title="Willas Tyrell">Willas Tyrell</a> by the Dornish prince Oberyn Martell in a jouse fanned the animosity between the families, though Willas himself does not hold any grudge against Oberyn, as both men knew what happened was simply an unfortunate accident.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the second novel, Varys reports Tyrion that prince Doran Martell has called his banners, and there are rumors that he intends to join Renly (Renly tells Catelyn the same thing). Knowing that an alliance between Renly and Dorne will be disastrous to the Lannisters, Tyrion take steps to prevent it by offering prince Doran to marry Myrcella to Trystane, his sister’s killer, a seat at the Small Council, and some castles on the Marches. Despite Cersei's protests, Tyrion sends Myrcella to Dorne as a token of good faith. The Martells stay neutral throughout the war, although it is unclear whether the reason is Tyrion's offer, or the Martells bide their time. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">George R.R. Martin has said that the production team isn't certain how many members of House Martell they can fit into the TV series. Doran has three children, while Oberyn and Ellaria have eight daughters. Even for House Tyrell, which has four children in the current generation, two have apparently been cut from the TV series (Loras and Margaery's older brothers from the books). Conversely, in Season 2 the production team thought they might have to cut <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>'s only child, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shireen","link":"Shireen Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shireen Baratheon|Shireen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-2851409056cca94780eb3" href="/wiki/Shireen_Baratheon" title="Shireen Baratheon">Shireen</a>, but subsequently was able to introduce her in Season 3. While Oberyn and Ellaria do appear in Season 4, it remains to be seen how many other Martells can be introduced into the TV continuity in later seasons.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Martell's name might have been inspired by Charles Martel (Charles the Hammer), who led the Franks to turn back the Muslim invasion of France at the Battle of Tours in 732.
