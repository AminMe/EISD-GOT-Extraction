</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Slynt","link":"House Slynt","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Slynt]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/House_Slynt" title="House Slynt">House Slynt</a> is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"noble house","link":"noble house","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[noble house]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Noble_house" title="Noble house">noble house</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. It currently holds fealty directly to King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey Baratheon","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey Baratheon</a>, who ordered the founding of the house as a reward for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Janos Slynt","link":"Janos Slynt","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Janos Slynt]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Janos_Slynt" title="Janos Slynt">Janos Slynt</a>'s assistance in securing the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> for him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">For his loyalty, Slynt was named <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Harrenhal","link":"Lord of Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Harrenhal" title="Lord of Harrenhal">Lord of Harrenhal</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a> is a large and impressive (but also ill-omened) castle in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverlands","link":"Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Riverlands" title="Riverlands" class="mw-redirect">Riverlands</a>, on the lake known as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gods Eye","link":"Gods Eye","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gods Eye]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Gods_Eye" title="Gods Eye">Gods Eye</a>. However, Harrenhal was held by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Whent","link":"House Whent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Whent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/House_Whent" title="House Whent">House Whent</a>, a vassal house of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tullys","link":"House Tully","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tully|Tullys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/House_Tully" title="House Tully">Tullys</a>, so Slynt couldn't take possession of the castle until the current war between the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannisters","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister|Lannisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannisters</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Starks","link":"House Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark|Starks]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">Starks</a> is resolved. Before the war could end, Janos was exiled to the Wall to join the Night's Watch for his crimes, where he was eventually executed for insubordination by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a>, bringing an end to House Slynt as quick as its rise.
</p>

However, he then betrays Eddard and has his men kill Eddard's bodyguards and retainers, with Eddard being imprisoned.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[You Win or You Die]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> As a reward for his service, Janos Slynt is named the Lord of Harrenhal by the king and his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"small council","link":"small council","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[small council]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Small_council" title="Small council" class="mw-redirect">small council</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Pointy End]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 2","link":"Season 2","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">Season 2</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Lord Janos loses Harrenhal after being exiled to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a>. Now under occupation by House Lannister, Harrenhal is granted to Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a>, and House Slynt has no seat.
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Janos is executed for insubordination by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander","link":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander of the Night's Watch|Lord Commander]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch" title="Lord Commander of the Night's Watch">Lord Commander</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a>. As Slynt apparently left no family or heirs behind, this effectively means the end of House Slynt.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Slynt is founded as a reward for Janos Slynt's assistance in arresting Eddard Stark. Janos takes for his sigil a bloody spear, gold on a night-black field.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-55749321156cca96f8b46c" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a> ridicules both Slynt's ascension to lordship, and his choice of sigil when he hears of it, commenting that a "bloody meat cleaver would be more appropriate", given the fact that Slynt himself was the lowborn son of a butcher. At Tywin's order, Tyrion dismisses Janos when he arrives in the capital, exiling him to the Night's Watch and stripping his family of Harrenhal - which he never actually set foot in. Tyrion felt Janos wasn't trustworthy enough to keep around the royal court, but he did allow Janos's children to keep their recently ennobled status. "House Slynt" therefore continued to nominally exist, but possessed no lands. Tyrion said that he would try to find lesser lands for Janos's sons to hold but it is unclear if he ever did. Otherwise, Janos's sons continue to appear as hangers-on at the royal court.
</p>
