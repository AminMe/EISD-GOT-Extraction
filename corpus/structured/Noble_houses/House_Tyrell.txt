</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>House Tyrell of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Highgarden","link":"Highgarden","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Highgarden]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Highgarden" title="Highgarden">Highgarden</a></b> is one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Houses","link":"Great Houses","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Houses]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Great_Houses" title="Great Houses" class="mw-redirect">Great Houses</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. Mace's son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Loras","link":"Loras Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Loras Tyrell|Loras]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Loras_Tyrell" title="Loras Tyrell">Loras</a> is a noted <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"tournament","link":"tournament","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[tournament]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Tournament" title="Tournament">tournament</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"knight","link":"knight","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[knight]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Knight" title="Knight" class="mw-redirect">knight</a> (and, secretly, the lover of Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly Baratheon","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a>). <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">House Tyrell began as a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"cadet branch","link":"Cadet branch","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cadet branch|cadet branch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Cadet_branch" title="Cadet branch">cadet branch</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Gardener","link":"House Gardener","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Gardener]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/House_Gardener" title="House Gardener">House Gardener</a>, the ancient Kings of the Reach. The Tyrells became senior servants of the main Gardener line, serving for centuries as castellans of the royal castle at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Highgarden","link":"Highgarden","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Highgarden]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Highgarden" title="Highgarden">Highgarden</a>. Over time they rose to prominence as one of the strongest noble Houses in the Reach, and even intermarried with the royal line - though so did many other powerful Houses from the Reach.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest|Targaryen Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">Targaryen Conquest</a>, King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mern Gardener","link":"Mern Gardener","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mern Gardener]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Mern_Gardener" title="Mern Gardener" class="mw-redirect">Mern Gardener</a> was burned alive at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Field of Fire","link":"Field of Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Field of Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Field_of_Fire" title="Field of Fire">Field of Fire</a> by the Targaryen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a>, along with all of his immediate heirs. Subsequently, Mern's steward <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harlen Tyrell","link":"Harlen Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harlen Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Harlen_Tyrell" title="Harlen Tyrell">Harlen Tyrell</a> voluntarily surrendered Highgarden to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon Targaryen","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen|Aegon Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon Targaryen</a>. Aegon rewarded Harlen by making him Lord of Highgarden and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Paramount of the Reach","link":"Lord Paramount of the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Paramount of the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Lord_Paramount_of_the_Reach" title="Lord Paramount of the Reach">Lord Paramount of the Reach</a> - ahead of other Houses from the Reach which were actually more closely related to House Gardener.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"House Tyrell viewers guide\">[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/guide\/houses\/tyrell\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 guide to houses, House Tyrell entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Chief among these is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Florent","link":"House Florent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Florent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/House_Florent" title="House Florent">House Florent</a> of Brightwater Keep, which claims direct male-line descent from the old Gardener kings, while House Tyrell only claim descent from House Gardener through the female line.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Harlen_Tyrell_surrender_to_Aegon_the_Conqueror.png|thumb|Harlen Tyrell surrenders to Aegon the Conqueror.]]","title":"Harlen_Tyrell_surrender_to_Aegon_the_Conqueror.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Harlen Tyrell surrender to Aegon the Conqueror","caption":"Harlen Tyrell surrenders to Aegon the Conqueror.","captionParsed":"Harlen Tyrell surrenders to Aegon the Conqueror."}}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" alt="" src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/b/b7/Harlen_Tyrell_surrender_to_Aegon_the_Conqueror.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20130615085357" width="180" height="101" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Harlen Tyrell surrender to Aegon the Conqueror.png" data-image-key="Harlen_Tyrell_surrender_to_Aegon_the_Conqueror.png"  type="image" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Tyrell's overall disposition is adequately represented by their sigil of a rose: a rose's beauty often hides the fact that it has thorns. The Tyrells do try to be just and fair most of the time, however this often lulls their enemies into thinking that like the Starks, they are utterly beholden to honorable conduct. In reality, the Tyrells are just as cunning as the Lannisters in court politics and intrigue, though they often prefer a more subtle approach. Thus they strike a pragmatic balance between the Starks' honor and the Lannisters' ruthlessness.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Throughout history, House Tyrell has played by the strategy of following the sure path, siding with whoever is most likely to win in any conflict. During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest|Targaryen Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">Targaryen Conquest</a>, they surrendered Highgarden to the invading forces of Aegon the Conqueror, and in return the Tyrells were elevated as the new Lords Paramount of the Reach. During <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>, House Tyrell remained loyal to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a>, as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> was a rebel unlikely to win, and the Tyrells owed their rule of the Reach to the Targaryens. Forces of House Tyrell managed to inflict the only loss Robert suffered during the war, albeit an indecisive one, at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Ashford","link":"Battle of Ashford","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Ashford]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Ashford" title="Battle of Ashford">Battle of Ashford</a>. However, the vast army and resources of House Tyrell were tied up on a siege of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a> (held for Robert by his brother; <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>). After the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mad King","link":"Mad King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mad King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Mad_King" title="Mad King" class="mw-redirect">Mad King</a>'s death, the Tyrells surrendered and swore fealty to Robert Baratheon. <img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Tyrell_surrender_to_Robert.png|thumb|Mace Tyrell surrenders to Robert Baratheon.]]","title":"Tyrell_surrender_to_Robert.png","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Tyrell surrender to Robert","caption":"Mace Tyrell surrenders to Robert Baratheon.","captionParsed":"Mace Tyrell surrenders to Robert Baratheon."}}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" alt="" src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/3/35/Tyrell_surrender_to_Robert.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20130615075308" width="180" height="101" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Tyrell surrender to Robert.png" data-image-key="Tyrell_surrender_to_Robert.png"  type="image" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the current generation, House Tyrell is a stable and honestly loving family, with none of the internal rivalries that are to be found in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a>. Thus the relationships between Lord Mace and his children, and between siblings like Loras and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Margaery","link":"Margaery Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Margaery Tyrell|Margaery]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Margaery_Tyrell" title="Margaery Tyrell">Margaery</a>, are genuine and uncomplicated.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Military strength</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The Reach is the most fertile and populous region of Westeros, allowing House Tyrell to gain immense wealth (through taxation of the large population) and also to muster an enormous army of 100,000 men. The Tyrells can typically raise and equip almost twice as many soldiers as any of the other Great Houses in Westeros.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The HBO Viewer's Guide for Season 1 lists Mace Tyrell and Alerie Hightower as having four children – <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Willas","link":"Willas Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Willas Tyrell|Willas]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Willas_Tyrell" title="Willas Tyrell">Willas</a>, Garlan, Loras and Margaery – in keeping with the books. Willas and Garlan were not mentioned at all on-screen, were removed from the Viewer's Guide for Season 2, and did not reappear in the Viewer's Guide for Season 3. Loras was explicitly confirmed as being the heir to Highgarden in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Climb","link":"The Climb","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Climb]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/The_Climb" title="The Climb">The Climb</a>". Willas and Garlan's roles in the narrative (the Tyrell who was to wed Sansa Stark and the Tyrell who asked for Margaery to marry Joffrey) had already been given to Loras on-screen. 
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Tyrell is a powerful noble house. The Reach is the most populous region of the Seven Kingdoms and the Tyrells can field the largest army on the continent, although the Lannisters, being richer, can better-equip their troops. This makes the Tyrells formidable enemies but excellent allies.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Tyrell never ruled as "kings and queens", as opposed to the Starks, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannister","link":"Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Lannister" title="Lannister" class="mw-redirect">Lannisters</a>, or <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arryn","link":"Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Arryn" title="Arryn" class="mw-redirect">Arryns</a>. Before King Aegon I Targaryen's invaded and united the Seven Kingdoms, the Tyrells were stewards to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Gardener","link":"House Gardener","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Gardener]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/House_Gardener" title="House Gardener">House Gardener</a>, the Kings of the Reach. The Tyrells had responsibility for maintaining the castle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Highgarden","link":"Highgarden","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Highgarden]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Highgarden" title="Highgarden">Highgarden</a>, the seat of royal power in the Reach. In the battle known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Field of Fire","link":"Field of Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Field of Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Field_of_Fire" title="Field of Fire">Field of Fire</a>, King Mern IX Gardener and all of his issue were burned alive by Aegon's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a>. Harlen Tyrell, King Mern's steward, surrendered Hightower to King Aegon and was rewarded with the title of Lord Paramount of the Reach and Warden of the South.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Florent","link":"House Florent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Florent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/House_Florent" title="House Florent">House Florent</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brightwater Keep","link":"Brightwater Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brightwater Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Brightwater_Keep" title="Brightwater Keep">Brightwater Keep</a> has long disputed the suitability of the Tyrells to the rule of the Reach, on the grounds that they can claim a closer blood-relationship to the old kings of House Gardener. House Florent is a cadet branch of House Gardener, founded by a younger son of the main Gardener line, while House Tyrell can only claim descent from House Gardener through the female line.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As a result the Florents have often bucked the authority of the Tyrells in the past three centuries since the Targaryen conquest. It came as little surprise when after Renly died and House Tyrell led their vassals in switching to support King Joffrey and the Lannisters, House Florent was the only major House from the Reach to instead switch allegiance to Stannis. Indeed, Stannis's wife is herself a Florent.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Thus the Tyrells, while always maintaining firm control of the Reach, have always feared that they have an uneasy grip on their rule. While the Tyrells possess very fertile lands from which they can raise large armies, there are other powerful noble families in the Reach, such as House Florent, which don't particularly respect them the way the vassals of the Starks and Lannisters respect them because they used to be their kings. A great deal of skill at court politics has thus been needed to keep the Tyrells' powerful vassals in check, though Tyrells like Lady <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Olenna","link":"Olenna Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Olenna Tyrell|Olenna]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Olenna_Tyrell" title="Olenna Tyrell">Olenna</a> possess this in abundance. Therefore, House Tyrell is always on the lookout to secure a marriage into a royal line, to strengthen their prestige and cement their rule over the Reach.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Martells and Tyrells have been sworn enemies for many generations. Over the centuries, they have fought border wars beyond count, and raided back and forth across mountains and marches even when at peace. The enmity between the two houses waned a bit after Dorne became part of the Seven Kingdoms, but the maiming of Willas Tyrell by the Dornish prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Oberyn Martell","link":"Oberyn Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Oberyn Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Oberyn_Martell" title="Oberyn Martell">Oberyn Martell</a> in a jouse fanned the animosity between the families, though Willas himself does not hold any grudge against Oberyn, as both men knew what happened was simply an unfortunate accident.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"D.B. Weiss","link":"D.B. Weiss","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[D.B. Weiss]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> summed it up in a Season 2 featurette, in the current generation House Tyrell is a closet matriarchy of sorts. While dependable, honorable, and loyal, the men of House Tyrell aren't particularly intelligent or adept at politics. Instead, the female members of House Tyrell (such as Margaery and her father's mother Olenna) are the intelligent ones who actively plan out the political course that House Tyrell will take. An exception to this is Lord Mace's eldest son Willas, who unlike his father and two younger brothers (<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Garlan","link":"Garlan Tyrell","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Garlan Tyrell|Garlan]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Garlan_Tyrell" title="Garlan Tyrell">Garlan</a> and Loras), is said to be quite well-educated.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Since being elevated to rule of the Reach, during any major Westeros-wide conflict the Tyrells have frequently followed the strategy of pragmatically siding with whoever seems most likely to win, rather than fighting for abstract principles of law or honor. During the Targaryen Conquest they surrendered to the Targaryens willingly in return for their favor. In Robert's Rebellion they sided with the Mad King because they owed the Targaryens everything, and didn't think Robert Baratheon could defeat him. During the civil war known as the Dance of the Dragons, however, the Tyrells judged that the faction of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","link":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenyra Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Rhaenyra_Targaryen" title="Rhaenyra Targaryen">Rhaenyra Targaryen</a> and the faction of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II Targaryen","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II Targaryen</a> were so evenly matched that it was not obvious at the outset which side would win. With neither side having a clear advantage over the other, to the surprise of both, the Tyrells declared their neutrality and took no part in the war (though their bannermen were heavily divided between the two sides).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">By the end of the fifth novel, Kevan becomes concerned because the Tyrells increase their power gradually on the expense of the Lannisters: they have three of the seats in the Small Council; a hundred of Highgarden men have been added to the gold cloaks; Randyll Tarly and Mace Tyrell have both brought armies to King’s Landing, while most of the Lannister troops are away in the riverlands.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Tyrell" is pronounced "TI-rul", as opposed to "Tie-rell", etc. In spite of this, several characters, including Lady Olenna, have inconsistently alternated with pronouncing it "TIE-rell".<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Dark Wings, Dark Words]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Even <i>within the same scene</i> in Season 3's "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Walk of Punishment","link":"Walk of Punishment","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Walk of Punishment]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69371430356cca942db651" href="/wiki/Walk_of_Punishment" title="Walk of Punishment">Walk of Punishment</a>," Cersei says "Tie-rell" while Tywin says "Tih-rul".
