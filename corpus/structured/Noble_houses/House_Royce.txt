<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Royces are the most powerful family in the Vale after the Arryns, and they are extremely loyal to House Arryn. House Royce is descended from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a>, which is a very rare exception in the Vale, as the First Men were almost completely driven from the Vale during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andal Invasion","link":"Andal Invasion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andal Invasion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Andal_Invasion" title="Andal Invasion">Andal Invasion</a> six thousand years ago. This extends to the point that in modern times the nobility of the Vale are considered to represent the purest <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andal","link":"Andal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Andal" title="Andal" class="mw-redirect">Andal</a> bloodlines, exterminating the local First Men instead of intermingling with them. However, House Royce managed to survive, and is very proud of their descent from the First Men, to the point that they include runes of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Tongue","link":"Old Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a> of the First Men in their sigil.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The members of House Royce are:
</p>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Lord Yohn Royce, called Bronze Yohn, Lord of Runestone, the ruler of the main branch of the family.
<ul><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Ser Andar Royce, his eldest son and heir.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Ser {Robar Royce}, his second son, known as Robar the Red since joining Renly Baratheon's Rainbow Guard. Slain by Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Loras Tyrell","link":"Loras Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Loras Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Loras_Tyrell" title="Loras Tyrell">Loras Tyrell</a>.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Ser {Waymar Royce}, a ranger of the Night's Watch. Killed by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Others","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers|Others]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">Others</a>.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Ysilla Royce, his daughter.
</li></ul>
</li></ul>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, House Royce has a cadet branch ruling from the Gates of the Moon. However, since the Gates of the Moon do not apparently exist in the TV series, it is unclear if this cadet branch has also been eliminated. This branch consists of:
</p>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Nestor Royce, a cousin of Yohn's, Keeper of the Gates of the Moon, High Steward of the Vale, ruler of the cadet branch of the family.
<ul><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Albar Royce, Nestor's son and heir.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Myranda Royce, Nestor's daughter.
</li></ul>
</li></ul>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lady Jeyne of House Royce was Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Arryn","link":"Jon Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a>'s first wife who died delivering a stillborn daughter.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Two other members are the deceased Lady Perra of House Royce, Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Walder Frey","link":"Walder Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Walder Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Walder_Frey" title="Walder Frey">Walder Frey</a>'s first wife and Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stevron Frey","link":"Stevron Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stevron Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Stevron_Frey" title="Stevron Frey">Stevron Frey</a>'s mother, and Lady Ryella of House Royce who is married to Ser Arwood Frey, Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Walder Frey","link":"Walder Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Walder Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Walder_Frey" title="Walder Frey">Walder Frey</a>'s grandson. Mya Stone, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bastard","link":"Bastardy","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bastardy|bastard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Bastardy" title="Bastardy">bastard</a> daughter of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>, serves as a mountain guide to the cadet branch of House Royce.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Relationship to House Stark</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">House Royce holds a blood-relationship to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the North","link":"the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">the North</a>: while it is an old connection, they still consider the Starks their kin, combined with their shared heritage as descendants of the First Men.  Eddard Stark's paternal grandfather was Edwyle Stark, and Edwyle's younger sister Jocelyn Stark married Benedict Royce (apparently from the junior branch of the family).  Jocelyn and Benedict had several children and at least three daughters, who married into <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Waynwood","link":"House Waynwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Waynwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/House_Waynwood" title="House Waynwood">House Waynwood</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Corbray","link":"House Corbray","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Corbray]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/House_Corbray" title="House Corbray">House Corbray</a>, and House Templeton.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lord Yohn Royce was the most vocal proponent among the Vale lords for openly joining Robb Stark's side during the War of the Five Kings (though Lady Lysa was able to suppress these sentiments and enforce the Vale's neutrality).  After the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>, House Royce is close to open revolt over Lysa's failure to aid Robb Stark in his war, and the Waynwoods, Redforts, Belmores, and Templetons are giving them every support.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After hearing of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of Winterfell","link":"Sack of Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Sack_of_Winterfell" title="Sack of Winterfell">Sack of Winterfell</a>, and the assumed death of his younger brothers, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s mother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark|Catelyn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn</a> urged her son to officially name one of his distant cousins from House Royce as his heir. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark|Arya]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a> was also assumed dead at the time, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark|Sansa]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a> had just been forced to marry <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a>, and both correctly feared that should he die, the Lannisters were planning on installing any child born between Sansa and Tyrion as their puppet to rule the North. Robb disagreed on the grounds that it was still a distant relationship, and said he would prefer to name his bastard half-brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> as his heir, because given that Robb was a king he could simply declare Jon to be <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"legitimized","link":"Legitimization","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Legitimization|legitimized]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-3352763556cca95ba5018" href="/wiki/Legitimization" title="Legitimization">legitimized</a>. Despite Catelyn's objections, Robb issued a decree that legitimized Jon Snow and name him his heir until a son was born to him from Jeyne Westerling, and several of the lords signed the decree as witnesses. It is unknown what became of the decree, as it is no longer mentioned in the books.
</p>
