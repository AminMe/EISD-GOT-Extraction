</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>House Qoherys</b> is an extinct noble house of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138221508556cca97ce085d" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a>. Their sigil was a flaming saltire, red and yellow, between 4 white skulls, on black.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Their seat was the castle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138221508556cca97ce085d" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>, which was granted to them by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon the Conqueror","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138221508556cca97ce085d" href="/wiki/Aegon_the_Conqueror" title="Aegon the Conqueror" class="mw-redirect">Aegon the Conqueror</a> as a reward for their service in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138221508556cca97ce085d" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">War of Conquest</a>. However, the Qoherys eventually became extinct, and the castle was passed on to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Towers","link":"House Towers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Towers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138221508556cca97ce085d" href="/wiki/House_Towers" title="House Towers">House Towers</a>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138221508556cca97ce085d" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Qoherys' backstory is the same. They were battle-companions of Aegon the Conqueror, and like <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138221508556cca97ce085d" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a> they were of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian","link":"Valyrian","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-138221508556cca97ce085d" href="/wiki/Valyrian" title="Valyrian" class="mw-redirect">Valyrian</a> descent.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The founder of House Qoherys was the castellan of Dragonstone. Their line did not last long: soon after Aegon the Conqueror's death, less than forty years later, the founder's grandson was killed in a rebellion, and all of the castle's inhabitants were put to the sword, rendering House Qoherys extinct. They were replaced as the rulers of Harrenhal by House Harroway.
</p>
