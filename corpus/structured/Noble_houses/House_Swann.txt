<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110229628356cca97c529aa" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, they are regarded as proud, powerful and cautious. During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-110229628356cca97c529aa" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, even though Ser Balon Swann remains in court, and Ser Donnel Swann declares for Renly, Lord Gulian remains in his castle without raising his army.
</p>
