</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>House Lothston</b> is an extinct noble house of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-72433267056cca97e4ff67" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a>. Their sigil was a black bat on a field divided bendwise, silver and gold.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Their seat was the castle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-72433267056cca97e4ff67" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>, which was granted to them after the extinction of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Strong","link":"House Strong","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Strong]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-72433267056cca97e4ff67" href="/wiki/House_Strong" title="House Strong">House Strong</a>. However, the Lothstons also died out, like all the Houses to hold Harrenhal, and the castle was passed on to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Whent","link":"House Whent","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Whent]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-72433267056cca97e4ff67" href="/wiki/House_Whent" title="House Whent">House Whent</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">One of the members of House Lothston is the stuff of tales. Lady <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Danelle Lothston","link":"Danelle Lothston","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Danelle Lothston]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-72433267056cca97e4ff67" href="/wiki/Danelle_Lothston" title="Danelle Lothston">Danelle Lothston</a>, also known as "Mad Lady Lothston", who was said to send a giant bat to capture children for her cookpots, to bathe in blood, and serve feasts of human flesh.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-72433267056cca97e4ff67" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Lothston's backstory is the same. They are considered an ill-omened house, and their sigil is reviled in several places. Descendants of the Lothstons still live in exile in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-72433267056cca97e4ff67" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a>. </p>
