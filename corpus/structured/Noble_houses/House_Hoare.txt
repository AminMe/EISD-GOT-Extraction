</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>House Hoare</b> is an extinct <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great House","link":"Great Houses","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Houses|Great House]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Great_Houses" title="Great Houses" class="mw-redirect">Great House</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. It ruled over the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Islands","link":"Iron Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Iron_Islands" title="Iron Islands">Iron Islands</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a> when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon I Targaryen","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon I Targaryen</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"invaded","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest|invaded]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">invaded</a> the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. Their sigil depicts per saltire and with two heavy gold chains crossing between a silver longship on black, a dark green pine on white, a cluster of red grapes on gold, and a black raven flying in a blue sky.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In past centuries, it was said that following the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Way","link":"Iron price","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron price|Old Way]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Iron_price" title="Iron price">Old Way</a> of reaving and raiding, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> held dominion along the coasts as far inland as the sound of the waves was heard. While this may be somewhat exaggerated, House Hoare of the Iron Islands conquered and held lands as far afield as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bear Island","link":"Bear Island","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bear Island]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Bear_Island" title="Bear Island">Bear Island</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The North","link":"The North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">The North</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Arbor","link":"The Arbor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Arbor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/The_Arbor" title="The Arbor">The Arbor</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Reach","link":"the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/The_Reach" title="The Reach">the Reach</a>. However, as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kings","link":"King in the North","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King in the North|Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">Kings</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> consolidated the North and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kings","link":"King of the Reach","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Reach|Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Reach" title="King of the Reach">Kings</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Gardener","link":"House Gardener","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Gardener]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/House_Gardener" title="House Gardener">House Gardener</a> consolidated the Reach, the ironborn were eventually driven from Bear Island and the Arbor.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Three generations before the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest|Targaryen Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">Targaryen Conquest</a>, House Hoare launched a second era of expansion, this time pushing inland to conquer all of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a> from the rule of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm King","link":"Storm King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Storm_King" title="Storm King">Storm King</a>, which had conquered the Riverlands some three centuries before Aegon's landing. The leaders of House Hoare ruled as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kings of the Iron Islands and the Riverlands","link":"King of the Iron Islands and the Riverlands","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Iron Islands and the Riverlands|Kings of the Iron Islands and the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Iron_Islands_and_the_Riverlands" title="King of the Iron Islands and the Riverlands" class="mw-redirect">Kings of the Iron Islands and the Riverlands</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The sigil of House Hoare was diagonally quartered, displaying (clockwise from the top) a longship, a pine tree, red grapes, and a black raven, representing all the distant lands that had been under the rule of the House: the longship for the Iron Islands, the green pine for the Bear Island (the northern border of their dominion), the cluster for the Arbor (the southernmost) and the black raven for Raventree Hall in the Riverlands; all bound by the iron chains of the ironborn.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harren Hoare","link":"Harren Hoare","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harren Hoare]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Harren_Hoare" title="Harren Hoare">Harren Hoare</a> (whose grandfather had conquered the Riverlands) intended to build the greatest castle on the entire continent, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>, on the north shore of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gods Eye","link":"Gods Eye","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gods Eye]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Gods_Eye" title="Gods Eye">Gods Eye</a> lake in the Riverlands. It was completed on the same day that Aegon Targaryen set foot in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. Harren was very cruel and arrogant, thus many of his vassals in the Riverlands - led by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Edmyn Tully","link":"Edmyn Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Edmyn Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Edmyn_Tully" title="Edmyn Tully">Edmyn Tully</a> - rebelled against him and sided with Aegon Targaryen's invading force. The high walls of Harrenhal could have repelled any attack from the ground, but Aegon's dragons flew right over them. Dragonfire roasted King Harren alive within his own tower, along with all of his sons, rendering House Hoare extinct.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the extinction of House Hoare, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> elected a new house to rule over them, under the overall rule of the Targaryen Kings on the Iron Throne: <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Greyjoy","link":"House Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/House_Greyjoy" title="House Greyjoy">House Greyjoy</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to tales and stories, the flaming ghosts of Harren and his sons still haunt <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harrenhal","link":"Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Hoare of Orkmont is an ancient ironborn family from the time of the First Men.  During the era of the kingsmoot, several Hoares were chosen as kings.  The most famous ironborn king in this era:  under King Qhored Hoare the realm of the ironborn reached the peak of its expansion, and it was said that all the western coast of Westeros where the salt of the sea could be smelled or heard the crashing of waves were under their control. Although it is unlikely that this assertion is literal, since with all probability places like Oldtown or Lannisport have never been ruled from the Iron Islands, it is still remarkable that the ironborn could hold places as distant as the Arbor and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bear Island","link":"Bear Island","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bear Island]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Bear_Island" title="Bear Island">Bear Island</a>.  Qhored Hoare personally ended the line of House Justman, rulers of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"The Riverlands","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Riverlands|the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a> at the time.  This plunged the Riverlands into anarchy for six years, until House Teague seized power there.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the next decades, the power of House Hoare would diminish and many of the conquests would be lost. Bear Island was lost to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rodrik Stark","link":"Rodrik Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rodrik Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Rodrik_Stark" title="Rodrik Stark">Rodrik Stark</a> in a wrestling match. He gave it to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mormonts","link":"House Mormont","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Mormont|Mormonts]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/House_Mormont" title="House Mormont">Mormonts</a>. The possessions of the ironborn were reduced to the Isles and the coast of the Riverlands.    
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Greyiron put an end to the kingsmoots after seizing power and ruled as hereditary kings for 1,000 years, though the other ironborn Houses chafed under their rule.  About 4,000 years ago, however, the Andals finally got around to conquering the Iron Islands - they had first invaded the eastern portions of Westeros 6,000 years ago, but it took them many centuries before they finally came to the Iron Islands, because they were far away on the western coast, separated from the mainland, and very poor in and of themselves making them not an attractive target.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Hoare was not an Andal family, but along with several other ironborn Houses they allied with invading Andal adventurers in order to overthrow the hated reign of the Greyirons.  This is similar to how even <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a> started out as a First Men family before intermarrying with the Andals.  The head of House Hoare married the daughter of one of the Andal chieftains who came to the isles:  in a subsequent contest House Hoare were proclaimed as the new hereditary kings of the Iron Islands.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Andals that came to the Iron Islands acculturated to the ironborn traditions, however, and little changed on the isles.  House Hoare (now partially descended from the Andals) continued to rule the Iron islands for almost four thousand years.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">About three generations before the Targaryen Conquest, the ironborn under House Hoare entered into a new great age of conquest.  The ironborn took all of the Riverlands from the Storm Kings, who had killed the last River King 300 years before. Harren the Black ordered the construction of Harrenhal, the greatest castle of the Seven Kingdoms, on the shores of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gods Eye","link":"Gods Eye","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gods Eye]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Gods_Eye" title="Gods Eye">Gods Eye</a>, a long away from the sea. The construction took forty years, and was finished the day Aegon I and his sisters landed in Westeros. Believing himself safe within his walls, Harren decided to remain within his castle. Aegon burned it with his dragon, incinerating Harren and his sons alive and thus ending the Hoare line. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Harren's own younger brother was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","link":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander of the Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch" title="Lord Commander of the Night's Watch">Lord Commander of the Night's Watch</a> at the time of the Targaryen Conquest, but kept to his vows of neutrality and stayed at the Wall instead of leaving to help his family, even as his own brother burned at Harrenhal and his entire House was wiped out.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Hoare officially went extinct when Harren died, but it only went extinct in the male-line. House Volmark, an ironborn noble family from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harlaw","link":"Harlaw","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harlaw]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/Harlaw" title="Harlaw">Harlaw</a>, descends from House Hoare through the female line (much as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tyrell","link":"House Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">House Tyrell</a> descends from the officially extinct <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Gardener","link":"House Gardener","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Gardener]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-31357706256cca95607cad" href="/wiki/House_Gardener" title="House Gardener">House Gardener</a> through the female line).  Specifically, House Volmark claims descent from the younger sister of Harwyn Hoare, the grandfather of Harren Hoare.  House Volmark never had a particularly strong claim against House Greyjoy, however, due to a combination of the ironborn being misogynistic and not placing much value on female-line descent, and because of the unusual elected-kingship customs of the Iron Islands.  The Volmarks did try to lead uprisings against the Greyjoys in the first two generations after the Targaryen Conquest, but they had little support and were easily put down.  House Volmark is still active in the present day.  
</p>
