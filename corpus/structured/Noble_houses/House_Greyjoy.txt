<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131797447356cca94925907" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Greyjoys have been a powerful family in the Islands for possibly thousands of years, long ruling the formidable castle of Pyke. When King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harren the Black","link":"Harren the Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harren the Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131797447356cca94925907" href="/wiki/Harren_the_Black" title="Harren the Black" class="mw-redirect">Harren the Black</a> was slain by King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon the Conqueror","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131797447356cca94925907" href="/wiki/Aegon_the_Conqueror" title="Aegon the Conqueror" class="mw-redirect">Aegon the Conqueror</a> during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131797447356cca94925907" href="/wiki/Targaryen" title="Targaryen" class="mw-redirect">Targaryen</a> invasion, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131797447356cca94925907" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> elected Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vickon Greyjoy","link":"Vickon Greyjoy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vickon Greyjoy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131797447356cca94925907" href="/wiki/Vickon_Greyjoy" title="Vickon Greyjoy">Vickon Greyjoy</a> of Pyke to succeed him as ruler of the isles, now oath-sworn to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131797447356cca94925907" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>. The Greyjoys have ruled ever since. The Greyjoys keep themselves distant from the other Great Houses, rarely taking part in events on the mainland (which they call the green lands) and, at various times, even returning to their ancient practices of raiding the nearby coastlines.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After holding themselves aloof during <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-131797447356cca94925907" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>, Lord Balon Greyjoy rebelled against the Iron Throne, declaring himself King of the Isles. Balon's belief was that Robert, who had only been on the throne for five years, did not command the total support of the nobles due to his status as a usurper and would not be able to rally the support needed to crush the rebellion. He was proven wrong and was defeated, with his fleet burned and two eldest sons slain during the war.
