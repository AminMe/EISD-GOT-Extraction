<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Once a noble family of the vast <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian","link":"Valyrian","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Valyrian" title="Valyrian" class="mw-redirect">Valyrian</a> Freehold, an empire spanning most of the eastern continent, the Targaryens were given control of the island of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonstone","link":"Dragonstone","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Dragonstone" title="Dragonstone" class="mw-redirect">Dragonstone</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> some centuries ago. After the obliteration of Valyria in the Doom, the Targaryens launched a devastating invasion of Westeros using three <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a> to spearhead their attack. In a short period of time, six of the seven formerly independent kingdoms had surrendered to the Targaryen leader, King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon I","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen|Aegon I]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon I</a>, unifying the continent under his rule. The last hold-out, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a>, joined the kingdom through a political and marriage alliance some two centuries later.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"Viewers Guide\">[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/guide\/houses\/targaryen\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 guide to houses, House Targaryen entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

At the end of this war Aerys and Rhaegar were dead and the Targaryen power-base in Westeros destroyed. Aerys's youngest children, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys","link":"Viserys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys Targaryen|Viserys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen|Daenerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a>, were taken by Targaryen loyalists into exile in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> of the East.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref name=\"Viewers Guide\" \/>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Generations of compound inbreeding have preserved in the Targaryen bloodline the classic Valyrian features of silver-white (platinum blonde) hair, and very fair, pale skin. Allegedly, this also preserved in their bloodline the ability to successfully bond with and ride dragons. They also seem to be somewhat more tolerant of extreme heat and high temperatures than other people, though they are by no means invulnerable to fire – or at least, not all of them.
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Having seized as much of Xaro's wealth as she and the remnants of her <i>khalasar</i> could carry, Daenerys and her retainers flee Qarth by ship. Daenerys reluctantly takes Jorah's advice to make for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Astapor","link":"Astapor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Astapor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Astapor" title="Astapor">Astapor</a>, a city in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a> known for its warrior-slaves. Shortly after their arrival, Daenerys is nearly assassinated by one of the remaining <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Warlocks","link":"Warlocks","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Warlocks]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Warlocks" title="Warlocks">Warlocks</a>, but is rescued by Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a>, who has tracked her down to pledge fealty to House Targaryen once again.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Targaryen's fortunes take a dramatic turn when Daenerys double-crosses the Good Masters of Astapor, seizing their elite Unsullied and burning the slave-owning elite with the dragon she pretended to sell to him. Daenerys thus becomes the first Targaryen in generations to march at the head of her own conquering army.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[And Now His Watch is Ended]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Military strength</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Essos</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">House Targaryen's military strength has fluctuated over the last few years under Daenerys Targaryen's leadership. Since the death of her husband, she has commanded a khalasar containing only a few dozen riders, less than half of whom are warriors, as well as a single trained <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"knight","link":"knight","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[knight]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Knight" title="Knight" class="mw-redirect">knight</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a>. During her time in the Red Waste and Qarth, her dragons were not large enough to provide any meaningful assistance.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">By the time Daenerys reached <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Astapor","link":"Astapor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Astapor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Astapor" title="Astapor">Astapor</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a>, her dragons had grown enough to provide limited air support in certain engagements, and she had gained the loyalty of an additional knight, Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a>. It should be pointed out that although limited, the dragons are capable of providing the only air support in the known world.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of Astapor","link":"Sack of Astapor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of Astapor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Sack_of_Astapor" title="Sack of Astapor">Sack of Astapor</a>, Daenerys gained an army of 8,000 fully trained <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Unsullied","link":"Unsullied","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Unsullied]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Unsullied" title="Unsullied">Unsullied</a>, elite warrior-eunuchs who fight in large, disciplined phalanx formations, as well as several hundred Unsullied-in-training who serve as auxiliaries.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Shortly after arriving at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yunkai","link":"Yunkai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yunkai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Yunkai" title="Yunkai">Yunkai</a>, Daenerys is able to secure the affection of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daario Naharis","link":"Daario Naharis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daario Naharis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Daario_Naharis" title="Daario Naharis">Daario Naharis</a>, a lieutenant in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Second Sons","link":"Second Sons (mercenary company)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Second Sons (mercenary company)|Second Sons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Second_Sons_(mercenary_company)" title="Second Sons (mercenary company)" class="mw-redirect">Second Sons</a>. During a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"parley","link":"parley","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[parley]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Parley" title="Parley">parley</a> between Daenerys and the captains of the Second Sons, Daario points out that Daenerys's Unsullied are impressive infantry, but she still possesses no cavalry, siege weapons, or ships. Later that night, Daario slays the captains of the Second Sons, makes himself captain, and pledges the company to Daenerys. The Second Sons add 2,000 armored cavalry to the growing Targaryen army.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Westeros</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">There are no official allies or vassals of House Targaryen left in Westeros. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Varys","link":"Varys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Varys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> is really a Targaryen loyalist and possesses a vast spy network, but by himself he commands no military forces and cannot be considered a military asset.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">King Robert Baratheon expressed fear that even after all these years, there are still a sizeable number of noble Houses in Westeros who are either secret Targaryen loyalists, or who later developed some grievance with Robert's rule, and would flock to the Targaryen banner if they attempted to retake the Iron Throne in open war. It is not clear to what extent paranoia influenced his opinions, since Ser Jorah stressed to Dany that the number of Targaryen loyalists in Westeros is far lower than Viserys liked to believe. Viserys, at least, believed that because House Tyrell of the Reach and House Martell of Dorne fought on the Targaryen side during Robert's Rebellion, the prospects were good that they'd be willing to join his cause and rebel against Robert. Thus far, there has been no evidence that dissatisfied houses would support a Targaryen return, and House Tyrell has become closely aligned with the Lannister-backed House Baratheon.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Targaryen moved from Valyria to Dragonstone, then the most far-flung outpost of the Valyrian Freehold, apparently in fear of a prophecy predicting the destruction of Valyria a century before it happened. After the fall of Valyria, the Targaryens stayed on Dragonstone for a hundred years, apparently hoarding the strength of their dragons and debating whether to invade Westeros or to attempt to seize control of the Valyrian colony-states which later became known as the Free Cities. When the time came, the Targaryens ultimately assisted the Free Cities in asserting their independence and then proceeded to invade Westeros.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Targaryen ruled Westeros for 283 years, during which time they survived substantial civil wars (including the Dance of Dragons and no fewer than five Blackfyre Rebellions), plague (the Great Spring Sickness), and an attempted foreign invasion (in the War of the Ninepenny Kings). They were brought down when the Mad King's insanity became too dangerous to be ignored.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Targaryens adopted the religion and many of the customs of Westeros, but they clung to two old Valyrian customs in defiance of public disapproval: Targaryen princes and kings were allowed to have multiple wives, and Targaryens were allowed to marry brother to sister<span data-rte-entity="mdash">&mdash;</span>something considered an abominable sin elsewhere in Westeros.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The generations of Targaryen inbreeding produced a distinctive set of physical features shared by all of their members during the House's three centuries of rule. This includes a generally pale appearance with silver-white (platinum blonde) hair and purple irises. While pale, they are not albinos, although their eyebrows are white. The actors playing Daenerys and Viserys are actually both dark-haired, so they had to wear wigs, although the production team decided not to dye their eyebrows to match. The TV series opted not to portray the Targaryens with purple eyes, partially due to the logistical difficulty of matching purple contact lenses for actors in every shot. The TV series originally filmed Viserys and Daenerys wearing violet contact lenses, but show creators David Benioff and Dan Weiss quickly abandoned their use. As they explained in the Season 1 Blu-ray episode commentaries, "actors act with their eyes, and [the lenses] really hurt the emotion."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.denofgeek.us\/tv\/21095\/46-things-we-learned-from-the-game-of-thrones-blu-rays Den of Geek]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149932726756cca94007397" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Targaryen" is pronounced "Tar-GAIR-ee-in".
</p>
