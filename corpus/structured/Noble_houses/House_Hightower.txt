<h2>In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Hightower is a powerful family with strong ties to the Tyrells. The family is very old, existing since the Dawn of Days when they were petty-kings. During the coming of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andals","link":"Andals","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andals]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/Andals" title="Andals">Andals</a> and the creation of the Kingdom of the Reach, the Hightowers allied with the newcomers. Thus, they were able to keep their wealth, lands, and privileges. House Hightower rarely takes part in wars within the realm. They were integral in the foundation of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Citadel","link":"the Citadel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Citadel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/The_Citadel" title="The Citadel">the Citadel</a> and continue to be patrons of both the Order of Maesters and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Faith of the Seven","link":"the Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/The_Faith_of_the_Seven" title="The Faith of the Seven" class="mw-redirect">the Faith of the Seven</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">House Hightower is said to be as rich as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a> and can field three times as many swords as other Tyrell bannermen, as they have vassals of their own, such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Cuy","link":"House Cuy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Cuy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/House_Cuy" title="House Cuy">House Cuy</a>; more, if they took everyone possible out of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Oldtown","link":"Oldtown","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Oldtown]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/Oldtown" title="Oldtown">Oldtown</a>. The four-decked <i>Honor of Oldtown</i> is the flagship of the HightowerÂ fleet. In the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, so far the Hightowers have followed the Tyrell lead, declaring for <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly Baratheon","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a>Â on the outbreak of the conflict, and not changing to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-124916087356cca95a7da01" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>'sÂ side after Renly's assassination.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The house is led by Lord Leyton Hightower. The sigil of House Hightower is a stone white watchtower, with a fire on the top. Their words are "We Light the Way".
</p>
