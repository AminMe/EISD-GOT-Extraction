<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Storm's End's construction was attributed to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Durran","link":"Durran","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Durran]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Durran" title="Durran">Durran</a>, a legendary figure of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Age of Heroes","link":"Age of Heroes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Age of Heroes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Age_of_Heroes" title="Age of Heroes">Age of Heroes</a>, who earned the hatred of the sea god and the wind goddess when he took <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Elenei","link":"Elenei","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Elenei]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Elenei" title="Elenei">Elenei</a>, daughter of the two gods, as his wife. In rage, the two gods sent massive storms to destroy Durran's keep. Durran defied the gods and built a larger and more formidable keep, but it was also destroyed. Durran's seventh keep, built allegedly with the aid of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bran the Builder","link":"Brandon Stark (the Builder)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brandon Stark (the Builder)|Bran the Builder]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Brandon_Stark_(the_Builder)" title="Brandon Stark (the Builder)">Bran the Builder</a>, withstood the rage of Elenei's parents and gained the name of Storm's End.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"The Complete Guide to Westeros: The Age of Heroes\" featurette, Season 1 Blu-ray boxset<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Storm's End became the seat of the rulers of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stormlands","link":"the Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm Kings","link":"Storm Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Storm_Kings" title="Storm Kings" class="mw-redirect">Storm Kings</a>, for millennia. When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon the Conqueror","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Aegon_the_Conqueror" title="Aegon the Conqueror" class="mw-redirect">Aegon the Conqueror</a> invaded <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>, he sent his commander and rumored half-brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Orys Baratheon","link":"Orys Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Orys Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Orys_Baratheon" title="Orys Baratheon">Orys Baratheon</a>, to take Storm's End. The last of the Storm Kings, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Argilac the Arrogant","link":"Argilac the Arrogant","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Argilac the Arrogant]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Argilac_the_Arrogant" title="Argilac the Arrogant" class="mw-redirect">Argilac the Arrogant</a>, left the safety of his stronghold and met Baratheon in open battle. Argilac was defeated, and Orys Baratheon took Argilac's domains, sigil, motto and daughter, becoming the founder of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a> <img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"The Complete Guide to Westeros: House Baratheon\" featurette, Season 1 Blu-ray boxset<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">One of the mightiest fortresses on the entire continent, Storm's End has endured many sieges but has never fallen to any attacker in its millennia-long history.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/map\/special\/battleground-westeros\/2\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, special features - Battleground Westeros, Siege of Storm's End entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Its seaward wall is 80 feet thick with a 150 feet drop into the sea below. The castle is said to be protected by spells woven into its walls that prevent magic from penetrating its defenses.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/locations\/storms-end\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, Storm's End entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>'s <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion|rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">rebellion</a>, Storm's End was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"besieged","link":"Siege of Storm's End","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Siege of Storm's End|besieged]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Siege_of_Storm's_End" title="Siege of Storm's End">besieged</a> for over a year by the forces of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tyrell","link":"House Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">House Tyrell</a>, who had remained loyal to the Mad King, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a>. The castle garrison, commanded by Robert's brother <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon|Stannis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis</a>, was reduced to eating dogs and rats and were on the verge of resorting to cannibalism when they were saved by a shipload of onions, smuggled into the castle by the noted smuggler <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Davos","link":"Davos Seaworth","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Davos Seaworth|Davos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Davos_Seaworth" title="Davos Seaworth">Davos</a>. After Aerys' death, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> bloodlessly raised the siege, as Lord Mace Tyrell realized that the war was lost, and he swore fealty to King Robert. Davos was raised to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"knighthood","link":"knighthood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[knighthood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Knighthood" title="Knighthood">knighthood</a> for his actions.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">To Stannis's displeasure, he was made Lord of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonstone","link":"Dragonstone","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/Dragonstone" title="Dragonstone" class="mw-redirect">Dragonstone</a> by his brother, leaving their infant brother Renly as the titular Lord of Storm's End. Stannis considered this a slight, as Dragonstone is even colder and harsher than Storm's End, despite Dragonstone being a fortress of great tactical importance and a history as a seat for Princes.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-22510918156cca85b26865" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Storm's End consists of a massive curtain wall surrounding a single immense drum tower. Unlike many other castles, which consist of a complex of different buildings, Storm's End is entirely contained within one large building. This is due to the immense storms that frequently batter the castle.
</p>
