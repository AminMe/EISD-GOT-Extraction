<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">According to legend, the Nightfort served as the seat of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's King","link":"Night's King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Night's_King" title="Night's King">Night's King</a>, who had been seduced by a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"woman","link":"Night's Queen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Queen|woman]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Night's_Queen" title="Night's Queen">woman</a> from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"beyond the Wall","link":"beyond the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[beyond the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Beyond_the_Wall" title="Beyond the Wall">beyond the Wall</a>. He brought her back to the Nightfort and performed dark sacrifices to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Walkers","link":"White Walkers","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Walkers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a>. However, he was defeated by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King in the North","link":"King in the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King in the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">King in the North</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brandon the Breaker","link":"Brandon Stark (the Breaker)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brandon Stark (the Breaker)|Brandon the Breaker]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Brandon_Stark_(the_Breaker)" title="Brandon Stark (the Breaker)">Brandon the Breaker</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/King-beyond-the-Wall" title="King-beyond-the-Wall" class="mw-redirect">King-beyond-the-Wall</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joramun","link":"Joramun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joramun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Joramun" title="Joramun">Joramun</a> who joined forces to destroy him.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Night's Watch (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The Nightfort, although ruined, contains a secret passage under the Wall, which leads to a well up inside the castle. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Samwell Tarly","link":"Samwell Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Samwell Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a> tells <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gilly","link":"Gilly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gilly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Gilly" title="Gilly">Gilly</a> about it, as he read it in a book.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Rains of Castamere (episode)|The Rains of Castamere]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bran Stark","link":"Bran Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bran Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hodor","link":"Hodor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hodor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Hodor" title="Hodor">Hodor</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer","link":"Summer","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">Summer</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jojen Reed","link":"Jojen Reed","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jojen Reed]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Jojen_Reed" title="Jojen Reed">Jojen Reed</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Meera Reed","link":"Meera Reed","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Meera Reed]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Meera_Reed" title="Meera Reed">Meera Reed</a> arrive at the Nightfort and camp in the chamber containing the well. Bran tells his friends the story of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"rat cook","link":"rat cook","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[rat cook]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Rat_cook" title="Rat cook" class="mw-redirect">rat cook</a>, a former cook at the Nightfort who was insulted by a king who stayed at the castle. He killed the king's son and served his flesh to his father inside a pie. The gods, furious at this insult to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ancient laws of hospitality","link":"Guest right","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Guest right|ancient laws of hospitality]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Guest_right" title="Guest right">ancient laws of hospitality</a>, turned the cook into a white rat cursed to feed on its own offspring forever.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The group then encounter Samwell Tarly and Gilly, who show them how to leave the castle via the tunnel.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Mhysa]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Nightfort is the oldest and largest castle on the Wall, and was likely the first major stronghold of the Night's Watch after its founding, which according to myth was almost eight thousand years ago. The Nightfort became an ill-reputed place over the centuries, with dark tales surrounding it (of varying truthfulness):
</p>
<ul><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">One of the oldest of these is the legend of the Night's King, a Lord Commander in the early years of the Watch who declared himself king of the Wall after falling in love with a white demon woman from the lands beyond. Ultimately the Stark King from Winterfell, Brandon the Breaker, and a King-Beyond-the-Wall named Joramun united to defeat the Night's King, and his name was cursed and erased from history.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">In another of the Nightfort's stories, an <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andal","link":"Andals","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andals|Andal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Andals" title="Andals">Andal</a> King visited the Nightfort. The cook, for some unspecified offense, killed the King's son and served his flesh to his father in a pie. The gods, angry at this violation of the laws of hospitality, transformed the cook into a massive rat that would only feed on its offspring. The tale of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rat Cook","link":"Rat Cook","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rat Cook]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Rat_Cook" title="Rat Cook">Rat Cook</a> became an infamous myth about a violation of the sacred law of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Guest right","link":"Guest right","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Guest right]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Guest_right" title="Guest right">Guest right</a>.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">In another story, a young girl called Danny Flint disguised herself as a boy in order to serve on the Wall, but was raped and murdered by unknown people.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">In yet another story, seventy-nine brothers of the Watch deserted from the Nightfort, sought refuge in the lands of Lord Ryswell, father of one of the deserters, but he captured them and sent them back to the Nightfort, where they were buried alive in the Wall so they would never abandon their posts.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">The Nightfort is also where King Sherrit called down a curse on the Andals.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">It is where a ranger dubbed "Mad Axe" went insane and killed several of his sworn brothers.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Symeon Star-Eyes saw hellhounds fight at the Nightfort in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Age of Heroes","link":"Age of Heroes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Age of Heroes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Age_of_Heroes" title="Age of Heroes">Age of Heroes</a>.
</li><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">Finally, three centuries before the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Targaryen" title="Targaryen" class="mw-redirect">Targaryen</a> invasion, the commanders of the garrisons at the Nightfort and Snowgate went to war against one another, but they were both executed by the Starks for betraying their vows.
</li></ul>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The Nightfort remained occupied up until two centuries ago. By this time the Nightfort had become mostly unoccupied and fallen into disrepair, with most of the administrative functions of the Watch transferred to the more centrally-located Castle Black to its east. When Queen Alysanne Targaryen visited the Wall on her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragon","link":"dragon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon" class="mw-redirect">dragon</a>, Silverwing, she convinced the Watch that the Nightfort had to be abandoned. She convinced her husband, King Jaehaerys I, to fund a new, smaller castle to be built at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Deep Lake","link":"Deep Lake","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Deep Lake]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Deep_Lake" title="Deep Lake">Deep Lake</a> a few miles to the east, and the Nightfort was abandoned. By the time of the books, Deep Lake itself has also been abandoned.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the Nightfort there is a hidden gate, known as the Black Gate, that allows passage to the other side of the Wall. It is set deep in a wall of the well at the center of the kitchens and is made of white weirwood with an old wrinkled face on it. Only a brother of the Night's Watch can open the Black Gate, by reciting the vow of the Watch.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">There are actually two other abandoned castles between the Nightfort and Castle Black. From west to east, they are Deep Lake and Queensgate. Deep Lake was actually built as a smaller replacement to guard the section of the Wall near the Nightfort, until it was also abandoned. In the TV series, of course, Samwell does not explicitly state that the Nightfort is the castle immediately west of Castle Black, he only says that it is some distance west of it (without mentioning whether or not there are intervening castles).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"battle of Castle Black","link":"battle of Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[battle of Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castle_Black" title="Battle of Castle Black">battle of Castle Black</a>, Jon allows <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon|Stannis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis</a> and his troops to reside in the Nightfort as a payment for assisting with Watch, although it is "rats and ruins", as Stannis calls it. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Othell Yarwyck","link":"Othell Yarwyck","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Othell Yarwyck]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Othell_Yarwyck" title="Othell Yarwyck">Othell Yarwyck</a> estimates it will be half a year before the castle can be made fit for habitation. He and his builders have been working hard to restore it. Later he reports Jon that they restored most of the keep and put a roof back on the kitchens. The castle needs food, furnishings and firewood, but it may serve.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Selyse","link":"Selyse Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Selyse Baratheon|Selyse]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-27254240756cca861a090e" href="/wiki/Selyse_Baratheon" title="Selyse Baratheon">Selyse</a> arrives at Castle Black on her way to occupy the Nightfort. Jon warns her that his builders have done all they can to make the Nightfort ready to receive her and her daughter, but it is a grim and cold place, much of it still remains in ruins. She scoffs that the Nightfort is the place her husband has chosen for their seat, and there they shall abide. She in no great haste, though, to abandon the comforts of Castle Black for the shadows of the Nightfort.
</p>
