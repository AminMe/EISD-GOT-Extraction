<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Tyrosh</b> is one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> located to the east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. It is situated on an island at the eastern end of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a>, an island chain that extends across the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a>. According to legend, there used to be a land-bridge linking Westeros and Essos, of which the Steptones are the only remnants. Therefore, Tyrosh is the closest of the Free Cities to Westeros, located not far from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> and the island of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tarth","link":"Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Tarth" title="Tarth">Tarth</a> (off the coast of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stormlands","link":"the Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a>).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Due to its central location - between Westeros and the other Free Cities, and its proximity to constant wars between <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lys","link":"Lys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Lys" title="Lys">Lys</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myr</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Disputed Lands","link":"Disputed Lands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Disputed Lands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Disputed_Lands" title="Disputed Lands">Disputed Lands</a> - Tyrosh is often seen as a major world hub for hiring the services of various professional <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"mercenary","link":"mercenary","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[mercenary]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">mercenary</a> companies, to serve in the various conflicts of these different regions.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tyrosh is also well-known for its <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pear-brandy","link":"Food and Drink","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Food and Drink|pear-brandy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Food_and_Drink" title="Food and Drink">pear-brandy</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[You Win or You Die]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Wars to Come]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">People from Tyrosh are known as <b>Tyroshi</b>. As the new captain of the mercenary company, Daario pledges the allegiance of the Second Sons to Daenerys.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Second Sons (episode)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Tyrosh is one of the most powerful of the Free Cities. Far larger than the Dornish capital of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sunspear","link":"Sunspear","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sunspear]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Sunspear" title="Sunspear">Sunspear</a> in Westeros, it is a great port with a large harbor guarded by the Bleeding Tower. The city has a reputation for being boisterous and loud, and mercenaries and sellswords are commonly hired in Tyrosh.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Like most of the Free Cities, Tyrosh was founded as a colony of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a>. Established as a military fort to monitor and control shipping activity in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a>, the city's real fame arose from a completely unexpected source; the waters surrounding Tyrosh were home to an unusual type of sea snail that produced richly colored dyes when properly treated. Colorful garments soon became immensely popular with Valyrian nobility, and thousands of merchants flocked to Tyrosh to take advantage of the commercial opportunities. The fort grew into a proper city within a single generation, and over the centuries Tyroshi dyers were able to develop a huge variety of different colors through various processes. In the present day, the Tyroshi still have a love for loud and garish colors, often dyeing not only their clothes but their hair in bright, gaudy hues.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tyrosh is also known for its creation of ornate armor and elaborate torture devices. The armorsmiths of the city produce intricate helms shaped like animals and birds and often decorated with precious metals. Pear brandy is the signature drink of the city as well as a popular export.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tyrosh is actively involved in the slave trade, with slaves outnumbering freeborn citizens three to one. The Tyroshi are famously aggressive in their search for slaves, even sailing to lands beyond the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wall","link":"Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Wall" title="Wall" class="mw-redirect">Wall</a> to capture <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wildlings","link":"Wildlings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wildlings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Wildlings" title="Wildlings" class="mw-redirect">Wildlings</a> for the slave market. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The leader of Tyrosh is the Archon, chosen from among a council made up of the city's wealthiest and most prominent citizens. A number of different religions have a presence in Tyrosh; a monument known as the Fountain of the Drunken God is dedicated to the eponymous deity, and the Temple of Trios honors the three-headed god of the same name. The faith of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"R'hllor, the Lord of Light","link":"Lord of Light","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Light|R'hllor, the Lord of Light]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Light" title="Lord of Light" class="mw-redirect">R'hllor, the Lord of Light</a>, also has followers in Tyrosh (as in many of the other Free Cities).   
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Free Cities of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myr</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lys","link":"Lys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Lys" title="Lys">Lys</a> war incessantly over the nearby <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Disputed Lands","link":"Disputed Lands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Disputed Lands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Disputed_Lands" title="Disputed Lands">Disputed Lands</a>. Tyrosh is sometimes drawn into these wars, as is the more distant city of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tyrosh has had a volatile history. Following the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom of Valyria","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom of Valyria</a>, it became an independent city-state. However, a century later it was attacked by Volantis, which was trying to reforge the Valyrian Freehold. Having already taken Myr and Lys, Volantis overreached by attacking Tyrosh. Both <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pentos","link":"Pentos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pentos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a> and the Westerosi <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm King","link":"Storm King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Storm_King" title="Storm King">Storm King</a> allied with Tyrosh to defeat Volantis, which also lost Myr and Lys due to rebellions and uprisings.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Two and a half centuries later, Tyrosh was attacked and captured by the Band of Nine, nine powerful warlords who were supporting one another and doing together what could not be achieved alone. Alequo Adarys was installed as Archon of Tyrosh. The Band's military strength was broken in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","link":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Ninepenny Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings" title="War of the Ninepenny Kings">War of the Ninepenny Kings</a>, when the King on the Iron Throne launched a pre-emptive strike before they could attack <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. Adarys managed to retain control of Tyrosh for six years before being deposed. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hoster Tully","link":"Hoster Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hoster Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Hoster_Tully" title="Hoster Tully">Hoster Tully</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a>'s father, and the young <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a> all fought in the War of the Ninepenny Kings, Selmy winning fame for his slaying of Maelys the Monstrous, the last of the Blackfyre Pretenders, in combat.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-198718493756cca884afcf7" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Tyroshi" is pronounced "Ti-ROH-shee". "Tyrosh" is pronounced "Ti-ROHsh", as opposed to "Tie-rosh".
