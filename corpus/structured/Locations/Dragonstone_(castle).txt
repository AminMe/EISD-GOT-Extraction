<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Dragonstone</b> is the castle that stands upon the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"eponymous island","link":"Dragonstone (island)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone (island)|eponymous island]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Dragonstone_(island)" title="Dragonstone (island)">eponymous island</a> located in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackwater Bay","link":"Blackwater Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackwater Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Blackwater_Bay" title="Blackwater Bay">Blackwater Bay</a>. It is the ancestral seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a> and the current stronghold of a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"cadet branch","link":"House Baratheon of Dragonstone","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon of Dragonstone|cadet branch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon_of_Dragonstone" title="House Baratheon of Dragonstone">cadet branch</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At the time the series begins, it is held for King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Baratheon","link":"Robert Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> by his brother, Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon|Stannis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis</a>. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Chamber of the Painted Table","link":"Chamber of the Painted Table","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Chamber of the Painted Table]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Chamber_of_the_Painted_Table" title="Chamber of the Painted Table">Chamber of the Painted Table</a> is inside the castle. The castle was built using advanced <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyria","link":"Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Valyria" title="Valyria" class="mw-redirect">Valyrian</a> stonemasonry techniques, long since lost.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/locations\/dragonstone\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, Dragonstone entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> As a result it has many exotic features unique in all of Westeros, including massive dragon-shaped gargoyles. The entire castle is dominated by dragon motifs and reliefs.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The castle itself is very strong, due to its advanced Valyrian design, which combined with its isolated location make it an ideal redoubt and refuge - first for the Targaryens, and now for its current lord, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>.  Because of these features and its geographic position, it is extremely difficult to breach and can be held by a relatively small garrison against a vastly larger force.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[Complete Guide to Westeros]]: Dragonstone<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>

<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->Following the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Trident","link":"Battle of the Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Trident" title="Battle of the Trident">Battle of the Trident</a> and prior to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of King's Landing","link":"Sack of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Sack_of_King's_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">Sack of King's Landing</a>, Queen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaella Targaryen","link":"Rhaella Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaella Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Rhaella_Targaryen" title="Rhaella Targaryen">Rhaella Targaryen</a> and Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys Targaryen","link":"Viserys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys Targaryen</a> were sent to Dragonstone for their protection. Queen Rhaella perished during a stormy night giving birth to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> fell and Robert Baratheon took the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>, Stannis Baratheon was sent to deal with what remained of the royal family and their retainers. However, Prince Viserys and Princess Daenerys were smuggled across the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> by a loyal knight. Enraged, Robert stripped Stannis of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a> and gave it to their younger brother, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon|Renly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly</a>, and gave Dragonstone to Stannis, to rein in the Targaryen loyalists there. In later years, Robert tried to humor Stannis that he was actually honoring him with lordship of Dragonstone, because under the Targaryens it was the traditional seat of the heir to the throne, but Stannis scoffed at such pleasantries.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Dragonstone is a volcanic island located a couple of hundred miles north-east of King's Landing. The island has a small port and a large castle on it, built by the Targaryens some 500 years ago, when they settled Dragonstone as a trading outpost of the great <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a>. When Valyria was destroyed in the cataclysm known as the Doom a century later, Dragonstone was the largest Valyrian stronghold to survive intact. The Targaryens refused to help the colony-states along the west coast of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> maintain Valyrian rule. They would later become the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Targaryens instead waited for another century building up their strength, then invaded and subdued <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> three hundred years ago. They built a new capital at King's Landing, while Dragonstone became the primary holding of the King's heir (who was also named "Prince of Dragonstone"). After the deposing of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aerys II Targaryen","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Aerys II Targaryen</a>, Robert Baratheon appointed his brother Stannis as Lord of Dragonstone until his own son, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon|Joffrey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a>, became old enough for the office. This has caused a rift between the brothers, as Stannis feels that he should rightfully be commanding the ancestral Baratheon castle of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a> instead of their youngest brother, Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon|Renly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Dragonstone is the home port of part of the Royal Fleet. In addition, Dragonstone commands the loyalty of a number of smaller houses located on surrounding islands in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> and on nearby parts of the coastal mainland. Dragonstone is a cold, drafty and harsh place. Lord Stannis and his family usually prefers to spend as much time as possible at court away from the island, but have recently returned to Dragonstone to attend to business there just before the start of the series.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Dragonstone is one of the strongest castles in all of the Seven Kingdoms, due to its advanced Valyrian design, using strong stonemasonry techniques now lost to the world. Its isolated island location greatly enhances its already formidable defensive prowess: a vast fleet of ships would be required simply to transport troops and large siege weapons to its location, and even then they would be forced to make a risky amphibious landing while under fire from the castle's own catapults. The castle itself is not on the sheer scale of the greatest castles of the realm such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm's End","link":"Storm's End","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm's End]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Storm's_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a> or the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-97356824256cca84cd4869" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a>, but both of those castles can be assaulted by land armies. The entire point of building so strong a fortress on such an isolated island was to make the perfect refuge and redoubt for greater noble Houses from elsewhere: the indigenous "lands" of Dragonstone are just a few fishing villages and could never had supported the construction of such a powerful castle on their own.
</p>
