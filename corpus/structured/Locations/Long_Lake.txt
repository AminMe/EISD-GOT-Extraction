<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Long Lake is one of the largest lakes in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. It is of historical importance as the site of a major battle. In 184 AL, one hundred and fourteen years before the start of the novels, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King-beyond-the-Wall","link":"King-beyond-the-Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King-beyond-the-Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/King-beyond-the-Wall" title="King-beyond-the-Wall" class="mw-redirect">King-beyond-the-Wall</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Raymun Redbeard","link":"Raymun Redbeard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Raymun Redbeard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/Raymun_Redbeard" title="Raymun Redbeard">Raymun Redbeard</a>, assembled a large army and, thanks to the laxity of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>, was able to scale <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> at an under-defended location. The army made it as far as the shores of Long Lake, where it was caught between the armies of the Starks coming up from the south and the Umbers pursuing the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildlings","link":"wildlings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildlings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/Wildlings" title="Wildlings" class="mw-redirect">wildlings</a> from the north. Lord Willam Stark and Lord Harmond Umber, the Drunken Giant, destroyed the wildling host. However, Lord Stark was beheaded. His younger brother, Artos the Implacable, slew Raymun Redbeard and ended the battle.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The tardy forces of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> arrived too late to join in the fighting, so Artos ordered them to bury the dead. The lax <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander","link":"Lord Commander","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170768487556cca932c7454" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander" title="Lord Commander">Lord Commander</a>,Â Jack Musgood, became known as 'Sleepy Jack' for allowing the wildling army to cross the Wall unmolested.
</p>
