<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-76117765556cca870ca055" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Titan of Braavos is a massive stone and bronze statue that guards the entrance into the lagoon where Braavos is situated. It is the primary line of defense for Braavos and lets out a loud blast whenever a ship approaches the entrance to warn the Arsenal of its coming.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Titan is listed as one of the nine Wonders Made by Man in the eponynous book written by explorer Lomas Longstrider.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">It bears a strong resemblance to the Colossus of Rhodes. Listed as one of the Seven Wonders of the World, it was destroyed during an ancient earthquake.
</p>
