<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-135581849556cca8d801a93" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Ashemark is the seat of House Marbrand.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Oxcross","link":"Battle of Oxcross","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Oxcross]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-135581849556cca8d801a93" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Oxcross" title="Battle of Oxcross">Battle of Oxcross</a> opened up the northern Westerlands to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robb Stark","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-135581849556cca8d801a93" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s army, his forces easily captured Ashemark.  For some reason, the TV series doesn't refer to a minor engagement at the "Battle of Ashemark", instead referring to a "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Yellow Fork","link":"Battle of the Yellow Fork","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Yellow Fork]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-135581849556cca8d801a93" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Yellow_Fork" title="Battle of the Yellow Fork">Battle of the Yellow Fork</a>" taking place around the same point.  It seems that they changed the name to avoid confusion with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ashford","link":"Ashford","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ashford]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-135581849556cca8d801a93" href="/wiki/Ashford" title="Ashford">Ashford</a>, which is a castle in the Reach.  It's possible that the "Yellow Fork" is meant to be the tributary of the Tumblestone, which is itself a tributary of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Trident","link":"Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-135581849556cca8d801a93" href="/wiki/Trident" title="Trident" class="mw-redirect">Trident</a> River system, with its three main forks also named after colors:  Red, Blue, and Green.  
</p>
