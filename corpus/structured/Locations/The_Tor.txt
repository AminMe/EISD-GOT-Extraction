<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-145211178356cca901aefa3" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Tor is the seat of House Jordayne, one of the most powerful Dornish houses.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-145211178356cca901aefa3" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a> named this castle and its house as a tribute to fellow fantasy author <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert Jordan","link":"wikipedia:Robert Jordan","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:Robert Jordan|Robert Jordan]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-145211178356cca901aefa3" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Jordan" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Robert Jordan">Robert Jordan</a>, author of the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wheel of Time","link":"wikipedia:The Wheel of Time","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:The Wheel of Time|Wheel of Time]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-145211178356cca901aefa3" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wheel_of_Time" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:The Wheel of Time">Wheel of Time</a></i> series. Tor Books are the publishers of the <i>Wheel of Time</i> books and the head of House Jordayne is Lord Trebor (a palindrome of 'Robert').
</p>
