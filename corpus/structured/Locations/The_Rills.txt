<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-76662167156cca8c20602d" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Rills are a region of hills, lakes and rivers south-west of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Torrhen's Square","link":"Torrhen's Square","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Torrhen's Square]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-76662167156cca8c20602d" href="/wiki/Torrhen's_Square" title="Torrhen's Square">Torrhen's Square</a> and north-west of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barrowton","link":"Barrowton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barrowton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-76662167156cca8c20602d" href="/wiki/Barrowton" title="Barrowton">Barrowton</a>. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Ryswell","link":"House Ryswell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Ryswell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-76662167156cca8c20602d" href="/wiki/House_Ryswell" title="House Ryswell">House Ryswell</a> holds the Rills for the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Starks","link":"House Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark|Starks]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-76662167156cca8c20602d" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">Starks</a> of Winterfell.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Ryswells are noted for their honor. According to legend, a previous lord of the Rills arrested his own son for deserting the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-76662167156cca8c20602d" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>, returning him to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-76662167156cca8c20602d" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> for trial and execution. The same lord later took the black himself to help amend for his son's treachery.
</p>
