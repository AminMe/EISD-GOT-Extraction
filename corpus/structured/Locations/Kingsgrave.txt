<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153834872356cca91067038" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels Kingsgrave is the seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Manwoody","link":"House Manwoody","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Manwoody]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153834872356cca91067038" href="/wiki/House_Manwoody" title="House Manwoody">House Manwoody</a>, a loyal vassal house of the Martells.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The name "Kingsgrave" as well as the Manwoody sigil - a crowned skull - refer to the fact that an old <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King of the Reach","link":"King of the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-153834872356cca91067038" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Reach" title="King of the Reach">King of the Reach</a> was slain in battle there centuries ago.
</p>
