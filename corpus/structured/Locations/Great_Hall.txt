<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The <b>Great Hall</b> is a location within the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a>, seat of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ruler","link":"King of the Andals and the First Men","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Andals and the First Men|ruler]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Andals_and_the_First_Men" title="King of the Andals and the First Men">ruler</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. In it stands the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>, the symbol of the power and authority of the King over all of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. Within the Great Hall, the King, or his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King|Hand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand</a>, in the former's absence, holds court, receives petitioners and oversees official ceremonies.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> Characteristics </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Great_hall1x04.jpg|thumb|300px|right|The Great Hall during the reign of Robert Baratheon]]","title":"Great_hall1x04.jpg","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Great hall1x04","align":"right","caption":"The Great Hall during the reign of Robert Baratheon","width":300,"captionParsed":"The Great Hall during the reign of Robert Baratheon"}}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" alt="" src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/a/a9/Great_hall1x04.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20121227202442" width="300" height="169" class="image thumb alignRight withCaption" data-image-name="Great hall1x04.jpg" data-image-key="Great_hall1x04.jpg"  type="image" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->The Great Hall consists of a large, cavernous room. Its entrance is made up of two huge bronze and wood doors. The Iron Throne sits on the opposite side, atop a raised dais climbed up to by two set of wide steps of rough black stone; small chairs may be placed when the King or the Hand is accompanied by members of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"small council","link":"small council","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[small council]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Small_council" title="Small council" class="mw-redirect">small council</a>. The floor is made of different types of marble. Overhead is the towering vaulted ceiling, with massive arches and columns. On the right side of the Hall stands a raised gallery that connects to other areas of the Red Keep. To the left side of the Hall, several windows with stained-glass depictions of the seven-pointed star of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a> allow light to enter.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the reign of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a>, the walls of the Hall were decorated with the skulls of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a>, from the largest to the smallest one.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Cripples, Bastards and Broken Things]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> However, after <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>, the skulls were removed and moved to the cellars of the Red Keep. Vine motifs were placed as decorations of the Hall's columns and several candelabra placed to illuminate the place. Behind the throne, a tall stained-glass window depicting stags - the sigil of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a> - was installed.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the demise of King Robert, his successor, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey Baratheon","link":"Joffrey Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey Baratheon</a>, ordered the Hall to be further modified, as he desired the throne room's appearance to mirror the one it had during the time of the Targaryens. The vine motifs and candelabra are removed and replaced with large, metallic braziers constantly ablaze. The stained-glass window behind the throne is also replaced with one depicting the seven-pointed star in yellow and red - the colors of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Lannister","link":"House Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-163538250456cca89bb33bc" href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a>.
</p>
