</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Slaver's Bay</b> is the name given to an area on the southern coast of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a>, located to the south-east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> and to the west of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qarth","link":"Qarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Qarth" title="Qarth">Qarth</a>. The name can refer to the bay itself or the entire region. As the name implies, the cities of Slaver's Bay are the hub of the international <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"slave trade","link":"Slavery","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slavery|slave trade]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slave trade</a>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Geography</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Slaver's Bay has a hot, semi-arid climate. Agriculture is maintained through extensive irrigation systems.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The area borders the Red Waste, which then leads on further to enter into the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki sea","link":"Dothraki sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Dothraki_sea" title="Dothraki sea" class="mw-redirect">Dothraki sea</a>, which is located to its north. It is noted as the location of the three great slave-cities of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Meereen","link":"Meereen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Meereen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Meereen" title="Meereen">Meereen</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Astapor","link":"Astapor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Astapor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Astapor" title="Astapor">Astapor</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yunkai","link":"Yunkai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yunkai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Yunkai" title="Yunkai">Yunkai</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The country of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazar","link":"Lhazar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Lhazar" title="Lhazar">Lhazar</a>, noted for its unwarlike shepherd-folk, is located up the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Skahazadhan","link":"Skahazadhan","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Skahazadhan]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Skahazadhan" title="Skahazadhan">Skahazadhan</a> River from Meereen, to the northeast of Slaver's Bay. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> enjoy pillaging areas close to Slaver's Bay and driving captives to the slave cities in return for riches. They particularly enjoy pillaging Lhazar, since it is located conveniently close to Meereen.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The bay also used to be the heartland of the fallen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a>, with the shattered peninsula of Valyria located to the west of the bay.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The region later known as Slaver's Bay was once the heartland of the great <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ghiscari Empire","link":"Ghiscari Empire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghiscari Empire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Ghiscari_Empire" title="Ghiscari Empire">Ghiscari Empire</a>, which flourished well over five thousand years ago, and was one of the earliest civilizations in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the known world","link":"The Known World","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Known World|the known world]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/The_Known_World" title="The Known World">the known world</a>. About five thousand years ago, the new power of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a> began to rise to the west, across the Gulf of Grief. Valyrian expansion was driven by their discovery of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragons","link":"Dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">Dragons</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"volcanic mountains","link":"Fourteen Fires","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fourteen Fires|volcanic mountains]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Fourteen_Fires" title="Fourteen Fires">volcanic mountains</a> of the Valyrian peninsula, which they mastered and rode as weapons of war. The two great empires developed an intense rivalry, and clashed in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"a series of five massive wars","link":"Valyrian-Ghiscari Wars","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian-Ghiscari Wars|a series of five massive wars]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Valyrian-Ghiscari_Wars" title="Valyrian-Ghiscari Wars" class="mw-redirect">a series of five massive wars</a>, but the vast slave-legions of the ancient Ghiscari could not withstand Valyrian dragonfire. Five thousand years ago, the Valyrians finally succeeded in destroying the Ghiscari Empire, burning its capital city <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Ghis","link":"Old Ghis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Ghis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Old_Ghis" title="Old Ghis">Old Ghis</a> to the ground, conquering its surrounding lands and colonies, and enslaving the survivors. Five thousand years of Valyrian mastery obliterated much of the original Ghiscari culture.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Four hundred years before the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom of Valyria","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom of Valyria</a> destroyed the great civilization within a single cataclysmic day - after which Valyria's outlying colonies and conquests reasserted their independence. While their colonies to the west of Valyria developed into the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a>, in a parallel development, their colonies to the east overthrew their Valyrian overlords and became the independent city-states of Slaver's Bay. The three most powerful of these cities became Astapor, Yunkai, and Meereen.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The ruling elites of Slaver's Bay continue to practice large-scale slavery; indeed, their entire economy centers around it. In times of peace they breed them, and in times of war they capture them. Many slaves are taken from distant lands, even places far across the sea such as the continent of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sothoryos","link":"Sothoryos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sothoryos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Sothoryos" title="Sothoryos">Sothoryos</a>. Due to this ethnic melting pot, combined with thousands of years of Valyrian domination, little of the original culture of the old Ghiscari Empire has survived. Even the language spoken in present-day Slaver's Bay is a derivative of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Valyrian","link":"High Valyrian","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Valyrian]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/High_Valyrian" title="High Valyrian">High Valyrian</a>, not the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Ghiscari","link":"Old Ghiscari","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Ghiscari]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Old_Ghiscari" title="Old Ghiscari">Old Ghiscari</a> language - which is functionally dead. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Low Valyrian","link":"Low Valyrian","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Low Valyrian]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Low_Valyrian" title="Low Valyrian">Low Valyrian</a> language spoken in Slaver's Bay is, however, very distinct from the other Low Valyrian languages spoken in the Free Cities, as it was geographically separated and subjected to unique local influences. A few constructions and loanwords from Old Ghiscari such as "mhysa" ("mother") survive into Slaver's Bay Low Valyrian, but it fundamentally remains a derivative of their old Valyrian conquerors.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Despite the vast chronological and cultural gap between them, the local ruling slavemasters of Slaver's Bay pride themselves on surviving scraps of Ghiscari culture, and like to think of themselves as a continuation of the Ghiscari Empire's old glories - extending to the point that each of the slaver-cities uses a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harpy","link":"Harpies","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harpies|Harpy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Harpies" title="Harpies" class="mw-redirect">Harpy</a> as its sigil, as the old Ghiscari Empire did.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Slaver's Bay is a region in south-central Essos where slaves are bought, trained, and sold in massive numbers.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The city of Meereen is located 550 leagues (1,650 miles) east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a>. The land-route from the Free Cities to Slaver's Bay, employing Valyrian roads crossing the northern end of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Peninsula","link":"Valyrian Peninsula","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Peninsula]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Peninsula" title="Valyrian Peninsula">Valyrian Peninsula</a> via the city of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mantarys","link":"Mantarys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mantarys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Mantarys" title="Mantarys">Mantarys</a>, is known as the "demon road" and is considered exceptionally dangerous. The majority of trade and travel between the Free Cities and Slaver's Bay takes place by sea, with ships giving the ill-omened ruins of Valyria a wide berth.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dawn Age","link":"Dawn Age","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dawn Age]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Dawn_Age" title="Dawn Age">Dawn Age</a>, the old <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ghiscari Empire","link":"Ghiscari Empire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghiscari Empire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Ghiscari_Empire" title="Ghiscari Empire">Ghiscari Empire</a> that once ruled the region was bountiful: forests provided abundant wood, fields were plentiful, and the hills were filled with copper for weapons. Copper stopped being as important as it used to be, however, after the forging of iron was discovered and spread across Essos (this iron age began over 6,000 years ago, and the Ghiscari themselves did switch to iron weapons). When the Valyrians conquered the region in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ghiscari Wars","link":"Ghiscari Wars","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghiscari Wars]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Ghiscari_Wars" title="Ghiscari Wars">Ghiscari Wars</a> five thousand years ago, the flames from the dragons burned out all of the forest, so they no longer had lumber. Without the trees to hold the soil in place, it baked and blew away in the wind, and crop yields became meager. No longer possessing valuable surpluses of timber, crops, or useful metals, it is easy to see how the entire economy of the region became so heavily reliant upon slavery. This is ultimately one of the reasons that Daenerys Targaryen's intervention encounters such difficulty - such as the invented scene with the slave <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Fennesz","link":"Fennesz","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fennesz]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-170241925456cca860dc9d1" href="/wiki/Fennesz" title="Fennesz">Fennesz</a> (a condensation/representation of several slaves in the novels), who points out to her that their economy has been so heavily based on slavery for so long that simply removing it left many former slaves without livelihoods.
</p>
