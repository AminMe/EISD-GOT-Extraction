<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>The Twins</b>, sometimes known as <b>The Crossing</b>, is a castle in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a>. It is the seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Frey","link":"House Frey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Frey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/House_Frey" title="House Frey">House Frey</a>, a vassal house of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tully","link":"House Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/House_Tully" title="House Tully">House Tully</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a>. It consists of two near-identical towers and a fortified bridge over the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Green Fork","link":"Green Fork","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Green Fork]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/Green_Fork" title="Green Fork" class="mw-redirect">Green Fork</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"River Trident","link":"River Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[River Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/River_Trident" title="River Trident" class="mw-redirect">River Trident</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Twins represents the only crossing point over the Green Fork for hundreds of miles in either direction. It is a major barrier to travelers and merchants traveling from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the North","link":"the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">the North</a> to the western <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverlands","link":"Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/Riverlands" title="Riverlands" class="mw-redirect">Riverlands</a>. It lies directly on the main route from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Riverrun","link":"Riverrun","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Riverrun]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/Riverrun" title="Riverrun">Riverrun</a>. Moving from one castle to the other while avoiding the Twins requires a lengthy detour hundreds of miles to the south or hazardously traversing the bogs and swamps of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Neck","link":"the Neck","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Neck]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/The_Neck" title="The Neck">the Neck</a> to the north. Travelers heading south who cannot pass southwest over the Twins must pass southeast along the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsroad","link":"Kingsroad","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsroad]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/Kingsroad" title="Kingsroad">Kingsroad</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Baelor]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Freys' rise began six hundred years before the events of the series with the building of a stone bridge over the Green Fork. It took three generations to build the Twins, but afterwards they quickly grew wealthy by charging passing travelers for the use of the Crossing.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season1\/#!\/locations\/twins\/ HBO viewers guide, season 1 map, The Twins entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/locations\/the-twins\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, The Twins entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> The bridge is a massive arch of smooth grey stone, wide enough for two wagons to pass abreast.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[House Frey (Histories & Lore)]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Production Designer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gemma Jackson","link":"Gemma Jackson","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gemma Jackson]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/Gemma_Jackson" title="Gemma Jackson">Gemma Jackson</a> explained the visual aesthetic of the Twins, and the underlying reasoning behind it:
</p>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>"In Season 1, we built a grubby little space.  It was really old and painted and water was coming down - it was rather sinister.  We wanted to get an aspect of that again.  Because Lord Walder is mean, we did this beaten up, old leather [for the furniture].  We used his sigil on the arches - you can see very old bits of paint just coming through.  The idea is the place is getting incredibly shabby, and he doesn't want to spend any money on it."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Walk of Punishment]]\", HBO-Go Inside-the-Episode feature<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</dd></dl>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Freys actually are a wealthy family - but they gained their wealth relatively recently, starting six hundred years ago when they built the first bridge across the river and began exacting river tolls.  The older noble Houses of the Riverlands look down on them as upstarts.  Lord Walder became rich by being miserly, however.  He has no sense of shame, so he'd rather have a leaky roof in need of repair and worn down furniture, than spend any money on replacing them, regardless of the public appearance this to the other noble families.  He's too cheap to buy nice things.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Also, Jackson noted that the twin turret sigil of House Frey was worked into the furniture:  the corner posts of the back of Lord Walder's chair look like the two castle turrets of the Twins.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-28712511456cca863d05ca" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, House Frey was a minor house of the Riverlands until six hundred years ago. The Frey's Crossing was initially defended by wooden castles at either end. Using the wealth generated by their toll they replaced the wooden castles with strong stone ones, complete with portcullises, moats and barbicans. The bridge itself is covered and wide enough for two wagons to travel abreast. The Crossing has its own tower, named the Water Tower. House Frey's rise gained them vassal houses of their own: Houses Charlton, Erenford and Haigh.
</p>
