<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->The <b>Frostfangs</b>, also known as the <b>Frostfang Mountains</b>, are a mountain range in the far north of the continent of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. The bulk of the mountain range lies to the northwest of the Wall between the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lands of Always Winter","link":"Lands of Always Winter","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lands of Always Winter]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" href="/wiki/Lands_of_Always_Winter" title="Lands of Always Winter">Lands of Always Winter</a> to their west and the Haunted Forest to their east. They are named for their jagged peaks. Wildlings are thought to know hidden valleys that can provide shelter when crossing the mountains.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/locations\/frostfang-mountains\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, frostfangs entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

</li></ul>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, the Frostfangs extend from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> northwards into the Lands of Always Winter and the remote wildling kingdom of Thenn. They are jagged and hostile peaks, always covered in snow. The Skirling Pass is the most notable pass through the mountains, leading from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Haunted Forest","link":"Haunted Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Haunted Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" href="/wiki/Haunted_Forest" title="Haunted Forest">Haunted Forest</a> to the Frozen Shore. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qhorin Halfhand","link":"Qhorin Halfhand","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qhorin Halfhand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" href="/wiki/Qhorin_Halfhand" title="Qhorin Halfhand" class="mw-redirect">Qhorin Halfhand</a>, a ranger of the Night's Watch, is an experienced scout who knows the mountains and the pass almost as well as a wildling.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">There are three main passes through the Frostfangs, of which the Skirling Pass is the southernmost. All three are relatively near the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Fist of the First Men","link":"Fist of the First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Fist of the First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" href="/wiki/Fist_of_the_First_Men" title="Fist of the First Men">Fist of the First Men</a>. The Skirling Pass is the southernmost. North of it is another pass known as the Giant's Stairs. Northernmost of the three is the pass through the mountains which has been carved by a major tributary of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Milkwater","link":"Milkwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Milkwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-52059236156cca87db8ad7" href="/wiki/Milkwater" title="Milkwater">Milkwater</a> River. Of these passes the Milkwater is the easiest to travel through; in contrast, the Skirling Pass (and possibly also the Giant's Stairs) can become snowbound and impassable in cold weather.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The southern Frostfangs (south of the Wall) are actually part of the North, and the mountain tribes who live there are not wildlings but are sworn to House Stark. While they do live a somewhat rougher life than other people in the North, they are on good terms with Winterfell. Indeed, Ned Stark's own grandmother was a member of the Northern mountain clans. When people refer to "the Frostfangs", they are usually referring to the bulk of the range which is north of the Wall and inhabited by the wildlings.
</p>
