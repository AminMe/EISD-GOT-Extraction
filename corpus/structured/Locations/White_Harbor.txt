<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, White Harbor is the main center of trade and merchant activity in the North. Though the smallest of the major cities of Westeros, it is still far larger than any other settlement north of Gulltown and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Braavos","link":"Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Braavos" title="Braavos">Braavos</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The city's harbor is defended by an outer sea wall almost a mile long, with tall towers located every 100 feet along the wall. The mouth of the White Knife River is guarded by the Seal Rock, named for the animals that dot its flanks. The city itself contains an old, mostly-abandoned <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"godswood","link":"godswood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[godswood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Godswood" title="Godswood">godswood</a>, with most religious worship taking place at the Snow Sept. The oldest building in the city is the old keep, the Wolf's Den, which now serves as the city prison. The city is built around the flanks of a hill, with the Manderly keep, New Castle, located the summit of the hill, which commands a wide view of the harbor and surrounding land.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The original settlement was formed around the Wolf's Den fortress, founded at the order of King Jon Stark several millennia ago to provide a trading link with the rest of Westeros, and defend the mouth of the White Knife River against raiders from the sea (both from other kingdoms and from Essos).  Previously, pirates would pass up the White Knife to plunder and capture slaves from the heart of the North, even near Winterfell.  The Starks would bestow rule of the Wolf's Den on various loyal retainers, or younger Stark sons and cousins who wound create their own <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"cadet branches","link":"Cadet branch","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Cadet branch|cadet branches]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Cadet_branch" title="Cadet branch">cadet branches</a> of the family.  The most successful of these was House Greystark, which ruled the Wolf's Den for five centuries, but then joined <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Bolton","link":"House Bolton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Bolton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/House_Bolton" title="House Bolton">House Bolton</a> in a revolt against their Stark cousins, by the end of which the Greystarks were exterminated.  After that it changed hands several times:  House Flint held it for one century, and House Locke for two.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Raiders from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Three Sisters","link":"Three Sisters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Three Sisters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Three_Sisters" title="Three Sisters">Three Sisters</a>, small islands in the Bite notorious as pirate's dens, once captured the Wolf's Den and used it as a base to attack the rest of the North before they were repulsed.  About two thousand years ago, the North turned the tide and invaded the Three Sisters - but in response, the Sistermen turned south to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Vale of Arryn","link":"The Vale of Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Vale of Arryn|the Vale of Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale of Arryn</a> and begged that if the Arryns would help them repulse the Northmen, they would bend the knee to them and become part of the Vale.  This led to a thousand years of on and off war between the North and the Vale over the Three Sisters:  under Osgood Arryn the Wolf's Den was besieged, and it was later captured and burned by his son Oswin.  The Sistermen, however, really didn't want to be ruled by anyone except themselves, so they then switched sides to join the Northmen against the Valemen.  The wars seesawed back and forth like this for a thousand years:  when the North gained enough strength to retake the Wolf's Den and then recapture the Three Sisters, the Sistermen would ally with the Arryns against the Starks, but when the Arryns gained enough strength that they could recapture the Three Sisters and then threaten the Wolf's Den, the Sistermen would switch back to the Starks' side.  The wars continued until about a thousand years ago, when the North simply lost interest in ruling the Three Sisters.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At some unspecified point in time, old King Edrick Stark ruled for nearly a hundred years, but by the end of his reign grew so feeble that slavers from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a> captured the Wolf's Den and used it as a permanent base for slaving raids against the rest of the North.  They held it for some time, until Edrick's great-grandson King Brandon "Ice-eyes" Stark attacked the slavers in the middle of a long and cruel winter, and gave the survivors over to the freed slaves, who hung their former captives' entrails in the local Weirwood <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"heart tree","link":"Heart tree","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Heart tree|heart tree]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Heart_tree" title="Heart tree">heart tree</a> as a blood offering to the Old Gods.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Manderlys, exiled from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingdom of the Reach","link":"Kingdom of the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingdom of the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_the_Reach" title="Kingdom of the Reach">Kingdom of the Reach</a>, were given control of White Harbor a thousand years ago.  The skilled rule of the Manderlys helped grow "the Wolf's Den" from a large town into a true southern-style "city", "White Harbor", albeit the smallest of the five cities in Westeros.  It is thus the only settlement of the North in which a significant portion of the population follows the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a> instead of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Gods of the Forest","link":"Old Gods of the Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Gods of the Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Old_Gods_of_the_Forest" title="Old Gods of the Forest">Old Gods of the Forest</a>, but there have been no major religiously-based tensions between the Manderlys and their neighbors.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Located on the southeastern coast of the North, White Harbor has faced various external threats such as directly facing the hostile border with the Vale of Arryn, and frequent attacks from pirates (coming from the Three Sisters, the Stepstones, or the parts of western Essos that became the Free Cities).  On the other hand, White Harbor has never been attacked by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildlings","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk|wildlings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildlings</a>:  they have made raids along the northern coasts of the North by sailing small boats <i>around</i> the Wall, but they do not possess large sea-worthy vessels capable of sailing nearly as far south as White Harbor.  Being located on the <i>east</i> coast of Westeros, White Harbor has also never been attacked by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"Ironborn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ironborn|ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a>:  any ironborn raiders would <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"have to sail all the way around Westeros","link":"The Laws of Gods and Men","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Laws of Gods and Men|have to sail all the way around Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/The_Laws_of_Gods_and_Men" title="The Laws of Gods and Men">have to sail all the way around Westeros</a> to get that far, passing all four of the larger cities in Westeros (Lannisport, Oldtown, King's Landing, and Gulltown), each of which has its own defensive fleets which would intercept them first.  Even then, as White Harbor is the smallest and least wealthy of these five cities, the ironborn would have little to gain by making such a long voyage.  Indeed, during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dance of the Dragons","link":"Dance of the Dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dance of the Dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Dance_of_the_Dragons" title="Dance of the Dragons">Dance of the Dragons</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen|Aegon II]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II</a> offered that if the Greyjoys joined his side, they would be free to attack White Harbor, which had declared for his sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaenyra","link":"Rhaenyra Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaenyra Targaryen|Rhaenyra]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Rhaenyra_Targaryen" title="Rhaenyra Targaryen">Rhaenyra</a>.  The Greyjoys, however, didn't see much to gain from that, preferring to attack the wealthier and closer cities of Lannisport and Oldtown, so they declared for Rhaenyra instead.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> and Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rodrik Cassel","link":"Rodrik Cassel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rodrik Cassel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Rodrik_Cassel" title="Rodrik Cassel">Rodrik Cassel</a> use a ship from White Harbor to get to King's Landing around the same time that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-25339977656cca87e5c28e" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> and his party, travelling overland, arrive. However, in the television series there is no indication they used this method of travel.
</p>
