<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68659313256cca925d38f0" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Vaith is the seat of House Vaith. Almost ninety years before the events of the series, Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Duncan the Tall","link":"Duncan the Tall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Duncan the Tall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68659313256cca925d38f0" href="/wiki/Duncan_the_Tall" title="Duncan the Tall">Duncan the Tall</a> and his squire <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Egg","link":"Aegon V Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon V Targaryen|Egg]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-68659313256cca925d38f0" href="/wiki/Aegon_V_Targaryen" title="Aegon V Targaryen">Egg</a> visited Vaith and offended Lady Vaith by unknown means. Lady Vaith almost had them killed for insolence, but relented and they were able to go free. According to Egg, Lady Vaith was insane but it is unclear if this was a realistic assessment of her character.
</p>
