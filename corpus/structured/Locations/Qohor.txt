<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Soon after the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom of Valyria","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom of Valyria</a>, four hundred years prior to the War of the Five Kings, Valyria's outlying colonies were thrown into chaos.  This period of civil wars between the Free Cities and invasions from without is remembered as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Century of Blood","link":"Century of Blood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Century of Blood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Century_of_Blood" title="Century of Blood">Century of Blood</a>.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Without the Valyrians and their <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a> to hold them in check anymore, mounted <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> hordes burst forth from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"central plains of Essos","link":"Dothraki Sea","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki Sea|central plains of Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Dothraki_Sea" title="Dothraki Sea">central plains of Essos</a>, rampaging across the continent.  <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Khal","link":"Khal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Khal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Khal" title="Khal">Khal</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Temmo","link":"Temmo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Temmo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Temmo" title="Temmo">Temmo</a> led his <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khalasar","link":"khalasar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khalasar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a> numbering in the tens of thousands towards Qohor, intending to raze the city completely. However, the horde was decisively defeated in the resulting <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Qohor","link":"Battle of Qohor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Qohor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Qohor" title="Battle of Qohor">Battle of Qohor</a> by 3,000 <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Unsullied","link":"Unsullied","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Unsullied]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Unsullied" title="Unsullied">Unsullied</a> warrior-eunuchs purchased from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Astapor","link":"Astapor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Astapor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Astapor" title="Astapor">Astapor</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a>. Temmo himself as well as his sons were killed, and Qohor was saved.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Qohor is still the closest of the Free Cities to the Dothraki Sea, but ever since Temmo's defeat it has been guarded by a standing garrison of Unsullied purchased from Slaver's Bay.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Qohor is the easternmost of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a>. It lies beyond <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pentos","link":"Pentos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pentos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Norvos","link":"Norvos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Norvos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Norvos" title="Norvos">Norvos</a>, close to the edge of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki Sea","link":"Dothraki Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Dothraki_Sea" title="Dothraki Sea">Dothraki Sea</a>. The city lies next to the vast woodland known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Forest of Qohor","link":"Forest of Qohor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Forest of Qohor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Forest_of_Qohor" title="Forest of Qohor">Forest of Qohor</a>. Situated on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qhoyne","link":"Qhoyne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qhoyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Qhoyne" title="Qhoyne">Qhoyne</a>, a tributary of the vast River <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoyne","link":"Rhoyne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Rhoyne" title="Rhoyne">Rhoyne</a>, Qohorik warships control territory down its banks south to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dagger Lake","link":"Dagger Lake","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dagger Lake]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Dagger_Lake" title="Dagger Lake">Dagger Lake</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As in most of the Free Cities, trade is a central part of the economy of Qohor. The city's earliest function was as a lumber camp, and the forest has always been the primary source of its wealth. Qohorik barges carry lengths of timber downriver to the towns and cities of the lower Rhoyne, including <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a>. Many types of animals can be found in the forest, their pelts valuable and highly prized. Silver, tin, and amber can also be found in the forest, and the artisans of Qohor produce high-quality wood carvings and fine tapestries.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Qohor is most famous, however, for its blacksmiths. The quality of metalwork produced in the city cannot be matched even by the finest smiths of Westeros. Qohor is also the only place in the known world where the knowledge of reforging <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian steel","link":"Valyrian steel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian steel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Valyrian_steel" title="Valyrian steel">Valyrian steel</a> has been preserved. The secrets of this process are so zealously guarded that one Maester Pol, who resided in Qohor for several years, was exiled from the city three times- the last of which involved having his hand chopped off- for asking too many questions about it.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The primary deity worshipped in Qohor, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Black Goat","link":"Black Goat of Qohor","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Black Goat of Qohor|Black Goat]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Black_Goat_of_Qohor" title="Black Goat of Qohor">Black Goat</a>, has a very negative reputation (the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bearded Priests of Norvos","link":"Bearded Priests of Norvos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bearded Priests of Norvos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Bearded_Priests_of_Norvos" title="Bearded Priests of Norvos">Bearded Priests of Norvos</a> in particular consider it a demon).  In the religion the Black Goat demands daily blood sacrifices at its temples:  while animals such as calves, bullocks, and horses are typical sacrificial victims, on significant holy days condemned criminals make the march to the altar and face the knives of the priests. When the city is facing great danger, it is not unheard of for the nobility to offer up their own children in the hope that the Black Goat will offer its protection.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Qohor is an essential link in the trade routes that connect western and eastern Essos. Its position at the western edge of the Dothraki Sea makes Qohor the ideal starting point for merchant caravans traveling east across the grasslands to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vaes Dothrak","link":"Vaes Dothrak","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vaes Dothrak]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Vaes_Dothrak" title="Vaes Dothrak">Vaes Dothrak</a>, where they meet and trade with merchants who travel west from the distant lands beyond the Bones (the immense mountain ranges that runs from the northern to southern coasts of the continent), such as the empire of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yi Ti","link":"Yi Ti","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yi Ti]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185510280256cca8762ec12" href="/wiki/Yi_Ti" title="Yi Ti">Yi Ti</a>. By the same token, Qohor is the first of the Free Cities encountered by traders returning from Vaes Dothrak with exotic wares and spices from the Further East. Due to this frequent influx of foreign influences, Qohor has a more "exotic" feel than many of the other Free Cities. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">"Qohor" is pronounced "Kwoh-hor", as opposed to "koh-hor".
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Location</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Qohor is shown in the incorrect location on the Season 2 Viewer's Guide map. This map has Qohor northwest of its proper location, on the wrong tributary of the Rhoyne (and much further away from the Forest of Qohor). However, the "Areas of Control" map for Season 2 puts Qohor in its correct location, matching that of the novels.
</p>
