<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Samyrian</b> is a city located in the east of Essos on the shores of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jade Sea","link":"Jade Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jade Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Jade_Sea" title="Jade Sea" class="mw-redirect">Jade Sea</a>, north-east of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Waste","link":"Red Waste","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Waste]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Red_Waste" title="Red Waste">Red Waste</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lhazar","link":"Lhazar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lhazar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Lhazar" title="Lhazar">Lhazar</a>, north of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bayasabhad","link":"Bayasabhad","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bayasabhad]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Bayasabhad" title="Bayasabhad">Bayasabhad</a>, and south-east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vaes Dothrak","link":"Vaes Dothrak","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vaes Dothrak]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Vaes_Dothrak" title="Vaes Dothrak">Vaes Dothrak</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/locations\/qarth HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, Qarth entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> The name "Samyrian" comes from the HBO Season 2 Viewers' Guide, but it may be the same city that is called "Shamyriana" in the books and "Samyriana" in <i>The Worlds of Ice and Fire </i>​book. <h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, no city called "Samyrian" has appeared. A city called "Shamyriana" has been mentioned. Like <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bayasabhad","link":"Bayasabhad","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bayasabhad]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Bayasabhad" title="Bayasabhad">Bayasabhad</a>, it is known for its warrior-women. Merchants from Shamyriana trade at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vaes Dothrak","link":"Vaes Dothrak","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vaes Dothrak]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Vaes_Dothrak" title="Vaes Dothrak">Vaes Dothrak</a>. It is unclear if Samyrian and Shamyriana are the same city or different locations.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><i>The Lands of Ice and Fire</i> map collection does feature a city called "Samyriana", located east of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki sea","link":"Dothraki sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Dothraki_sea" title="Dothraki sea" class="mw-redirect">Dothraki sea</a> in roughly the same location but on the edge of a vast desert rather than on the coast of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jade Sea","link":"Jade Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jade Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/Jade_Sea" title="Jade Sea" class="mw-redirect">Jade Sea</a> (the Jade Sea having been moved on those maps so it runs south of Essos rather than forming a large bay within it).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ran, the founder of Westeros.org and co-author of <i>The Worlds of Ice and Fire</i> book with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"George R.R. Martin","link":"George R.R. Martin","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[George R.R. Martin]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" href="/wiki/George_R.R._Martin" title="George R.R. Martin">George R.R. Martin</a>, weighed in on the issue: while <i>The Lands of Ice and Fire</i> is meant to be the new canonical geography, the name "Samyriana" appearing on its maps is itself a misprint, and the proper spelling is indeed "Shamyriana". However, because the city was mentioned only once in the first novel, rather than live with a known misprint on the official maps, subsequent reprints of the first novel may just be revised to use the spelling "Samyriana".<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/asoiaf.westeros.org\/index.php\/topic\/63618-the-lands-of-ice-and-fire-a-new-map-book-out-in-oct-2012\/page__st__180#entry3751336 Westeros.org forum]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-185410829056cca8b493875" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> Either way, the name of the city is supposed to end in an "a", and the HBO Season 2 map is in error.
</p>
