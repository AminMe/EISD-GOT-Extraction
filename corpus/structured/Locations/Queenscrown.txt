<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-21794874856cca8cd688cd" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels Queenscrown was renamed after Queen Alysanne Targaryen stayed there. The tower's merlons were repainted in gold in honor of her visit.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Queenscrown consists of a single tall tower located on an island in the midst of a lake. There is a causeway located just under the water, but its location is difficult to see. This makes attacking the holdfast difficult. A small village and inn used to lie on the shores of the lake near the holdfast. The holdfast has been abandoned out of fear of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildling","link":"wildling","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildling]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-21794874856cca8cd688cd" href="/wiki/Wildling" title="Wildling" class="mw-redirect">wildling</a> raids from the north. While the village has long-since fallen into disrepair, the tower remains relatively intact.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">It is at Queenscrown (and not an abandoned mill as depicted on the show) that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bran Stark","link":"Bran Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bran Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-21794874856cca8cd688cd" href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a> and his companions stay overnight as they waited for a storm to pass. While they waited, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"a man","link":"Old man","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old man|a man]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-21794874856cca8cd688cd" href="/wiki/Old_man" title="Old man">a man</a> passing through arrived and stayed in the ruined village to wait out the storm too. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jon Snow","link":"Jon Snow","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jon Snow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-21794874856cca8cd688cd" href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> arrived with the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildlings","link":"wildlings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wildlings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-21794874856cca8cd688cd" href="/wiki/Wildlings" title="Wildlings" class="mw-redirect">wildlings</a> he was travelling with and they captured the man. Jon was ordered to kill the man, but balked and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ygritte","link":"Ygritte","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ygritte]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-21794874856cca8cd688cd" href="/wiki/Ygritte" title="Ygritte">Ygritte</a> killed the man anyway.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Curiously, the map in HBO's Viewer's Guide does specify the location of Queenscrown, but no dialogue in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Rains of Castamere","link":"The Rains of Castamere (episode)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Rains of Castamere (episode)|The Rains of Castamere]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-21794874856cca8cd688cd" href="/wiki/The_Rains_of_Castamere_(episode)" title="The Rains of Castamere (episode)">The Rains of Castamere</a>" specified that this was the identity of the ruins where Bran's party took shelter. The Season 3 Blu-ray might provide more information in the future.
</p>
