<h2>In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111585925256cca8c673734" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels Raventree Hall is ancient, dating back to the days of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111585925256cca8c673734" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a>. The walls are covered in moss, with a tower at each angle in the wall. Two huge, square towers flank the gate. Inside the walls are an outer ward, a large timber keep and a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"godswood","link":"godswood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[godswood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111585925256cca8c673734" href="/wiki/Godswood" title="Godswood">godswood</a>. The godswood contains a single <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"weirwood","link":"weirwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[weirwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111585925256cca8c673734" href="/wiki/Weirwood" title="Weirwood">weirwood</a> of massive size. Hundreds of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ravens","link":"ravens","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ravens]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111585925256cca8c673734" href="/wiki/Ravens" title="Ravens">ravens</a> roost on the dead weirwood, which is said to have been poisoned by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brackens","link":"House Bracken","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Bracken|Brackens]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-111585925256cca8c673734" href="/wiki/House_Bracken" title="House Bracken">Brackens</a>, the Blackwoods' ancestral foes.
</p>
