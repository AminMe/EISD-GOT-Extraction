<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Disputed Lands is a region of open countryside, rivers and at least two large lakes, located at the far south-western tip of the Essos continent. The region stretches from the Sea of Myrth to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Sea","link":"Summer Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Summer_Sea" title="Summer Sea">Summer Sea</a> and from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoyne","link":"Rhoyne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Rhoyne" title="Rhoyne">Rhoyne</a> River. Myr lies to the north, Lys lies on an island off the south coast, Tyrosh lies on an island off the west coast and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a> is to the east, at the mouth of the Rhoyne.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The region was formerly part of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a>. After the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom of Valyria","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom of Valyria</a>, the surrounding cities became independent and made competing claims over the region, a period of chaotic warfare known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Century of Blood","link":"Century of Blood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Century of Blood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Century_of_Blood" title="Century of Blood">Century of Blood</a>. Eventually Volantis launched a huge military campaign which not only overran the region but conquered Myr and Lys, ruling them for more than half a century. When Volantis attempted to seize Tyrosh, it faced a massive counter-offensive by the Tyroshi military, aided by forces from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pentos","link":"Pentos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pentos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a> and the Westerosi <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm King","link":"Storm King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Storm_King" title="Storm King">Storm King</a> . <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Braavos","link":"Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Braavos" title="Braavos">Braavos</a> sponsored an uprising in Lys led by a Lyseni prince using Braavosi warships, while Myr launched a rebellion of its own. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon the Conqueror","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Aegon_the_Conqueror" title="Aegon the Conqueror" class="mw-redirect">Aegon the Conqueror</a>, some years before his own invasion of Westeros, is said to have even provided aid. The bulk of the war was fought in the Disputed Lands, leaving it a smouldering ruin after the war ended with the defeat of Volantis. However, soon afterwards the alliance against Volantis fell apart, and Lys and Myr have been fighting over the region between them ever since.  <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sellsword companies","link":"Mercenary","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mercenary|Sellsword companies]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">Sellsword companies</a> are very active in the Disputed Lands and see much of their regular action there.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Numerous wars have been fought over the Disputed Lands. One of the more notable was forty years ago, when nine notable warlords, including Alequo Aldarys of Tyrosh and Maelys Blackfyre, a claimant to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> of Westeros, banded together to achieve together what they could not alone. In the resulting War of the Ninepenny Kings, the Disputed Lands and Stepstones were conquered, along with the Free City of Tyrosh. King Aegon V Targaryen dispatched a large fleet and army to the Stepstones which destroyed the warlords' army, with the young Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a> slaying Maelys the Monstrous, last of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackfyre Pretenders","link":"House Blackfyre","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Blackfyre|Blackfyre Pretenders]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/House_Blackfyre" title="House Blackfyre">Blackfyre Pretenders</a>, in battle. The conflict has become known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","link":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Ninepenny Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-106071754756cca8948a11d" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings" title="War of the Ninepenny Kings">War of the Ninepenny Kings</a>.
</p>
