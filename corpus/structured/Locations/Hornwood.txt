<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-118407432056cca8be721e9" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Hornwood and its lands are located between the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Manderlys","link":"House Manderly","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Manderly|Manderlys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-118407432056cca8be721e9" href="/wiki/House_Manderly" title="House Manderly">Manderlys</a> of White Harbor and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Boltons","link":"House Bolton","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Bolton|Boltons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-118407432056cca8be721e9" href="/wiki/House_Bolton" title="House Bolton">Boltons</a> of the Dreadfort and have been contested between the two in the past. The Hornwoods maintain their independence from both due to their loyalty to the Starks.
