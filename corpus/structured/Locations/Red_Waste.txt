<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">After pillaging the lands of Lhazar, the Dothraki <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khalasar","link":"khalasar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khalasar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a></i> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Khal Drogo","link":"Khal Drogo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Khal Drogo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Khal_Drogo" title="Khal Drogo" class="mw-redirect">Khal Drogo</a> skirts the northern edge of the Red Waste.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[Baelor]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> It is in these border regions where Drogo falls from his horse and his <i>khalasar</i> scatters, and also where <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen|Daenerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a> manages to hatch her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragon","link":"dragon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon" class="mw-redirect">dragon</a> eggs.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[Fire and Blood]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 2","link":"Season 2","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 2]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">Season 2</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Daenerys and her retainers travel across the Red Waste in search of succor. After Daenerys's horse dies from lack of water, she sends three of her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"bloodrider","link":"bloodrider","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[bloodrider]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Bloodrider" title="Bloodrider">bloodriders</a> in search of civilization. Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a> tells Daenerys that he has not been as far east as the Red Waste, but he has heard that it does have an end and it is possible to cross it.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[The North Remembers]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> One of Daenerys's bloodriders, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rakharo","link":"Rakharo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rakharo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Rakharo" title="Rakharo">Rakharo</a>, is executed by a Dothraki <i>khalasar</i> and his head sent back to her.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[The Night Lands]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Red Waste is extremely large, taking weeks to cross, and is unrelentingly hostile. Water is scarce and food sources almost non-existent. The wasteland is located east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a> and west of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jade Sea","link":"Jade Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jade Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Jade_Sea" title="Jade Sea" class="mw-redirect">Jade Sea</a>. The city of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qarth","link":"Qarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Qarth" title="Qarth">Qarth</a> lies southeast of the waste. Its southern edge runs along the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Sea","link":"Summer Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Summer_Sea" title="Summer Sea">Summer Sea</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">There are ruins in the waste, suggesting that at one time it was much more pleasant and habitable than now. One such ruined city is called Vaes Tolorro, the City of Bones. Several of the ruined cities were actually colonies of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qarth","link":"Qarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Qarth" title="Qarth">Qarth</a> which were destroyed by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> when they swept across Essos after the Doom of Valyria, during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Century of Blood","link":"Century of Blood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Century of Blood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-63887067456cca8817ebde" href="/wiki/Century_of_Blood" title="Century of Blood">Century of Blood</a>. The Dothraki never reached as far as Qarth itself, however, because of the vast distance across the Red Waste. These oasis-colonies were once suitable for habitation due to large irrigation projects, but when the Dothraki sacked them these were destroyed, and the surrounding desert expanded through the ruins.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Red Waste is not strictly speaking a true "rolling sands" desert, though it is a desert in the broad definition that it receives virtually no rainfall - more precisely, a rocky desert. It is actually far more harsh and dangerous than even a dry sandy desert, due to its low rocky hills, barren windswept plains, and dry riverbeds. It is more of a blasted wasteland than a simple sandy desert, which explains why it is known as the "Red Waste" and not simply the "Red Desert".
</p>
