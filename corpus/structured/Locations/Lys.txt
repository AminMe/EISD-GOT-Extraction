<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Like the other Free Cities, Lys was founded as a colony of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a>. After the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom of Valyria","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom of Valyria</a>, four centuries before the War of the Five Kings, it became an independent city-state. Lys has frequently feuded with the neighboring Free City of Myr, located to the north.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Irogenia of Lys","link":"Irogenia of Lys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Irogenia of Lys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Irogenia_of_Lys" title="Irogenia of Lys">Irogenia of Lys</a> was a famous courtesan from the city, whose beauty is legendary, and is said to have been the most attractive and skilled courtesan to have ever lived.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Kingsroad]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Salladhor and his sellsail ships were in the rearguard of Stannis's fleet during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Blackwater","link":"Battle of the Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a>, and thus were not caught in the disastrous <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildfire","link":"Wildfire","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wildfire|wildfire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a> explosion that destroyed most of Stannis's fleet. After Stannis and his few remaining forces fled back to Dragonstone, Salladhor decided that Stannis would probably lose the war and never be able to pay him, so he stopped serving him.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Valar Dohaeris]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Outside of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yunkai","link":"Yunkai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yunkai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Yunkai" title="Yunkai">Yunkai</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> meets with the captains of the sellsword company known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Second Sons","link":"Second Sons (mercenary company)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Second Sons (mercenary company)|Second Sons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Second_Sons_(mercenary_company)" title="Second Sons (mercenary company)" class="mw-redirect">Second Sons</a>, in the hope of swaying them from her enemies' service to her own. The meeting does not go well, as their crass captain <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mero","link":"Mero","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mero]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Mero" title="Mero">Mero</a> makes lewd and insulting remarks to Daenerys, openly saying that he thinks he might have had sex with her once in a pleasure-house in Lys.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Second Sons (episode)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"<i>A Song of Ice and Fire<\/i>","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire|''A Song of Ice and Fire'']]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire"><i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i></a> novels, Lys is one of the more powerful of the Free Cities, as well as the most beautiful: the land is covered with palm and fruit trees, the surrounding waters are abundant with fish, and sunsets in Lys are said to be the most beautiful in the world. The island chain known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a> lies to the northwest of the city and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Disputed Lands","link":"Disputed Lands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Disputed Lands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Disputed_Lands" title="Disputed Lands">Disputed Lands</a> to the northeast. Lys frequently wars with the cities of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myr</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrosh","link":"Tyrosh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrosh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Tyrosh" title="Tyrosh">Tyrosh</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Volantis","link":"Volantis","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Volantis]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a> over control of both these regions.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As with most of the Free Cities, Lys was originally a colony of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a>, specifically a retreat/resort. Since it was founded from scratch by the Freehold rather than being a conquered territory, Lys retains stronger Valyrian bloodlines than any of the other Free Cities. Even the smallfolk often possess Valyrian features, including light-colored hair, skin and eyes. The Valyrians themselves had platinum-blonde to silvery-white hair and purple eyes (as exemplified by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryens","link":"Targaryens","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Targaryens]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Targaryens" title="Targaryens" class="mw-redirect">Targaryens</a>), though in the present-day common features associated with the Lysene are blonde hair and blue eyes. Even the Targaryen rulers of Westeros sometimes looked towards Lys in search of wives and lovers, as much for their Valyrian blood as their beauty. In contrast to Lys, the inhabitants of the city's great rival Myr tend to have dark hair, dark eyes and olive skin.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slavery","link":"Slavery","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slavery]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">Slavery</a> is legal in Lys and it has a fairly large slave population, outnumbering freeborn citizens three to one. The pillow-houses of the city are far-famed, and they are known for producing the finest bed-slaves and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"prostitutes","link":"prostitutes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[prostitutes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Prostitutes" title="Prostitutes" class="mw-redirect">prostitutes</a> in the world. Beauty and blood purity are highly valued by the Lysene, who deliberately breed attractive slaves together in the hope that their offspring will be even more lovely and refined. Besides its bed-slaves and prostitutes, Lys is known for its production of tapestries, perfumes and sweet wines. The alchemists of the city also produce two rare and deadly posions; the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"tears of Lys","link":"tears of Lys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[tears of Lys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Tears_of_Lys" title="Tears of Lys">tears of Lys</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the strangler","link":"the strangler","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the strangler]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/The_strangler" title="The strangler">the strangler</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lys is home to a native love goddess (whose image graces the city's coinage), but temples to many deities can be found within its walls, including a temple to the red god R'hllor, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Light","link":"Lord of Light","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Light]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Light" title="Lord of Light" class="mw-redirect">Lord of Light</a>. Another of these deities the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Weeping Woman","link":"Weeping Woman","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Weeping Woman]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Weeping_Woman" title="Weeping Woman">Weeping Woman</a> or the Weeping Lady of Lys, whose statue can also be seen in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House of Black and White","link":"House of Black and White","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House of Black and White]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/House_of_Black_and_White" title="House of Black and White">House of Black and White</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Braavos","link":"Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Braavos" title="Braavos">Braavos</a>. So far, it is not clear in-universe if the Weeping Woman and the love goddess are the same figure or if they belong to separate traditions. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While the Lysene are noted for their love of the sensual arts, they are also formidable sailors. Lys's navy is one of the most powerful on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer","link":"Summer Sea","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Sea|Summer]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Summer_Sea" title="Summer Sea">Summer</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea|Narrow]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow</a> seas, aside from that of Braavos, and Lysene freesails can be found in many of the ports of the world. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Valyria was destroyed in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria|Doom]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom</a>, Lys became an independent state. It was then conquered by Volantis, which was trying to reforge the Freehold. Lys and Myr remained subservient to Volantis until the city overreached and launched an attack on Tyrosh. Myr rose in rebellion and an exiled Lysene lord liberated the city with the help of 100 warships loaned to him by Braavos. Additional forces from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pentos","link":"Pentos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pentos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a> and the Westerosi <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Storm King","link":"Storm King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Storm King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Storm_King" title="Storm King">Storm King</a> helped defeat Volantis and destroyed its armies in what are now the Disputed Lands.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lys regained its independence following the war, but the alliances soon fell apart, so that Myr and Lys have been perpetually warring with each other for the better part of the past three hundred years. The great rivalry of Myr and Lys is usually played out in the Disputed Lands between them, so-called because they are constantly changing hands between the two. Volantis and Tyrosh sometimes get dragged into these wars on one side or the other, as the alliances shift around.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eddard Stark","link":"Eddard Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eddard Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> came to arrest Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of Bear Island","link":"Lord of Bear Island","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of Bear Island]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Lord_of_Bear_Island" title="Lord of Bear Island">Lord of Bear Island</a>, for selling poachers to slavers, Jorah and his wife <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lynesse","link":"Lynesse Hightower","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lynesse Hightower|Lynesse]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Lynesse_Hightower" title="Lynesse Hightower">Lynesse</a> fled to Lys where Jorah attempted to make his living as a sellsword, but he was unable to provide an income acceptable to Lynesse and she ran out on him to become a concubine to the Lysenes merchant Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tregar Ormollen","link":"Tregar Ormollen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tregar Ormollen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Tregar_Ormollen" title="Tregar Ormollen">Tregar Ormollen</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-6607253556cca86b259e9" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Lys" is pronounced "LEESE", as opposed to "Liss", "Lice", etc.
</p>
