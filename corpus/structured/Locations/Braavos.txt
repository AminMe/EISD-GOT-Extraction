<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Braavos was founded as a secret city by escaped slaves and refugees from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a>. After the Doom of Valyria, it rose to prominence, becoming a major trade and banking center. Its most powerful institution is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Bank of Braavos","link":"Iron Bank of Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Bank of Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Iron_Bank_of_Braavos" title="Iron Bank of Braavos">Iron Bank of Braavos</a>.
</p>

<p data-rte-fromparser="true">King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a> and Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Davos Seaworth","link":"Davos Seaworth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Davos Seaworth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Davos_Seaworth" title="Davos Seaworth">Davos Seaworth</a> visit Braavos to treat with the Iron Bank. <p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:Braavos wideshot.jpg|thumb|A wider view of the entire city of Braavos]]","title":"Braavos_wideshot.jpg","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"Braavos wideshot","caption":"A wider view of the entire city of Braavos","captionParsed":"A wider view of the entire city of Braavos"}}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" alt="" src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/9/99/Braavos_wideshot.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20150502214538" width="180" height="101" class="image thumb withCaption" data-image-name="Braavos wideshot.jpg" data-image-key="Braavos_wideshot.jpg"  type="image" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->The architecture and costuming of Braavos, which first prominently appeared on-screen in Season 4, were heavily inspired by the Dutch Golden Age of the 16th to 17th centuries:

<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Frank Doelger","link":"Frank Doelger","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Frank Doelger]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Frank_Doelger" title="Frank Doelger">Frank Doelger</a> (Executive Producer) "Once we decided we were using the Netherlands in the 16th and 17th century as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the model for the buildings","link":"Wikipedia:Dutch Baroque architecture","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Dutch Baroque architecture|the model for the buildings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Baroque_architecture" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Dutch Baroque architecture">the model for the buildings</a> of Braavos, Michele then picked up on that, and designed the costumes for Braavos along the same lines."

<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When the Dutch Republic achieved independence after the Eighty Years War ended, its resulting government was a non-hereditary general assembly - in theory; in practice it was dominated by oligarchical merchant families. Still, this led to a strong anti-monarchist sentiment during the Dutch Golden Age, a time espousing egalitarian values. Architectural designs from the period have a marked emphasis on sobriety and restraint - in contrast with wealthy kings and nobles each trying to outdo each other with increasingly more opulent architectural designs. In many ways this matches how Braavosi society is described in the novels: founded by escaped slaves from all races who fled from Valyrian control, the Braavos are strongly opposed to the slave trade, and has no hereditary nobles or kings. Like the Dutch Republic, Braavosi politics are largely dominated by wealthy merchant-princes and bankers.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Physically, due to its many canals and romance culture vaguely similar to medieval Italian city-states, Braavos in the novels is vaguely similar to real-life <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Venice","link":"Wikipedia:Venice","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Venice|Venice]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venice" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Venice">Venice</a>. However, in terms of climate and weather, it is one of the northernmost of the Free Cities (at the same latitude as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Eyrie","link":"The Eyrie","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Eyrie|the Eyrie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/The_Eyrie" title="The Eyrie">the Eyrie</a> in Westeros), and is noted for having cooler temperatures with mists rolling in off the water - more akin to northern European cities like Amsterdam. All-in-all Bruges (one of the places often referred to as "the Venice of the North") is the closer analogy than Venice - and indeed, the TV series based the look of Braavos on the Dutch Republic (albeit, Bruges was <i>near</i> the Dutch Republic but was not ruled by it). The giant statue known as the Titan of Braavos is analogous to that of the ancient Greek Colossus of Rhodes.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Indeed, Production Designer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Deborah Riley","link":"Deborah Riley","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Deborah Riley]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Deborah_Riley" title="Deborah Riley">Deborah Riley</a> said in a Season 4 featurette: "David and Dan had always said that perhaps an inspiration for Braavos could be Venice. And the thing that I think was quite exciting about Braavos (in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Laws of Gods and Men","link":"The Laws of Gods and Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Laws of Gods and Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/The_Laws_of_Gods_and_Men" title="The Laws of Gods and Men">The Laws of Gods and Men</a>") was being able to show wealth, but being able to show it through an austerity, through the notion that less is more."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.makinggameofthrones.com\/production-diary\/the-titan-of-braavos]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Braavos lies at the northwestern-most end of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> continent, roughly due east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Vale of Arryn","link":"the Vale of Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Vale of Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale of Arryn</a>. It is the only one of the Nine Free Cities which was not a former colony of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a>. The city was founded by galley slaves after they staged a mutiny and killed their Valyrian overseers. Directed by the Moonsingers, they sailed as far north as they could in the Narrow Sea in search of a refuge. They established the Secret City of Braavos and kept its location secret for over four centuries, accepting other refugees and escaped slaves in the process. Because of the nature of its founding, Braavos does not keep slaves, does not like to do business with those who keep slaves, and frequently uses its military and economic power to encourage other states to give up slavery. After the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom of Valyria","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom of Valyria</a> four hundred years ago Braavos revealed itself to the world, and as the other Free Cities were left in chaos for decades after the Doom, Braavos rose to become the most powerful of the Free Cities. Due to its unique origin, Braavos is sometimes referred to as the Bastard Son of Valyria.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Because the Valyrians took slaves from across the vast reaches of the continent of Essos, its slave population was very diverse. Due to its origins as a refuge for slaves escaping from Valyria, Braavos is ethnically and culturally very diverse, with much more of a "cosmopolitan" and polyglot society than even the other Free Cities. There is no one majority religion in Braavos as a result: worshipers of all major religions may be found there (even the Faith of the Seven and the Lord of Light have at least small temples in Braavos), as well as numerous minor local religions. As a result of this diversity, there is no set "Braavosi ethnicity" or set of physical features. This is in contrast with some of the other Free Cities, such as Myr (whose inhabitants tend to have dark features and olive-skin) or Lys (whose inhabitants have classic Valyrian looks of pale skin, blue eyes, and blonde to white hair).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The city sprawls across a hundred islands in a vast lagoon. The nearby mainland is mostly marshland, while the seaward sides of the city are protected by tall, mountainous islands which form a semi-circle around the city. There is only one channel large and deep enough to accommodate ocean-going ships. This channel is defended by a massive statue called the Titan of Braavos, which serves as landmark, lighthouse and defensive fortification. Just beyond the Titan lies a vast shipyard called the Arsenal, which is capable of assembling a war galley out of local materials in just a day. The city proper lies beyond.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The city itself consists of a hundred unwalled islands linked by bridges and separated by water, either natural channels or canals. Several parts of the city have sunk over the years, although the tops of the buildings are still visible emerging about the waves. The city is served by two immense harbors, the Ragman's Harbor in the west were foreign ships are berthed and the Purple Harbor in the north, used by locals. All ships have to submit to inspection from officials at the Chequy Port before being directed to a berth.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The city is ruled by the Sealord, though various nobles have a say in the city's affairs. The city is religiously tolerant, with many temples to many deities (including the Westeros <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a>) located on the Isle of the Gods. A sect known as the Moonsingers are the most popular religion in Braavos, however. The city has a strong military force, consisting of the largest fleet of ships on the Narrow Sea and the resources necessary to hire large armies of mercenaries and freeriders very quickly. The city also has a powerful banking institution, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Bank","link":"Iron Bank of Braavos","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Bank of Braavos|Iron Bank]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Iron_Bank_of_Braavos" title="Iron Bank of Braavos">Iron Bank</a>, which holds the debts of many other governments (including the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> of Westeros), furthering Braavosi influence and power. Currently, the Iron Bank of Braavos is bigger than the banks of all the other Free Cities put together, and plays a major role in financial transactions across both Essos and Westeros.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Braavos is strongly allied to the Free City of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pentos","link":"Pentos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pentos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a>, located further south along the coast. The Braavosi influence has seen Pentos outlaw slavery within the city (though very wealthy Pentoshi like Illyrio Mopatis privately flout this with their "servants").
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At the time of the War of the Five Kings, Braavos is more or less the most powerful of the Free Cities. The Free City with the largest population is Volantis, the first colony of Valyria which used to be the most powerful. However, Volantis entered into a decline three hundred years ago after attempting and failing to conquer all of the other Free Cities. While still one of the more powerful of the group, due to its decline and internal problems supporting its large population, Volantis was edged out by Braavos as the most powerful overall. This is despite the fact that Braavos actually has a relatively small population, and physically all of Braavos could easily fit within Volantis's harbor.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The historical development of Braavos also has some loose parallels with ancient <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Athens","link":"Wikipedia:Athens","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Athens|Athens]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Athens" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Athens">Athens</a>. Braavos is actually located on very poor swamp lands, and does not actually produce many of its own products for export: the Braavosi economy is based on banking, and maintaining a large merchant fleet which transports goods between the different Free Cities and Westeros. The ancient Athenian historian <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Thucydides","link":"Wikipedia:Thucydides","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Thucydides|Thucydides]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thucydides" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Thucydides">Thucydides</a>, writing in the fifth century B.C., pointed out that Athens was also located on lands which were actually poor relative to other parts of Greece, yet it rose to dominate much of the rest of Greece. Thucydides theorized that because Athens started out on poor lands, it wasn't a frequent target of foreign invasions, while other more wealthy cities were constantly being invaded or fighting trade wars. Thus while these other cities may have been physically richer, they could not capitalize on this raw wealth, while poor Athens enjoyed enough long-term political stability that it eventually became a major hub for the development of regional commerce. Similarly, Braavos is relatively small, and has few natural resources, but because it wasn't reliant on Valyria it enjoyed political and economic stability during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Century of Blood","link":"Century of Blood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Century of Blood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Century_of_Blood" title="Century of Blood">Century of Blood</a>, the hundred years of constant civil wars between the Free Cities following the Doom of Valyria. Because Braavos was so stable, its banks and shipping became the most reliable, until it dominated commerce throughout the rest of the Free Cities.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-160933922456cca8459d0d2" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Braavos" is pronounced "BRA-vos ".
</p>
