</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Norvos</b> is one of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> located to the east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. It lies in the interior plains of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> on the Noyne, a tributary of the massive Rhyone River. The Free City of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pentos","link":"Pentos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pentos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a> is located to the southwest and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qohor","link":"Qohor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Qohor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Qohor" title="Qohor">Qohor</a> to the southeast. Norvos is on the route leading from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki Sea","link":"Dothraki Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Dothraki_Sea" title="Dothraki Sea">Dothraki Sea</a>, and as such pays tribute to passing <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"khalasar","link":"khalasar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[khalasar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasars</a></i>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/locations\/pentos\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, Pentos entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> People and things from Norvos are known as <b>Norvoshi</b>.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Norvos is described as a city in two parts. The low city sprawls along the river with numerous docks and wharves, while the high city extends along the hilltops. The two parts are linked by a massive stone staircase known as the "Sinner's Steps" and both are walled and fortified. Norvos was founded as a colony by religious dissidents from Valyria, who wanted the freedom to enforce their strict religion without the influence of outsiders - they became the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bearded Priests of Norvos","link":"Bearded Priests of Norvos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bearded Priests of Norvos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Bearded_Priests_of_Norvos" title="Bearded Priests of Norvos">Bearded Priests of Norvos</a>.  Today the city is still a theocracy ruled by the Bearded Priests - nominally it is ruled by a council of magisters, but they have no real power, and are appointed and removed at whim by the Bearded Priests.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Bearded Priests enforced an austere and conservative religion, instructing the populace that sex should only be engaged in rarely, expressly for the production of children and not pleasure.  Moreover, Norvos is one of the northern Free Cities, yet in the hills far away from the warming waters of the coasts, giving it a much more cold, dour, and reserved aspect - compared to some of the southern Free Cities such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lys","link":"Lys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Lys" title="Lys">Lys</a>, with its warm climate and pleasure houses filled with scantily clad prostitutes.  Further away from the stern watch of the Bearded Priests, however, in the lower city of Norvos there is still mirth enough to be found in the taverns of the working class commoners.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Bears are used as entertainment in the city, and are known to dance up and down the Sinner's Steps. The city has three bells - each with a distinctive sound - which ring out at different times of the day to signal various activities. The bells are so important to the functioning of Norvos that they even have their own names; Noom, Narrah and Nyel.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The city is mercantile, its wealth based upon its strategic position on the trade route from Pentos to Qohor and the Dothraki sea. Minerals are mined from the Hills of Norvos, which stretch from the city to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shivering Sea","link":"Shivering Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shivering Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Shivering_Sea" title="Shivering Sea">Shivering Sea</a>. The city is famous for the axes it produces, as well as for high-quality textiles. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Myr","link":"Myr","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Myr]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Myr" title="Myr">Myr</a> is also famous for its textiles and produces fine lace and carpets, but Norvos is world-renowned for its fine tapestries.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Soldiers and warriors are trained in the city, recruited by the Bearded Priests when they are just boys. Some Norvosi prefer the use of axes in battle to the more common choice of swords.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Areo Hotah","link":"Areo Hotah","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Areo Hotah]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Areo_Hotah" title="Areo Hotah">Areo Hotah</a> is the only significant character from Norvos to appear within the first five novels (though he is a POV narrator).  Only two other named characters from Norvos are even mentioned. Lady Mellario herself is important to Areo and Doran's backstory, but because she moved back to Norvos she is mentioned but does not appear within the narrative. The only other named Norvoshi was Byan Votris, captain of the trade caravan to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vaes Dothrak","link":"Vaes Dothrak","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vaes Dothrak]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Vaes_Dothrak" title="Vaes Dothrak">Vaes Dothrak</a> which contained the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wineseller","link":"Wineseller","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wineseller]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Wineseller" title="Wineseller">Wineseller</a> who tried to poison Daenerys.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">It is not yet clear if the TV-version of Areo will still be from Norvos, or if the TV series will give the explanation that he is from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Islands","link":"Summer Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Summer_Islands" title="Summer Islands">Summer Islands</a> but grew up in Norvos, or if he was simply living in the Summer Islands immediately before he came to Dorne (and never lived in Norvos). When other characters such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Xaro Xhoan Daxos","link":"Xaro Xhoan Daxos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Xaro Xhoan Daxos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Xaro_Xhoan_Daxos" title="Xaro Xhoan Daxos">Xaro Xhoan Daxos</a> or <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Salladhor Saan","link":"Salladhor Saan","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Salladhor Saan]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" href="/wiki/Salladhor_Saan" title="Salladhor Saan">Salladhor Saan</a> were changed to be black in the TV series, the writers introduced the explanation that they were originally "from" the Summer Islands before moving to their current home (i.e. Xaro directly states that he was originally from the Summer Islands). The Summer Islands are very close to Dorne, being located directly south from Dorne across the Summer Sea, so it actually isn't implausible to find Summer Islanders living in Dorne. Then again, the Free Cities are ethnically very mixed and Areo could very easily just happen to be a black man from Norvos (also, given that he was sold by his poor family into the service of the Bearded Priests of Norvos in the novels, he might just be a freed slave). Actor DeObia Oparei has himself stated in interviews that Areo is from Norvos, but no official TV materials have ever mentioned it, so Oparei might just be going by the character in the novels - leaving it unclear if this is also the case in the TV continuity. Thus TV-Areo is <i>assumed</i> to still be from Norvos, unless stated otherwise.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/watchersonthewall.com\/michael-mcelhatton-plus-new-interviews-with-deobia-oparei-and-lena-headey\/]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-93965369656cca87c3bcf7" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
