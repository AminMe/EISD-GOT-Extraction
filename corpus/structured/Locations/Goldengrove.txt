<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Goldengrove is the seat of House Rowan. In the novels, Lord Mathis Rowan is one of Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mace Tyrell","link":"Mace Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mace Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/Mace_Tyrell" title="Mace Tyrell">Mace Tyrell</a>'s closest and most trusted advisors (along with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Paxter Redwyne","link":"Paxter Redwyne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Paxter Redwyne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/Paxter_Redwyne" title="Paxter Redwyne">Paxter Redwyne</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Randyll Tarly","link":"Randyll Tarly","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Randyll Tarly]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/Randyll_Tarly" title="Randyll Tarly">Randyll Tarly</a>) and has a reputation for steadfast prudence that is known even outside <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Reach","link":"the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/The_Reach" title="The Reach">the Reach</a>. He accompanies King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Renly Baratheon","link":"Renly Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Renly Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a> on his campaign. Notably, in the novels it is to him that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> makes the remark about Renly's followers being the '<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"knights","link":"knights","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[knights]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/Knights" title="Knights" class="mw-redirect">knights</a> of summer', rather than Renly himself as in the TV series.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels it was Mathis Rowan who sent <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dareon","link":"Dareon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dareon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/Dareon" title="Dareon">Dareon</a> to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-65409327956cca8e4c6332" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> after finding him abed with one of his daughters.
</p>
