<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->The <b>Stepstones</b> are a chain of about a dozen or so islands located east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> and just west of the coast of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> (in particular, the region known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Disputed Lands","link":"Disputed Lands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Disputed Lands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Disputed_Lands" title="Disputed Lands">Disputed Lands</a>).  They are infamously filled with pirate dens, an uncontrollable border-zone periodically fought over by different Free Cities and even the Seven Kingdoms but not really controlled by anyone.  Pirates, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sellsails, and sellswords","link":"Mercenary","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mercenary|sellsails, and sellswords]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellsails, and sellswords</a> that infest the Stepstones prey on merchant shipping that passes through them into and out of the Narrow Sea.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to myth, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> were once joined by a great land-bridge - known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arm of Dorne","link":"Arm of Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arm of Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Arm_of_Dorne" title="Arm of Dorne">Arm of Dorne</a> - across which the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a> invaded Westeros. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Children of the Forest","link":"Children of the Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Children of the Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Forest" title="Children of the Forest">Children of the Forest</a> destroyed the land-bridge with magic more than ten thousand years ago, leaving behind only the shattered islands as evidence of its passing.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season1\/#!\/locations\/lys\/ HBO viewers guide, season 1 map, Lys entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />  In the present day, the Stepstones mark the border between the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> to the north and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Sea","link":"Summer Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Summer_Sea" title="Summer Sea">Summer Sea</a> to the south. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free City","link":"Free City","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free City]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Free_City" title="Free City" class="mw-redirect">Free City</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrosh","link":"Tyrosh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrosh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Tyrosh" title="Tyrosh">Tyrosh</a> is located on the northeastern-most island in the chain. 
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, two of the islands in the Stepstones chain have been named so far: Bloodstone and Grey Gallows. Bloodstone is the largest, and the westernmost, located closest to Dorne. Grey Gallows is located south of Bloodstone. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Free city <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrosh","link":"Tyrosh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrosh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Tyrosh" title="Tyrosh">Tyrosh</a> itself is located on the easternmost and northernmost of the Stepstones - though it is usually not considered when generally speaking about "the Stepstones" as a border zone filled with pirate dens, given that Tyrosh is a strong and self-reliant city-state controlling large amounts of territory on the mainland.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Attempts by both the Seven Kingdoms and the Free Cities to conquer all of the Stepstones have failed. Apart from the fact that rival Free Cities are always competing over them, the local pirates and outlaws consistently resist any attempts made to impose outside rule. The Stepstones are predominantly lawless pirate-dens, but all sea shipping to the northern Free Cities (Myr, Pentos, Lorath, and particularly <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Braavos","link":"Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Braavos" title="Braavos">Braavos</a>) has to pass through them. Shipping from nearby Lys, and even to an extent from Volantis, is also sometimes in danger of attacks from the pirates of the Steptones.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Periodically the pirates in the Stepstones grow to such numbers that fleets from Westeros and the Free Cities gather in major campaigns to wipe them out, but new pirates always replace them eventually.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Occasionally, pirates from the Stepstones have even invaded the east coasts of Westeros in large numbers.  During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest|Targaryen Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">Targaryen Conquest</a>, pirates from the Stepstones used the distraction of Aegon the Conqueror and his dragons landing to the north to invade Cape Wrath in the east of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stormlands","link":"The Stormlands","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Stormlands|the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a> (though the Targaryens themselves drove them out before too long).  During the reign of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaehaerys I Targaryen","link":"Jaehaerys I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaehaerys I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen" title="Jaehaerys I Targaryen">Jaehaerys I Targaryen</a> over two hundred years ago, Myrish pirates from the Stepstones even seized and held the eastern half of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tarth","link":"Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Tarth" title="Tarth">Tarth</a> island, northeast of Cape Wrath and a considerable distance away from the Stepstones themselves.  The Targaryens cleared them out easily enough with their dragons, but Jaehaerys I's eldest son Prince Aegon ended up dying in the battle nonetheless.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daemon Targaryen","link":"Daemon Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daemon Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Daemon_Targaryen" title="Daemon Targaryen">Daemon Targaryen</a> briefly conquered most of the Stepstones using his dragon <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Caraxes","link":"Caraxes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Caraxes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Caraxes" title="Caraxes">Caraxes</a> and an army of sellswords and adventure-seekers, and established his own Kingdom of the Stepstones - his own proxy-kingdom nominally independent from but allied with the Iron Throne.  In truth it was never really a functional realm in its own right so much as it was whichever islands his sellsword army happened to control at a given time.  He played at war like this for several years but later abandoned the Stepstones altogether, which collapsed back into pirate control not long afterwards.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Stepstones were conquered by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maelys the Monstrous","link":"Maelys the Monstrous","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maelys the Monstrous]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Maelys_the_Monstrous" title="Maelys the Monstrous" class="mw-redirect">Maelys the Monstrous</a>, one of the Blackfyre Pretenders, in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","link":"War of the Ninepenny Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Ninepenny Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Ninepenny_Kings" title="War of the Ninepenny Kings">War of the Ninepenny Kings</a>, as a staging area for an invasion of Westeros. King Jaehaerys II sent an army to destroy his forces before they were ready, and Maelys Blackfyre was killed in battle by Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Barristan Selmy","link":"Barristan Selmy","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Barristan Selmy]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-113379202356cca87b6639f" href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a>.
</p>
