<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">​People and politics</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The people of Qarth are known as the <b>Qartheen</b> and call their home "The greatest city that ever was or will be." Qarth is an oligarchy ruled by a council of nobles and merchants known as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Thirteen","link":"the Thirteen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Thirteen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/The_Thirteen" title="The Thirteen">the Thirteen</a>, most of which are members of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pureborn","link":"Pureborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pureborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Pureborn" title="Pureborn">Pureborn</a>, an aristocracy descending from the deposed <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kings of Qarth","link":"King of Qarth","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of Qarth|Kings of Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/King_of_Qarth" title="King of Qarth">Kings of Qarth</a>. Although they rule by consensus, a member of the Thirteen can overrule the decision of the rest by invoking <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sumai","link":"sumai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[sumai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Sumai" title="Sumai">sumai</a></i>, pledging their life and their blood to back up their decision.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[Garden of Bones]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Status is quite important among the Qartheen, and they tend to be very concerned about appearances; the men wear vests which are covered in extravagant gold tracing and jewelry on the front, and women sometimes dye their hair to appear more exotic. Both men and women decorate their clothing with gold pieces shaped like insects and encrusted with jewels. Appearances, however, are not only a matter of status among the Qartheen, but also of survival; among the Thirteen for example, those who do not hold hereditary seats will only remain on the council as long as they can appear wealthy and powerful before the eyes of the other twelve members. Any failure to keep up appearances and they will be removed and quickly replaced.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">When Daenerys first enters Qarth in "Garden of Bones", the wide shot of the city inside of the gates is not a location shot but almost entirely a special effects production, aside from the gates themselves and a few bushes. The production team wanted the gates themselves to have a look of age and a foreboding appearance, and inspired by the books they are covered with animal designs. The physical exterior set of the gates of Qarth was over 100 feet wide. It was constructed in Dubac quarry outside of Dubrovnik, Croatia, which had recently been flattened because it was going to be converted into a cemetery - thus the "Garden of Bones" outside the gates was about to be converted into an actual boneyard.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Costume Designer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Michele Clapton","link":"Michele Clapton","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Michele Clapton]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Michele_Clapton" title="Michele Clapton">Michele Clapton</a> explained that she wanted to show how the Qartheen are very opulent, but also that everything is a facade and for show. Thus the front of their costumes are lavish and ornate to impress others when they face them, but they don't bother to decorate the back of their clothing at all:
</p>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd><i>"I always in my head, saw Qarth as a sort of mirage, so with that in mind, it's a sort of case of taking to Gemma about the architecture, obviously the climate. We started actually with the fabrics, really. We dyed all the silk, and then printed, it's almost like gilded gates on it, and then I had a painter who came in and actually painted the sort of fronds and the flowers and the gold behind the gate. With their underskirts, we actually had them baked in clay and then shook them out, and so it gives this lovely crumpled sort of weight to the fabric, that you really can't get any other way. And then with those costumes I wanted them to be able to be worn in different ways, so when they come out of the gates they lift it up over their heads and it protects them from the heat, and other times they can be down and the jacket takes prominence. And then again I wanted it to be like a facade, like a theatrical piece, so that when they turn around a lot of the backs are just linen, or hessian [burlap], so it's all a front, it's all a show."</i><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.hbogo.com\/#series\/video&assetID=GOROSTGP35015?videoMode=embeddedVideo?showSpecialFeatures=true\/ \"Garden of Bones\" In-Episode Guide]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Qarth is a great and immensely rich city which guards the Straits of Qarth, the waterway connecting the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Sea","link":"Summer Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Summer_Sea" title="Summer Sea">Summer Sea</a> to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jade Sea","link":"Jade Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jade Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Jade_Sea" title="Jade Sea" class="mw-redirect">Jade Sea</a>. Trade ships travel from Westeros to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Asshai","link":"Asshai","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Asshai]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Asshai" title="Asshai">Asshai</a> and back again via Qarth carrying varied cargoes. The proud Qartheen refer to Qarth as "the Queen of Cities".
</p>
<h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> Architecture </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The city's landward side is protected by a vast desert known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Waste","link":"Red Waste","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Waste]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Red_Waste" title="Red Waste">Red Waste</a>, which even the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki","link":"Dothraki","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> fear to cross. As a further protective measure, the architecture of the city incorporates a triple-layered defensive wall, and each layer is elaborately decorated with carvings: the outermost layer is 30 feet high, constructed of red sandstone, and decorated with carvings of various animals. The middle layer is 40 feet high, constructed of grey granite and carved with scenes of soldiers at war. The innermost layer is 50 feet high, constructed of black marble and features intimate scenes of lovemaking. The famed explorer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lomas Longstrider","link":"Lomas Longstrider","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lomas Longstrider]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Lomas_Longstrider" title="Lomas Longstrider">Lomas Longstrider</a> was so impressed with the walls of Qarth that he included them in his book <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Wonders Made by Man","link":"Wonders Made by Man","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wonders Made by Man]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Wonders_Made_by_Man" title="Wonders Made by Man">Wonders Made by Man</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Inside the gates, the wealth of the city is on full display. Qarth's buildings are painted in rich shades of rose, umber and violet and seem to shimmer as if in a dream. Fountains carved in the shape of fantastic beasts can be seen in every open square, and the streets are lined with bronze statues. Places of interest include the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hall of a Thousand Thrones","link":"Hall of a Thousand Thrones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hall of a Thousand Thrones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Hall_of_a_Thousand_Thrones" title="Hall of a Thousand Thrones">Hall of a Thousand Thrones</a>, where the rulers of the city sit on immense wooden chairs inlaid with gilding and gems; an arcade where great statues- three times larger than life size- of past Qartheen heroes are displayed atop marble columns; and a vast aviary where many brightly colored birds flutter beneath the latticed ceiling. 
</p>
<h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> People &amp; Culture </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The Qartheen pride themselves on their sophistication and consider weeping in times of strong emotion to be a mark of civilization. The Qartheen fashion for women leaves one breast exposed, while men favor beaded silk skirts. They seat themselves on cushions on the floor, and wealthier Qartheen sometimes decorate their noses with jewels. Daenerys Targaryen adopts Qartheen dress while she is the guest of Xaro Xhoan Daxos. Dothraki refer to the Qartheen people, who are known for their pale skin, as "Milk Men"
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The city is ruled by a council known as the Pureborn, though in practice the council has to balance its interests against those of three powerful guilds; the Thirteen, the Ancient Guild of Spicers, and the Tourmaline Brotherhood. The rulers of the city are advised by the highly enigmatic warlocks of Qarth, who dwell in the forbidding House of the Undying. Xaro is a member of the Thirteen in the books, but they are only one of three powerful merchant guilds who compete with the Pureborn for control of Qarth. This makes Xaro slightly less politically powerful in the books than in the TV series, in which Qarth is simply ruled by a council of merchant-princes known as "the Thirteen". Nonetheless Xaro is still a member of the Qartheen political leadership in the books, making him a very powerful as well as very wealthy man.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Aside from its famous walls, Qarth is protected from within by a group of soldiers known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Civic Guard","link":"Civic Guard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Civic Guard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Civic_Guard" title="Civic Guard">Civic Guard</a>. This organization consists of both foot soldiers and mounted troops on camels (i.e. "camelry"). In a further attempt to display the city's wealth, the helmets of Qartheen camel riders are adorned with black silk plumes, the camels' blankets dyed in many rich colors, and their saddles inlaid with gems. Qarth also boasts a powerful navy that protects and controls the vital shipping straits.    
</p>
<h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> Port of Qarth </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Qarth's port is one of the greatest in the world. Shaped like an enormous horseshoe, the harbor extends for several miles and is jam-packed with activity. Numerous businesses- including brothels. gaming dens, warehouses and winesinks- line the streets, along with temples devoted to various gods. The whole area functions as a massive marketplace, where goods can be purchased for a fraction of what they would cost a bazaar in the city itself (as long as the buyer asks no questions about the item's provenance). The various trade guilds of Qarth each have designated areas of the harbor where they can moor their ships and warehouse their goods. The far end of the harbor is where ships from Westeros, the Summer Islands and the Free Cities are permitted to land. In <i>A Clash of Kings</i>, the plethora of gifts offered to Daenerys Targaryen gives testament to the scale and reach of Qartheen trading connections:

<h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> History &amp; Geography </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><i>The Lands of Ice and Fire</i> map book (which features a significantly different outline of eastern Essos to the TV show) establishes that the Jade Gates (another name for the Straits of Qarth) separate Essos from a large island called Great Moraq, with Qarth located at the mid-point of the straits. The naming conventions of the surrounding locations - such as the island of Qal to the south and the town of Qarkash to the west - suggest that Qarth has a sphere of influence which extends to those settlements. These maps show that Qarth is linked to the city of Kayakayanaya and the region of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Yi Ti","link":"Yi Ti","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Yi Ti]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Yi_Ti" title="Yi Ti">Yi Ti</a> by coastal roads.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><i>The World of Ice and Fire</i> sourcebook reveals that Qarth is the sole surviving city established by the Qaathi people, who first rose in the southeastern grasslands of central Essos, known today as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki Sea","link":"Dothraki Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Dothraki_Sea" title="Dothraki Sea">Dothraki Sea</a>. A truly ancient people, the Qaathi predate the rise of Valyria - and possibly extend as far back as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dawn Age","link":"Dawn Age","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dawn Age]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Dawn_Age" title="Dawn Age">Dawn Age</a>. Along with other contemporary peoples, the Qaathi often came into conflict with the rising power of the Sarnori civilization of the western grasslands, losing more often than they won. Pushed further and further south, the Qaathi began founding new city-states in the lands now known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Waste","link":"Red Waste","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Waste]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Red_Waste" title="Red Waste">Red Waste</a>. Unfortunately, as the centuries passed, the land became drier and drier, and the Qaathi were well on their way to collapse when the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom of Valyria","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-186405582656cca84aefb60" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom of Valyria</a> struck. Taking advantage of the chaos, the Dothraki pushed into the Red Waste and destroyed nearly all of the remaining Qaathi cities. Qarth was able to survive because it was located so deep within the expanding desert that the Dothraki did not venture so far into such a harsh landscape.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Ironically, all this chaos led to the resurgence of Qarth. Forced to look to the sea, the Pureborn swiftly constructed a fleet and took control of the Jade Gates, the strait between Qarth and the island of Great Moraq. With the Valyrian fleet destroyed and Volantis focused on conquering western Essos, no one opposed the Qartheen as they established their control over this key direct route between west and east, thus gaining immense wealth levying tolls on passing trade.
</p>
