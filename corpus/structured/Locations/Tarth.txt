<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Tarth</b> is an island in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> off the southeastern coast of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>, separated from the mainland by the Straits of Tarth and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shipbreaker Bay","link":"Shipbreaker Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shipbreaker Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Shipbreaker_Bay" title="Shipbreaker Bay">Shipbreaker Bay</a>. It is part of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stormlands","link":"the Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a> and is held by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tarth","link":"House Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/House_Tarth" title="House Tarth">House Tarth</a>, vassals of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Baratheon","link":"House Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/House_Baratheon" title="House Baratheon">House Baratheon</a>, from their seat on <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Evenfall Hall","link":"Evenfall Hall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Evenfall Hall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Evenfall_Hall" title="Evenfall Hall">Evenfall Hall</a>. Being situated in the Narrow Sea, Tarth is located fairly close to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> of the eastern continent, particularly <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrosh","link":"Tyrosh","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrosh]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Tyrosh" title="Tyrosh">Tyrosh</a>. It is also known as <b>the Sapphire Isle</b> due to the blue of the surrounding waters. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The castle of Tarth, located on the west coast of the island, is called Evenfall Hall. It is the home of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brienne of Tarth","link":"Brienne of Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brienne of Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Brienne_of_Tarth" title="Brienne of Tarth">Brienne of Tarth</a>, sometimes called the Maid of Tarth. The island is ruled by Brienne's father, Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Selwyn Tarth","link":"Selwyn Tarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Selwyn Tarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Selwyn_Tarth" title="Selwyn Tarth">Selwyn Tarth</a>.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1"> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 3","link":"Season 3","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 3]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Season_3" title="Season 3">Season 3</a> </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Locke","link":"Locke","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Locke]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Locke" title="Locke">Locke</a> was about to let his men rape Brienne, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> told him who she was and that Tarth was called the "Sapphire Isle" because every sapphire in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> was mined on it, to point out the wealth of her father. Locke relents and orders his men not to harm Brienne, as it would be more worthwhile to ransom her unharmed.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Walk of Punishment]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Brienne later pointed out that the "Sapphire Isle" is actually so-called because of the blue of its waters: there are no actual sapphire mines on the island.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[And Now His Watch is Ended]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 5","link":"Season 5","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">Season 5</a> </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">While sailing south from King's Landing to Dorne, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> spots an island on the horizon and asks the ship's captain if it is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Estermont","link":"Estermont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Estermont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Estermont" title="Estermont">Estermont</a>; he is corrected that it is Tarth.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Sons of the Harpy (episode)|Sons of the Harpy]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Tarth is said to be a beautiful island. It is called the Sapphire Isle for the clear blue waters surrounding the island (but there are no real sapphires in it). The island contains mountains, waterfalls, high meadows, and shadowed vales.  Tarth is also apparently rich in marble - marble used in the construction of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Eyrie","link":"The Eyrie","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Eyrie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/The_Eyrie" title="The Eyrie">The Eyrie</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Vale","link":"The Vale of Arryn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Vale of Arryn|the Vale]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale</a> had to be shipped from Tarth.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Being located in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a>, Tarth is closer to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> than some other parts of Westeros, though it is unfortunately within range of the pirate dens in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a> to the south.  During the long and otherwise peaceful reign of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaehaerys I Targaryen","link":"Jaehaerys I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaehaerys I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen" title="Jaehaerys I Targaryen">Jaehaerys I Targaryen</a>, pirate-lords from the Stepstones actually invaded and seized the eastern side of Tarth and had to be driven off.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andal Invasion","link":"Andal Invasion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andal Invasion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-40398406156cca877b13ca" href="/wiki/Andal_Invasion" title="Andal Invasion">Andal Invasion</a>, Tarth was one of the first locations that the Andals captured in the Stormlands.  House Tarth itself was an Andal family - though they intermarried heavily with the local First Men population.  Their bloodline and the local title of the ruler of the island, "the Evenstar", are said to extend back to the dawn of days.
</p>
