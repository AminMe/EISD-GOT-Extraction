<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-162611917556cca883188bf" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Water Gardens are located on a beach next to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Sea","link":"Summer Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-162611917556cca883188bf" href="/wiki/Summer_Sea" title="Summer Sea">Summer Sea</a>, three leagues to the west of Sunspear on a coastal road (about nine miles). They were raised by Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maron Martell","link":"Maron Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maron Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-162611917556cca883188bf" href="/wiki/Maron_Martell" title="Maron Martell">Maron Martell</a> as a gift for his new bride, Princess <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys Targaryen","link":"Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)|Daenerys Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-162611917556cca883188bf" href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen_(daughter_of_Aegon_IV)" title="Daenerys Targaryen (daughter of Aegon IV)">Daenerys Targaryen</a> to mark the union of Dorne to the rest of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-162611917556cca883188bf" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>. Pale pink marble paves the gardens and courtyard. Terraces overlooking the numerous pools and fountains of the Water Gardens, shaded by blood orange trees, can be reached via a fluted pillar gallery leading to a triple archway.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Water Gardens are pleasant in autumn: hot days, cool nights, the salt breeze blowing in from the sea, and fountains and pools to admire and play in. It is one of the few places, if not the only one, in the Seven Kingdoms where <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"highborn","link":"Noble house","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Noble house|highborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-162611917556cca883188bf" href="/wiki/Noble_house" title="Noble house">highborn</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"lowborn","link":"smallfolk","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[smallfolk|lowborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-162611917556cca883188bf" href="/wiki/Smallfolk" title="Smallfolk">lowborn</a> children can play as equals. One day when the sun was hot, Princess Daenerys took pity on the children of the servants and guards and allowed them to play in the pools with the highborn children, starting a tradition that is still kept in Dorne to this day.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, Prince <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doran Martell","link":"Doran Martell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doran Martell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-162611917556cca883188bf" href="/wiki/Doran_Martell" title="Doran Martell">Doran Martell</a> is known to have taken residency at the Water Gardens for some time, and is known to enjoy taking his mind off his troubles by watching the children playing in the pools.
</p>
