</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The <b>Great Sept of Baelor</b>, also called the <b>Great Sept</b> or just the <b>Sept of Baelor</b>, is a massive <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sept","link":"sept","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[sept]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Sept" title="Sept">sept</a>, the center of religious worship for the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a> and the seat of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Septon","link":"High Septon (title)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Septon (title)|High Septon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/High_Septon_(title)" title="High Septon (title)" class="mw-redirect">High Septon</a> of the Faith. It is located in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, the capital of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>, and is the largest single building in the city, though the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a> complex sprawls over a larger area. Great religious ceremonies are held there. It is also the sept personally used by the royal family, thus royal weddings are held at the Great Sept in massive ceremonies.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The sept is named after King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baelor Targaryen","link":"Baelor Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Baelor Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Baelor_Targaryen" title="Baelor Targaryen">Baelor Targaryen</a>, a king noted for his religious piety and pacifism, who ordered its construction. A statue of King Baelor is located in the square in front of the sept.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the episode "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Baelor","link":"Baelor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Baelor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Baelor" title="Baelor">Baelor</a>", the scene in front of the Great Sept of Baelor was shot at <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Fort Manoel","link":"wikipedia:Fort Manoel","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:Fort Manoel|Fort Manoel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_Manoel" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Fort Manoel">Fort Manoel</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"G\u017cira, Malta","link":"wikipedia:G\u017cira, Malta","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:G\u017cira, Malta|G\u017cira, Malta]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gżira,_Malta" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Gżira, Malta">Gżira, Malta</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>Roberts, Josh (March 26, 2012). [http:\/\/www.smartertravel.com\/blogs\/today-in-travel\/game-of-thrones-in-the-real-world-see-where-the-hbo-hit-was-filmed.html?id=10712211 'Game of Thrones' in the Real World: See Where the HBO Hit Was Filmed] SmarterTravel.com.<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The large hole in the dome of the roof of the main sanctum is known as an <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Oculus","link":"Wikipedia:Oculus","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Oculus|Oculus]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oculus" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Oculus">Oculus</a>, like the one in the roof of the Pantheon in Rome. Oculi by definition are open to the sky, so rain or snow will enter the building through them. Usually, buildings that utilize oculi are so tall that light rain and snow will evaporate long before they hit the floor although a drain should be used in the event of heavy rain.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The demo video by visual effects company <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rodeo FX","link":"Rodeo FX","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rodeo FX]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Rodeo_FX" title="Rodeo FX">Rodeo FX</a> revealed that while most of the Great Sept's interior is a fully realized set, the giant statues of the Seven are digital creations added in during post-production. The pedestals for each of the statues are real, and taller than the actors, while the statues on top of them (which are usually out of frame) are CGi creations.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">Early concept art for the Great Sept looks very different from the final version. Instead of a large but more or less traditional-looking medieval cathedral, it consists of seven huge spokes leaning in against each other, somewhat like the real-life <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Cathedral of Bras\u00edlia","link":"Wikipedia:Cathedral of Bras\u00edlia","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Cathedral of Bras\u00edlia|Cathedral of Bras\u00edlia]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathedral_of_Brasília" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Cathedral of Brasília">Cathedral of Brasília</a> in Brasil. Another, different design was also used in the "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Complete Guide to Westeros","link":"Complete Guide to Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Complete Guide to Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Complete_Guide_to_Westeros" title="Complete Guide to Westeros" class="mw-redirect">Complete Guide to Westeros</a>" animated featurettes from the Season 1 Blu-ray set. Within the TV series itself, the odd spoke-like design can be briefly glimpsed in a few establishing wide-shots of the entire city (specifically, the opening of the first King's Landing scene in the first episode, "Winter is Coming") - but in these few cases it was always in the background, small and out of focus.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While the Great Sept of Baelor was mentioned since Season 1, its inner and outer designs did not <i>prominently</i> appear until Season 3, when the entire structure was given a full introduction in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"And Now His Watch is Ended","link":"And Now His Watch is Ended","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[And Now His Watch is Ended]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/And_Now_His_Watch_is_Ended" title="And Now His Watch is Ended">And Now His Watch is Ended</a>". Even when Ned Stark is executed on the steps of the Great Sept in Season 1, the scene is filmed in such a way that there is no establishing wide-shot of the entire building. The design was apparently still in flux until early Season 3.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, the Great Sept of Baelor is located atop Visenya's Hill in King's Landing. It consists of the traditional seven-sided structure, albeit on a huge scale, with seven slender towers topped by crystals rising from it. There is a grand square in front of the Sept, from where the High Septon can address the masses.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Prior to the invasion of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon the Conqueror","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Aegon_the_Conqueror" title="Aegon the Conqueror" class="mw-redirect">Aegon the Conqueror</a>, the center of the Faith in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a> was the Starry Sept of Oldtown. When Aegon captured Oldtown after his great victory on the Field of Fire, the High Septon advised Lord Hightower to surrender the city and swear fealty. The High Septon had received a vision from the Seven advising him to bless Aegon's cause. Aegon, his family and his followers converted to the Faith, burning the idols of the old gods of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyria","link":"Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Valyria" title="Valyria" class="mw-redirect">Valyria</a>, but refused to give up the ancient Valyrian practices of polygamy and incest. When Aegon died and the throne passed to his son, born of incest, the Faith launched a bloody uprising against him which was eventually crushed.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Relations between <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">House Targaryen</a> and the Faith were repaired when King Baelor I Targaryen ascended the throne. Septon and king, Baelor was holy and devout, and ordered the building of a huge sept in King's Landing for the glory of the Seven. The building, completed after Baelor's death, was named in his honor.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the tradition of the Seven and the laws of the city, blood is not allowed to be spilled on the site of the Great Sept. Joffrey's execution of Eddard on the steps breaks this law and custom, to the distress of the High Septon. Tensions between the Crown and the Faith remain high long after the execution, mainly due to the outrage of the faithful who believed Stark's execution profaned the sept.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-164085962156cca86f7b7e3" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Baelor" is pronounced "BAY-lor".
</p>
