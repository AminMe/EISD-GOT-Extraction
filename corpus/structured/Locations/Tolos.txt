<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32565736956cca8cfb7d47" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Tolos is connected by a Valyrian road to Mantarys and Bhorash. Tolos is the last inhabited city before <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Meereen","link":"Meereen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Meereen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32565736956cca8cfb7d47" href="/wiki/Meereen" title="Meereen">Meereen</a> several hundred miles to the east (Bhorash being a ruin). Travel overland between Tolos and Meereen is dangerous, due to the proximity of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dothraki Sea","link":"Dothraki Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dothraki Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32565736956cca8cfb7d47" href="/wiki/Dothraki_Sea" title="Dothraki Sea">Dothraki Sea</a> to the northeast and also the harsh nature of the landscape. The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Black Cliffs","link":"Black Cliffs","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Black Cliffs]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32565736956cca8cfb7d47" href="/wiki/Black_Cliffs" title="Black Cliffs">Black Cliffs</a> lie between Tolos and Bhorash.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Tolosi are noted as being the finest slingers in the world. They use soft lead balls instead of stones, which do significantly more damage.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Tolos, Elyria, and Mantarys are the only three surviving cities on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Peninsula","link":"Valyrian Peninsula","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Peninsula]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32565736956cca8cfb7d47" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Peninsula" title="Valyrian Peninsula">Valyrian Peninsula</a>, each in the northern sections not too close to Valyria itself when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Doom","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria|the Doom]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-32565736956cca8cfb7d47" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">the Doom</a> struck.  They were colonies ruled directly by governors from the Valyrian Freehold, not "Free Cities" that had purchased contracts for some limited rule over local affairs.  After the Doom, because they were located on the east coast, Tolos and Elyria developed developed closer economic and social ties with the Ghiscari cities of Slaver's Bay to the east.  They then came into something of an odd position, as while more akin to the Valyrians and their colonies in the Free Cities, Tolos and Elyria fell into the cultural orbit of the main Ghiscari cities on the other side of Slaver's Bay, and came to be thought of as more of an extension of that region - though not quite fully belonging to either Slaver's Bay or the Free Cities.  Mantarys, however, is located far from the coast, up in the foothills of the Painted Mountains, and as a result it became increasingly isolated.
</p>
