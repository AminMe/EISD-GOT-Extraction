<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149967843756cca91e9b567" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, High Heart was sacred to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Children of the Forest","link":"Children of the Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Children of the Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149967843756cca91e9b567" href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Forest" title="Children of the Forest">Children of the Forest</a>. Around its crown stands a ring of thirty-one <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"weirwood","link":"weirwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[weirwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149967843756cca91e9b567" href="/wiki/Weirwood" title="Weirwood">weirwood</a> stumps. The hill is considered a very safe place due to its relative height compared to the very flat surrounding land that it makes it nearly impossible to be approached unseen. The nearby smallfolk avoid the area as it is said to be haunted by the slaughtered Children who had been killed by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andal","link":"Andals","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andals|Andal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-149967843756cca91e9b567" href="/wiki/Andals" title="Andals">Andal</a> King Erreg the Kinslayer.
</p>
