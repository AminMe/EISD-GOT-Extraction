<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Skagos is nominally part of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the North","link":"the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">the North</a> and is ruled by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Stark","link":"House Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>. However, in practice Skagos is located so far away from the centers of civilization that its inhabitants are virtually ignored and allowed to pursue their own customs and practices. According to myth, unicorns live on Skagos. There is a small kernel of truth to this, but the "Skagosi Unicorn" is essentially just an enormous one-horned species of goat. The island is mountainous, with treacherous currents surrounding it.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The people of Skagos are descendants of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a>, who refer to themselves as Skagosi ("stoneborn" in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Old Tongue","link":"Old Tongue","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Old Tongue]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/Old_Tongue" title="Old Tongue">Old Tongue</a>), although outsiders refer to them as "Skagossons" or, derisevely, "Skaggs". Many centuries ago they sailed to Skane and depopulated the island, killing all of the men and taking the women back to Skagos as captives. Rumors by mainlanders say that the Skagosi are savage cannibals, who ate the people of Skane. They later fell under the authority of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King in the North","link":"King in the North","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King in the North]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/King_in_the_North" title="King in the North">King in the North</a>, and then the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>. One century ago, the Skagosi rebelled and were defeated in a brief, bloody war that cost the life of the then-Lord of Winterfell and hundreds of his men. Nevertheless some Skagosi do business with the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-168373454756cca887cfecd" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> . They are also said to still enforce the tradition of "First Night".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Skagos is also home to three noble houses:
