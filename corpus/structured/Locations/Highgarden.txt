<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"<i>A Song of Ice and Fire<\/i>","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire|''A Song of Ice and Fire'']]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-96930687156cca859acbdf" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire"><i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i></a> novels, Highgarden is among the most beautiful castles in the Seven Kingdoms. Situated on a broad, symmetrically shaped hill, the castle includes three concentric tiers of white stone walls, which increase in height and thickness as one travels upwards. Between the outermost and middle walls is Highgarden's famous "briar maze", a labyrinth of beautifully maintained hedges that serves two important purposes; entertainment for the castle's inhabitants and deterrence against enemies (any invaders who manage to penetrate the outer wall would have no easy task finding their way through the elaborate growth of thorns and greenery).
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The inner rings of Highgarden are a beautiful complex of towers, courtyards, colonnades and statuary, and greenery is just as prominent inside the walls as in the fields outside; ivy, grapevines and climbing roses can be seen snaking around the buildings, and gardens and arbors are filled with flowers. Water features are also common inside the walls, with pools, fountains and even man-made waterfalls visible throughout the complex.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Highgarden is also notable for its places of worship. The castle sept is lined with rows of stained-glass windows depicting <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"external","text":"the Seven","link":"http:\/\/gameofthrones.wikia.com\/wiki\/The_Seven","linktype":"text","wasblank":false,"wikitext":"[http:\/\/gameofthrones.wikia.com\/wiki\/The_Seven the Seven]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-96930687156cca859acbdf"  class="text" href="http://gameofthrones.wikia.com/wiki/The_Seven">the Seven</a> as well as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Garth Greenhand","link":"Garth Greenhand","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Garth Greenhand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-96930687156cca859acbdf" href="/wiki/Garth_Greenhand" title="Garth Greenhand" class="mw-redirect">Garth Greenhand</a>, the legendary first king of the Reach. Highgarden's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"godswood","link":"godswood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[godswood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-96930687156cca859acbdf" href="/wiki/Godswood" title="Godswood">godswood</a>, instead of the typical single heart tree, contains three tall <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"weirwood","link":"weirwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[weirwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-96930687156cca859acbdf" href="/wiki/Weirwood" title="Weirwood">weirwood</a> trees known as the Three Singers. Said to have been planted by Garth himself, the branches of the trees have become so enmeshed over the centuries that they resemble a single tree with three trunks.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">A fine selection of horseflesh can be found in Highgarden's stables, and pleasure barges offer river cruises on the Mander. Outside the walls, meadows and fields of flowers can be seen in every direction, along with fruit orchards rich in peaches, melons and fireplums.
</p>
