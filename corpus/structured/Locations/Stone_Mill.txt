<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-194546610956cca8f48d65a" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Stone Mill is located several miles to the south of Riverrun on the western banks of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Fork","link":"Red Fork","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Fork]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-194546610956cca8f48d65a" href="/wiki/Red_Fork" title="Red Fork" class="mw-redirect">Red Fork</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Trident","link":"Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-194546610956cca8f48d65a" href="/wiki/Trident" title="Trident" class="mw-redirect">Trident</a>. Tywin Lannister attempts to cross the Red Fork over multiple fords to the north and south of Riverrun, but his main push is at the Stone Mill, where his vanguard under Gregor Clegane attacks the defenders commanded by Edmure Tully. Tully successfully defeats the push and is able to prevent Tywin's crossing. However, the battle has greater significance in the novels, as it delays Tywin enough to hear about <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-194546610956cca8f48d65a" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>'s push on <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-194546610956cca8f48d65a" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, allowing him to turn his army around and march to the city's relief during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Blackwater","link":"Battle of the Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-194546610956cca8f48d65a" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a>. Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Leo Lefford","link":"Leo Lefford","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Leo Lefford]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-194546610956cca8f48d65a" href="/wiki/Leo_Lefford" title="Leo Lefford">Leo Lefford</a> is killed during the battle in the books. Edmure is likewise berated by Robb for his actions, which are considerably more serious in the books.
</p>
