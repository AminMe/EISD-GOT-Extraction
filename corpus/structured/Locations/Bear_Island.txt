<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">During the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Age of Heroes","link":"Age of Heroes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Age of Heroes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Age_of_Heroes" title="Age of Heroes">Age of Heroes</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rodrik Stark","link":"Rodrik Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rodrik Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Rodrik_Stark" title="Rodrik Stark">Rodrik Stark</a> allegedly won control of the island in a wrestling match with an ironborn. Rodrik gave Bear Island to the Mormonts who have been loyal supporters of the Starks ever since.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The North (Histories & Lore)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Lord Commander <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jeor Mormont","link":"Jeor Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jeor Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> was born on Bear Island, as was his son, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont|Jorah]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah</a>. Jorah and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"his wife","link":"Lynesse Hightower","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lynesse Hightower|his wife]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Lynesse_Hightower" title="Lynesse Hightower">his wife</a> were forced from his land and fled into exile after he sold poachers he had caught on Bear Island. Following Jorah's disgrace, Jeor's younger sister <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maege Mormont","link":"Maege Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maege Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Maege_Mormont" title="Maege Mormont">Maege Mormont</a> became <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lady of Bear Island","link":"Lady of Bear Island","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lady of Bear Island]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Lady_of_Bear_Island" title="Lady of Bear Island" class="mw-redirect">Lady of Bear Island</a>.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">According to legend, in ancient times, Bear Island was ruled by House Woodfoot, who were wiped out when the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> conquered the island. Bear Island is home mostly to woodsmen, crofters and fisherfolk, as well as the bears that give the island its name. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">There is a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"warrior woman tradition","link":"Warrior women","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Warrior women|warrior woman tradition]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Warrior_women" title="Warrior women">warrior woman tradition</a> on Bear Island, because with all of the husbands and fathers away for long periods of time on fishing boats or working in the fields, the local women of the island had to be prepared to defend their homes and children from raids at a moment's notice (raids from both the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"ironborn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> further south, and from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wildlings","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk|wildlings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildlings</a> who avoided the Wall entirely by using boats to cross the bay from the Frozen Shore on the other side).  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Mormonts' seat is a huge wooden hall, defended by an earthen <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"palisade","link":"Wikipedia:Palisade","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Palisade|palisade]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palisade" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Palisade">palisade</a>. According to the semi-canonical <i>A Song of Ice and Fire Roleplaying Campaign Guide: A Game of Thrones Edition</i> from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Green Ronin Publishing","link":"wikipedia:Green Ronin Publishing","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[wikipedia:Green Ronin Publishing|Green Ronin Publishing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-214278206156cca86d17366" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Ronin_Publishing" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Green Ronin Publishing">Green Ronin Publishing</a> it is named Mormont Keep.
</p>
