It is the headquarters of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Warlocks of Qarth","link":"Warlocks of Qarth","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Warlocks of Qarth]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Warlocks_of_Qarth" title="Warlocks of Qarth" class="mw-redirect">Warlocks of Qarth</a>. It is a large tower without any visible openings and surrounded by trees which are used to make <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shade of the evening","link":"Shade of the evening","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shade of the evening]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Shade_of_the_evening" title="Shade of the evening">Shade of the evening</a>.

<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Daenerys","link":"Daenerys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Daenerys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Daenerys" title="Daenerys" class="mw-redirect">Daenerys</a>, Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jorah Mormont","link":"Jorah Mormont","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jorah Mormont]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kovarro","link":"Kovarro","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kovarro]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Kovarro" title="Kovarro">Kovarro</a> reach the House of the Undying. While trying to figure out a way inside, Jorah loses sight of Dany. She enters inside and with a flamed torch begins her search for her <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"Dragon","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragon|dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon" class="mw-redirect">dragons</a>. She finds herself in an illusion of the charred remains <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Hall","link":"Great Hall (Red Keep)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Hall (Red Keep)|Great Hall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)" title="Great Hall (Red Keep)" class="mw-redirect">Great Hall</a> of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a>, covered in snow; then she finds herself in another illusion in which she emerges from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> in a snowy storm and then enters a tent where she meets with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogo","link":"Drogo","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drogo]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Drogo" title="Drogo">Drogo</a> and their son <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaego","link":"Rhaego","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaego]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Rhaego" title="Rhaego">Rhaego</a>. Aware of the illusion, she leaves and finds herself in a room with her chained dragons, and soon <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Pyat Pree","link":"Pyat Pree","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Pyat Pree]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Pyat_Pree" title="Pyat Pree">Pyat Pree</a> chains Daenerys up as well. The warlock states that he intends to keep her prisoner forever. However, as the dragons are not muzzled, they are able to burn Pree to death at Daenerys' command.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Valar Morghulis]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the House of the Undying is the abode of the Undying Ones, the leaders of the Qartheen warlocks. It is described as a grey, ancient ruin, is long and low, with no towers or windows. It is coiled like a stone serpent through a grove of black-barked trees with inky blue leaves which are used to make Shade of the evening.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Daenerys saw no vision of Drogo and their son. She saw many visions, some of them of the past, others are presumed to be foreshadowing of future events:
</p>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>A beautiful, naked woman being ravished by four of the dwarves who serve the house.
</li><li>A little girl (presumably Daenerys herself) runs barefoot toward a big house with a red door - Daenerys' childhood home in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Braavos","link":"Braavos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Braavos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Braavos" title="Braavos">Braavos</a>.
</li><li>A throne room with dragon skulls on the walls, where an old man in rich robes with dark eyes and long silver-grey hair (presumably her father, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Mad King","link":"Aerys II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aerys II Targaryen|the Mad King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">the Mad King</a>) sits on a barbed throne and appears to give the order to burn the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Keep","link":"Red Keep","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Keep]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a> during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sack of King's Landing","link":"Sack of King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sack of King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Sack_of_King's_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">Sack of King's Landing</a>.

</li><li>A splendor of wizards who claim to be the Undying of Qarth and offer to teach Daenerys the secret speech of dragonkind. These are just a glamour of the Undying to hide their true visages.
</li><li><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Viserys","link":"Viserys Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Viserys Targaryen|Viserys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys</a> screams as the molten gold runs down his cheeks and fills his mouth.
</li><li>A tall lord with copper skin and silver-gold hair stands beneath the banner of a fiery stallion, a burning city behind him. This may be a glimpse at what <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaego","link":"Rhaego","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaego]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Rhaego" title="Rhaego">Rhaego</a>'s future would have been, had he lived.
</li><li>Rubies fly like drops of blood from the chest of a dying prince (presumably <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegar Targaryen","link":"Rhaegar Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar Targaryen</a>), and he sinks to his knees in the water and with his last breath murmuring a woman's name.
</li><li>A feast of slaughtered corpses holding cups, spoons, and food, with a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dead man","link":"Robb Stark","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robb Stark|dead man]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">dead man</a> with a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"wolf's","link":"Direwolf","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Direwolf|wolf's]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Direwolf" title="Direwolf" class="mw-redirect">wolf's</a> head sitting on a throne wearing an iron crown. Daenerys and readers were led to assume that this vision was symbolic, but it turned out to be <i>literally</i> true and foreshadowing the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>: with the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Northern","link":"The North","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The North|Northern]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">Northern</a> leadership slaughtered by their own hosts at a wedding feast, and an actual <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"direwolf's","link":"Grey Wind","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Grey Wind|direwolf's]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Grey_Wind" title="Grey Wind">direwolf's</a> head sewn onto Robb Stark's corpse as a final desecration.
</li><li>A red sword glowing like sunset, raised in the hand of a blue-eyed king who casts no shadow (perhaps <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>).
</li><li>A cloth dragon sways on poles amidst a cheering crowd.
</li><li>From a smoking tower, a great stone beast takes wing, breathing shadow fire.
</li><li>Daenerys' <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"silver mare","link":"The silver","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The silver|silver mare]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/The_silver" title="The silver">silver mare</a> trotting through the grass, to a darkling stream beneath a sea of stars.
</li><li>A corpse stands at the prow of a ship, eyes bright in his dead face, grey lips smiling sadly.
</li><li>A blue flower grows from a chink in a wall of ice, and fills the air with sweetness.
</li><li><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mirri Maz Duur","link":"Mirri Maz Duur","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mirri Maz Duur]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Mirri_Maz_Duur" title="Mirri Maz Duur">Mirri Maz Duur</a> shrieks in the flames, a dragon bursting from her brow.
</li><li>A bloody corpse of a <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"naked man","link":"Wineseller","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wineseller|naked man]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Wineseller" title="Wineseller">naked man</a>, bouncing and being dragged behind a silver horse.
</li><li>A white lion runs through <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"grass taller than a man","link":"Ghost grass","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghost grass|grass taller than a man]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Ghost_grass" title="Ghost grass">grass taller than a man</a>.
</li><li>Beneath the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mother of Mountains","link":"Mother of Mountains","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mother of Mountains]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Mother_of_Mountains" title="Mother of Mountains">Mother of Mountains</a>, a line of naked <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"crones","link":"Dosh khaleen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dosh khaleen|crones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Dosh_khaleen" title="Dosh khaleen">crones</a> creep from a great lake and kneel shivering before her, their grey heads bowed.
</li><li>Ten thousand <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"slaves","link":"Slavery","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slavery|slaves]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> lift bloodstained hands as Daenerys races by on her silver, crying "Mother, mother!", reaching for her, touching her. <p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Undying told Daenerys obscure prophecies:
</p>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>Three fires must you light, one for life and one for death and one to love.
</li><li>Three mounts must you ride, one to bed and one to dread and one to love.
</li><li>Three treasons will you know, once for blood and once for gold and once for love.
</li></ul>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the visions, the Undying Ones suddenly attacked Daenerys. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Drogon","link":"Drogon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Drogon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-177264094856cca86331129" href="/wiki/Drogon" title="Drogon">Drogon</a> saved her by setting the room on fire and attacking the Undying, ripping their rotten flesh apart. Daenerys and Drogon escaped from the burning house. Pyat Pree was angry at Daenerys for burning the palace. He drew a knife and charged at her, but was stopped by Drogon, Jhogo and Rakharo.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Although Daenerys defeated the Undying Ones, soon she learned that as a result of burning the palace, the whole city has grown hostile to her overnight. Pyat Pree gathered the surviving warlocks together to work ill on her. Thus she had to leave Qarth.
</p>
