<h2>History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><img data-rte-meta="{"type":"image","wikitext":"[[File:HarrenhalPreConquest.jpg|thumb|right|Harrenhal before its destruction.]]","title":"HarrenhalPreConquest.jpg","params":{"thumbnail":true,"alt":"HarrenhalPreConquest","align":"right","caption":"Harrenhal before its destruction.","captionParsed":"Harrenhal before its destruction."}}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" alt="" src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/7/70/HarrenhalPreConquest.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20140108064644" width="180" height="138" class="image thumb alignRight withCaption" data-image-name="HarrenhalPreConquest.jpg" data-image-key="HarrenhalPreConquest.jpg"  type="image" />
<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harren Hoare","link":"Harren Hoare","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Harren Hoare]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Harren_Hoare" title="Harren Hoare">Harren Hoare</a>'s grandfather led the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"ironborn","link":"Ironborn","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ironborn|ironborn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> to conquer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Riverlands","link":"the Riverlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Riverlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a>, previously occupied by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stormlands","link":"the Stormlands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Stormlands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a>. The ironborn cared nothing for the inhabitants of the mainland, and their rule was brutal and tyrannical - far more than any previous occupier. To consolidate their hold on the Riverlands, Harren's grandfather commissioned construction of a great fortress on the northern shore of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gods Eye","link":"Gods Eye","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gods Eye]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Gods_Eye" title="Gods Eye">Gods Eye</a> lake, in the very heart of the region. The largest and greatest fortress ever built in Westeros, it took three generations to finish. The ironborn broke the backs of the Riverlanders in forced labor to complete an instrument for their own domination. The great castle was finally finished in King Harren's time, and he arrogantly named it "Harrenhal" after himself. On the very same day that the last brick was laid, however, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon Targaryen","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen|Aegon Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon Targaryen</a> and his sisters landed with their army from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonstone","link":"Dragonstone","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Dragonstone" title="Dragonstone" class="mw-redirect">Dragonstone</a> at the mouth of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackwater","link":"Blackwater Rush","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackwater Rush|Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Blackwater_Rush" title="Blackwater Rush">Blackwater</a> River, along with their three <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragons","link":"dragons","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragons]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a>, beginning the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">War of Conquest</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Harren thought the walls of his massive castle could withstand any assault, but he did not realize that dragons could simply fly over them. In the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Burning of Harrenhal","link":"Burning of Harrenhal","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Burning of Harrenhal]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Burning_of_Harrenhal" title="Burning of Harrenhal">Burning of Harrenhal</a>, Aegon used his dragon <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Balerion","link":"Balerion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Balerion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Balerion" title="Balerion">Balerion</a> to roast King Harren and all of his sons alive within their own tower. Harrenhal was blasted with dragonfire and left half-ruined.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Afterwards, Aegon Targaryen granted Harrenhal to his bannermen of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Qoherys","link":"House Qoherys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Qoherys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Qoherys" title="House Qoherys">House Qoherys</a>, which eventually became extinct. They were followed by Houses <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Towers","link":"House Towers","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Towers|Towers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Towers" title="House Towers">Towers</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harroway","link":"House Harroway","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Harroway|Harroway]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Harroway" title="House Harroway">Harroway</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Strong","link":"House Strong","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Strong|Strong]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Strong" title="House Strong">Strong</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lothston","link":"House Lothston","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lothston|Lothston]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Lothston" title="House Lothston">Lothston</a>. All of them, like the Hoares and Qoherys before them, died out, leading to the castle of be considered a cursed place. Several tales are told of Harrenhal, including the tale of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mad Lady Lothston","link":"Danelle Lothston","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Danelle Lothston|Mad Lady Lothston]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Danelle_Lothston" title="Danelle Lothston">Mad Lady Lothston</a>, the flaming ghosts of Harren and his sons, and of servants who go to sleep and are found turned to ashes the following day.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[Complete Guide to Westeros]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Harrenhal is seen as something of a white elephant: while it is technically the biggest castle in Westeros, it is so ridiculously large that the surrounding area cannot possibly produce the necessary food to support the massive army needed to fully man it. Rulers of Harrenhal usually lose money and resources simply by possessing it. As a result, Harrenhal is rarely if ever fully manned. Moreover, it was never fully repaired after being blasted with dragonfire by the Targaryens three hundred years ago, which combined with the fact that the garrison is never as large as the fortifications require, means that Harrenhal really isn't one of the most defensibly formidable castles in Westeros.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[Complete Guide to Westeros]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The surrounding lands subject to Harrenhal are actually some of the richest and most fertile in all of Westeros, being located in the watersheds of both the Trident River and Gods Eye lake. In peacetime, a noble House can actually become quite wealthy from holding Harrenhal - provided that they hire only a skeleton force to defend it instead of a full garrison. It is a testament to the sheer size of the castle that even the most productive farmlands in Westeros cannot support enough troops to fully garrison it. Nonetheless, the unwary often see Harrenhal as a great reward or prize in war, without pausing to consider that there is actually little net profit in holding it.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[What is Dead May Never Die]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Concept artist Kimberley Pope explained the elements she came up with for Harrenhal's design: "I added the thorns, dead trees, and suggested it would be nice to have the lake as a sulphuric crater, toxic and dead, still suffering from the devastation brought about by the burning of Harrenhal." The walls of Harrenhal's major towers are also supposed to appear so large that to characters inside the complex they seem like mountains in the background.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/www.hbogo.com\/#series\/video&assetID=GOROSTGP35015?videoMode=embeddedVideo?showSpecialFeatures=true\/ \"Garden of Bones\" In-Episode Guide]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Production Designer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gemma Jackson","link":"Gemma Jackson","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gemma Jackson]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Gemma_Jackson" title="Gemma Jackson">Gemma Jackson</a> said that in order to develop the look of Harrenhal, she visited Cambodia and the ruins of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Angkor Wat","link":"Wikipedia:Angkor Wat","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Wikipedia:Angkor Wat|Angkor Wat]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angkor_Wat" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Angkor Wat">Angkor Wat</a> to get a feel for how a vast but crumbling castle complex should appear, with many broken walls overgrown with vines and creepers. Many of the walls were broken when it was burned with dragonfire, and more have since fallen into ruin but not been repaired, because the entire castle is too big to adequately maintain. To emphasize this, the paths through the Harrenhal set were designed to be deliberately confusing, so the camera is never quite sure what is right around the next bend: informal "entrances" to different sections of the castle will suddenly appear as characters pass through large broken gaps in old walls. The Lannister troops have also haphazardly assembled some wooden constructions in their makeshift camp within the older stone walls - and because all of the trees were burned away long ago, they mostly just broke up whatever old rotting furniture or half-burned support beams they could find within the castle itself and hastily hammered them together into new forms. The combined effect gives Harrenhal a confusing, maze-like quality. Jackson also added large broken stones around the bases of many of the walls, to emphasize that they are crumbling into ruin, and their upkeep is so neglected that no one even expends the effort to clear the fallen stones away. <img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"Garden of Bones\" In-Episode Guide<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Harrenhal is a colossal castle consisting of five massive towers (the Tower of Dread, the Widow's Tower, the Wailing Tower, the Tower of Ghosts, and the Kingspyre) and immense walls. It occupies thrice as much land as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Winterfell","link":"Winterfell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Winterfell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>: its <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Godswood","link":"Godswood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Godswood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Godswood" title="Godswood">Godswood</a> spans 20 acres, its stables hold a thousand horses, and its kitchens are as large as Winterfell's great hall. The great hall contains some 30 hearths.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The castle was built over 300 years ago by House Hoare, the ruling house of the Iron Islands which had overrun and conquered the Riverlands. It took three generations to construct. King Harren the Black completed the castle, boasting that it was impregnable. Unfortunately, the day he completed the castle was the same day that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon the Conqueror","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Aegon_the_Conqueror" title="Aegon the Conqueror" class="mw-redirect">Aegon the Conqueror</a> invaded Westeros. His <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"dragon","link":"dragon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[dragon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon" class="mw-redirect">dragons</a> were not obstructed by the towering walls and roasted Harren and all his heirs alive in the tallest tower of the castle, now known as the Kingspyre.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Since Harren's death the castle has passed through a number of other noble houses (<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Qoherys","link":"House Qoherys","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Qoherys|Qoherys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Qoherys" title="House Qoherys">Qoherys</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Towers","link":"House Towers","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Towers|Towers]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Towers" title="House Towers">Towers</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Harroway","link":"House Harroway","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Harroway|Harroway]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Harroway" title="House Harroway">Harroway</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Strong","link":"House Strong","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Strong|Strong]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Strong" title="House Strong">Strong</a>, and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lothston","link":"House Lothston","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Lothston|Lothston]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/House_Lothston" title="House Lothston">Lothston</a>), each of which has come to ruin for one reason or another. Also, every castellan to command the castle's garrison has come to a horrific end, even when they are far away from the castle. This has led to the common superstition in Westeros that Harrenhal is cursed, which is the reason <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a> never set foot there ever since he was granted the castle. Prior to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"War of the Five Kings","link":"War of the Five Kings","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of the Five Kings]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a> House Whent, loyal vassals to Lord <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hoster Tully","link":"Hoster Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hoster Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Hoster_Tully" title="Hoster Tully">Hoster Tully</a> of Riverrun, held the lordship of Harrenhal.

<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">So far, Roose Bolton is the only one who evaded the alleged curse.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Harrenhal is considered extremely ill-reputed place not only due to the alleged curse: according to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaime Lannister","link":"Jaime Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaime Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a>, Harrenhal has seen more horror in its three hundred years than <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Casterly Rock","link":"Casterly Rock","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Casterly Rock]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Casterly_Rock" title="Casterly Rock">Casterly Rock</a> had witnessed in three thousand.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Due to its central location in contested borderlands, since its construction Harrenhal has fallen to hostile siege more times than any other major castle in Westeros (within the same three hundred year time period). Aegon the Conqueror infamously burned and half-ruined the castle not long after it was finished using his dragon Balerion. It was also attacked during the Faith Militant uprisings - or rather, the early localized rebellions against Aegon I's sons which later coalesced into a wide-scale conflict. A rebel leader styling himself Harren the Red, claiming to be Harren the Black's bastard, established himself at Harrenhal but was crushed. Harrenhal was one of the focal points of the major campaigns during the Dance of the Dragons and changed hands multiple times. It was probably attacked during the First Blackfyre Rebellion. Ironically, the one major continent-wide war which Harrenhal was not directly besieged in was <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Robert's Rebellion","link":"Robert's Rebellion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Robert's Rebellion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Robert's_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a> - yet in many ways, the spark of the rebellion began at Harrenhal. It was during a great <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"tournament","link":"Tournaments","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tournaments|tournament]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Tournaments" title="Tournaments" class="mw-redirect">tournament</a> at Harrenhal that <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhaegar&nbsp;Targaryen","link":"Rhaegar\u00a0Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhaegar\u00a0Targaryen|Rhaegar&nbsp;Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar<span data-rte-entity="nbsp">&nbsp;</span>Targaryen</a> controversially crowned <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lyanna Stark","link":"Lyanna Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lyanna Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Lyanna_Stark" title="Lyanna Stark">Lyanna Stark</a> as the Queen of Love and Beauty - and his later abduction of Lyanna set the entire war in motion.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the Dance of the Dragons, Harrenhal gained the dubious distinction of being one of the few castles to have been burned out <i>twice</i> with dragonfire. While it is loosely said that it fell to "dragons" during the War of Conquest, only one dragon was actually present - though Aegon I's mount Balerion the Black Dread, the largest Targaryen dragon, was more than sufficient to bring it to ruin. During the Dance, however, which occurred 130 years later, the faction loyal to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon II","link":"Aegon II Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon II Targaryen|Aegon II]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Aegon_II_Targaryen" title="Aegon II Targaryen">Aegon II</a> captured Harrenhal, aided by his younger brother Aemond One-Eye riding the dragon <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vhagar","link":"Vhagar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Vhagar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Vhagar" title="Vhagar">Vhagar</a>. Later the strategic situation changed and the army had to withdraw, but to deny use of the castle to their enemies, Aemond used Vhagar to burn out every wooden structure in the entire complex. So ultimately multiple "dragons" did burn Harrenhal - just not at the same time.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">By the War of the Five Kings, Harrenhal had fallen into such disrepair that Shella Whent's household had completely abandoned three of the five great towers of the castle, letting them fall into ruin. Even in the two remaining towers, they only occupied and continued to service the bottom fifth of each tower - put another way, only about two fifteenths of the castle's potential living space was actually being used and maintained.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After House Lannister takes Harrenhal, most of its household keep on serving the occupiers. However, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaqen H'ghar","link":"Jaqen H'ghar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaqen H'ghar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Jaqen_H'ghar" title="Jaqen H'ghar">Jaqen H'ghar</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rorge","link":"Rorge","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rorge]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Rorge" title="Rorge">Rorge</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Biter","link":"Biter","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Biter]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Biter" title="Biter">Biter</a> free the Northern prisoners after Lord Tywin and the Mountain leave the castle under the command of Amory Lorch. The Brave Companions seize the chance to turn on Lorch and deliver the castle to Roose Bolton, who was commanding the Northern forces east of the Trident, with whom they had previously made a deal. Later Roose leaves Harrenhal to attend <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Edmure Tully's wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding|Edmure Tully's wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Edmure Tully's wedding</a>, granting the command of the castle to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Vargo Hoat","link":"Locke","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Locke|Vargo Hoat]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Locke" title="Locke">Vargo Hoat</a>. Hoat is killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Mountain","link":"The Mountain","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Mountain]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/The_Mountain" title="The Mountain" class="mw-redirect">The Mountain</a>. When the Mountain is called back to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, he gives the command to Polliver, who is killed by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Hound","link":"The Hound","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Hound]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/The_Hound" title="The Hound" class="mw-redirect">The Hound</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">According to the TV series official <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"pronunciation guide","link":"pronunciation guide","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[pronunciation guide]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-69487134856cca84766cc5" href="/wiki/Pronunciation_guide" title="Pronunciation guide">pronunciation guide</a> developed for the cast and crew, "Harrenhal" is pronounced "HAIR-in-hall".
</p>
