<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, tortured slaves in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Astapor","link":"Astapor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Astapor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-180795922156cca8e42b2d7" href="/wiki/Astapor" title="Astapor">Astapor</a> are put on display in the "Plaza of Punishment", not a "Walk of Punishment" on the battlements. This change arose from complications in the shooting location: the old city section of Essaouira, Morocco, where the scenes in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Slaver's Bay","link":"Slaver's Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slaver's Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-180795922156cca8e42b2d7" href="/wiki/Slaver's_Bay" title="Slaver's Bay">Slaver's Bay</a> are filmed features an esplanade of gunpowder cannons, which could not be physically removed. This posed something of a problem, as Production Designer <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gemma Jackson","link":"Gemma Jackson","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gemma Jackson]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-180795922156cca8e42b2d7" href="/wiki/Gemma_Jackson" title="Gemma Jackson">Gemma Jackson</a> explained:
</p>
<dl data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><dd>"A whole line of cannons...they were absolutely immovable. And we don't have cannons in our world [Westeros and Essos]... We decided to incorporate them into the Walk of Punishment and we built 'cannon covers'. And actually, the repetition of them made them look even better. I don't think anybody would question that they weren't there, all the time."<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/wicnet.tumblr.com\/image\/48010977825 \"From the Set: The Walk of Punishment\"], HBO Behind-the-Scenes extra feature, reposted on WinterIsComing.net<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-180795922156cca8e42b2d7" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</dd></dl>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-180795922156cca8e42b2d7" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels the corresponding location is the Plaza of Punishment. It is located just inside the main city gates rather than along the harbor, but has a similar function, as this is where new slaves are first taken when brought into the city. There they go past rebellious slaves who have been punished or executed. Daenerys purchase of the Unsullied is completed at the Plaza of Punishment because it is larger than the Plaza of Pride.
</p>
