</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><b>Oldtown</b> is the second largest city in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Seven Kingdoms","link":"Seven Kingdoms","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Seven Kingdoms]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>, and by far the oldest major city on the continent, dating back to the time of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a>. </p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The major highway known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Roseroad","link":"Roseroad","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Roseroad]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Roseroad" title="Roseroad">Roseroad</a> begins at Oldtown, then runs northeast past <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Highgarden","link":"Highgarden","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Highgarden]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Highgarden" title="Highgarden">Highgarden</a> before finally reaching <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> on the east coast.  The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Searoad","link":"Searoad","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Searoad]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Searoad" title="Searoad">Searoad</a> also forks off from the Roseroad at Highgarden, going northwest so travelers from Oldtown can reach <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lannisport","link":"Lannisport","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lannisport]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Lannisport" title="Lannisport">Lannisport</a>.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Oldtown is the seat of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Hightower","link":"House Hightower","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Hightower]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/House_Hightower" title="House Hightower">House Hightower</a>, who hold fealty to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tyrell","link":"House Tyrell","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tyrell]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">House Tyrell</a> of Highgarden, from the eponymous <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hightower","link":"The Hightower","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Hightower|Hightower]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/The_Hightower" title="The Hightower">Hightower</a>. The city is also notable as the site of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Citadel","link":"Citadel","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Citadel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Citadel" title="Citadel" class="mw-redirect">Citadel</a>, the home of the order of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"maesters","link":"maesters","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[maesters]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">maesters</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/locations\/oldtown\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, Oldtown entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" /> It is also the site of the Starry Sept, the former center of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a> in Westeros before it was moved to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Sept of Baelor","link":"Great Sept of Baelor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Sept of Baelor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Great_Sept_of_Baelor" title="Great Sept of Baelor">Great Sept of Baelor</a> in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>.

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Oldtown was the largest city in Westeros before the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"House Targaryen","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Targaryen|Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/House_Targaryen" title="House Targaryen">Targaryen</a> conquest. It survived for so long by canny politicking by the ruling Hightowers, who trace their ancestry back to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a>. When the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Andals","link":"Andals","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Andals]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Andals" title="Andals">Andals</a> invaded, the Hightowers, then kings in their own right, welcomed them openly and swore fealty to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King of the Reach","link":"King of the Reach","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King of the Reach]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/King_of_the_Reach" title="King of the Reach">King of the Reach</a>. Oldtown's pre-eminence was confirmed when the maesters constructed the Citadel within the city and the Faith of the Seven built the Starry Sept there.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">During the invasion of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon the Conqueror","link":"Aegon the Conqueror","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon the Conqueror]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Aegon_the_Conqueror" title="Aegon the Conqueror" class="mw-redirect">Aegon the Conqueror</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"High Septon","link":"High Septon (title)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[High Septon (title)|High Septon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/High_Septon_(title)" title="High Septon (title)" class="mw-redirect">High Septon</a> of the time prayed and fasted for seven days and seven nights on whether the Hightowers should resist Aegon's army. He received a vision showing Oldtown's destruction should they attempt this. At his recommendation, the then-Lord Hightower threw open the city gates and welcomed Aegon into the city with open arms. Aegon dated the first day of his reign to his welcome into Oldtown.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Oldtown is almost as large and populous as King's Landing, but is far cleaner, more beautiful and more efficiently run. King's Landing was rapidly constructed within the first century of rule by the Targaryen dynasty. This rapid expansion didn't keep pace with organized "city-planning", resulting in numerous slums forming such as the district known as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Flea Bottom","link":"Flea Bottom","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Flea Bottom]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Flea_Bottom" title="Flea Bottom">Flea Bottom</a>. Many of these slum districts are poorly organized, ugly, and filthy because their placement wasn't thought out during the rush to slap down new buildings. In contrast, Oldtown is thousands of years old and grew only in incremental steps which were well planned out beforehand. The result is that Oldtown is a much better organized and beautiful city without the squalid slums of King's Landing, while being only slightly smaller than the capital itself.  Oldtown also has its own <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"City Watch","link":"City Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[City Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">City Watch</a>, separate from the City Watch of King's Landing.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Oldtown is famed for its meandering, cobbled streets and many fine inns and septs. It has an immense port which plays host to ships from across the Seven Kingdoms, as well as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> and even the distant <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Islands","link":"Summer Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Summer_Islands" title="Summer Islands">Summer Islands</a>. The port and the entire city is dominated by the High Tower, a massive, seven-sided, stepped lighthouse rising from Battle Island. More than 800 feet high, it is the tallest structure in Westeros, higher even than <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>. It serves as House Hightower's keep, as well as a lighthouse.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Oldtown has had the misfortune, as a large city, of being struck twice by major plagues in the past century.  In 209 AL, a great pandemic known as the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Great Spring Sickness","link":"Great Spring Sickness","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Spring Sickness]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Great_Spring_Sickness" title="Great Spring Sickness">Great Spring Sickness</a> swept across all of the Seven Kingdoms.  It killed tens of thousands, particularly in the major cities.  It was bad in Lannisport, worse in Oldtown, but worst of all in King's Landing, where two out of every five persons died.  The death count wasn't quite as severe in Oldtown, but still high.  Only one generation later, during Grand Maester Pycelle's youth (220s to 230s AL), Oldtown itself was hit by an even worse disease, the "grey plague" (a more deadly cousin of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Greyscale","link":"Greyscale","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Greyscale]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Greyscale" title="Greyscale">Greyscale</a>).  According to Pycelle, the outbreak killed up to half of Oldtown's population, and three quarters of the maesters in the Citadel.  The only reason it didn't become another, even more destructive pandemic is because Lord Quenton Hightower ordered the gates of the city sealed and all ships in the harbor burned to prevent the disease being spread further.  While this did prevent another pandemic, many trapped inside the city's quarantine caught the disease from those who were already sick, and perished.  After the quarantine ended, the survivors dragged Lord Quenton and his young son from their horses and slit their throats.  By the time of the War of the Five Kings, some seventy years later, Oldtown's population is slightly smaller than that of King's Landing, but it may have been larger over a century ago, before the double-blow of these two epidemics, and when King's Landing was smaller.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">No one knows exactly old the city really is:  fading back into the mists of history and oral tradition, there was always a major settlement at the mouth of the Honeywine (though it did grow and expand considerably over the millennia).  The First Men apparently founded the original town that later became a city relatively soon after they first began migrating into Westeros 12,000 years ago, across the nearby land-bridge at the time linking <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dorne","link":"Dorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a> and Essos.  A few maesters argue that there may have been even earlier settlements at the mouth of the Honeywine that the First Men built over - perhaps a trading post maintained by anyone from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrians","link":"Valyrians","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrians]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Valyrians" title="Valyrians">Valyrians</a> to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Rhoynar","link":"Rhoynar","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Rhoynar]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Rhoynar" title="Rhoynar">Rhoynar</a> to the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Summer Islands","link":"Summer Islands","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Summer Islands]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Summer_Islands" title="Summer Islands">Summer Islands</a> (or a combination of them), where their fishing fleets could take on fresh water and perhaps even trade with the non-human races that dominated Westeros's wild forests at the time (the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Children of the Forest","link":"Children of the Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Children of the Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Children_of_the_Forest" title="Children of the Forest">Children of the Forest</a> and the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Giants","link":"Giants","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Giants]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-48497645556cca8670c79d" href="/wiki/Giants" title="Giants">Giants</a>). The base of the Hightower and underground levels seem to be the remnant of some ancient structure, made of an oily black stone found in a few other ruins across the world.  The evidence, however, is very sketchy and inconclusive.
</p>
