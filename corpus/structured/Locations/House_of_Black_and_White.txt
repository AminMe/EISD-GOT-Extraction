<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Structure</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The sole entrance to the temple is a door, one half ebony, the other pale <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"weirwood","link":"weirwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[weirwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Weirwood" title="Weirwood">weirwood</a> - thus one black door and one white door.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> Sanctuary </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The interior of the House features a central sanctuary with a large pool in the center. The water filling the pool is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"poisoned","link":"Poison","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Poison|poisoned]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisoned</a>, and is given out to those who are suffering and come to the temple to seek the release of death.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The sanctuary is lined with statues of many gods from many different faiths, from across both <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Essos","link":"Essos","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Essos]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Westeros","link":"Westeros","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Westeros]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. Specifically they are gods that represent death and the unknown, such as <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stranger","link":"the Stranger","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Stranger]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/The_Stranger" title="The Stranger">the Stranger</a> from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Faith of the Seven","link":"Faith of the Seven","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Faith of the Seven]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a>. The Faceless Men believe that all of these death gods are really one god who has revealed Himself to humanity in different ways: the Many Faced God of Death.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[High Sparrow (episode)|High Sparrow]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->The deepest level of the House of Black and White is the Hall of Faces, an enormous vault with stone pillars, where the faces of the hundreds who die in the House of Black and White are kept to be employed as the basis for the face-changing abilities of the Faceless Men.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> History </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 5","link":"Season 5","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">Season 5</a> </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> is brought to the House of Black and White upon her arrival to Braavos. She is turned away by the building's <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"doorkeeper","link":"Jaqen H'ghar","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaqen H'ghar|doorkeeper]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Jaqen_H'ghar" title="Jaqen H'ghar">doorkeeper</a>, a priest, but remained in the vicinity for several days hoping to be let in. After a very brief stint as a street urchin, the doorkeeper retrieves her and allows her entry into the House.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The House of Black and White]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Jaqen allows Arya to train as a Faceless Man, as long as she can become "no one", which means ridding herself of all things that belong to herself. Arya throws her possessions into the ocean, except for her sword, which she hides in the beach rocks. The Waif, believes Arya to not be ready, however Jaqen implies whether or not it is all the same to the Many-Faced God. Her training is slow, to only cleaning the temple and washing the bodies of the dead. She is unknowingly tested into playing the game of faces, which she does not understand at first but slowly learns what it means to become no one. After passing Jaqen's game, she is allowed into the Hall of Faces, then she is given a task to assassinate a gambler who works at the docks.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Arya gives up on her contract as she spots Meryn Trant, one of Arya's personal targets. She lies to Jaqen and continues to follow Trant. Arya takes a face from the hall and uses it to disguise as one of Trant's underaged brothel victims. As Trant dies, Arya reveals herself to him.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After returning the face to the hall she is caught by Jaqen and the Waif. Trant's life was not hers to take, and as punishment, only death can pay for life. Jaqen drinks poison that Arya thought was meant for her and dies. Arya is horrified by the death of her "friend", only to find out that the waif was now "Jaqen". He explains that Jaqen was no one, something that Arya compromised for herself. Arya removes the dead Jaqen's face to find even more faces underneath. Jaqen was never a real person, just a face. And faces can only be used by those who are "no one". To "someone", a face is poison, and Arya Stark is rendered blind.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Behind the scenes</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Hall of Faces was actually built as a fully realized set: instead of making one pillar slightly taller than the actors filled with masks and then doubling it up digitally, multiple pillars over thirty feet tall were built to fill out the entire room. The production team even invested the time and resources to make fully 600 <i>individualized</i> faces masks in this set, each unique and handcrafted.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[https:\/\/www.youtube.com\/watch?v=YrNiER8xWWE ]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the House of Black and White sits upon a rocky knoll made of dark grey stone. It has no windows and has a black tile roof. Its wooden doors are twelve feet high and carved. The left door is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"weirwood","link":"weirwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[weirwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Weirwood" title="Weirwood">weirwood</a>, the right ebony. In the center of the doors is a carved moon face ebony on weirwood, weirwood on ebony. Its grey stone steps lead down to the dock.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, the <i>left</i> door at the entryway is made of white weirwood, and the <i>right</i> side door is made of black ebony. The TV series reversed this, for unknown reasons, so that the right door is white and the left door is black.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the center of the main room on the main floor lies a pool ten feet across. Statues of gods stand around the room. There are statues of thirty gods in all. Among them are the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Weeping Woman","link":"Weeping Woman","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Weeping Woman]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Weeping_Woman" title="Weeping Woman">Weeping Woman</a>, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lion of Night","link":"Lion of Night","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lion of Night]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/Lion_of_Night" title="Lion of Night">Lion of Night</a>, Hooded Wayfarer, Bakkalon, the Moon-Pale Maiden, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Stranger","link":"The Seven","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Seven|the Stranger]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-29877412956cca84885235" href="/wiki/The_Seven" title="The Seven">the Stranger</a>, and the Merling King.
</p>
