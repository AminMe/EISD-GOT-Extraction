<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} --><b>Eastwatch</b>, or <b>Eastwatch-by-the-Sea</b>, is a castle and port located at the far eastern end of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>, where the Wall drops into an inlet of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Shivering Sea","link":"Shivering Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Shivering Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Shivering_Sea" title="Shivering Sea">Shivering Sea</a> called the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Bay of Seals","link":"Bay of Seals","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Bay of Seals]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Bay_of_Seals" title="Bay of Seals">Bay of Seals</a>. There are nineteen castles along the Wall, but as their numbers fell over the centuries the Night's Watch had to abandon sixteen of them. Eastwatch-by-the-Sea is one of only three manned castles left on <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>, along with <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Castle Black","link":"Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Shadow Tower","link":"The Shadow Tower","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Shadow Tower]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/The_Shadow_Tower" title="The Shadow Tower">The Shadow Tower</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">As the only castle on the Wall located on the sea, Eastwatch-by-the-Sea serves as the port for the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. The capital city of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, as well as the cities of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"White Harbor","link":"White Harbor","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[White Harbor]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/White_Harbor" title="White Harbor">White Harbor</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Gulltown","link":"Gulltown","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Gulltown]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Gulltown" title="Gulltown">Gulltown</a>, are also located on the eastern coast of the continent, so it is relatively easy for provisions and news to reach Eastwatch.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season1\/#!\/locations\/castle-black\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, Castle Black entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />

<h3 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 5","link":"Season 5","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 5]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Season_5" title="Season 5">Season 5</a> </h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">During his stay at Castle Black while organising his campaign in the North, Stannis Baratheon advises the recently elected Lord Commander of the Night's Watch Jon Snow to appoint <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Alliser Thorne","link":"Alliser Thorne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Alliser Thorne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Alliser_Thorne" title="Alliser Thorne">Alliser Thorne</a> as commander of Eastwatch, to keep him from causing trouble at Castle Black.  Jon instead says he intends to keep Alliser close where he can keep an eye on him, and instead treats Alliser with respect by making him the new <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Ranger","link":"First Ranger","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Ranger]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/First_Ranger" title="First Ranger">First Ranger</a> in recognition of his service.  Cotter Pyke presumably retains command of Eastwatch.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[High Sparrow (episode)]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, Eastwatch-by-the-Sea serves as the main resupply post for the Night's Watch, with ships stopping there from King's Landing, the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Cities","link":"Free Cities","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Cities]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> far to the south, the island of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ibben","link":"Ibben","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ibben]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Ibben" title="Ibben">Ibben</a>, and even some wildling fisherfolk who are permitted to trade there. There is also some clandestine smuggling going on there, which is tolerated only as far as that it helps the Watch. The Night's Watch possesses a small "fleet" of ships at Eastwatch, but they are not frequently used and thus often left unmanned and tied up at anchor.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">At the start of the books, less than 200 soldiers are based at the Eastwatch.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the novels, it is specified that when King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stannis Baratheon","link":"Stannis Baratheon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stannis Baratheon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a> sailed to the Wall to save the Night's Watch during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of Castle Black","link":"Battle of Castle Black","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of Castle Black]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/Battle_of_Castle_Black" title="Battle of Castle Black">Battle of Castle Black</a>, his forces landed at Eastwatch, then proceeded west along the north side of the Wall until they reached Castle Black.  Black brothers from Eastwatch came with them, to lead them along Ranger paths used in scouting beyond the Wall, so they could reach Castle Black in time.  
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Naming</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the novels, the castle is named Eastwatch-by-the-Sea almost constantly. In the TV series, it is called "Eastwatch" the one time it is mentioned in dialogue in the first season, as well as on the map on the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"HBO","link":"HBO","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[HBO]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-24553309656cca880b4cc5" href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> website. However, from Season 2 onwards it has usually been called by the full name "Eastwatch-by-the-Sea", indicating that the two terms are interchangeable.
</p>
