<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137884955956cca8dab3fb8" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, there is no mention of a geographic feature called "Osric's Hill" near Craster's Keep.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The only mention of anything or anyone by the name of "Osric" in the books is "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Osric Stark","link":"Osric Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Osric Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137884955956cca8dab3fb8" href="/wiki/Osric_Stark" title="Osric Stark">Osric Stark</a>", a former <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","link":"Lord Commander of the Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord Commander of the Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137884955956cca8dab3fb8" href="/wiki/Lord_Commander_of_the_Night's_Watch" title="Lord Commander of the Night's Watch">Lord Commander of the Night's Watch</a> four hundred years before the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen Conquest","link":"War of Conquest","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[War of Conquest|Targaryen Conquest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-137884955956cca8dab3fb8" href="/wiki/War_of_Conquest" title="War of Conquest">Targaryen Conquest</a>, meaning he lived about seven hundred years before the War of the Five Kings.  Osric was apparently the youngest Lord Commander in recorded history, being elected when he was only 10 years old - though he served in the office for another 60 years after that.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">It seems that the TV writers invented "Osric's Hill" as a name for a height that Jon could see Mance's army from, and that they chose the name "Osric" because plausibly, in the TV-continuity, it is a geographic feature that the Night's Watch itself named after one of their famous Lord Commanders from the past (for example, that Osric fought a large battle against wildlings there, etc.).
</p>
