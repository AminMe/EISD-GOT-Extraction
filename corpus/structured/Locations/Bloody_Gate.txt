<h2>History</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3>Background</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">The Bloody Gate is a series of battlements placed across the mountainous <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Eastern Road","link":"Eastern Road","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Eastern Road]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Eastern_Road" title="Eastern Road">Eastern Road</a> as it winds its way into the Vale of Arryn from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Mountains of the Moon","link":"Mountains of the Moon","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Mountains of the Moon]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Mountains_of_the_Moon" title="Mountains of the Moon">Mountains of the Moon</a>.  The gate is built along a natural choke point where the road narrows significantly into a tight ravine, so that only a few people can walk abreast.  There are two long parapets built into the stone of the mountains. The pass, narrow where it meets the gate, is watched over by twin watchtowers, which are joined by a covered bridge of grey stone that arches above the road.  Archers are positioned atop the cliffs along the length of the narrow ravine, and train their aim on any who approach the gate.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Bloody Gate is commanded by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Knight of the Gate","link":"Knight of the Gate","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Knight of the Gate]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Knight_of_the_Gate" title="Knight of the Gate">Knight of the Gate</a>, a position appointed by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lord of the Eyrie","link":"Lord of the Eyrie","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lord of the Eyrie]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Lord_of_the_Eyrie" title="Lord of the Eyrie">Lord of the Eyrie</a>. The Knight asks all travelers to identify themselves with the traditional line, "Who would pass the Bloody Gate?"
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Bloody Gate has been the primary defensive strongpoint of the Vale for thousands of years. According to myth, more than a dozen armies were destroyed trying to take it during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Age of Heroes","link":"Age of Heroes","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Age of Heroes]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Age_of_Heroes" title="Age of Heroes">Age of Heroes</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[[The Vale (Histories & Lore)]]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 4","link":"Season 4","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 4]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">Season 4</a></h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">After fleeing <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King Joffrey's assassination","link":"Purple Wedding","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Purple Wedding|King Joffrey's assassination]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Purple_Wedding" title="Purple Wedding">King Joffrey's assassination</a>  in <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"King's Landing","link":"King's Landing","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[King's Landing]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/King's_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sansa Stark","link":"Sansa Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sansa Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> is led to the Vale by <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Petyr \"Littlefinger\" Baelish","link":"Petyr Baelish","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Petyr Baelish|Petyr \"Littlefinger\" Baelish]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr "Littlefinger" Baelish</a> in his ship.  Rather than risk detection by entering the Vale through a major port, however, they apparently landed near the mouth of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Trident","link":"Trident","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Trident]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Trident" title="Trident" class="mw-redirect">Trident</a> River to enter the Vale by land from the west, posing as common travelers.  Baelish identifies himsef to the Knight of the Gate, Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Donnel Waynwood","link":"Donnel Waynwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Donnel Waynwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Donnel_Waynwood" title="Donnel Waynwood">Donnel Waynwood</a>, and claims that the girl with him is his niece "Alayne".<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[First of His Name]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Later, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Arya Stark","link":"Arya Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Arya Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Sandor Clegane","link":"Sandor Clegane","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Sandor Clegane]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor Clegane</a> arrive at the Bloody Gate as they head to the Eyrie, where Sandor hopes to ransom Arya to her aunt <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Lysa Arryn","link":"Lysa Arryn","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Lysa Arryn]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Lysa_Arryn" title="Lysa Arryn">Lysa Arryn</a>, her one remaining relative who is both free and wealthy.  When Ser Donnel asks him the traditional line "Who would pass the Bloody Gate?", Sandor quips "The bloody Hound!" in response, and announces that Lysa's niece Arya is his traveling companion.  Ser Donnel respectfully says that if that is the case, she has his condolences, as Lady Lysa died only three days before (secretly, pushed out of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Moon Door","link":"Moon Door","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Moon Door]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Moon_Door" title="Moon Door" class="mw-redirect">Moon Door</a> by Baelish himself).  Sandor and Arya are momentarily stunned at this last in a long line of disappointments, particularly after the rest of Arya's family was killed at the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Red Wedding","link":"Red Wedding","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Red Wedding]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a> just as they arrived.  Arya bursts out giggling uncontrollably at the irony of her misfortune - but then continues to laugh at Sandor's bad luck, because he was so sure that his plan to ransom her to Lysa would work.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Mountain and the Viper]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />  They both turn back and travel away from the gate, and are not followed by the guards.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[The Children]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Knight of the Gate during the first novel is <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ser","link":"Ser","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ser]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Ser" title="Ser" class="mw-redirect">Ser</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Brynden Tully","link":"Brynden Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Brynden Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Brynden_Tully" title="Brynden Tully">Brynden Tully</a>. After Ser Brynden renounces the post to aid <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Tully","link":"House Tully","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Tully]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/House_Tully" title="House Tully">House Tully</a>, the title is granted to Ser <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Donnel","link":"Donnel Waynwood","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Donnel Waynwood|Donnel]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Donnel_Waynwood" title="Donnel Waynwood">Donnel</a> of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"House Waynwood","link":"House Waynwood","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[House Waynwood]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/House_Waynwood" title="House Waynwood">House Waynwood</a>.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books the Bloody Gate is first depicted in the first novel, <i>​A Game of Thrones</i>​, when <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Catelyn Stark","link":"Catelyn Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Catelyn Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> takes <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tyrion Lannister","link":"Tyrion Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tyrion Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> to the Eyrie for she suspects he has attempted on Bran's life (at the time point corresponding to "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Wolf and the Lion","link":"The Wolf and the Lion","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Wolf and the Lion]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/The_Wolf_and_the_Lion" title="The Wolf and the Lion">The Wolf and the Lion</a>"), however the Bloody Gate is absent from <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Season 1","link":"Season 1","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Season 1]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">Season 1</a>, and is not introduced until Season 4.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The proximity of the Bloody Gate to the Eyrie in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First of His Name","link":"First of His Name","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First of His Name]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/First_of_His_Name" title="First of His Name">First of His Name</a>" indicates that this location has been seemingly merged with the Gates of the Moon. In the books, the Eyrie is built at the peak of a mountain known as the Giant's Lance, while another castle known as the Gates of the Moon stands at the foot of the mountain, guarding the only pass to the summit. During the winter years, the Arryns move their seat to the Gates of the Moon, but then return to the Eyrie when summer arrives.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Then again, in "<a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"The Children","link":"The Children","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[The Children]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-100428808656cca89316bd3" href="/wiki/The_Children" title="The Children">The Children</a>", Brienne and Arya state that their current location is about 30 miles west of the Eyrie, and 10 miles west of the Bloody Gate.  This indicates that the Eyrie and Bloody Gate are still located about 20 miles apart in the TV continuity.  Therefore it isn't clear if the Bloody Gate in the TV series is meant to be at the very foot of the mountain that the Eyrie is located on - though the Eyrie can still be <i>seen</i> in the distance from the Bloody Gate.
</p>
