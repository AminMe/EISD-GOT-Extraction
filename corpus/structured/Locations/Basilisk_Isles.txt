<h2 data-rte-spaces-before="1" data-rte-spaces-after="1" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"> In the books </h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Basilisk Isles are the same. To the south lies Zamettar, in the Sothoryos mainland, to the east Ax Isle and to the southwest, the Isle of Toads. 
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Basilisk Isles are dangerous pirate dens even worse than the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Stepstones","link":"Stepstones","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Stepstones]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Stepstones" title="Stepstones">Stepstones</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Narrow Sea","link":"Narrow Sea","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Narrow Sea]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a>.  They are in a perfect location to serve as bases for pirates to prey on the major shipping lanes rounding the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Peninsula","link":"Valyrian Peninsula","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Peninsula]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Peninsula" title="Valyrian Peninsula">Valyrian Peninsula</a>, who then sell captured crews off into slavery.  Life in the Basilisk Isles is nasty, brutish, and short.  It is said that the mud-and-blood towns of the Basilisk Isles teem with escaped <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"slaves","link":"Slavery","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Slavery|slaves]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a>, slavers, skinners, <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"whores","link":"Prostitution","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Prostitution|whores]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">whores</a>, hunters, "brindled men", and worse.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ghiscari Empire","link":"Ghiscari Empire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghiscari Empire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Ghiscari_Empire" title="Ghiscari Empire">Ghiscari Empire</a> built outposts in the Basilisk Isles over 5,000 years ago, including the city Gogossos on the Isle of Tears which was used as a transport hub for slaver raids against the mainland.  The city was captured by the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Valyrian Freehold","link":"Valyrian Freehold","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Valyrian Freehold]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Valyrian_Freehold" title="Valyrian Freehold">Valyrian Freehold</a> in the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Third Ghiscari War","link":"Ghiscari Wars","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ghiscari Wars|Third Ghiscari War]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Ghiscari_Wars" title="Ghiscari Wars">Third Ghiscari War</a>.  The now-Valyrian slaving outposts in the Basilisk Isles survived the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Doom of Valyria","link":"Doom of Valyria","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Doom of Valyria]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Doom_of_Valyria" title="Doom of Valyria">Doom of Valyria</a> (which occurred about 400 years before the War of the Five Kings), but about eighty years later a terrible plague swept through the isles, completely depopulating them.  Gogossos was left in ruins ever since.  
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">After the plague the isles were shunned for a full century, but eventually pirates began to resettle them (during the long reign of King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Jaehaerys I Targaryen","link":"Jaehaerys I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Jaehaerys I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-23184520156cca8951aaac" href="/wiki/Jaehaerys_I_Targaryen" title="Jaehaerys I Targaryen">Jaehaerys I Targaryen</a> in Westeros).  Ever since, various lawless pirate fiefdoms have maintained shifting control over different islands in the chain.  Every generation or so they grow to be such a problem that fleets from the Free Cities will join in alliance to scour them out, killing hundreds of pirates, but new pirate dens quickly pop up again to replace them like mushrooms in the night.
</p>
