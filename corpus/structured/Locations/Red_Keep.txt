<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Specific Locations</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-filler="true"></p><h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Great Hall</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Great Hall is the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"massive throne room","link":"Great Hall (Red Keep)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Great Hall (Red Keep)|massive throne room]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Great_Hall_(Red_Keep)" title="Great Hall (Red Keep)" class="mw-redirect">massive throne room</a> of the Red Keep and where the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Iron Throne","link":"Iron Throne","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Iron Throne]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> stands. The King (or his Hand, in the event of the King's absence), holds court and receives petitioners within the Great Hall. It is also employed for official ceremonies.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Maegor's Holdfast</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maegor's Holdfast","link":"Maegor's Holdfast","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maegor's Holdfast]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Maegor's_Holdfast" title="Maegor's Holdfast">Maegor's Holdfast</a> is a stout tower in the center of the Red Keep. It was named after King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Maegor Targaryen","link":"Maegor Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Maegor Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Maegor_Targaryen" title="Maegor Targaryen">Maegor Targaryen</a>, who built the Red Keep. It serves as a redoubt when the city is attacked. The royal apartments are also located in Maegor's Holdfast.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Maegor's Holdfast served as a refuge for Queen Cersei and the highborn ladies of the Red Keep during the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Battle of the Blackwater","link":"Battle of the Blackwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Battle of the Blackwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a>.
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Tower of the Hand</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tower of the Hand","link":"Tower of the Hand","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tower of the Hand]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Tower_of_the_Hand" title="Tower of the Hand">Tower of the Hand</a> is the location of the quarters and offices of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>. The Hand's family, household and guards also live and work in the Tower of the Hand.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">When <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Tywin Lannister","link":"Tywin Lannister","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Tywin Lannister]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a> assumes his Hand duties for King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Joffrey","link":"Joffrey","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Joffrey]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Joffrey" title="Joffrey" class="mw-redirect">Joffrey</a> he arranges for the meetings of the small council to be held in the Tower of the Hand to assert his own authority.
</p>

<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Traitor's Walk</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Traitor's Walk is an outer corridor on one of the sides of the Red Keep with view to one of its outer walls. The heads of criminals and traitors are placed on spikes over said wall, visible both to the exterior of the Red Keep and whoever passes through the corridor.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Following the arrest and execution of <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Ned Stark","link":"Ned Stark","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Ned Stark]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Ned_Stark" title="Ned Stark" class="mw-redirect">Ned Stark</a>, his head and those of his entire household were placed on the wall beside the Traitor's Walk.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>\"[[Fire and Blood]]\"<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The dungeons</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The dungeons of the Red Keep are divided in four levels:
</p>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">On the upper level are cells with high narrow windows where common criminals are confined together.
</li></ul>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>The second level has smaller, personal cells without windows for highborn captives and valuable <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"hostage","link":"hostage","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[hostage]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Hostage" title="Hostage">hostages</a>.
</li></ul>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li data-rte-spaces-before="1">The <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Black Cells","link":"Black Cells","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Black Cells]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Black_Cells" title="Black Cells">Black Cells</a> are the third level. Prisoners accused of treason and other high crimes are kept within the Black Cells, as was the case of Ned Stark. <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Varys","link":"Varys","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Varys]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> has access to the Black Cells, though he dons the disguise of a gaoler when entering.
</li></ul>
<ul data-rte-empty-lines-before="1"><li>The fourth level is spoken of only in rumors. Once a man is taken here, he never sees the light of day again and is subjected to the most painful torments.
</li></ul>
<h3 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Secret passages</h3>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Red Keep, according to rumors, is full of miles of secret passages running behind the walls and below the floors. Maegor the Cruel had the builders of the Red Keep executed after the castle was completed to keep the secret of the passageways.
</p>

<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true">In <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i>, the Red Keep is a major castle complex located at the south-eastern corner of the city of King's Landing, directly overlooking the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackwater Rush","link":"Blackwater Rush","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackwater Rush]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Blackwater_Rush" title="Blackwater Rush">Blackwater Rush</a> and <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Blackwater Bay","link":"Blackwater Bay","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Blackwater Bay]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Blackwater_Bay" title="Blackwater Bay">Blackwater Bay</a>. It is built atop Aegon's High Hill, the tallest hill of the city, and affords a panoramic view of the rest of the city. It consists of a number of interconnected buildings, such as the main keep, a secondary castle called Maegor's Holdfast and the Tower of the Hand, the seat of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Hand of the King","link":"Hand of the King","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Hand of the King]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>. White Sword Tower is the base of operations for the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Kingsguard","link":"Kingsguard","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Kingsguard]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>. The Red Keep is so-named due to the redstone bricks used to build it.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Red Keep was built approximately three centuries prior to the events of the series and replaced an earlier wooden fort called "Aegonfort", built by King <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Aegon I Targaryen","link":"Aegon I Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Aegon I Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Aegon_I_Targaryen" title="Aegon I Targaryen">Aegon I Targaryen</a> where he first set foot on the continent and used as his base of operations in his conquest of Westeros. After Aegon deemed the fort unsuitable for rule, he took the royal court to <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Dragonstone","link":"Dragonstone (castle)","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Dragonstone (castle)|Dragonstone]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Dragonstone_(castle)" title="Dragonstone (castle)">Dragonstone</a>, and ruled there for many years until his death. The construction of the Keep was an immense undertaking, spanning the reign of the first few <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Targaryen","link":"Targaryen","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Targaryen]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-183192314056cca85c03b51" href="/wiki/Targaryen" title="Targaryen" class="mw-redirect">Targaryen</a> kings, although modifications and additions to the castle have been made ever since.
</p>
