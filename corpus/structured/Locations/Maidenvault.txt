<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-126262974756cca8d731be9" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the Maidenvault was so-called because of the three maiden princesses imprisoned there. It is a long, slate-roofed building located behind the royal <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"sept","link":"sept","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[sept]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-126262974756cca8d731be9" href="/wiki/Sept" title="Sept">sept</a> within the Red Keep. Its entry has two tall carved doors. Due to their imprisonment, the three sisters Daena, Elaena, and Rhaena became popularly known as the "Three Maidens in the Tower".
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">While his father was still alive, Baelor had actually been made to marry his eldest sister, Daena. While the wedding ceremony was held, the marriage was never consummated, and after his father died Baelor had it officially annulled.
</p>
