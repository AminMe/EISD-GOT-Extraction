<!-- RTE::{"spaces":0,"type":"LINE_BREAK"} -->The <b>Fist of the First Men</b> is a landmark in the wilderness <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"beyond","link":"Beyond the Wall","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Beyond the Wall|beyond]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/Beyond_the_Wall" title="Beyond the Wall">beyond</a> <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"the Wall","link":"the Wall","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[the Wall]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>. It is an ancient ring fort located at the crown of a defensible round hill with an excellent view of the surrounding countryside. Ranging parties from the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Night's Watch","link":"Night's Watch","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Night's Watch]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/Night's_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> use it to orient themselves and also as a possible fallback position in case of combat with the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Free Folk","link":"Free Folk","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Free Folk]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">Free Folk</a>. It can only be reached by a steep climb on stony ground. It has served as a natural defensive position since the time of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"First Men","link":"First Men","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[First Men]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a>.<img data-rte-meta="{"type":"ext","placeholder":1,"wikitext":"<ref>[http:\/\/viewers-guide.hbo.com\/game-of-thrones\/season2\/#!\/locations\/fist-of-the-first-men\/ HBO viewers guide, season 2 map, Fist of the First Men entry]<\/ref>"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" class="placeholder placeholder-ext" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw==" type="ext" />
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">The Fist is located many days north of the Wall, deep in territory held by the wildlings. It is located at the western edge of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Haunted Forest","link":"Haunted Forest","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Haunted Forest]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/Haunted_Forest" title="Haunted Forest">Haunted Forest</a>, where the foothills of the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Frostfang mountains","link":"Frostfangs","wasblank":false,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Frostfangs|Frostfang mountains]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/Frostfangs" title="Frostfangs">Frostfang mountains</a> begin. It commands a view overlooking the <a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"Milkwater","link":"Milkwater","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[Milkwater]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/Milkwater" title="Milkwater">Milkwater</a> river.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">Physically, the ring fort is a simple circle of large megalithic stones around the top of the hill. The stones are incredibly ancient, the site having been used as a defensive position for well over six thousand years. While the Fist isn't really a castle or even a structure, due to its positioning on a lone hill commanding the surrounding flat plain, it is one of the more defensible positions that the Night's Watch can use north of the Wall.
</p>
<h2 data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books</h2>
<p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the <i><a data-rte-meta="{"type":"internal","text":"A Song of Ice and Fire","link":"A Song of Ice and Fire","wasblank":true,"noforce":true,"wikitext":"[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]"}" data-rte-instance="130814-200326818356cca86015aa5" href="/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire" title="A Song of Ice and Fire">A Song of Ice and Fire</a></i> novels, the ring fort was built by the First Men well over six thousand years ago, centuries before the Andals invaded Westeros. It is called "The Fist" of the First Men, because the hill looks like a fist thrust into the sky from the flat landscape surrounding it, and the jagged remaining stones circling the top look like the knuckles of a fist.
</p><p data-rte-fromparser="true" data-rte-empty-lines-before="1">In the books, the trees of the Haunted Forest come very close to the base of the Fist. In the TV series there is no forest visible near the Fist. This is probably due to the fact that these scenes were filmed on a glacier in Iceland, and there were no trees nearby.
</p>
